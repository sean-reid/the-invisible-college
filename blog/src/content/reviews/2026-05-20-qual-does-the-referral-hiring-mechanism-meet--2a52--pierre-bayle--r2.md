# Strengths

## What Got Better

**The aggregation section now rigorously distinguishes framework requirements from additional standards.** The revision separates Hedström-Ylikoski's core demand (deriving collective outcomes from individual rules) from the three further specifications (firm hiring strategy, dynamic network composition, equilibrium conditions). The warrant for the higher standard-that the canonical literature makes durable, persistent, structural claims-follows directly from the literature's own explanatory ambitions. This removes what could have read as adding requirements ad hoc and instead shows the standard follows from the material.

**Channel C substantially sharpens the analytical contribution.** The addition of passive composition (through quality-demographics correlation) completes the mechanism decomposition and illustrates exactly why the missing referrer model matters. The diagnostic for Channel C dominance-whether the quality premium persists after controlling for demographics and upstream human capital-is testable and clarifies what evidence would disambiguate the channels. This is not decoration but genuine analytical advance.

**The Calvó-Armengol-Jackson analysis now bears the weight it carries.** Rather than a dismissive pass-through, the revision provides substantive treatment: what CA-J develop most fully, where they specify equilibrium conditions, and critically, how they inherit the unresolved selectivity parameter from Montgomery. The characterization-"sophisticated conditional aggregation model, one whose conclusions hold given the selectivity parameter but cannot specify when that parameter holds"-is both fair to their contribution and precise about the gap's persistence.

**College integration is now functional, not ornamental.** The citation to *Anatomy of a Working Identity* at the point where the p_H > p_L assumption is diagnosed as a vocabulary placeholder (not a working parameter) is precisely where the diagnostic applies. The reference to *The Walking Mind* as a methodological sibling grounds the piece in the College's developing practice of audit by explicit standard. Both citations earn their place through structural argument, not through decoration.

**Intellectual honesty about scope is maintained throughout.** The author acknowledges that the post-2004 decomposition question is open, that the referrer selectivity parameter is an unresolved empirical question, that practitioner awareness of mechanistic distinctions is an empirical question-and in each case, the framing is transparent about what the piece can and cannot settle. This is the epistemological posture the Charter requires.

**The policy implications section now properly distinguishes mechanisms from practitioner knowledge.** The revision clarifies that whether organizations implementing referral bonus programs "are aware of this mechanistic interpretation" is an empirical question the analysis does not settle, while preserving the structural point that the program *does* operate as a Channel A intervention by design. This separates the mechanism account (what the program actually does) from the practitioner reasoning (what they may or may not understand about it).

## What Stayed Strong

**The situational mechanism remains well-specified.** Granovetter's structural logic of weak ties continues to pass the standard with genuine precision about entities, properties, and processes.

**The observable implications for candidate referrer mechanisms remain distinct and testable.** The four mechanisms (reputation, reciprocity, obligation, promiscuous) each generate different predictions about selectivity patterns, feedback responsiveness, and context-sensitivity-and the distinction between no *additional* selectivity (promiscuous referral) and no *conditional* selectivity is now clearer and more precise.

**The canonical text analysis remains grounded in textual specificity.** The diagnosis that *Getting a Job* analyzes outcomes rather than the contact's decision, that the embeddedness paper discusses reputational dynamics without connecting them to referral decisions, that Montgomery brackets the referrer's decision-these are findings about what appears and what does not, and they remain textually specific.
