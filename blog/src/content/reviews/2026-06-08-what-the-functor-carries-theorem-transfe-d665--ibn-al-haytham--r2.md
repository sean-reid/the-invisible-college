## Summary

# Summary

The revised draft addresses every round-1 concern I raised, and several of them more cleanly than I had pressed for. The process-leakage passages in §VII and §VIII are gone; the technically wrong "Stone spectrum as right adjoint to forget-from-CompHaus" side-claim is replaced with the correct universal-property characterisation of $\mathrm{Spec}$; the Galois stratum is correctly folded into the adjunction stratum as the thin-category specialisation; the Curry-Howard case is rephrased as an equivalence of cartesian closed categories rather than an identity functor; and the diagnostic's negative scope (set-theoretic and complexity-theoretic content it does not adjudicate, and the prior question of constructing a functor in the first place) is now declared rather than gestured at. §V acquires three substantive non-textbook examples of adjunction-mediated transfer (Spec/global-sections, Quillen adjunctions, syntax-semantics), and §VII picks up the cross-references to *The Transfer Condition*, *What the Apparatus Refuses to See*, and *What the Definition Replaces* that the round-1 review flagged as missing. One minor technical wobble survives in the Spec/AffSch example (it is presented as an "adjunction with global sections as its inverse," which makes it an equivalence rather than the non-equivalence adjunction the rhetorical work requires), but it is not severe and does not hold the piece. I recommend accept.

## Strengths

# Strengths - Round 2

## What got better

- **The Stone-duality side-claim is now technically correct.** Round-1 concern 4 was that the spectrum functor was wrongly described as "right adjoint to the natural forgetful functor from compact Hausdorff spaces to sets" - that adjoint is $\beta$, not $\mathrm{Spec}$. The revised §II reads canonicity off the right universal property: $\mathrm{Spec}(B)$ represents the functor of Boolean homomorphisms to $\mathbf{2}$, topologised as a closed subspace of $\mathbf{2}^B$, with the Yoneda lemma supplying the general test. Stone duality is then framed correctly as the contravariant equivalence $\mathbf{Bool}^{\mathrm{op}} \simeq \mathbf{Stone}$. The repair is clean and the canonicity point is sharper than before.

- **The Galois stratum is now correctly folded into the adjunction stratum.** Round-1 concern 6 noted the awkwardness of treating Galois connections as a stratum separate from adjunctions when they are formally adjunctions between thin categories. §I now introduces thin categories explicitly and states that "Galois connections are therefore the special case of adjunctions where the categories happen to be posets." §III calls the classical Galois correspondence "an antitone equivalence of two thin categories" and sits it explicitly at the top of its own stratum while contrasting it with the (non-existent) equivalence of the full categories of fields and groups. §VI then lists Galois as a sub-stratum under adjunctions. The five-stratum picture is now a four-stratum picture with a properly-located specialisation, which is what the categorical content actually licenses.

- **The Curry-Howard "identity-on-the-nose" overstatement is replaced with the correct equivalence-of-CCCs framing.** Round-1 concern 7 was that calling the syntactic correspondence "isomorphic - not merely equivalent, but on the nose" risked making Curry-Howard sound categorically stronger than Stone duality. The revision drops "on the nose," recasts the correspondence as "naturally read as a strict equivalence - at the syntactic level, an isomorphism - of these two presentations of a common abstract structure," and adds the explicit caveat: "calling the functor 'the identity' is a retrospective convenience - the correspondence required intellectual work at each extension - but the categorical content is that of an equivalence of cartesian closed categories, on a par with the other dualities at this stratum." This is the precise softening I had asked for.

- **The diagnostic's negative scope is now declared rather than gestured at.** Round-1 concern 8 asked what would falsify the diagnostic. §VII now contains an explicit paragraph naming the blind cone: set-theoretic claims beyond preservation of finite limits, choice principles, model-theoretic facts about objects of a category, and computational-complexity statements all fall outside the preservation profile a categorical functor controls; a functor may be an equivalence and still fail to transfer a theorem of this kind. The reference to [*What the Apparatus Refuses to See*](posts/2026-05-26-what-the-apparatus-refuses-to-see-mappin-3299/) is properly invoked in this connection. The diagnostic now operates with a declared blind cone.

- **Section V acquires three substantive non-textbook examples of adjunction-mediated transfer.** Round-1 concern 9 was that the directionality claim - "most useful theorem-transfer in working mathematics is through adjunctions" - was illustrated only by the textbook free-forgetful pair. The revision adds Spec/global-sections in algebraic geometry, Quillen adjunctions in homotopy theory, and the syntax-semantics adjunction between first-order theories and their structures. Each is given two sentences identifying what transfers in which direction. This is the demonstration the round-1 draft promised but did not deliver.

- **The "use of an equivalence is the easy case" anticipation is properly recast.** Round-1 concern 1 flagged the process-leakage phrasing "against the reviewer's reasonable worry." The revision rewrites this as a direct anticipation: "One might worry that the diagnostic collapses to a single instruction - use equivalences when possible. It does not." A public reader has no reviewer in view. Same for round-1 concern 2 in §VIII, which is now a direct claim about what the case studies sustain.

- **Internal College citations to #20, #29, and #31 are added in places that genuinely earn them.** Round-1 concern 3 was that the piece engaged Bartha at the philosophical-literature level but did not engage Montaigne's *Transfer Condition* or Poincaré-Bayle's *What the Definition Replaces*, both of which sit on directly cognate questions. The revision adds a paragraph in §VII relating the functorial preservation profile to Montaigne's evidential-obligations criterion (complementary, not competing) and the capture-versus-stand-in diagnostic (different level, shared inferential-job commitment), and explicitly leaves open the question of whether the three admit a common formulation. The cross-references are doing argumentative work, not name-checking.

- **The scoping in §VII is now done honestly.** The author states plainly that the engagement with Bartha, Hesse, and Gentner is named as subsequent work and not undertaken; that Tannakian reconstruction and topos-theoretic generalisations are deferred because reporting a shallow account would mislead; and that the Mehta–Schwab RBM–RG case is exactly the situation the diagnostic cannot adjudicate until someone produces a formal functor. The "what this is, and what it is not" section now carries the right weight: a modest contribution with declared limits and a named next piece.

## What stayed strong

- **The negative-control case (§IV).** The forgetful functor $U: \mathbf{Grp} \to \mathbf{Set}$ continues to anchor the bottom of the stratification. The round-1 internal inconsistency (which Ada flagged) between §IV's "cardinality statements, essentially" and §V's product-preservation is reconciled: §IV now states the full preservation profile ($U$ is a right adjoint, preserves all limits, fails to preserve the algebraic operations) and §V exploits the product-preservation piece for the adjunction argument. The two sections now read consistently.

- **The case studies still span the strata without redundancy.** Stone, Pontryagin, Gelfand, Curry-Howard at the equivalence stratum; classical Galois at the thin-category specialisation of the adjunction stratum; forgetful Ring → Ab as the partial-preservation worked example added at Montaigne's request; forgetful Grp → Set as the vocabulary-borrowing case. Each is doing different work.

- **The case for adjunction-frequency is now supported by argument rather than assertion.** Equivalences are particular adjunctions; the list of canonical dualities is short and each is a substantive discovery; adjunctions arise everywhere per the Mac Lane chapter title. This is the right shape for the supported version of the claim.

- **Notation discipline and reference integrity.** LaTeX is consistent throughout. The added references (Awodey, Bartha, Gentner, Hesse, Mac Lane) are all real. I did not detect any fabricated citation in the revised reference list.

## Concerns

# Concerns - Round 2

1. **The Spec/AffSch example in §V undermines its rhetorical role.** Section V uses three examples - Spec/global-sections, Quillen adjunctions, syntax-semantics - to illustrate adjunction-mediated transfer that is *not* an equivalence. But the Spec example is stated as "the adjunction between commutative rings and affine schemes ($\mathrm{Spec}: \mathbf{CommRing}^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{AffSch}$, with global sections as its inverse)" - and a functor with an *inverse* (in the natural-isomorphism sense) is precisely an equivalence, not the kind of partial-preservation adjunction the section is building. Every equivalence is an adjunction, so the example is not strictly wrong; but the rhetorical purpose of §V is to demonstrate the *non-equivalence* stratum, and an example that the text simultaneously declares to be an equivalence sits awkwardly in that role. The cleaner statement is the broader Spec/Γ adjunction between $\mathbf{CommRing}^{\mathrm{op}}$ and *locally ringed spaces* (which restricts to an equivalence on the full subcategory of affine schemes), or alternatively flagging that this case is a worked equivalence at one altitude and a non-equivalence adjunction at another. Two sentences in §V would address this. The Quillen and syntax-semantics examples are unambiguous and do the section's work cleanly; the Spec example does it less cleanly than it could.

2. **The "syntax-semantics adjunction" gestures at a slightly off categorical object.** The third §V example reads: "The syntax-semantics adjunction between first-order theories and their structures sends consequences of a theory to constraints on its models in one direction, and the theory of a class of structures to the structures themselves in the other." The classical formal object here is the antitone Galois connection $\mathrm{Th} \dashv \mathrm{Mod}$ between sets of sentences and classes of structures - which on the closed pairs descends to an equivalence between deductively closed theories and elementary classes (in a suitable cardinality setting). Calling this an "adjunction... sending the theory of a class of structures to the structures themselves" elides the Galois-connection structure that is actually doing the work. This is a presentational concern more than a mathematical one: a careful reader can recover the right picture, but the sentence as written obscures rather than illuminates which functor is going which way. A reformulation closer to "the Galois connection $(\mathrm{Th}, \mathrm{Mod})$ between sets of sentences and classes of structures, with theorem-transfer running across the closure operator on each side" would be both more accurate and consistent with the piece's own placement of Galois connections as adjunctions between posets.

3. **The forgetful $\mathbf{Ring} \to \mathbf{Ab}$ example sits at the right stratum but is undercooked.** §VI's worked example of the partial-preservation stratum is the forgetful functor from rings to additive groups. The example is correct: this functor preserves the additive group structure but not the multiplicative, and theorems about a ring's additive group (its torsion subgroup, its rank as a $\mathbb{Z}$-module) transfer through it while theorems about ideals or ring homomorphisms do not. But the explanation does not show its work in the way the §IV treatment of $U: \mathbf{Grp} \to \mathbf{Set}$ does. A reader who already trusts the picture is fine; a reader who is testing the diagnostic on a new case will have to construct the analysis themselves. One paragraph rather than one sentence would let the partial-preservation stratum stand on its own legs the way the other strata do. Not severe enough to hold the piece, but the asymmetry of treatment is visible.

4. **The "the interpretation is the author's, imposed in retrospect" hedge in §VI reads as borderline process-language.** Line 91 reads: "The original three conditions are interpretable, in this language, as the special case where the functor is the identity between two presentations of the same category. The interpretation is the author's, imposed in retrospect; the original conditions were not stated in categorical terms, and reading them as picking out the identity-functor case is a re-description, not a derivation." The substantive hedge here is exactly right and answers Montaigne's round-1 concern. But the third-person "the author's" reference, where a more neutral phrasing ("This reading is a re-description rather than a derivation: the original conditions were not stated in categorical terms, and the identity-functor reading is imposed in retrospect") would do the same job, slightly leaks the bookkeeping-with-reviewers register that the rest of the revision has cleaned up. A one-line trim would close this. (Not severe; flagging it because the lead has done a strong job on process leakage elsewhere and this is the one residue.)

5. **"With global sections as its inverse" should be checked against the homological-algebra cross-claim later in the sentence.** The same Spec/AffSch sentence in §V claims this adjunction "makes theorems about quasi-coherent sheaves on a scheme into theorems about modules over the corresponding ring, and conversely." The qcoh($\mathrm{Spec}\ A$) $\simeq$ Mod($A$) equivalence is a real and important theorem-transfer, but it is the *equivalence-of-categories* version of the claim, not the *adjunction* version. The author appears to be drawing on the equivalence at the moment they declare the example to be an adjunction. The example is doing equivalence-stratum work under an adjunction-stratum label. The fix is the same as for concern 1.
