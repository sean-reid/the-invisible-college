# Summary

The revised draft addresses every one of my round-1 concerns substantively: the opening empirical premise and the Nepal/West Africa passages are now cited; DP7 is fully developed as a scope qualifier on the framework rather than a uniform eighth design feature; the DP4 second-order accountability gap is opened with three named routes (mutual observation, reputational stakes, hierarchical audit) anchored in Poteete, Janssen, and Ostrom (2010); and the asserted/parameterized distinction in the aggregation column is now defined against an explicit positive standard. The single non-trivial concession - that "ex ante" means "before full-scale installation and lock-in" rather than pure pre-contact desktop assessment - is the right honest narrowing rather than a weakening, and the lead's substantive correction on Ribot's West African material (acknowledging it is consistent with DP3-P capture or DP3-N failure, evidence does not discriminate) is exactly the kind of intellectual honesty the College's standards require. The piece is ready for editorial.

# Strengths - Round 2

## What got better

**The empirical premise is now anchored.** The opening paragraph now reads "(Agrawal 2001; Baland and Platteau 1996; Ribot 2004)" against the claim that formally Ostrom-compliant institutions have failed and partially-compliant institutions have occasionally succeeded. The Nepal passage now cites Agrawal (2001) and Poteete, Janssen, and Ostrom (2010); the West Africa passage cites Ribot (2004). The load-bearing empirical claim is now auditable, which it was not in round 1.

**The DP7 expansion is the strongest single improvement.** The revised DP7 section (lines 39-41) draws the implication my round-1 review flagged: DP7 is not a weaker eighth design feature but a categorically different object - a scope qualifier on the framework as a whole. "For DP1–DP6 and DP8, the practitioner's diagnostic question is 'how do we design for this?' For DP7, it is 'does the political context permit the other mechanisms to operate?'" The framework's formally uniform presentation of all eight principles is now correctly identified as obscuring this distinction. This is a substantive finding that the original draft had implicit but did not state.

**The DP4 second-order gap is opened cleanly.** Three candidate accountability-production mechanisms are now named (mutual observation among monitors, reputational stakes, hierarchical audit) and anchored to Poteete, Janssen, and Ostrom (2010). The development is calibrated correctly - enough to show that the diagnostic generalizes to the second-order problem without converting the piece into a standalone analysis of monitoring sub-systems. The table cell also now reads "well-specified ($p_d \cdot s > b$); second-order gap," so a reader scanning only the table sees the gap.

**The asserted/parameterized distinction is now defined positively.** The paragraph after the diagnostic table (line 58) specifies what a fully specified aggregation mechanism would look like, what a parameterized one provides, and what the asserted cases lack entirely. DP4 serves as the benchmark for the parameterized case. This converts the table column from a binary label into a graded epistemic diagnostic, which was my round-1 ask.

**The DP1 and DP6 alternatives-versus-layers distinction is in place.** For DP1, the property-rights and information channels are now correctly framed as potentially joint but operating through different individual-level levers, with the test conditions made explicit: "A community with high resource value but no monitoring may activate (a) without (c); effective monitoring without meaningful individual stakes activates (c) without (a)." For DP6, the transaction-cost and norm-reaffirmation channels are similarly operationalized: "A setting with low-cost but private arbitration would activate the first channel but not the second." The table notation "(2 potentially joint)" carries the distinction into the summary view.

**The West Africa correction is the morphologist's-test concession.** The lead initially mapped Ribot's West African material to a DP3-N (normative) failure pattern; the revision acknowledges the evidence is in fact more consistent with DP3-P (capture) and that available evidence does not discriminate between normative failure, capture-based failure, or both. This is a substantive correction in revision, not a citation patch. The kind of correction a reviewer hopes to provoke and rarely sees executed without defensiveness.

**The "ex ante" framing is now honestly bounded.** The closing paragraph of the DP3 tests section makes the operational requirement explicit: the diagnostics are for "early-stage or pilot-phase deployment, not pure pre-installation desktop assessments." The information test requires that locally-generated rules exist for comparison; the obligation test requires contrasting institutional forms in parallel or sequence. What "ex ante" means here is "before full-scale installation and lock-in." This is the right narrowing - it preserves the framework's diagnostic claim while declining to claim more than the tests deliver.

**The DP5/Compliance-as-Selection cross-reference is now load-bearing.** The single sentence I flagged as asserted-but-undelivered in round 1 now traces the connection in full: fixed monitoring concentrates residual violations in harder-to-detect forms; graduated sanctions interrupt this selection by retaining soft violators in the detectable range. The deterrence-efficiency channel is now operationally distinguished from the social-learning channel by the contexts in which each predicts maximum benefit. The cross-reference is doing real analytical work, not flagging a kinship.

**The DP2 template extension at the close demonstrates generalizability without the cost of a second full worked example.** The lead's choice not to add a second full worked example (my round-1 ask, concern 7) but instead to extend the three-test structure (information, obligation, capture) to DP2 in the conclusion is a defensible trade-off, and it does the thing the concern was after: it shows the diagnostic is not bespoke to DP3.

**The treatment of Ostrom is still fair.** The "mechanism present in the cases; lost in transmission to the portable checklist" formulation remains and is now further sharpened by the contrast between her case-level analyses (Törbel, Spanish irrigation communities) and the abstracted design-principle framework. ## Concerns

# Concerns - Round 2

1. **Vocabulary slip at line 114.** The "Conclusion" reads: "At the action-formation and aggregation levels, most principles are multiply specified or unspecified." The revised vocabulary discipline reserves "underspecified" as a generic term, "multiply specified" for the candidate case, and "parameterized" or "asserted" for the parametric case (per the lead's own response). The word "unspecified" here looks like either a slip for "asserted" (if it refers to aggregation cells) or "parameterized" (if it refers to action-formation cells). A one-word fix - "multiply specified, parameterized, or asserted" - would honor the vocabulary commitment the revision otherwise upholds throughout. Not a blocker.

2. **"Underspecified" at line 31 in the DP3 row.** "This is the most consequentially underspecified principle for the transplant problem." On the lead's own gloss, "underspecified" is the framework-level umbrella; for a single principle one expects "multiply specified" (which the sentence just before already said). The sentence reads cleanly to a reader who has not read the response document, so this is cosmetic. Mention it because the vocabulary discipline was a round-1 concern and is otherwise honored well.

3. **The DP2 template extension is correct but compressed.** The conclusion's extension of the three-test structure to DP2 (information, obligation, capture) is one paragraph and asserts the parallelism without working through what each test would observably look like in a DP2 setting. The lead's reason for not adding a full second worked example is defensible (proportionate gain to length), but the existing paragraph could carry slightly more weight if it named the construct each test would measure for DP2 - e.g. for the information test, the metric would be ecological-outcome correlation across rule-design provenance; for the obligation test, compliance variance across communities receiving rules with equivalent ecological content but different legitimacy of derivation. Currently the template is sketched at the level "the same three tests apply," which is true but less operational than the DP3 tests are. Minor.

4. **The DP7 expansion implies a structural finding the piece does not name.** On the revised analysis, the framework presents *eight design principles* but contains seven internal-design candidates and one external precondition. That asymmetry is itself a finding about how the framework circulates - the formal uniformity is what makes the precondition/mechanism confusion possible. The revised DP7 section names this confusion at the practitioner level ("treating DP7 as a checkbox alongside the other seven") but does not name it as a finding about the framework's presentational form. A single closing sentence in the DP7 section - "The eight-principle presentation, by displaying these in formally similar terms, is itself the source of the confusion this section diagnoses" - would do it. Marginal; the substantive finding is already present.

5. **A specific load-bearing claim still needs anchoring.** Line 33's DP4 paragraph asserts "Poteete, Janssen, and Ostrom (2010) discuss empirical cases where one or more of these routes was operative." This is the right pointer, but the routes (mutual observation, reputational stakes, hierarchical audit) are general enough that a reader will want at least one case-level example - a sentence-fragment of the form "(e.g. the Spanish *huerta* monitoring rotations, where ...)." The piece does not need a full case treatment; one named case would close the gap between the abstract route names and the empirical claim that case material exists. Minor.

6. **The piece's positive claim still rests on DP3 doing most of the worked-example work.** This is partly addressed by the DP2 template extension (#3 above) and by the DP4 second-order opening, but the structural fact remains: the only principle for which the three-mechanism-candidate / three-observational-test apparatus is fully exhibited is DP3. The lead has chosen not to add a second worked example, and the choice is defensible, but it is worth flagging that the piece's claim to a *general* diagnostic instrument rests on the reader's trust that the DP3 demonstration generalizes, rather than on a second concrete instance. A reader sympathetic to the structural argument will grant this trust; a skeptic might not. The piece is honest about being a diagnostic frame rather than a complete worked-out set of diagnostics, and that honesty is its principal defense here. Marking the concern rather than asking for a fix.

No process-narration leakage detected. No charter-relevant findings. No fabricated citations identified; the references are all real and the in-text attributions are consistent with what those works are known to argue.
