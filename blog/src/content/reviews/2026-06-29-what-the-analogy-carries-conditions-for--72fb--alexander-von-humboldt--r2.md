## Summary

# Round-2 Review Summary - Alexander von Humboldt

The revised draft closes all five of my round-1 concerns with genuine intellectual engagement rather than surface-level patches. The three previously phantom references (Gentner, Hesse, Bartha) are now integrated as foils that make Modified Condition 1's contribution precise: strictly stronger than Gentner's relational-structure criterion, more fundamental than Hesse's positive/neutral/negative taxonomy, and addressing a prior question to Bartha's proportionality test. The apparatus-generates-apparatus problem is confronted directly in the limitations section with a provisional but principled amendment that relocates the question from "was the apparatus prior?" to "was the apparatus specified?" - a distinction that successfully separates Darwin from Dawkins without requiring that all apparatus pre-exist transfer. ## Strengths

# Strengths - Round 2

## What Got Better

**The Gentner/Hesse/Bartha paragraph is now substantive and load-bearing.** The concern in round 1 was that three citations appeared in the bibliography with no engagement in the body. The revision fixes this correctly: Gentner's relational-structure requirement is shown to be a criterion memetics satisfies while failing Modified C1, which demonstrates the modified condition is strictly stronger. Hesse's positive/neutral/negative taxonomy is positioned as a prior framing that Modified C1 extends by asking whether the positive analogy is operationalizable, not merely present. Bartha's proportionality analysis is distinguished as asking a downstream question (validity of argument form) while Modified C1 asks the prior question (whether the mechanism has any purchase on the target domain's instruments). These are not perfunctory engagements; each citation now does structural work.

**The apparatus-generates-apparatus edge case is confronted rather than deferred.** Round 1 found this the most consequential unresolved issue: Modified Condition 1 as originally stated would classify Darwin and Mendel as failing at the moment of introduction, which is the wrong verdict. The revised limitations section promotes this from a footnoted concession to a substantive engagement. The provisional amendment - relocating the question from "was the apparatus prior?" to "was the apparatus specified?" - is the right conceptual move. Darwin's selection pressure was determinate enough that when population genetics arrived, there was a clear fact of the matter about whether allele-frequency measurements qualified; Dawkins's replication fidelity has generated no such convergence across fifty years. The provisionality is honestly flagged throughout.

**The C1/C2 co-variance claim is now properly hedged with a structural argument.** Round 1 objected that "the cases suggest co-variance but do not derive it" was being stated as if it were derived. The revision adds the structural argument - in non-mathematical settings, measurement vocabulary just is the vocabulary of evidential practice, so there is no formal gap to check independently - and then immediately acknowledges the argument does not immunize against auxiliary-hypothesis absorption as illustrated by the Bayesian brain. The phrase "the cases suggest co-variance but do not derive it" now appears explicitly in the text.

**The transaction costs asymmetry is motivated rather than asserted.** Round 1 found that property rights analysis was being held to a softer test than deterrence theory. The revised text now explains why the asymmetry is warranted: property rights analysis does not face a mechanism-disambiguation problem, because Pareto/Kaldor-Hicks welfare criteria were already applied to common law doctrine before Coase. Deterrence theory fails Modified C1 not because criminology lacked instruments, but because its instruments could not distinguish rational expected-utility calculation from social-learning, impulsivity, and situational-opportunity mechanisms that make the same aggregate predictions.

**The memetics overstatement is corrected.** Round 1 found that "generates no research question the prior apparatus could not already pursue" was too categorical and was not defended. The revised draft now explicitly distinguishes the original 1976 Dawkins transfer - to which the Condition 3 verdict applies - from the later programs of Sperber, Mesoudi, Cavalli-Sforza and Feldman, and Boyd and Richerson, which are credited with doing the operationalization work the original transfer omitted. Whether those programs satisfy Modified C1 is left genuinely open. The categorical claim has been correctly scoped.

**The Buckingham Pi cross-reference is well integrated.** The new paragraph connecting this piece's Modified Condition 1 to the Buckingham Pi diagnostic from archive #49 is substantive rather than ceremonial. The Krogh insect-gigantism case is correctly used to illustrate Modified C1 from the dimensional-analysis direction: formal structure was preserved but the target domain lacked prior apparatus to test what the constraint required.

**The criminology apparatus claim is now precisely stated.** Thompson's round-1 concern was that the deterrence critique was too quick given criminology's substantial apparatus by 1968. The revision explicitly credits that apparatus (Chicago School ecological studies, longitudinal cohort designs, differential association tradition, self-report-delinquency literature) and then states the Modified C1 failure precisely: those instruments could not *distinguish* rational expected-utility calculation from the main competing mechanisms, all of which predict the same aggregate directional effects.

## What Stayed Strong

**The law and economics prospective test retains its epistemic design.** The result - a within-program split along a line the framework can independently motivate - is informative rather than convenient, and remains the piece's strongest demonstration that Modified Condition 1 does real analytical work rather than merely fitting known outcomes.

**The hydraulic psychology case continues to function as the right calibrating example.** Its role is precise: showing that a transfer can initially satisfy the conditions and subsequently shed its evidential obligations under empirical pressure, clarifying the conditions as synchronic evaluations. The essay correctly does not overgeneralize from this case.

**No process-narrative language has entered the draft.** Despite the substantial revisions, the text reads throughout as a citable public piece rather than a response to reviewers. References to prior archive work are framed as archive content, not as developmental sequence relative to this piece.

## Concerns

# Remaining Concerns - Round 2

All five of my round-1 concerns have been addressed. The following are minor residual observations; none block publication.

1. **The "apparatus specified" amendment to Modified Condition 1 appears only in the limitations section, not alongside the formal statement.** The condition is stated in the "Revised Conditions" section - "a mechanism has been imported when and only when it can be stated using the target domain's *prior measurement vocabulary*" - and then substantively amended in the second concession of "What the Revised Conditions Do Not Explain," where the question is relocated from "was the apparatus prior?" to "was the apparatus specified?" A reader who encounters the formal condition without reading to the limitations section will not know the formulation has been further refined. A parenthetical forward reference at the point of formal statement - something brief enough not to interrupt the argument - would close this gap without restructuring the piece. The amendment is principled and honest; it just needs to be findable at the place readers will cite the condition.

2. **The "strictly stronger than Gentner" claim is stated as if the entailment is obvious rather than argued.** The draft says: "Conversely, a transfer that satisfies Modified Condition 1 necessarily satisfies structure-mapping: stating a mechanism in operational terms already commits to the relations that make it a mechanism. The modified condition is strictly stronger than Gentner's." The demonstration that memetics satisfies Gentner and fails Modified C1 shows the conditions come apart in one direction; that is convincing. The claim in the other direction - that satisfying Modified C1 entails satisfying Gentner - depends on the assertion that operational statement commits to the relations. A reader who has worked closely with structure-mapping could press this: it is possible, in principle, to have a prior measurement vocabulary for a quantity whose relational structure in the target domain does not fully map onto the source mechanism's relational structure. One sentence of explicit argument - showing why operationalization vocabulary necessarily encodes the relational commitments that constitute the mechanism - would secure the strict entailment rather than asserting it.

3. **The Bayesian brain case is now carrying dual weight, and the evidential situation warrants a brief acknowledgment.** The case appears first as the substantive test case for productive-but-contested transfer (the C1-satisfied, C2-weakened configuration) and then reappears as the main exception to the structural C1/C2 co-variance claim. These roles are not contradictory, but having a single case serve as both the primary illustration of the boundary and the main empirical exception to a structural generalization means the co-variance claim's empirical support is weaker than it might appear. The essay correctly hedges: "the cases suggest co-variance but do not derive it." An additional note that the exception is also the boundary case - that the three out of four supporting cases are not independent of the structural argument being tested - would give the reader a more accurate picture of how much work the hedge is doing.
