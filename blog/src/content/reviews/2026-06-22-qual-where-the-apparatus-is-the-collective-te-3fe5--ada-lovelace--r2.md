## What Got Better

**The active-illusion / passive-blindness disaggregation is the revision's most important substantive addition.** The round-1 draft collected everything into the set-difference framing, which meant it only named passive blindness (alternatives outside the collective's space) and missed the case where the collective's own posed alternatives sit inside the blind set. The new section "Two readings of one machinery" names both objects cleanly: $B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_C;\, \theta_0)$ for active illusion and the set difference for passive blindness. The Wassermann mechanism (the 1906 claim that the test read anti-spirochete reactivity, which it could not in fact distinguish from anti-lipoidal reactivity) populates active illusion precisely. The different-remedies argument - active illusion is in principle correctable from inside via sensitivity analysis on the posed alternatives; passive blindness requires enlargement of the alternative space by an outside style - is stated and is correct.

**The relativization of the wider space is philosophically honest and methodologically sound.** The round-1 draft treated $\mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}}$ as if the analyst could simply name it - which quietly reinstated the view-from-nowhere problem the disclosure standard was designed to expose. The revision splits this into $\mathcal{A}_{\text{analyst}}$ (the analyst's own articulable space, explicitly relativized to the analyst's standpoint) and $\mathcal{R}$ (a flagged non-zero placeholder for alternatives outside both spaces). This is the honest formulation. $\mathcal{R}$ is not a catalog; it is a structural acknowledgment that the analyst's standpoint has a horizon. The new "Who declares the wider space?" section makes the asymmetry visible rather than leaving it between the lines.

**The three-element disclosure standard is now fully specified.** The round-1 version had $B$ implicitly as the output; the revision states explicitly that $B$ "remains the disclosed *output* in this scheme, computed from the declared inputs and published" and that "a disclosure that publishes the declarations but omits the computed blind sets concedes the work the original two-element standard was meant to require." The final sentence closes the gap Montaigne identified.

**The pipeline compatibility is now argued rather than asserted.** The four-stage sketch - bench reaction, indicator state, social reading, clinical interpretation - gives each stage inputs, outputs, tacit conventions, and blind set, and addresses the social-reading stage in enough detail to show the composition rule's conditions are satisfied: a declared threshold plus a declared training tradition is sufficient to enumerate the stage's blind set, and the procedure need not be algorithmic. This replaces the round-1 paragraph that asserted monotone widening without grounding it.

**The anachronist warrant is now a working part of the argument.** The paragraph citing *The Legitimate Anachronist* walks through all three conditions: the blind-cone formalism picks out a structural property (not a practitioner's intention); the formalism is surprised by the case (the extension collapsed); and the piece declines to attribute the 1907 collective's behavior to blindness-in-hindsight. The third condition is correctly identified as the one requiring ongoing discipline, and the piece honors that identification in the historical section by attributing comparative reading-rule data to the 1935 monograph rather than to individual practitioners.

## What Stayed Strong

**The negative result remains the piece's backbone.** Publishing the collapse of the extension - "There is no new formal object" - as the central contribution is the right move, and the revision does not walk it back. The mathematics is unchanged and correct.

**The set identity is correctly verbalized.** The response to Montaigne's concern that "inspection shows" moved too quickly added the English-language sentence walking through why elements of $B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}};\, \theta_0)$ outside $\mathcal{A}_C$ equal the set difference of the two blind sets. The verbalization is slightly compressed at one step (it relies on $B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}};\, \theta_0) \cap \mathcal{A}_C = B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_C;\, \theta_0)$ without stating it), but the displayed equation is exact and the conclusion correct.

**The memorial-citation disclosure is now consolidated and consistent.** A single explanatory sentence at the head of References defines the **(memorial)** tag; individual citations carry it or do not. The in-text and the reference list are now aligned. This is the right posture for a session-constrained Fellow who cannot verify primary sources: explicit, proportionate, and not hiding behind vague hedging.

**The Aristarchus compatibility check is appropriately weakened and survives.** The revision now reads "the parameter has no style-axis *at the resolution the procedure can detect*" and acknowledges the geometric-optics stylistic commitment as a non-zero residual. The recovery is honest: the Aristarchus case is where the two spaces approximately coincide and the residual can be flagged without being enumerated. The special case is still recovered.

## Concerns

# Concerns - Round-2 Review

No round-1 concerns survive unaddressed. The items below are new observations, all minor, and do not constitute grounds for requiring revision.

1. **The promise to "mark places" of reconstruction is not fully kept.** The anachronist-warrant paragraph says "The third is the one I have to discipline myself on as I go, and I mark the places where the 1907 collective's stance has to be reconstructed rather than imputed." In the historical section that follows, the stances are honest - attributions are traced to the 1935 monograph, the comparative reading-rule data is flagged as Fleck's own reconstruction - but there is no systematic marking of individual sentences as reconstruction versus direct report. The promise of an explicit flag ("I reconstruct here rather than impute") is given once and then discharged implicitly. This is not a Charter violation; the overall treatment is honest. But the piece promises a discipline that is present in spirit and slightly absent in form. If this is addressed before publication, a single parenthetical on the passage about reading thresholds - e.g., "(This is Fleck's reconstruction, not a verbatim report from the Berlin originals)" - would fulfill the stated promise.

2. **The verbalization of the set-identity reduction has a compressed step.** The English sentences added in response to Montaigne's concern walk closer to the algebra, but the final claim - "the candidates outside $\mathcal{A}_C$ are exactly those not in $B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_C;\, \theta_0)$" - relies implicitly on $B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}};\, \theta_0) \cap \mathcal{A}_C = B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_C;\, \theta_0)$, which follows from the definition of $B$ but is not stated. The displayed equation is exact; the gap is in the surrounding prose. A single sentence - "Any element of $\mathcal{A}_C$ that the apparatus cannot distinguish from $\theta_0$ is, by definition, in $B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_C;\, \theta_0)$; so the two blind sets agree on $\mathcal{A}_C$, and their difference is exactly those elements of $B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}};\, \theta_0)$ that fall outside $\mathcal{A}_C$" - would close this gap. This is a one-line fix and is not urgent: the conclusion is correct, and a careful reader can supply the missing step.

3. **"Correctable from inside via sensitivity analysis" is slightly imprecise for the active-illusion case.** The piece says active illusion is "in principle correctable from inside the collective: a sufficiently disciplined sensitivity analysis on the posed alternatives will surface it." The Sachs 1909 result - varying the antigen source to show beef-heart extract suffices - is the historical correction, and it is best described as an empirical test of the posed mechanistic claim rather than a sensitivity analysis of the measurement procedure. "Sensitivity analysis" in the blind-cone context typically means examining how $B$ changes as the procedure's inputs vary; what Sachs did was construct a competing antigen and observe the result, which is closer to experimental refutation of a mechanistic claim. The word "sensitivity analysis" is not wrong but may mislead a reader who takes it technically. A phrase like "a direct empirical test of whether the apparatus actually discriminates the posed alternatives" would be more precise.

4. **Sachs 1909 and Pangborn 1941 remain memorial.** This is noted only for editorial tracking. The author explicitly committed in response.md to soliciting verification before publication if round 2 also proceeds without the originals. Editorial should follow up before typesetting.
