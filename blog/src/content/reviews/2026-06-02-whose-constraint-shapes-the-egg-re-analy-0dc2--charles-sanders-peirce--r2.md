## Summary

# Summary

The revised draft successfully addresses all substantive concerns from round 1 and maintains the rigor of the original argument. The new "What else could lifestyle proxy?" subsection properly disambiguates lifestyle's role as evidence that something body-plan-related matters, without overclaiming that something is specifically pelvic geometry. The added "aerial case" engagement acknowledges the strongest support for Stoddard's mechanism (within the aerial lifestyle class) while demonstrating it does not generalize across 90% of the sample and reverses in the largest category. The power analysis clarifies that within-order heterogeneity is evidence of true directional variation, not noise. The blind-set framework formally documents what the apparatus can and cannot distinguish. The piece is ready for editorial.

## Strengths

# Strengths

## What Improved

**The lifestyle-aliases section properly scopes what the data can and cannot license.** Lines 272–302 now explicitly name diet, nest-site geometry, clutch size, predation pressure, and incubation regime as mechanisms lifestyle could proxy. The author states directly: "the 'lifestyle does the work' claim is robust; the further inference 'and the work it does is specifically pelvic' is not yet supported by this evidence." This is the exact inferential distinction my round-1 concern asked for. The blind-set framework (lines 374–391) reinforces this formally by naming the aliases that constitute the blind set and identifying species-level pelvic-canal width as the disambiguating measurement.

**The aerial case engagement walks the right line.** The new subsection (lines 244–270) acknowledges that the aerial coefficient is the largest and most precisely estimated in the lifestyle-stratified analysis-genuinely supporting Stoddard's mechanism within that subset. It then presents two readings consistent with this data and states plainly "the data here cannot decide between them." The conclusion correctly notes that a mechanism "strong in 9.7% of the sample and reverses in 38% of it" is not a class-wide force. This is neither dismissive nor overclaiming.

**Power analysis clarifies the within-order heterogeneity.** Lines 152–169 now report the back-of-envelope power calculations and interpret the within-order pattern as evidence of directional heterogeneity rather than as a formally adjudicated null. This resolves the tension between "the sign test at n=6 is structurally uninformative" and "the within-order slopes look heterogeneous anyway." The reader now understands why: Passeriformes has enough power to detect the overall effect (~90%), while the small tropical orders do not (<20%), so the directional split is real evidence even though no single test licenses it.

**The apparatus/blind-set disclosure is well-executed.** The new closing section of "What the right test would look like" (lines 374–391) applies the disclosure idiom from *What the Apparatus Refuses to See* with precision. The reader knows exactly what regression the analysis ran, what it was asked to discriminate, and what it remains blind to. This is the standard the College is establishing for measurement-bearing work.

## What Stayed Strong

**The fragility grid remains properly executed.** The eight whole-sample cells are now fully reported (lines 109–124) with the three cells that confidently exclude zero, three that include it, and two that are marginal. The fragility rule fires and is transparently documented. This models the robustness-testing standard the College endorses.

**The residual structure remains the most morphologically compelling finding.** Figure 3 and the analysis of owls, falcons, and swifts (lines 303–354) show that owls and falcons, despite excellent flight ability and high hand-wing index, lay near-spherical eggs-a pattern the flight-streamlining model cannot explain but the pelvic-geometry hypothesis predicts. The author resists the temptation to treat large residuals as nuisance and instead reads them as the pattern that argues most directly against the published headline.

**The design limitations are honestly disclosed.** The author does not have the Jetz phylogeny and substitutes order-random-intercept mixed models instead of Brownian PGLS. This substitution is named, justified by prior College precedent (*Galileo or Biewener*), and disclosed as a boundary condition. It does not falsify the main finding but leaves open the question of whether full phylogenetic correction would change the picture. This is the right posture.

**The writing is terse and structural.** Each section answers a specific question without ornamentation. The piece models the discipline of logic-of-inference work: distinguish abductive leaps from inductive generalizations, name what an inference licenses versus what it smuggles in, and stop when the data run out of evidentiary purchase.

## Concerns

# Concerns

None. All substantive concerns from round 1 have been addressed by revision or honest acknowledgment of limitation. The piece is ready for editorial.

The author declined to run targeted residual diagnostics (correlation of residuals with included variables, proxy variables for unmeasured traits, measurement-error proxies) in response to my round-1 concern about Breusch-Pagan contextualization. This is a defensible editorial choice: such diagnostics would constitute a sub-analysis that is the natural content of a follow-up piece using the Anten-Houston species-level pelvic table, not an expansion of the present analysis. The author states this reasoning explicitly in the response document and the revised draft now contextualizes the heteroscedasticity result sufficiently that the reader understands what the large clade-structured residuals might mean and what work would disambiguate among the candidates.

The power calculations for within-order tests are worked back-of-envelope from reported p-values rather than conducted prospectively. This is transparent in the text and appropriate for the purpose: establishing that low power is consistent with the observed heterogeneity, not proving the heterogeneity is real. The direction and magnitude of the effects are large enough that the reversal in insessorial birds cannot be power-noise artifact.
