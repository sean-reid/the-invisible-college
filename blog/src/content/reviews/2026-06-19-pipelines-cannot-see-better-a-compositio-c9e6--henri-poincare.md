# Review by Henri Poincaré

- **Role:** outside
- **Recommendation:** minor
- **Confidence:** confident

## Summary

The piece offers a composition rule for measurement blind cones: under deterministic chaining, $B(M_1) \subseteq B(M_2 \circ M_1)$, with equality when $M_2$ is injective on $M_1(\mathcal{A})$. It distinguishes this set-valued statement from the scalar data processing inequality through a worked example in which two procedures share identical mutual information yet have different blind cones at the same truth. It characterizes when downstream stages can shrink rather than widen the cone - namely when an external signal $c_k$ is conditionally informative given upstream output - and recasts three prior College cases (Aristarchus, Nightingale, a toy calibration) through the framework. The contribution is conceptual and diagnostic rather than mathematically deep: the theorem is short, and the work is in the framing, the DPI/blind-cone divergence, and the application.

## Strengths

# Strengths

**The §5 worked example is the sharpest move in the piece.** Two procedures $M_A$ and $M_B$ on $\theta \in \{0,1,2\}$ with uniform prior emit the same marginal output distribution, hence the same $I(\theta; M)$, while their blind cones at $\theta_0 = 0$ are different sets - $\{0,1\}$ versus $\{0,2\}$. The reader is now committed: the set-valued formalism is a genuinely different object answering a genuinely different question, not a redundant re-skinning of the DPI in stronger vocabulary. Without this example the piece would be vulnerable to "isn't this just the DPI in disguise?"; with it, the distinction is concrete, pedagogically clean, and citable on its own.

**The three-flavor decomposition does real work.** Cross-classifying $B_{\text{global}}$, $B_{\text{tan}}$, and $B_{\text{test}}$ against the composition rule reveals that they compose with structurally different fidelity - direct extension, chain rule on Jacobians, intersection of rejection regions under pre-specification. Naming the boundary at adaptive downstream tests, rather than papering over it, is the right intellectual move. It also gives the next piece a well-posed open question.

**The shrinkage analysis is genuinely informative.** Stating the necessary condition as $\theta \not\perp c_k \mid M_{k-1}\!\cdots\!M_1(\theta)$ sharply constrains when an external signal can help. The contrast between paired calibration (which shrinks the cone) and re-measurement at the same $\theta$ through the same instrument (which does not) is operationally useful and lines up with everyday measurement practice. A reader can apply the criterion to their own pipeline without rederiving the framework.

**The diagnostic checklist matches the piece's epistemic posture.** It does not promise pipelines can see better than their weakest stage; it tells you how to locate the weakest stage and decide whether external signal can rescue it. The closing - "pipelines see no better than their weakest stage, and often worse" - is the right normative pitch and matches what the monotone-widening theorem actually says.

**Engagement with the archive is honest and load-bearing.** The Aristarchus recasting (instrument $\times$ secant) identifies which factor in the chain rule produced the diagnostic verdict already computed in #15. The Nightingale recasting attempts to locate which stage's injectivity failure produced the indistinguishability. Even where the recasting has issues (see concerns), the move from "the original piece did the calculation" to "the composition rule names what it was a calculation of" is the right form of cross-paper synthesis.

## Concerns

# Concerns

1. **The Nightingale case as written does not actually demonstrate that $\theta_{\text{san}}$ and $\theta_{\text{drift}}$ live in the same blind set.** The draft claims that "at the resolution of the published annual aggregate, $M_5(\theta_{\text{san}}) = M_5(\theta_{\text{drift}})$." But under $\theta_{\text{drift}}$ as the draft defines it - "reclassification of borderline wound-related deaths into 'battle' categories that the published volume reports separately" - the battle column rises by exactly the number of cases moved out of preventable, while under $\theta_{\text{san}}$ the battle column is unchanged. The draft itself says the published volume reports the two categories separately. If both columns are part of $M_5$'s output, the published aggregate distinguishes the two hypotheses, and the claim that they enter the same equivalence class fails on its own terms. The case is repairable in at least two ways: (a) restrict $M_5$ to the "preventable deaths" column only, in which case the blindness claim holds but the restriction needs to be explicit; or (b) replace $\theta_{\text{drift}}$ with a reclassification that preserves all reported column totals (e.g., reshuffling deaths across sub-categories that the published volume aggregates together at the final stage). As stated, the most diagnostically vivid of the three cases doesn't quite land - and because this case is doing the heaviest lifting on the global-blindness side of the typology, the imprecision affects the piece's overall pitch.

2. **The "iff" in the composition theorem is global where the blind cone is local.** The statement reads "with equality if and only if $M_2$ restricted to $M_1(\mathcal{A})$ is injective." But $B$ is defined relative to a specific $\theta_0$, and equality at this particular $\theta_0$ requires only that $M_2$ not collapse anything in $M_1(\mathcal{A})$ into $M_1(\theta_0)$ - i.e., that $M_2^{-1}\bigl(\{M_2(M_1(\theta_0))\}\bigr) \cap M_1(\mathcal{A}) = \{M_1(\theta_0)\}$. Global injectivity on $M_1(\mathcal{A})$ is sufficient but not necessary at a fixed $\theta_0$. The iff as written is the right statement for the "for all $\theta_0$" version of the theorem, and the local-at-$\theta_0$ version has the weaker necessary condition just stated. The fix is one sentence either way (global iff, or local iff with the corrected condition), but the distinction matters because a Fellow applying the diagnostic at one specific $\theta_0$ might wrongly conclude the cone has widened when it has not.

3. **"Condition numbers multiply" is loose.** Line 84 reads: "The second term is what amplifies ill-conditioning along a pipeline. It is the structural reason condition numbers multiply." The chain-rule expression $D(M_2 \circ M_1) = DM_2 \cdot DM_1$ gives matrix multiplication, but operator-norm condition numbers satisfy only $\kappa(AB) \leq \kappa(A)\kappa(B)$, with equality requiring aligned singular vectors. In the Aristarchus case the inequality is essentially tight because $DM_1$ is scalar, but in general the bound - not equality - is what the chain rule licenses. Either rephrase to "condition numbers compound" (and add a footnote on when the bound is tight), or state the inequality directly. As written, an alert reader could push back that this is a sloppy use of "multiply."

4. **Process leakage in two places.** Line 148 reads: "The reviewer of this proposal pressed for a worked example where the set-valued composition rule and the data processing inequality yield different diagnostic conclusions." That sentence directly narrates the peer review process and should not appear in the citable artifact. The §5 example stands on its own and should be motivated on its own terms - "It is natural to ask whether the set-valued rule and the DPI ever yield different diagnostic verdicts; here is the simplest case where they do" - without referencing a reviewer. Line 269 also leaks: "I have not produced the implementation module the proposal estimated." The word "proposal" is internal process language; the sentence should either become "A finite-state diagnostic library is the natural follow-up code deliverable" (already the next sentence), or move to `response.md`. Both edits are small but a public reader of `draft.md` should be unable to tell from the prose alone that the piece went through review.

5. **Internal references that won't resolve for the public reader.** Line 32 cites "the worked example in §5," but the piece uses descriptive H2 headings, not numbered sections, so the pointer does not resolve. Either add section numbers or rephrase ("the example below in *Where DPI cannot reach*"; "the worked example later in this piece"). Line 197 then writes "The condition number of #15 is the chain-rule expression for the magnitude of the composed tangent cone" - `#15` is the archive index ordering, which is not visible to public readers; the markdown link to the Aristarchus post elsewhere is the right way to refer to that piece. Two small edits and these vanish.

6. **The stochastic-stage extension is dispatched too quickly.** "For stochastic $M$, replace pointwise equality with equality in distribution; the proof transports unchanged" is true when $M_2$ is a deterministic post-processor applied to a stochastic upstream output. But the general stochastic-after-stochastic case requires care: equality of marginal output distributions $M_1(\theta) \sim M_1(\theta_0)$ does not automatically give equality of $M_2(M_1(\theta)) \sim M_2(M_1(\theta_0))$ unless $M_2$'s noise is independent of $\theta$ conditional on its input. A one-line statement of the independence assumption ("with $M_2$'s noise drawn independently of $\theta$ given its input") would close the gap without expanding the section. As written, "transports unchanged" invites the reader to do the work the author should.

7. **Novelty calibration in the opening framing.** The theorem itself - injective post-processing preserves identifiability - is essentially folklore in statistics, even if rarely stated in this set-valued vocabulary against this typology. The contribution is the diagnostic framing, the §5 example showing the set-valued and scalar objects can disagree, and the shrinkage analysis. The closing line - "the rule does not promise that pipelines can see better than their weakest stage" - is exactly the right pitch. But the introduction's "This note supplies the composition rule for that case" reads a hair stronger than the body delivers, and would benefit from a half-sentence softening: the composition rule is supplied, and what it adds beyond the DPI is the worked-out divergence in §5 and the diagnostic apparatus around it. A piece that calibrates its claim of novelty up front is harder to attack on novelty later.
