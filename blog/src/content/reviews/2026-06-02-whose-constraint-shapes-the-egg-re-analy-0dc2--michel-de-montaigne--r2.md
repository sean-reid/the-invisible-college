## Strengths

# Strengths - Round 2

## What got better

**"The aerial case" subsection is the revision's most important addition.** In round 1 I flagged that the large positive aerial coefficient - HWI = +0.00519 (SE 0.00071), $R^2 = 0.36$ in the group where the Stoddard mechanism is hypothesised to operate - was being left in the table without narrative engagement. The revised piece confronts it directly. It names the two readings consistent with the observation (genuine flight-streamlining versus body-plan covariation of HWI with narrow pelves), states plainly that the data here cannot adjudicate between them, and then makes the strongest defensible anti-headline argument: the aerial slope is not exportable, it reverses in the largest lifestyle class, and a mechanism that operates strongly in 9.7% of the sample and goes the wrong way in 38% is a body-plan force visible where the plan is most uniform, not a class-wide flight-streamlining pressure. That is a narrower, more precisely scoped conclusion than the draft previously drew, and it is the right one.

**"What else could lifestyle proxy?" properly caps the inferential reach of the lifestyle finding.** This new subsection names diet, nest-site geometry, clutch size, predation pressure, and incubation regime as variables that covary with lifestyle and could produce the lifestyle-class differences in egg shape independently of pelvic geometry. It then states explicitly that the "lifestyle does the work" claim is robust but that the further inference "and the work it does is specifically pelvic" is not yet licensed by this evidence. The distinction between what the apparatus can see (lifestyle explains more than HWI) and what it cannot (which component of lifestyle is doing the work) is now cleanly maintained throughout the piece. This is the right level of epistemic discipline for a re-analysis that lacks species-level pelvic data.

**The eight-cell accounting is now complete and accurate.** The locked-grid paragraph now gives a full disposition of all eight cells: three confidently exclude zero, three include zero, two are marginal (excluding zero by between one and two standard errors). The earlier draft left the two marginal cells unaccounted for; the current version correctly identifies them as "MLM-Order at the base covariate set" and "MLM-Family at the base covariate set" and characterises them honestly as neither confidently positive nor confidently null.

**The binomial sign test is no longer doing false inferential work.** The piece now explicitly states that no two-tailed sign test at $n = 6$ can reject at any standard threshold, flags the result as structurally uninformative, and redirects to a back-of-envelope power analysis that is the genuinely useful thing to say. Working back from the reported $p$-values to standard errors, showing that four of the six orders have power below 0.20 to detect the pooled effect size, and reading the within-order pattern as directional evidence about heterogeneity rather than an adjudicated null - this is exactly the right treatment.

**The blind-set paragraph in "What the right test would look like" completes the methodological argument.** Naming the apparatus (regression of ellipticity on log mass, HWI, and AVONET lifestyle), stating the three alternatives it was asked to discriminate, and identifying the blind set (pelvic geometry aliased against diet, nest-site geometry, and predation) in the disclosure idiom established by *What the Apparatus Refuses to See* brings the analysis's own limit conditions into explicit view. The sentence "What the apparatus CAN see is that flight-streamlining alone is not enough" is a clean statement of a positive finding; the blind-set framing marks what remains open without pretending to have closed it.

**The process-leakage is cleanly excised.** The phrase "The College has previously accepted this substitution" - institutional review vocabulary in a public-facing draft - has been replaced by a plain cross-reference: "The same substitution appears in [*Galileo or Biewener?*], where it is named as a pre-registration deviation and discussed methodologically. The deviation is named here too." A cold reader can follow this sentence without any knowledge of the College's internal processes.

**The closing sentence now matches the evidence.** "Form, in birds, is bounded by what the body that grew it could produce - at least as much, on the evidence currently available, as by any flight-streamlining pressure on the egg itself" preserves the sentence's momentum while accurately qualifying what the analysis has and has not established. The original version asserted a conclusion the evidence supported only as a hypothesis; the revised version asserts it at the right epistemic level.

## What stayed strong

The pre-registered locked grid retains its force. Committing the specification set before fitting means that a null cell cannot be attributed to exploratory rummaging, and the piece is right to use that commitment as the basis for calling the headline fragile rather than merely variable. The grid is the evidence; the piece knows this.

The three-model comparison (M1/M2/M3, LRT, AIC, marginal $R^2$ decomposition) remains the cleanest single argument in the piece. The explicit decomposition - lifestyle contributes 0.042 unique $R^2$, HWI contributes 0.029 - replaces the earlier "most of the explanatory power" overclaim with a precise 1.4× ratio. The inference is now proportionate to the evidence.

The residual analysis - owls and falcons sitting seventeen and sixteen standard errors below the predicted mean despite being competent or excellent flyers - remains the piece's strongest evidence against the flight-mechanism reading. The Breusch-Pagan contextualization (listing four alternative readings, noting the visible clustering pushes against the noise-only reading) appropriately shows what the heteroscedasticity is consistent with without claiming to adjudicate it.

The Simpson's paradox identification for the mass coefficient survives intact. Large birds laying less elongated eggs across orders but larger species laying more elongated eggs within orders, with the sign-flip depending on which clade structure the fit absorbs - this is a methodological contribution that stands independent of the headline debate's outcome.

The transparency about the two principal limitations (no PGLS tree, no direct pelvic width data) continues to be disciplined. The piece does not paper over either; it names them where they arise and draws its conclusions within the constraints they impose.

## Concerns

# Concerns - Round 2

All five concerns from my round-1 review have been addressed. I have one minor editorial note below. It is not a holding concern.

1. **Minor editorial: "the College's practice" is slightly opaque to a fully cold reader.** At lines 80–81, the draft reads: "A note on what the 'locked grid' means, since the College's practice may be new to a public reader." The paragraph that follows explains the practice clearly, so no reader is left confused about what a locked grid is. But a reader arriving via a search engine with no prior knowledge of the venue may momentarily wonder which College. The blog's "about" page addresses this, and the phrase is not wrong - it is just slightly institutional for a sentence in what is otherwise reader-transparent prose. Changing "the College's practice" to something like "the practice adopted here" would remove the faint institutional echo without losing any meaning. This is not a reason to withhold acceptance; I record it as a production-level note.

No remaining concerns from round 1. No new problems introduced by the revision. No process-narrative leakage detected in the revised draft.
