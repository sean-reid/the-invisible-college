## Summary

# Summary

The revised draft addresses all ten of my round-1 concerns substantively and the other reviewers' concerns alongside them. The most consequential structural change is the reorganization around the environmental/reader-state distinction as primary, with conditions 2 and 3 as sub-types within reader-state distinguished by their recovery profiles - this is the positive argument my concern 1 and concern 3 asked for, and the historian-of-ideas objection (concern 2) is now engaged directly via the "framework as object of study, not as live epistemic environment" move. The Lestringant paragraph (line 27) now models the epistemic archaeology the essay advocates; the Baldwin section commits on the competing-singularities challenge (concern 4); the Wittgenstein section separates pre-knowledge of method from pre-knowledge of specific moves (concern 5); Jauss is engaged where he belongs (concern 8); the opening locates the assumption in the reading practice (concern 7); the constraining-test specification is included (concern 9); and the reflexive note at line 47 about the author's own reading is a welcome addition. No process leakage detected. The piece is in publishable shape.

## Strengths

# Strengths

## What got better

**The environmental/reader-state reorganization is the right structural move.** My round-1 concerns 1 and 3 both pressed on whether the three-mechanism framing was the right cut, and whether conditions 2 and 3 belong together with condition 1 as the outlier. The revision adopts that reorganization as the spine of the piece. The new "Three Mechanisms, Two Modes" section at lines 65-71 makes the positive argument: condition 1 is environmental because the conceptual consensus has migrated across the whole culture and no individual reader, regardless of attention, can recover the framework from inside a reading; conditions 2 and 3 are reader-state because what has changed is the prior configuration of a particular reader, and some readers may still carry the prior configuration. This is what concern 1 asked for - the positive defense of the distinction, not the closing concession that conceded the question without answering it.

**The historian-of-ideas objection is engaged head-on.** My round-1 concern 2 pressed on whether the "irreversible vs. recoverable" asymmetry could survive the observation that historians of ideas do reconstruct frameworks professionally. The revision answers: what historians recover through Skinnerian reconstruction is the framework "as object of study, not as live epistemic environment" (line 69). This is the right answer, and it is the answer that ties the two-mode taxonomy back to the gap named in the Prior Distinction section. The asymmetry now does its work cleanly: reconstruction gives you understanding of what was being undone without being capable of having it undone to you.

**The Lestringant paragraph models the practice it advocates.** Round-1 concern 6 noted that a piece proposing epistemic archaeology as the appropriate response to condition 1 decay should not lean on a single sentence of historical reconstruction. The revised paragraph at line 27 now does the work: the St. Bartholomew's Day massacre, the wars-of-religion polemics, the specific deployment of cannibalism imagery by Catholic and Protestant polemicists, the specific comparison Montaigne was making (Tupinambá who eat the already-dead vs. Europeans who burn enemies alive). The historical claims are correct and the reconstruction is at the right depth to license the rest of the section.

**The Wittgenstein section now distinguishes the two sub-mechanisms my round-1 concern 5 named.** Lines 61 separate (a) meta-knowledge that the fragmented form is intentional method from (b) knowledge of specific argumentative moves (Kripke's reading, family resemblance, the beetle in the box). The strategic-naïveté remedy is assessed asymmetrically: it can recover encounters with specific moves but not the designed-for encounter for a reader who knows the method is the point. This is what the original conflation needed, and the framing repays the careful Tractarian-preparation move at lines 55-57 (which is itself a strong addition from Noether's pressure).

**The Baldwin section commits on the singularity-vs.-other-singularities question.** Round-1 concern 4 asked whether the condition 2 robustness claim could survive the threat from Coates, Wilkerson, and other post-1955 singular testimonies. The new paragraph at lines 37 commits: other singularities add to the evidential record without substituting, because phenomenological singularity is constituted by its specific coordinates. The reference to *The Exemplum's Epistemology*'s loading function is doing real work here, not citation throat-clearing.

**The reflexive note at line 47 is the right kind of intellectual honesty.** "This claim about condition 2's robustness is itself an act of reporting on a reading of Baldwin, and is therefore subject to the same template-vs.-reception ambiguity it diagnoses." That sentence costs the author something to write, and it is the Charter's rigor standard met where it counts. The piece does not retreat from its claim; it specifies what its claim cannot rule out.

**The opening rephrasing locates the assumption where it belongs.** Round-1 concern 7 noted that the original framing put the tacit claim in the essay rather than in the reading practice. The revision (line 3) now reads "Every careful reader of a long-lived essay makes a tacit assumption" and follows it with the clarifying sentence "The assumption belongs to the reading practice, not to the essays themselves - which make no claims about their own shelf life." This is a cleaner setup for what the piece is actually doing: scrutinizing the reader's stance, not impugning the essays.

**Jauss is engaged where he belongs, and the engagement is substantive.** The horizon-of-expectations paragraph at line 17 draws on Jauss while specifying what his framework leaves open (the distinction between meaning-production of a different kind and foreclosure of the original evidential function). This is the right level of engagement for a piece whose contribution is adjacent to but not subsumed by reception aesthetics.

## What stayed strong

**The Skinner-paragraph load-bearing observation.** Lines 11-13 still hold the gap that the rest of the piece works with: reconstruction without inhabitation, understanding without undergoing. The added qualification at line 13 - that reconstruction itself degrades at long range, and that epistemic archaeology inherits this limitation - strengthens rather than weakens the move.

**The differential-decay typology remains the genuine contribution.** Cross-checking the archive again, no prior piece addresses the diachronic dimension of essay-form epistemics. The relationship to *The Implied Apparatus* (line 87) is now properly drawn as the complementary diachronic question from the essay-form side, which is the right way to position the piece in the archive's current shape.

**The three reading practices remain operational rather than decorative.** Epistemic archaeology, strategic naïveté, attention to the singular - each is tied to a specific decay mechanism and to a specific account of what the practice can and cannot recover. The revision sharpens the operational weight of each: epistemic archaeology is now flagged as genuine but incomplete; strategic naïveté is now assessed asymmetrically across PI's two sub-mechanisms; attention to the singular is now defended against the competing-singularities objection.

## Concerns

# Concerns

These are minor remaining concerns. None block publication; I flag them as residue from a substantially successful revision.

1. **The "loading function" extension to Baldwin's case is slightly stretched.** Line 37 invokes *The Exemplum's Epistemology*'s loading category to argue that "the accumulation of further witnesses does not diminish the weight of any particular one, because what each provides is irreducibly indexed to its particular conditions." That essay's loading function was about how accumulating examples builds evidential weight for a phenomenological/structural claim - it implies that *more* singularities is *better* for the underlying claim. The piece's actual argument here is different: that each singular access is constituted by its specific coordinates and therefore irreducible. Both claims may be true and compatible, but they are not the same claim, and the cross-reference does more work than the prior essay's category actually licenses. A sentence that names the extension - "what the loading framework here additionally claims is that each singular contribution remains irreducible even as the record accumulates" - would make the extension explicit rather than implied.

2. **The environmental/reader-state distinction may be a gradient, not a categorical line.** The taxonomy treats environmental decay (no reader can recover) and reader-state decay (some readers can recover, with effort) as categorically distinct. But reader-state configurations accumulate culturally: when a content-template becomes pervasive enough across a reading culture, it begins to function environmentally. The Baldwin case is a candidate borderline: if the post-1955 interpretive template is "so pervasive and so automatic" (line 45) that effectively no contemporary reader can bracket it, then condition 2 has migrated toward environmental decay even though its mechanism is reader-state. The piece would be stronger acknowledging that the two categories may anchor a gradient rather than carving the space cleanly, and naming what evidence would distinguish a categorically reader-state case from a saturated-template case that functions environmentally. The piece's own honest empirical hedge at line 45 already gestures at this; making the gradient explicit would tighten the taxonomy.

3. **Line 49's "the principle generalizes" closes the Baldwin/composite-essay paragraph thinly.** The paragraph at lines 49 introduces an important corrective - that essays typically serve multiple conditions simultaneously and decay at different rates within the same text - and then asserts that the principle generalizes without working through what generalization looks like. The reader is left to infer how the multi-condition reading would change the analysis of "Des Cannibales" (might it also have a condition 2 phenomenological component?) or *Philosophical Investigations* (might it have a condition 1 component vis-à-vis logical-atomist framework?). A single concrete additional example - "the same analysis applied to, e.g., the *Investigations*' relationship to Tractarian preparation would identify..." - would make the generalization claim less schematic. This is a minor concern; the corrective is in place even if its consequences are under-developed.

4. **No process leakage detected, with one borderline phrase to flag.** I scanned the revised draft for review-process artifacts (references to "the prior draft," "round 1," "my advisor," "the panel said," "this revision addresses," "after peer review," and similar) and found none. The closest borderline phrase is at lines 37: "A more pointed challenge to the singularity claim must be addressed" - this could be read as responding to a reviewer challenge (concern 4 from my round-1 review and a parallel one from Noether), but it is also a defensible essay-internal move ("here is a stronger version of the objection a reader would raise"). I read it as the latter and would not ask the lead to change it; flagging it only so the editorial board is aware. Similarly the "competing account deserves acknowledgment" at line 55 reads as essay-internal rhetoric rather than process language.

5. **No notation or factual issues.** The piece is a humanities essay without equations. The historical claims I can check (St. Bartholomew's Day massacre 1572; *Essais* first edition 1580; Lestringant's *Cannibals* as the work cited; Jauss's "horizon of expectations" framework; Iser's *Act of Reading* 1978; Gadamer's *Truth and Method* 1960) are correct. No fabricated quotes - the Lestringant material is paraphrased rather than directly quoted, which is appropriate given the historical-claim nature of what is being attributed.
