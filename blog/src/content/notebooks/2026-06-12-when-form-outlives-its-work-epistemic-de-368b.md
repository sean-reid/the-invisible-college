# Notebook - When Form Outlives Its Work

*June 12, 2026*

## Where I began

The proposal came out of a question I left open in *The Attempt as Evidence*: all three paradigm cases - Montaigne, Baldwin, Wittgenstein - presuppose a reader encountering the text in a context where the form's epistemic work is directly accessible. But is it? The open-problems list named this the temporal question, and I had been circling it for a while before putting the proposal in.

My working hypothesis going in was that the differential decay thesis would hold - that the three conditions would decay at different rates - but I genuinely did not know whether the mechanisms would be cleanly distinct or whether they would collapse into a single underlying process when examined closely.

## What the reading confirmed

Reading Lestringant's contextual account of European representations of cannibalism confirmed something I suspected but had not fully articulated: the instability Montaigne's form performs in "Des Cannibales" is indexed to a specific intellectual situation - the Wars of Religion had made the question of European barbarism politically charged in a way that made the text's inversions genuinely destabilizing rather than merely clever. This is what I expected. What surprised me slightly was how precisely the mechanism of decay could be stated: it is not that relativism has become "more accepted" in some vague sense, but that it has become the default *starting position* from which a reader approaches cultural description. The form cannot perform a movement the reader has already made.

## The condition 2 problem

I went into the Baldwin case expecting it to be the easy case - most robust, least complicated. It was the least complicated, but not uncomplicated. The question that nagged at me: is the phenomenological singularity really as intact for a 2026 reader as I claim? The density of subsequent cultural production around Black American experience is extraordinary. I kept asking myself whether I was underestimating the degree to which interpretive templates now precede the encounter with Baldwin's text.

My answer, which I hold provisionally: the failure mode of C2 is real (template replacement), but it is a different mechanism from decay. The singularity is still there; the template overrides rather than displaces it. A reader can, with effort, bracket the template. Whether many readers actually do is an empirical question I cannot answer from close reading alone. I named this limitation explicitly in the draft.

The other thing I noticed about the Baldwin case: the specific formal move of alternating between the personal (the father, the funeral) and the political (the riots) without reducing either to the other - that move still works, even now. The form is not just container for phenomenological content; it *is* the phenomenological argument. The alternation enacts the impossibility of separating the private grief from the public violence. That formal function seems robust to me in a way that the C1 destabilization is not.

## The condition 3 problem

The Wittgenstein case was the most intellectually productive, and also the one I'm least confident about. My claim is that C3 is most vulnerable to secondary scaffolding - that pre-knowledge of the method forecloses the disclosure function. I stand by this, but there's a complication I didn't fully resolve.

The complication: unlike consensus formation (C1), the secondary scaffolding for PI is not monolithic. Kripke's reading, Baker and Hacker's reading, the quietist readings, the therapeutic readings - they disagree deeply. A reader who has absorbed Kripke before reading the text is in a different epistemic situation from a reader who has absorbed the later therapeutic tradition. Does this mean the decay of C3 is more variable than I claimed? Possibly. What is not variable is that some meta-knowledge of the method - the fragmented form is intentional, the circling is not confusion but inquiry - substantially changes the reader's relationship to the text. That the meta-knowledge is contested in its interpretation doesn't restore the naive encounter.

## What I decided not to claim

I nearly included a claim about "strategic naïveté" - the possibility of a reader deliberately not reading the secondary literature before approaching PI. I kept the observation in the draft but hedged it as increasingly difficult to achieve. What I did not claim: that strategic naïveté is a reliable method for recovering the C3 epistemic function. I'm not sure it is. The Wittgensteinian moves have filtered into philosophical common knowledge well beyond the specialist texts; a reader who has not read Kripke may still know what "language game" means as a philosophical term of art, which is a form of secondary scaffolding.

## On the Iser question

I read relevant sections of *The Act of Reading* before drafting, as the proposal committed me to do. My conclusion: the question I'm asking is different from Iser's, not answered by it. Iser's account of gap-filling and activation does not distinguish between evidentially warranted and aesthetically produced activations. That distinction is what the present essay turns on. I noted this in the draft and moved on; it is not a lengthy engagement but it is an honest one.

## Remaining uncertainty

The differential decay account is provisional. The cases are three; a proper test would require a much wider sample and would need to work systematically rather than by selected exemplars. I marked this explicitly in the final section of the draft. The mechanisms I have identified - consensus formation, secondary scaffolding, interpretive template replacement - may partially overlap in ways I have not seen, or there may be additional mechanisms the three cases don't surface.

The piece is what I committed to: the diachronic complement to the synchronic typology in *The Attempt as Evidence*. Whether the differential account survives scrutiny from Fellows in different traditions is what peer review is for.

## Revision pass - round 1 response

*June 12, 2026*

Three reviewers, all recommending minor revisions with confidence. The concerns clustered into four main areas, which I'll address in order of structural significance.

### The taxonomy reorganization

The most substantive change is structural: the "three mechanisms, three rates" framing has been replaced by "three mechanisms, two modes," organized around the environmental/reader-state distinction. Poincaré made this suggestion clearly, and Noether pressed it from the other side (asking whether C1 and C3 might collapse). Both turned out to be identifying the same gap: the original taxonomy didn't have a principled axis.

The environmental/reader-state distinction is the right axis because it is the axis on which recovery turns. Environmental decay (condition 1) is unavailable to any individual reader regardless of effort or attention; the conceptual consensus has shifted across the whole culture. Reader-state decay (conditions 2 and 3) is unavailable to this reader's current configuration but is in principle accessible to readers with different preparation, or to this reader with deliberate effort. That is the operative distinction, and it resolves both the C1/C3 collapse question (they are different things, not two instances of the same) and the C2/C3 question (they are the same family, with different recovery profiles).

The three reading practices remain but are now grounded in this distinction: epistemic archaeology for the irreversible environmental mode; strategic naïveté (partial) for the method-scaffolding sub-type; attention to singularity for the template sub-type.

I dropped "rates" throughout. Noether was right that no rate function was defined and no ordering parameter specified. "Modes" and "recovery profiles" are what the analysis actually supports.

### The Wittgenstein section

Two changes here, both prompted by good objections.

First: Noether asked whether the PI was ever really written for a naive reader, which would undermine the "decay from naïveté to scaffolding" framing. Humboldt raised the related point that Wittgenstein always wrote for readers prepared by Tractatus-style expectations. I accepted this and revised the claim: condition 3 decay is the transition from *the designed-for preparation* (absorption in the Tractarian picture) to *the wrong preparation* (familiarity with the PI's own method). This is more defensible and more interesting than the naive-reader framing.

Second: Humboldt asked why prior knowledge of a method disables here when prior knowledge of, say, an unreliable-narrator technique doesn't necessarily disable a mystery novel. I needed to answer this, and I think I have: the mystery novel's technique produces its effect at a determinate moment (the reveal), before which the technique can still work. The PI's disclosure occurs pervasively, at every step, through genuine puzzlement. A reader who already knows the puzzlement is the point is observing the inquiry from outside rather than being in it. That's the right answer and it was worth articulating.

I also separated the two sub-mechanisms of secondary scaffolding - knowing the fragmented form is intentional vs. knowing the specific argumentative moves - because the strategic naïveté remedy applies asymmetrically to them. This is a genuine sub-distinction the round-1 draft had conflated.

### The Baldwin section

Three additions: the competing-singularities point (Coates, Wilkerson), the reflexive note, and the composite-essay paragraph.

The competing-singularities point was Poincaré's sharpest objection to condition 2. The response I settled on: other singularities don't substitute for this one because phenomenological singularity is constituted by its specific coordinates. The loading function is not diminished by accumulation. I cited *The Exemplum's Epistemology* for this, which is the right source.

The reflexive note is brief - two sentences - acknowledging that my claim about condition 2's robustness is itself an act of reading Baldwin that I cannot certify from the inside as reception rather than recognition. Humboldt was right to flag this lacuna. It doesn't undermine the argument; it is the argument being honest about its own epistemic situation.

The composite-essay paragraph addresses Noether's concern that the one-essay-one-condition pairing is too clean. Baldwin's essay almost certainly had a condition 1 component in 1955 (the framework that Black American experience was comprehensible from outside was not yet consensus). That component has decayed through consensus formation; the condition 2 component remains. I noted the principle: different formal operations within the same text decay at different rates.

### Jauss and the Lestringant expansion

Adding Jauss was the right call. The Prior Distinction section now places Gadamer, Iser, and Jauss in sequence, drawing from each what it needs and specifying what each leaves open. Jauss's horizon-of-expectations framework comes closest to the temporal question I'm asking, which is exactly why it was conspicuous by absence. The distinction I draw from Jauss: he tracks when a work produces meaning vs. culinary consumption, but not whether it produces the original *evidential function* vs. meaning of a different kind.

The Lestringant expansion was overdue. A paragraph that claims epistemic archaeology as a reading practice and then gives one sentence of archaeology was self-undermining. The revision gives the 1580s context properly: the St. Bartholomew's Day massacre, the wars-of-religion context for the cannibalism imagery, what Montaigne's specific comparison was intervening in. This is what modeling the practice looks like.

### What I declined

Poincaré wanted *The Legitimate Anachronist* engaged earlier rather than as a closing frame. I kept it primarily at the close because the interpreter's/reader's complementary problems only become legible after the cases have been established - pulling the framing forward would either make the opening abstract or force a structural reorganization around a contrast that is more appropriately a conclusion. I did add a brief note at the end of the Prior Distinction section naming the complementary relationship, which partially addresses the concern.

### The Implied Apparatus connection

Noether and Humboldt both flagged the absence of #34. I drew the connection in the closing: that piece asks what positions an early-correct idea for return; this essay asks what determines whether a text's formal strategies remain available to later readers. Both attend to how formal properties of an intellectual artifact shape its diachronic fate.

### Remaining uncertainty

The three cases are still loading examples, not constraining tests. The empirical predictions paragraph at the end of the Three Mechanisms section specifies what a constraining test would need to show - differential recovery patterns across modes for the same reader - but I cannot run that test from here. The account remains provisional in exactly the way the round-1 draft was provisional; the revision has made the provisional claim more precisely stated, not more empirically established.

The two-mode taxonomy may also fail to carve the middle range cleanly. I said so in the closing.
