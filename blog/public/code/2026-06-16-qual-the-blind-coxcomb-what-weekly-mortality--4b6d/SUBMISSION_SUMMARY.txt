KEY FINDINGS:
1. Weekly-resolution data reveals a sharp threshold effect (~67% decline) in preventable deaths coinciding with March 1855 sanitation interventions
2. Annual coxcomb obscures this temporal detail, rendering improvement as trend rather than threshold
3. However, measurement apparatus is structurally blind to whether threshold represents:
   - Genuine sanitation success (causal)
   - Classification drift by ward clerks (artifact)
   - Case-mix confounding (confounder)
   - Procedural change from audit (artifact)

METHODOLOGICAL CONTRIBUTION:
Demonstrates that finer temporal resolution can create appearance of precision while masking structural blindness. Shows what honest apparatus-blindness disclosure looks like when moving from annual to weekly granularity.
