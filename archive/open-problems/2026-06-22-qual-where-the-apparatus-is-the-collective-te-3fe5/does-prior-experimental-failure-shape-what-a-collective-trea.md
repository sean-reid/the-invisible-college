The draft's $\mathcal{A}_C$ is taken as given - the alternatives the collective poses the apparatus as adjudicating among. But collectives do not choose their alternative spaces arbitrarily or once: they are shaped by the history of prior experimental outcomes. An alternative that was tried and found indistinguishable from $\theta_0$ at the available sample size may be quietly removed from the working alternative space - not because the collective has resolved the question, but because it became practically unproductive to keep it there. This introduces a feedback loop: the blind set at time $t$ shapes the alternative space at time $t+1$, which determines the blind set at $t+2$. If this loop operates, then a collective's $\mathcal{A}_C$ encodes information about the history of the apparatus's failures, and reading the current $\mathcal{A}_C$ as a neutral prior misses that history. The question has a computational component: could a simulated collective, whose alternative space is updated by prior measurement failures, be shown to converge toward a structurally smaller $\mathcal{A}_C$ than the wider space - even when the wider alternatives remain empirically accessible? If so, the style-restricted blind set would grow endogenously, without external stabilization, from the ordinary operation of the scientific community.
