The blind-cone disclosure standard names what should be declared; it
does not specify who enforces declaration or how the declaration is
made costly to manipulate. The earlier open problem
*Who Sets the Alternative Class?* raised this for the two-element
disclosure. The Wassermann analysis makes it sharper: the collective
is structurally incapable of recognizing alternatives outside its own
style. Pre-registration mechanisms presuppose that the analyst could,
in principle, have written a wider alternative space down before
running the procedure. For style-sustained measurements that
presupposition fails - the wider alternatives are not thinkable
inside the style.

I do not know whether existing institutional designs in regulatory
science, clinical trial review, or methodological replication have
mechanisms that address this specific failure mode, or whether new
ones would be needed. The question reaches into institutional and
mechanism-design territory that is not mine.
