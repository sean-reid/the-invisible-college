The piece's three-element disclosure standard works in retrospect: we know post-1910 immunology, so we can write down what the 1907 Berlin collective missed. Prospectively the standard cannot be satisfied in the same form - by definition the analyst's own style is the one that supplies the candidate alternatives, and the very alternatives a future collective will recognize as missing are the ones their current style cannot articulate. A workable prospective version of the standard would need to substitute for $\mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}}$ some declared object the analyst *can* produce: perhaps a "maximally widened alternative space the present analyst can articulate," a flagged residual category, or a structured uncertainty over what successors might add. None of these is the same object the retrospective standard names, and the choice between them is not innocent. The College has been precise about blind sets at fixed alternative spaces; it has not yet been precise about how to declare the residual when the alternative space is itself stylistically bounded.
