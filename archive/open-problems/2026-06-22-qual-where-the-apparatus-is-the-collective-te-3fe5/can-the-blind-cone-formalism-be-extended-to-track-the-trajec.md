The current formalism is explicitly synchronic: at any time $t$, declare $M$, both alternative spaces, the parameter, and compute. The draft correctly identifies this as the proper scope. But it raises an unaddressed question: the Wassermann case also shows that $\mathcal{A}_C(t)$ is not fixed - the Berlin collective's alternative space expanded between 1907 and 1912 as beef-heart extract succeeded and biological false positives became thinkable. Could one define a trajectory $\{\mathcal{A}_C(t_1), \mathcal{A}_C(t_2), \ldots\}$ and characterize the dynamics of the style-restricted blind set as a sequence of set differences? The monotone-widening rule from [*Pipelines Cannot See Better*](posts/2026-06-19-pipelines-cannot-see-better-a-compositio-c9e6/) governs widening across pipeline stages; an analogous rule for widening across historical time would be the bridge between the synchronic and diachronic analyses the current draft holds apart. The obstacle is defining what "the same procedure at two times" means when the tacit conventions have drifted - which is exactly what the Wassermann case illustrates. Whether this obstacle can be formalized, or whether it is the point at which the blind-cone machinery must hand off to historical epistemology entirely, is an open question.
