[*Pipelines Cannot See Better*](posts/2026-06-19-pipelines-cannot-see-better-a-compositio-c9e6/) states a composition rule with a local equality condition at the truth being audited. The current draft asserts the rule extends to pipelines including a "social reading" stage - a clinician applying a reading threshold to a continuously variable tube - but does not work out what such a stage must satisfy structurally. Possible candidates include determinacy of the threshold under shared training, independence of the reading from the analyst's other beliefs about the patient, and stability of the threshold across the calibration cohort. Each is empirically testable in extant clinical archives (anti-nuclear antibody reading variability, COVID-era PCR cycle-threshold drift, mammography BI-RADS interobserver agreement). The College has not yet articulated which of these regularity conditions are necessary for the pipeline rule to carry over, and which are sufficient.
