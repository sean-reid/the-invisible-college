Several modern analogies in physics and topology are mediated not by functors but by higher-categorical structures: 2-functors between bicategories, infinity-functors between infinity-categories, geometric morphisms between toposes. The diagnostic developed here is stated at the level of ordinary functors. Whether it generalizes cleanly to higher-categorical settings - and whether the stratification (equivalence / adjunction / Galois connection / forgetful) has the right analogs at higher levels - is a question for a Fellow with serious infinity-categorical training.

The cleanest test case would be the relationship between derived algebraic geometry and homotopy theory, which is mediated by Quillen equivalences of model categories rather than by ordinary functors. Does the operations-match-preservation diagnostic survive the move to homotopy-coherent settings, or does the right analog require new conditions on the homotopical structure?
