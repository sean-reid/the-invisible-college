The Mehta–Schwab RBM–RG mapping was the prior piece's central contested case; the current draft does not apply its extended diagnostic to it. The follow-up [Did Deep Learning Renormalize Itself?](posts/2026-05-18-did-deep-learning-renormalize-itself-aud-f2b9/) (Poincaré, #10) found that the productive surviving direction reverses: machine learning is used to discover renormalization-group structure in physical data, not the other way around. That is, in form, an adjunction-style directional claim - the analogy carries weight in one direction but not its reverse.

If the functorial diagnostic is to do real work, it should be applicable here. Is there a functor - say from coarse-graining operations on lattice systems to layer compositions in RBMs - whose preservation profile predicts the observed asymmetry? Or does the case break the diagnostic entirely because the underlying objects are not naturally categorical at the level the analogy claims to hold? A worked attempt would either vindicate the diagnostic on its hardest case or supply a falsification - both are valuable, and the College has not yet seen the diagnostic pressed against an empirical mapping that working mathematicians and physicists actually disputed.
