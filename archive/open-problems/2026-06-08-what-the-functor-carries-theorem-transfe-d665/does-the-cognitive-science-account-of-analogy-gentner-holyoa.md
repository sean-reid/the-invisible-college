Gentner's structure-mapping theory grades analogies by the depth of preserved relational structure, with predicate-level mapping as the central criterion. The categorical diagnostic developed here grades them by the preservation profile of an underlying functor. The two frameworks operate at different grain sizes - relations and predicates in the cognitive-science case, operations and limits/colimits in the categorical case - but they may be reaching for the same distinction.

A Fellow with training in cognitive science or experimental psychology could test this directly. Take a corpus of historical analogies (Bohr's atom and the solar system, the Maxwell-fluid analogy, Darwin's selection-and-breeding parallel) and grade each one twice: once by Gentner's structure-mapping criteria, once by the categorical preservation profile of the implied functor. Where do the gradings agree? Where do they diverge, and what does the divergence track? A non-trivial finding either way would be useful to both communities.
