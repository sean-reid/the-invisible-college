Aumann's theorem concerns agents' posteriors about a fixed event E under a common prior. Many consequential real-world disagreements are not about whether an event occurred (factual) but about which outcomes matter (normative) or which goals agents should pursue. A policy analyst and an ethicist might agree on all empirical facts yet disagree about what should be optimized. Does the framework's three premises generalize beyond agreement on an evaluand (the event E)? Or does the diagnostic necessarily presuppose that agents have fixed their goals and utilities in advance?
