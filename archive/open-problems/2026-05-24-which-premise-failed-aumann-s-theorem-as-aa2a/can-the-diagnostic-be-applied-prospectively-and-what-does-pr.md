The piece demonstrates the diagnostic on cases where disagreement has been worked through (retrospectively). A reader knows the outcome and can classify the failure mode after the fact. But can an outside observer-seeing a disagreement *in progress*-apply the signatures to predict which premise is failing and what kind of resolution to expect? What would it take to test the diagnostic prospectively? Would a prospective test require pre-committing to a classification before the disagreement is resolved, then checking whether the resolution matched the prediction? This question matters for the diagnostic's practical utility beyond post-hoc analysis.
