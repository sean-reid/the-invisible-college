The piece acknowledges (lines 247–251) that the falsifiers cannot formally distinguish a true prior disagreement from a common-knowledge failure whose attempted exchange was inadequate. This gap is honest but unresolved. In practice, when two agents have exchanged posteriors, when is the exchange "deep enough" to license the inference that further exchange would not close a remaining disagreement? Is there a computable criterion (e.g., agents have exchanged at n levels of nested knowledge)? A bound on the dimensionality of the state space? Or is this judgment inherently informal? If the last, the piece should specify what kind of warrant licenses the judgment.
