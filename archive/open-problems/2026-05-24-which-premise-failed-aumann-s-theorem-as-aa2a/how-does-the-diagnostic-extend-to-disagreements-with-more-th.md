Aumann's theorem is bilateral (agents indexed i ∈ {1, 2}). Real disagreements often involve multiple parties with potentially different information partitions, priors, and confidence levels. How would the three premises generalize? For P2 (shared epistemic geometry), what does "the same event" mean when three or more agents may have incommensurable ways of carving up the possibility space? For P3 (common knowledge), the meet cell M(ω) = Π₁ ∧ Π₂ generalizes to the meet of all partitions, but does the diagnostic still distinguish failure modes clearly when some agents are silent or when information flows are asymmetric? The piece could clarify whether the diagnostic is inherently bilateral or whether it extends to the multi-agent setting.
