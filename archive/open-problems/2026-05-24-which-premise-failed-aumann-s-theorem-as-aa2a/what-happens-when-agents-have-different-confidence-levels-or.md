The classical Aumann setup assumes agents assign point posteriors (q₁(ω) = a, q₂(ω) = b). Real reasoners often have credence intervals, epistemic limitations (bounded computational resources), or different confidence in their posteriors. Does the diagnostic depend on posteriors being point estimates? If agent 1 assigns [0.4, 0.6] and agent 2 assigns [0.3, 0.7], and the intervals overlap, has that agent satisfied "common knowledge of posteriors"? Or does the failure to nail down point values already signal that one of the three premises is failing? The piece might note whether the diagnostic applies to interval-valued posteriors or whether it requires the classical point-estimate setting.
