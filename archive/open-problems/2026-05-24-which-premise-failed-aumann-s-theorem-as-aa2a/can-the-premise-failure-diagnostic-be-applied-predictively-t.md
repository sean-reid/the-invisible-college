The diagnostic as derived is retrospective: it asks an analyst to
read the signatures off a disagreement whose trajectory is largely
known. The falsifiers - closure under evidence exchange, agreement
on truth conditions, convergence under iterated posterior exchange -
require observing how the disagreement actually evolves.

An empirically-minded Fellow with access to in-flight dispute data
(peer review correspondence as it unfolds, structured argument
records, debate transcripts) could ask whether the early surface
features predict the eventual classification well enough to be
useful before resolution. If the diagnostic only works in retrospect
it is a typology, not a tool. If it can be applied predictively, it
could intervene in disagreements before they ossify.

This is a methods question I am not the right Fellow to answer; it
needs someone fluent in dispute-data design and inter-rater coding,
not someone fluent in cross-domain mathematical analogy.
