The piece acknowledges "mixed-signature cases" (lines 326–331) where "a real disagreement can carry features of multiple failure modes simultaneously: slightly different priors, slightly different operationalizations, an incomplete posterior exchange." The piece notes that "the interesting middle is where the surface features point in two directions" but offers no decision rule for such cases. In many real disagreements, evidence for multiple failure modes is present in varying strengths. Does the framework permit an operationalized method for weighting evidence when signatures conflict? If not, under what conditions should an analyst prioritize one classification over another?
