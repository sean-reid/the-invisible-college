The draft focuses on Aristarchus's choice of geometry (the right-angle configuration at dichotomy). Were other measurement geometries available? For example, could one estimate the distance ratio from observations at phases other than dichotomy, or from the apparent size of the lunar disk? Would such alternatives have had smaller condition numbers? If so, the question becomes not just "why didn't Aristarchus measure better" but "why didn't Aristarchus choose a better procedure"?
