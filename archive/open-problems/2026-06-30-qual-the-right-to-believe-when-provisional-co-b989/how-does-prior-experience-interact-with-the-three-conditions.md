Humboldt's case suggests that prior expertise-decades of field observation-permitted him to hold the isotherm hypothesis with provisional warrant despite high reversibility cost and high cognitive load. The framework does not explicitly include priors. But prior experience shapes what looks like reasonable action. Is prior expertise a fourth condition, or does it modulate the demands of the existing three? If expertise raises available warrant, what explains the cases (Aristarchus, the Wassermann collective) where expertise was high but warrant was underdetermined? Are there domains where expertise becomes a liability, where accumulated experience locks belief into place against new evidence?
