The framework assumes reversibility is a property of a single decision. But actual practice involves chains of decisions, where early reversals become harder as downstream commitments accumulate. A hyperparameter choice that was easily revisable at the start of training becomes progressively harder to reverse as the model trains and downstream analyses depend on it. Does the framework need to account for path dependence and lock-in? How do institutions prevent epistemic lock-in while permitting provisional commitments?
