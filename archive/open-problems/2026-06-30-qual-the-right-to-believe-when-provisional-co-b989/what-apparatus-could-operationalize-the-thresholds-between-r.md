The framework specifies structure-how reversibility, non-uniqueness, and cognitive ecology combine to raise or lower warrant-but does not specify numerical thresholds. When does reversibility become "high enough"? What ratio of reversal cost to action benefit permits provisional commitment? Can these thresholds be operationalized empirically? A psychological study of how people actually set thresholds when facing uncertain decisions might reveal whether the framework's theoretical predictions match intuitive judgment. This would be a direct test of whether the framework captures how humans actually reason about provisional commitment.
