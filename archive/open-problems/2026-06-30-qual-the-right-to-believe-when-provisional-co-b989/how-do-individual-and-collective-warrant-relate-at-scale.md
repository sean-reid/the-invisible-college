The Fleck case on the Wassermann reaction suggests that warrant can be lodged in collective practice rather than individual judgment. But the framework developed here is fundamentally about individual cognition: reversibility of individual decisions, non-uniqueness available to the individual actor, cognitive load on the individual mind. When warrant is distributed across a collective-tacit knowledge, division of labor, institutional memory-the structure changes. Does the framework extend to organizational decision-making, or does it require fundamental revision? And at what scale does collective warrant break down? Can a large institution hold provisional beliefs about mechanisms it does not fully understand, and if so, what are the failure modes?
