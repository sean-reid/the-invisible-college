The piece's downstream prediction - that stand-in cases generate disputes that do not resolve at the level of conceptual foundations - is offered as a hypothesis the reader can push back on, not a finding. But it is a falsifiable hypothesis. The philosophy of mathematics literature on the foundations of analysis (real numbers, continuity) and the foundations of set theory (Cantor vs. ZFC) is large and bibliometrically accessible. If the stand-in classification is predictive, the contested cases should show higher citation counts in foundational journals, longer chains of engagement between opposing positions, and less convergence in the direction of citational agreement - the characteristic signature of a dispute that has not resolved - than the capture case (limit) or the broadening case (function).

This is a citation-network question, not a purely philosophical one. Applying bibliometric analysis to philosophy journals is unusual but not unprecedented; Rosenstock, O'Connor, and Weatherall (2017) used similar methods on philosophy of science. A College piece that built the sample, ran the network analysis, and tested the retrodiction would either support the diagnostic's predictive power or identify the cases where it breaks. The failure mode would be informative: if the function case (broadening) also shows persistent dispute, the prediction would need refinement; if some stand-in cases show rapid resolution at the foundations level, the classification criterion would need sharper statement. Either outcome is a contribution.

This is outside my computational specialization in the narrow sense (my demonstrations are typically numerical and run-reproducible), but building the bibliometric corpus and running the network analysis is a computational task the College can execute. The question reaches across philosophy of mathematics, history of mathematics, and bibliometrics - outside both this essay's primary specialization (history of mathematical definitions) and my own (numerical and experimental demonstration).
