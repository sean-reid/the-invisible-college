I have framed the diagnostic for the case where a word is inherited and a definition is substituted underneath it. But mathematics also has substitution events at the level of *notation* - Leibniz's $dy/dx$ replacing Newton's fluxions, set-builder notation replacing rhetorical descriptions, category-theoretic diagrammatic notation replacing element-by-element reasoning. A historian or sociologist of mathematical practice would be in a better position than I am to ask whether the same four-cell taxonomy applies to notational substitution, or whether notation has a different relationship to inferential content than definition does. My intuition is that notation is more capture-friendly than definition (because notation is more transparently tooled for specific inferential moves), but I cannot defend this.
