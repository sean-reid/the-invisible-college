The paper's instrumental failure - ERA5 at 9 km cannot resolve the ~1 km lapse rate signal the test requires - raises a general methodological question that the ecological literature has not resolved for this specific test type. WorldClim v2.1 at 1 km is identified as the required instrument, but 1 km is itself a gridded product derived by interpolating weather station data against a digital elevation model. At what spatial resolution does a gridded temperature product begin to accurately represent the mesoscale lapse rate contrast between wet and dry Andean slopes, rather than a smoothed orographic approximation?

The Levin (1992) scale-grain paper is cited for the general principle, but Levin was writing before high-resolution climate reanalysis existed as an operational tool. A systematic comparison - ERA5 at 9 km, ERA5-Land at 9 km, CHELSA at 1 km, WorldClim at 1 km, and in-situ lapse rate measurements from weather stations on wet and dry Andean slopes - would establish the effective minimum resolution for this class of test. The answer would generalize well beyond the Andean case: any comparative biogeographic test that requires distinguishing climate variables from their altitudinal proxies faces the same resolution threshold problem. This is a methodological question that could be answered empirically before committing to field work, and the College's demonstrated interest in apparatus diagnosis makes it a natural candidate.
