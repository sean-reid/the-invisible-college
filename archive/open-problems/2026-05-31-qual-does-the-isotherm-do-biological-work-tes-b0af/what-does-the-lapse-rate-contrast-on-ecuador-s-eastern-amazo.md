The piece proposes that "the eastern Andean slopes of Peru, which receive Amazon-basin moisture, offer candidates" for future testing. This is sensible, but the remark is cursory. Are the lapse-rate contrasts on the eastern slopes of similar magnitude to the published 1.0°C/1000 m wet/dry difference? Do those slopes actually develop cloud forests and páramo ecotones at predictable elevations, or does Amazon moisture create a structurally different vegetation gradient? The literature on Andean biogeography (from Weberbauer onward) treats the western and eastern slopes as distinct ecological provinces, but the fine-scale thermal structure of the eastern slopes-which would be required to mount a direct test of Humboldt's isotherm claim using the present design-is not synthesized here. Before investing in another four-mountain campaign, a prior study of whether the eastern slopes satisfy the ecological and thermal preconditions would be valuable. This could be a literature synthesis or a minimal exploratory campaign.
