The piece treats detectability as a scalar D ∈ [0, 1] and the detection frontier as a threshold. There is a more general object available. A monitoring regime is a noisy channel from underlying violation types to observed signals, with a finite capacity set by what the criterion can resolve. The detection frontier is then a separability region in that channel - the subset of violation types whose induced signal distributions are distinguishable, at the regulator's confidence level, from non-violation. Detectability D becomes the divergence between a violation type's signal distribution and the non-violation baseline, and the threshold T becomes the regulator's separability tolerance. Cov(h, D) < 0 maps to a negative correlation between harm and signal-divergence across the violator population.

Three predictions seem to fall out of this framing that the verbal account does not make sharp. First, the act-itself / side-effect distinction has a natural channel-theoretic counterpart: side-effect Selection is the case where the violator does not know the channel encoding and exploits blind spots that exist accidentally; act-itself Selection is the case where the channel encoding is public and the violator can construct violations adversarially in the kernel of the encoding map. The two structural forms then respond differently to channel improvement: side-effect Selection benefits from increasing channel capacity (more dimensions of signal tracked); act-itself Selection benefits from channel randomization or stochastic auditing (preventing the violator from optimizing against a deterministic encoding). Whether the credit-rating versus clinical-trials cases bear this prediction out is testable.

Second, the framing connects directly to the adversarial-ML literature the revised draft now cites in passing. Detection mechanisms in adversarial ML have a well-developed theory of *certified robustness* - the set of input perturbations that provably do not change a classifier's output. A regulator could in principle specify the certified-robustness region of its monitoring criterion and use the size of that region as the formal analogue of the detection frontier's "thickness." The College does not currently have a Fellow with deep information-theory or adversarial-ML training, but the cross-domain payoff for an essay that worked the formal correspondence out carefully looks substantial. The current piece opens the door; an outside contribution could walk through.
