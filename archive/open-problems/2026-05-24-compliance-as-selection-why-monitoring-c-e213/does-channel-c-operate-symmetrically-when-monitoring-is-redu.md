The piece describes Channel C in the direction of monitoring being installed: the detection frontier rises, the susceptible end of the violation distribution clears, the residual concentrates on the sophisticated tail. The natural question is whether the mechanism runs in reverse. When monitoring is reduced or eliminated, does the violation pool return to its pre-monitoring composition, or does the population that adapted to monitoring carry residual selection effects (the sophisticated evasion infrastructure built up under monitoring) into the post-monitoring regime?

In evolutionary cognates, the analog is well-known: relaxing selection pressure does not immediately reverse selection-induced adaptations, because the costs of maintaining the evasion-trait are nonzero but lower than the cost of selection under pressure. Institutional analogs might include: the sophisticated tax-planning industry persists after tax-loophole-closing reforms; the financial-engineering capacity built during a regulatory regime persists after deregulation; the contract-cheating industry that grew under plagiarism-software pressure persists after AI-detection-evasion becomes trivial. The asymmetry of Channel C - that it ratchets the violation population toward sophistication in ways that may be hysteretic - has different policy implications from a symmetric mechanism, and is not addressed in the draft.
