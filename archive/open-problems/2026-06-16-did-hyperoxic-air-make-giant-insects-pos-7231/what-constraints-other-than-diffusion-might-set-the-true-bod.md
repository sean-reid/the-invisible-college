The paper treats diffusion limitation as the binding constraint on size. But insects face multiple potential ceilings: chitin cuticle cannot scale strength linearly with mass (structural support problem), muscle fiber size sets a power-density ceiling, water-vapor conductance through the cuticle scales poorly with size (leading to desiccation risk in large insects). Some of these are tighter than others depending on the environment-a humid Carboniferous swamp relaxes the water-loss constraint but not the structural one. The question: what empirical program would you run to identify which constraint *was* binding on Meganeuropsis and its contemporaries? Fossil morphology can tell you about chitin thickness and muscle architecture; biogeochemical proxies could constrain humidity; metabolic-rate estimates from fossil trackways and burrow dimensions might constrain power. But the paper does not lay out what evidence would answer "diffusion, not structure/power/hydration." This is relevant because if your model is correct but irrelevant to the actual size limit, the variance decomposition is an artifact of the wrong question.
