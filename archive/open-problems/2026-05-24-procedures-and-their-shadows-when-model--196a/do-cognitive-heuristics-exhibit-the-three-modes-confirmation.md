The framework treats optimization procedures as mathematical objects, but human hypothesis-testing shares the structural features the essay identifies: agents implicitly minimize a criterion (cognitive dissonance, prediction error, surprise) over a decision space (which hypothesis to endorse). If motivated reasoning or confirmation bias constitute misspecification of the cognitive "model" - the agent's prior over hypotheses systematically excludes the true state - then the framework predicts that the cognitive procedure will be drawn toward the region of the evidence space where the discrepancy between prior and data is largest.

This is the opposite of what naive accounts of confirmation bias predict. Naive accounts say that biased cognition converges toward evidence that *supports* the prior. The framework suggests a more nuanced picture: in "reveal" mode, the cognitive procedure should be drawn toward the evidence that most strains the prior - which is where the agent's attention becomes anxious, effortful, and easily tipped into motivated dismissal. In "amplify" mode, the procedure might produce confident-seeming endorsement of the prior precisely in the region where the prior-data gap is largest, because the cognitive loss function has treated the discrepancy as noise rather than as signal. The phenomenology of rationalization - the feeling of having considered and dismissed contrary evidence - may be the cognitive analogue of the bootstrap interval that is narrow because it has read dependence as information.

Whether this is empirically tractable depends on whether the "misspecification direction" can be operationalized in a cognitive experiment - for instance, by presenting subjects with evidence that is maximally inconsistent with a known prior and measuring both their certainty ratings and their recall. The framework predicts that the amplify mode would produce high certainty with systematic misrecollection of the contrary evidence; reveal mode would produce low certainty and accurate recall of the evidence that most troubled the prior. This distinguishes the two modes behaviorally and would require a different intervention strategy for each.
