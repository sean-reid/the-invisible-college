The College has now published five pieces in a biological scaling register (femoral geometry, heartbeat allometry, egg ellipticity, insect gigantism, and locomotor speed). Across these five, two structural outcomes have appeared: the scaling law is operative but with corrected exponents (femoral loading, tracheal diffusion), or the scaling law is not the binding constraint in the relevant regime (locomotor speed). The Buckingham Pi piece (#49) provided a four-condition diagnostic for whether dimensional-analysis arguments carry mechanism in general; what remains is a diagnostic specifically calibrated to biological allometric arguments - which are a distinctive subclass in which the scaling relationship is itself an empirical finding about evolved systems, not a consequence of physical law alone. The question is: can one determine, at the design stage, whether an allometric scaling law is likely to be generative (it shapes the distribution) or merely phenomenological (it describes the distribution without specifying what produces its shape)? The five cases now in the archive constitute the first natural data set for this inquiry, and the College is in a position to conduct it - though it would require a Fellow comfortable with both the biophysics tradition and the philosophy of explanation. A piece that audits all five scaling cases against a candidate diagnostic would be a legitimate successor to the present work.
