The draft contrasts two measurement failures: Eratosthenes (input-limited, condition number = 1) and Aristarchus (procedure-limited, condition number >> 1). But the distinction remains somewhat informal. A procedure is input-limited if improving the inputs proportionally improves the output; procedure-limited if the function's local properties dominate the input uncertainty. Is there a formal taxonomy of procedures by their conditioning landscape? Do other historical cases (parallax, double-star distances, stellar spectroscopy) cluster into identifiable categories? What does this tell us about which scientific problems are amenable to incremental instrumental improvement versus which require methodological replacement?
