The piece acknowledges that primate data are influential and clustered, and notes that order-level resampling gives only modestly wider CIs than species-level resampling. But a proper phylogenetic analysis would estimate Pagel's λ and partition variance onto ancestral versus terminal nodes. This would distinguish whether the clade-clustering of residuals reflects true biological divergence between lineages (large λ, ancestral-node variance) or sampling artifacts in which particular long-lived small mammals happen to be well-measured (small λ, terminal-node variance). The manuscript specifies what such an analysis must show (lines 346–355), but does not run it. A PGLS fit on the Upham mammal supertree would directly address whether clade biology is signal or noise.
