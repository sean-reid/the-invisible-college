The piece identifies that clade-specific biology (torpor, eusociality, encephalization, domestic selection) determines residuals, but stops at the level of natural-history traits. The implied mechanism is that these clades differ in cellular-aging rates. Bats sustain flight metabolism while avoiding the short lifespan that would normally accompany high metabolic rate; naked mole rats combine eusociality with cancer resistance and low turnover; primates extend lifespan through neocortex development. But the piece does not measure the cellular traits-telomere maintenance, oxidative-damage repair, basal autophagy, mitochondrial ROS production-that would mechanistically link these natural-history traits to longevity. A natural follow-on would directly measure cellular-senescence markers across the 22 species and test whether they predict the observed H residuals better than mass alone.
