The fundamental equations assume that b = 1/4 in L_max ∝ M^b is a universal scaling law, yet the clade analysis shows deviations by factors of 2–5 depending on biology. This suggests the quarter-power prediction is much less constraining than its theoretical derivations imply. Stahl's and Calder's exponents are usually justified through metabolic-rate scaling (surface-area arguments, oxygen-diffusion constraints, metabolic-level closure). If metabolic rate were the true driver of longevity variation, residuals should be independent of ecology and clade. What would it take to test whether metabolic theory can explain the observed clade-clustering of deviations? What measured metabolic rates (resting, maximal, per-cell) would be needed for a mechanistic test?
