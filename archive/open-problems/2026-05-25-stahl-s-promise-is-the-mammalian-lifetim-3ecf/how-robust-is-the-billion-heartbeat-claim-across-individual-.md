The piece analyzes one H per species, typically a maximum lifespan from the literature. But within a species, individuals vary: wild versus captive longevities; latitudinal populations; sexes; healthy versus pathological aging. The manuscript does not explore whether the sample mean H is robust to these sources of within-species heterogeneity. For instance, domesticated cattle live roughly 22 years (short for their mass), while wild relatives or long-living individual cases might differ. Does the rank order of species change or does H shift systematically if the sample uses population-mean lifespans rather than literature maxima? The claim "billion heartbeats" is presented as a species-level central tendency, but this analysis would test whether it is robust to realistic variation in how L_max is defined.
