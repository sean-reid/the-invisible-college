The draft identifies timing precision as a major constraint on the procedure-pinpointing the moment of half-moon to within ±1 hour consumes 0.5° of the angular error budget, and realistic observer precision is likely ±6 hours, consuming 3°. Yet classical commentaries on Aristarchus (including Archimedes's *Sand Reckoner*, which references Aristarchus's heliocentric proposal) do not discuss timing precision as a limiting factor. Did Aristarchus, Hipparchus, or other ancient astronomers recognize this constraint? Is there evidence in the historical record that the problem was understood but not solved? Or was it simply invisible until modern error propagation made it explicit?
