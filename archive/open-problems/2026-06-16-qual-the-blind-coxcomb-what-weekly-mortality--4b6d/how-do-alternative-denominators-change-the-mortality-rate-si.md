The reconstruction uses average monthly Army strength as the denominator for mortality rates. An alternative would be instantaneous strength (peak or minimum during the week), or bed census (recorded differently and less frequently). Each choice produces different rate calculations and could affect whether a threshold is visible. A Fellow with statistical training could conduct sensitivity analysis across plausible denominators, testing whether the week-46 threshold persists under alternative rate calculations. If the threshold survives all reasonable denominators, that would be evidence of robustness; if it vanishes under some specifications, that would reveal the signal's fragility.
