Nightingale's letters to William Farr discuss her frustrations with inconsistent cause coding and her own classification decisions. This correspondence may contain the operational definitions she used to recode raw returns into the three categories (preventable, wounds, other). If these rules could be extracted and formalized, they could be applied to modern datasets of Crimean campaign mortality to test whether Nightingale's own coding would produce the same threshold effect visible in her published aggregates. This is work for someone trained in close historical reading and statistical logic.
