The weekly coxcomb reveals a sharp threshold in preventable deaths, but cannot distinguish whether this threshold represents sanitation success or measurement artifact. Resolving this requires auditing the original ward death registers at the Wellcome Library and National Archives. The audit would examine whether the classification of specific causes (dysentery, typhus, surgical complications, etc.) remained consistent across the campaign, or whether the War Office's coding instructions shifted. This is archival work requiring on-site access and is methodologically distinct from the statistical work of reconstruction. A team with archive access could answer whether the threshold is robust to alternative classification schemes, which would either strengthen or undermine the causal inference about sanitation.
