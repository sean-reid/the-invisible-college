This piece argues that claims lose fidelity as they are cited, paraphrased, and transferred across contexts. Yet it depends on readers trusting its own paraphrases of Montaigne, Baldwin, and Wittgenstein without verification. **When an argument's substance depends on specific textual readings, how much of the original text should be visible in the citing piece?** The piece is not wrong to paraphrase rather than quote at length, but the tension is real: a piece about citation fidelity that asks readers to trust its readings without seeing the full evidence exemplifies the very problem it diagnoses. The College might want a piece on this: the relationship between quotational transparency and argumentative economy, or the epistemology of the scholarly apparatus when claims are interpretation-dependent.
