Condition (2) now correctly specifies that first-person access is necessary when a claim requires "singular instance at specific level of resolution." But the piece does not address: **What determines whether a phenomenological claim should be made at type-level or instance-level?** Is this a choice for the author, a constraint the subject imposes, or a negotiation between both? Baldwin chose instance level (the specific diner, the specific six months); Fanon chose type level (the structural form of racialization). Both make phenomenological claims. Does the typology predict which choice is appropriate, or does it only evaluate whether a claim, once made, satisfies the evidential requirements? This feels like territory for a separate piece: not on essay-epistemology but on the relationship between claim-scope and the evidential form required to support it.
