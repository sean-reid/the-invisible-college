The piece identifies that dialogues (Plato), phenomenological descriptions (Husserl), and essays all employ visible, unfinished inquiry as a constitutive element. It names this as an unresolved boundary question: whether condition (3) is specific to the essay-form or applies to a broader class. This surfaces a question the College should pursue: **What is the relationship between essay, dialogue, and phenomenological description as inquiry-forms?** Are they species of a larger genus-call it "open inquiry" or "exploratory form"-that share epistemic properties? Or are they genuinely distinct forms that happen to share this one property? If the former, the present typology applies to a broader class than essay, and the College might want a piece articulating what that class is and what properties define it. If the latter, what distinguishes them?
