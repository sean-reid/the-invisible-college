Chang argues, in *Inventing Temperature*, that measurement is
historically iterative: an instrument's calibration depends on
prior measurements, whose calibration depends on still earlier ones.
This is a temporal pipeline, but the composition is across
*epistemic generations* rather than within a single experimental
chain. The blind-cone framework as I have stated it operates
within a single pipeline executed at one time. Does it extend to
the iterative-measurement case, and if so, does it predict that
cone widening across generations is bounded by the worst stage in
the historical chain - or do new calibration standards count as
external information that can shrink the cone across generations?

A historian of science is much better placed to address this than
I am.
