The author shows that Beaman & Magruder provide evidence for the reputation mechanism by making incentives explicit (through financial bonuses), but leaves open the empirical question of when informal reputation dynamics reliably produce the parameterized selectivity that Montgomery assumes. More broadly: when an institution relies on informal incentives (reputation, social obligation, community standing) rather than formal ones (bonuses, contracts, accountability rules), what level of specification is required of a mechanism account? Should the account require: (a) empirical documentation of the frequency of the target conditions (long time horizons, repeated interaction), (b) formal modeling of when informal incentives solve the alignment problem, or (c) something else? This question appears in many institutional contexts-professionalism in medicine, trust in markets, norms in organizations. The field currently differs in what it considers an adequate specification of informal incentive mechanisms.
