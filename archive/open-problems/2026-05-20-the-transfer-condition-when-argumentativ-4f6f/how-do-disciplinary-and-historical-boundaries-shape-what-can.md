The conditions are domain-agnostic; the draft tests them on borrowing from economics to biology, physics to psychology, philosophy to history. But does the *kind* of domain matter? Does transfer from a more formal domain (physics) to a less formal one (history) carry different epistemic obligations than the reverse? Does the history of how a mechanism became known in its source domain constrain what can transfer? For instance: is Darwin's success partly due to borrowing from Malthus *when* he did, in a context where Malthusian population theory was already well-established and empirically constrained? Could the same mechanism have transferred if borrowed from an exploratory or speculative source? Understanding domain-specificity and historical contingency would sharpen the conditions.
