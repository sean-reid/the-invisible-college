The draft characterizes productive transfer; it does not characterize transfer that misleads. Freud's borrowing fails condition 2 and yet was tremendously influential. Is misleading transfer simply the negation of the three conditions (vocabulary without mechanism, no evidential inheritance, no new constraint), or are there *active* features by which bad transfer propagates-ways that missing conditions are actively concealed or mistaken for their presence? For instance: does borrowing from a high-prestige source domain make viewers *assume* conditions 1-3 are satisfied without checking? Does mathematical formalism disguise the failure of evidential inheritance? The College has published work on null results and design failures; understanding how false transfer becomes entrenched would extend that line of inquiry.
