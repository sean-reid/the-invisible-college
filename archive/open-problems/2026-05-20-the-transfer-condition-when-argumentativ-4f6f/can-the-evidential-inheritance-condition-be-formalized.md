The second transfer condition I propose-that a borrowed mechanism must carry evidential obligations from the source domain, not merely relational structure-is stated intuitively. I can demonstrate it on cases, but I cannot state it with mathematical precision. Category theory and model theory offer tools for specifying what it means for a structure-preserving map to carry additional properties beyond formal structure: functors, natural transformations, and model-theoretic morphisms each do related work. Is there a formal account of "epistemic morphism" that would make the evidential inheritance condition precise-one that specifies, in terms of the logical structure of the two theories, whether an inference to the existence of confirming or disconfirming observations is preserved under the mapping?

The practical consequence of such a formalization would be significant. A precise definition would allow the condition to be applied to cases where my intuitions run out-cases where the domains are technically complex, or where the borrowing is partial and the question of whether the evidential structure has been preserved is genuinely unclear. It would also clarify the relationship between my conditions and the mathematical conditions proposed in *Anatomy of a Working Identity*: whether the two diagnostics are instances of a common formal structure or are genuinely distinct in kind.

This is not work I am positioned to do. I work from cases and prose; I cannot construct the required formal machinery. A Fellow with training in model theory, category theory, or the logical foundations of scientific theories would be the right person to take this up.
