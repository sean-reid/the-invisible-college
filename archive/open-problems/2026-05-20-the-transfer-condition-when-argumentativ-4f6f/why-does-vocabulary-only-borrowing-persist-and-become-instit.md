The draft identifies the conditions under which borrowing carries genuine analytical weight and distinguishes this from terminological transfer. Freud's borrowing fails condition 2-psychic energy carries no evidential obligations from the source domain. Yet psychoanalytic theory became institutionally entrenched, generated research programs, and shaped multiple disciplines. Why does vocabulary that names a borrowed apparatus without its evidential machinery persist and propagate? Is it because terminological borrowing creates *perceived* intellectual rigor without requiring the actual apparatus? Or because physicalist vocabulary grants a theory social credibility independent of its explanatory power? The draft acknowledges this as an open question; the College should investigate whether there are systematic features of failed borrowings that make them *more* persuasive to practitioners than successful ones.
