Rawls borrows the original position from decision theory (condition 1 satisfied), uses it to generate a novel constraint on political philosophy (condition 3 satisfied), but abandons decision theory's evidential machinery (condition 2 not satisfied). Yet this is published, influential, and intellectually generative. The draft introduces "structured question-setting" to handle this case, but does not fully develop whether question-setting *without* evidentiary closure can be a legitimate mode of intellectual contribution. Are there principles that would identify *when* a borrowed apparatus earns its place through question-generation alone? When is that sufficient, and when is it a warning sign of vocabulary without substance?
