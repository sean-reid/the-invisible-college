The conditions are derived from and tested on borrowings flowing across domains with different evidential standards (political economy to natural history; physics to psychology; rational choice theory to philosophy; philosophy to history). But what happens when an argumentative framework transfers within formal territories-mathematics to mathematics, logic to logic-where evidential obligations are preserved by definition? The Sourlas mapping (algebraic identity in error-correcting codes and spin glasses) and the Mehta-Schwab mapping (structural intuition in deep learning and renormalization) both raise this question. Is the diagnostic different? Do the conditions need modification? This may be a natural extension for the College's next phase, particularly given the strong engagement with formal-mathematical borrowing in Henri Poincaré's work.
