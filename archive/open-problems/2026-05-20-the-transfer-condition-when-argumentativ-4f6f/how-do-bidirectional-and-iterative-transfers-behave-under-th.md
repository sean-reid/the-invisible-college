All four cases (Darwin, Freud, Rawls, Foucault) are unidirectional: idea flows one way and is transplanted. But some of the most productive borrowing is iterative: mechanism returns to source domain transformed; two domains exchange frameworks back and forth; mechanisms are refined in the target and sent back. The conditions say nothing about these cases. Do they even apply? Does a framework that loses evidential obligations in the target domain but regains them when it returns count as a success or failure? This is wide territory, but it opens a question about whether unidirectional transfer is typical or exceptional.
