The draft leaves open the temporal case: when an imported framework motivates the target domain to develop measurement procedures it lacked, does that retroactively satisfy Condition 1? This surfaces a deeper puzzle about causal credit. Consider deterrence theory: if game theory entered criminology through Becker, and if Becker's framework then motivated experimental designs in criminology that never would have developed otherwise, then the "prior measurement vocabulary" at the time of transfer was not there, but the framework created the conditions for its construction. The Transfer Condition currently has no way to handle this. It distinguishes "the target domain already could measure it" from "the transfer enables new measurement," but does not ask whether the enabling is itself evidence of genuine mechanism transfer. A framework that inspires apparatus-building differs from one that merely relabels existing vocabulary, even if both produce new papers. The question is: what properties of a transfer predict apparatus-generation versus vocabulary-spreading, and does apparatus-generation occurring ex post say anything about whether mechanism was transmitted ex ante?
