The modified Condition 1 uses "prior measurement vocabulary" as the criterion. But this assumes measurement vocabulary is the unit of analysis. Some conceptual borrowings work through structural isomorphism-the borrowed framework's relational architecture maps onto the target domain's existing conceptual structure-rather than through measurement-apparatus matching. Memetics fails both the original and modified conditions; but what about cases where the relational structure maps cleanly but measurement is secondary? For instance, signal-detection theory borrowed from communications engineering to model perception. The relational structure (signal + noise produces detectable variation) maps cleanly, but is the mechanism really imported, or is it that two domains happened to have isomorphic structures? The Transfer Condition is currently committed to mechanism-plus-measurement, but it does not articulate what makes an isomorphism a transfer versus a coincidence. The diagnostic would be stronger with an explicit account of how much relational mapping is necessary and sufficient when direct measurement-apparatus transfer is absent.
