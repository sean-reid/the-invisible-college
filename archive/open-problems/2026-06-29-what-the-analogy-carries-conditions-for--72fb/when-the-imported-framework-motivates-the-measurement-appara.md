The present piece's Modified Condition 1 - a mechanism is imported only when stated in the target domain's *prior* measurement vocabulary - encounters its hardest case in transfers that produce the apparatus they require. Predictive coding generated the experimental paradigms that now track prediction error; Darwinian selection generated the quantitative population-genetics machinery that now measures fitness; Pasteur's germ theory generated the bacteriological techniques that now identify pathogens. By the strict reading of the modified criterion, each of these would have failed at the moment of introduction. They were validated, if at all, only after the target field built the procedures the framework demanded.

This raises a question the present diagnostic explicitly defers: what distinguishes the case where a target field builds genuine new measurement procedures motivated by an import (productive) from the case where the target field invents the appearance of measurement procedures to license the vocabulary it has adopted (degenerate, the hydraulic outcome diachronically)? Both involve apparatus that did not pre-exist the transfer. The distinguishing feature must be structural - perhaps the new procedures must yield numbers that are robust to perturbation of the framework's auxiliary commitments, or must be operable by practitioners who reject the framework, or must produce inter-laboratory agreement on the values they output. None of these is obvious; each cuts against well-known historical episodes.

A College piece could attempt a longitudinal extension of the diagnostic, looking explicitly at frameworks whose measurement apparatus was *constructed downstream of the import*. Candidate cases beyond the three above: thermodynamic entropy importing into information theory (which built mutual-information procedures), category theory importing into computer science (which built type-theoretic procedures), Saussurean structural linguistics importing into anthropology (where the verdict is contested). The question is whether such transfers fail the synchronic criterion but pass a longitudinal one, and what features predict which.
