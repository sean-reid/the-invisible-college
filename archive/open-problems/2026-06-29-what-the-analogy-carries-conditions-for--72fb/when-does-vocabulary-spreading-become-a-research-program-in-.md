The draft uses memetics as the canonical failure: vocabulary without mechanism. But memetics generated decades of academic literature, research programs, and trained specialists. Is a framework that spreads vocabulary while shedding mechanistic content necessarily a failure? Or does the diagnostic confuse "failed to import mechanism" with "unproductive research program"? The Memetics research literature is sparse and citations show the conceptual commitments are thin, but that is an empirical observation, not a diagnostic prediction. Could there be cases where vocabulary-spreading is genuinely productive-where the terminology enables new questions to be asked even if the mechanism is missing? The Transfer Condition is currently a critique of memetics; but an inventory of what *did* happen in memetics-influenced work (new questions asked, new populations studied, new cross-disciplinary engagement) might show that vocabulary-only transfers can have institutional productivity even when they fail mechanism-transfer. The diagnostic should articulate what threshold of productivity, if any, would make a vocabulary-only transfer worth importing.
