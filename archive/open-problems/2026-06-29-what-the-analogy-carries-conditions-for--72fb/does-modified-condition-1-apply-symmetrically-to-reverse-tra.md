The essay's central case study is evolutionary biology lending to game theory (ESS). Game theory subsequently lent back to evolutionary biology: inclusive fitness theory, kin selection coefficients, and evolutionarily stable coalition structures were all reformulated using payoff matrices and Nash-equilibrium logic after Maynard Smith. The bi-directional case raises a structural question the essay does not address. If Domain A borrows from Domain B and thereby expands Domain B's measurement vocabulary (as ESS did for game theory's concept of invasion stability), then when Domain B borrows back, it is borrowing into the measurement vocabulary that Domain A's prior import helped create. Does Modified Condition 1 treat that expanded vocabulary as "prior" apparatus for the reverse transfer? If yes, then productive transfers can bootstrap each other's measurement capacity in ways that cumulatively satisfy the condition through rounds of exchange rather than through either domain's independent development. This is a plausible and important mechanism for how scientific fields mature, and it is invisible to a synchronic condition applied at a single moment of transfer. If no - if "prior" means prior to the entire bilateral exchange - then the condition may incorrectly classify the reverse transfer as failing even when it is demonstrably productive.
