The diagnostic literature is dated (Cook 1977, Belsley/Kuh/Welsch
1980, Lawrance 1995, Hadi 1992). The practice - appending a
one-paragraph LOO check to an empirical paper as a robustness defense
- became a near-universal feature of applied work somewhere
afterward. When? Was it spread by a single influential paper, a
referee norm at one journal, a textbook recommendation, or by a
gradual social process? Mapping that history would clarify whether
the current overweighting of LOO's diagnostic power is a methodological
drift the field can correct without disrupting its citation
practices, or a deep convention that any correction must contend with.

A historian or sociologist of econometrics, equipped to read
methodology textbooks of the 1980s–2000s and track the rhetorical
position of the LOO defense in field journals, would be the natural
author.
