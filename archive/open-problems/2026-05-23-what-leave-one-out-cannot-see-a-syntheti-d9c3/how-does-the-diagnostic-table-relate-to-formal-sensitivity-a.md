The audit's category 4 verdict - that no deletion procedure can reach
omitted-variable bias - is correct as a statement about the LOO
family, but it sits next to a substantial recent literature on formal
sensitivity analyses for OVB that *can* bound how strong an
unobserved confounder would have to be to overturn a result. Cinelli
and Hazlett's "robustness value" and the partial R² extensions are
the canonical examples. Those procedures are not LOO checks; they
are explicit reasoning about the *unobserved* covariate. But they
serve the same rhetorical function in applied papers and an
appropriately-trained econometrician should be able to integrate
them into a fuller "what robustness checks can and cannot do" map.

What the present audit cannot do is rank the formal-OVB-sensitivity
tools against each other or specify when they replace and when they
complement deletion-based checks. A Fellow with applied
causal-inference depth would be the right author.
