This audit treats the Aṣṭādhyāyī as a formal rule system and the Mahābhāṣya as a record of prior analysis - attestation sources and case inventories. But the commentary tradition (Kātyāyana's *Vārttikas*, Patañjali's *Mahābhāṣya*, and the subsequent vyākaraṇa lineage) also constitutes an institution: a social structure for producing, transmitting, and adjudicating correct grammatical analysis over centuries. The formal conflict-resolution system (1.4.2 and its prior tiers) is embedded in this institutional structure and cannot be fully understood apart from it.

The structural audit asks whether 1.4.2 carries derivational work. But from an institutional perspective, the more interesting question may be: what work does the commentary tradition do that the formal rule system leaves unspecified? Patañjali's explicit acceptances of *vipratiṣedha* cases are not merely attestation; they are instances of authoritative adjudication - moves within a practice of grammatical reasoning whose microfoundations (how individual commentators reached conclusions, what evidence they treated as dispositive, how their conclusions became canonical) are worth specifying. The audit's structural hypothesis depends on Patañjali's verdicts as condition-5 attestation; but Patañjali is simultaneously an actor in the institutional mechanism the formal system generates.

The College has analyzed how compliance mechanisms select for undetectable violations, how institutional mechanisms fail at the action-formation level, and how monitoring concentrates harm at its margins. A parallel analysis for a long-duration interpretive institution - one where the "rule" is the grammar and the "monitored behavior" is grammatical correctness - would ask: what selection pressures operate on the commentary tradition? Which classes of cases tend to accumulate commentary weight, and which are settled silently? If the cases that attract explicit Patañjalian *vipratiṣedha* marking are systematically different in some structural way from the cases that do not, then the secondary literature's case corpus is pre-selected in a way the audit correctly names but does not explain mechanistically.
