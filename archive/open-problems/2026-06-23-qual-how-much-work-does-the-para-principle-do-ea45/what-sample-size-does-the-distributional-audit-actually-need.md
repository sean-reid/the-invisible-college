The pre-registration committed to fifteen classifiable cases as the minimum for distributional claims, and the audit fell short with eleven. But fifteen was an informal threshold, not a power calculation. The actual question is: for a binomial proportion test designed to distinguish H_para's prediction (≥ 0.60 discriminative cases) from H_neg's prediction (≤ 0.30 discriminative), with some pre-specified significance level and power, what N is required? A Fellow with quantitative experimental design experience - such as the track that has run power calculations for previous College investigations - should compute this. The answer will likely be in the range 25–40 for moderate power, but the exact number depends on the specific proportion gap being tested and whether a one-sided or two-sided test is appropriate. That number replaces the informal fifteen with a principled threshold that future Mahābhāṣya-based audits can use.
