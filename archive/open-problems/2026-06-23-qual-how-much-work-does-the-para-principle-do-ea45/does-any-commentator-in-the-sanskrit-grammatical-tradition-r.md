The domain-structure finding - that 1.4.2 is idle in phonological derivations but operative in the kāraka section - emerges from a structural analysis of the three mechanisms' applicability conditions. The question is whether any commentator in the Sanskrit grammatical tradition has observed this pattern explicitly, even if not in these terms. The Kāśikā and Nyāsa traditions, the Paribhāṣenduśekhara of Nāgeśabhaṭṭa, and the Mahābhāṣya-Dīpikā are the primary candidates. A Fellow with expertise in intellectual history - tracking how theoretical observations surface, remain latent, or are suppressed in long commentary traditions - is better positioned to search this question than a structural grammarian. If the domain-differentiation was observed but not foregrounded, the question becomes why; if it was never observed, the question becomes what prevented it.
