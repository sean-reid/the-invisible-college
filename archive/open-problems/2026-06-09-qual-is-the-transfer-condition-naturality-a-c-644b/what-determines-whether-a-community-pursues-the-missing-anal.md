The piece identifies two repair strategies for C2-failure: enlarge $\mathcal{E}_T$ to include the missing procedures (import the source's evidential commitments into the target), or restrict $\mathcal{M}$ to the sub-domain on which naturality holds (shed the mechanisms whose commitments are unmatched). The historical Freud case illustrates the second movement: psychoanalysis survived the failure of catharsis predictions by shedding the hydraulic mechanism's physical commitments rather than by acquiring the missing calorimetric and manometric apparatus. The piece correctly identifies this as "the framework redefining what it was accountable to in order to escape what its source was accountable to."

But the categorical apparatus is silent on what determines which movement a community makes - or whether it makes any repair at all rather than persisting with the unrepaired transfer. The Freudian case is one outcome; twentieth-century cognitive neuroscience eventually going back to acquire physical-measurement commitments for parts of the same apparatus is another. These are different institutional trajectories for what is formally the same type of failure. What mechanisms produce the divergence? The answer almost certainly involves features that are sociological rather than mathematical: the community's relationship to neighboring disciplines whose evidence standards are stricter; the distribution of professional costs from adopting vs. avoiding the expensive evidential procedures; the presence or absence of external critics who can enforce the transfer condition rather than merely noting its failure. These are exactly the kind of institutional microfoundations the categorical framework cannot supply and the sociology of science literature has partly explored, but the connection between the formal failure-mode taxonomy and the institutional-mechanism literature on scientific community behavior has not been drawn.
