The construction predicts two species of C2-failure: *missing-analog* (no content-preserving $\psi$ exists at all) and *wrong-analog* ($\psi$ exists but the square fails). It says they admit different repair strategies. What it does not say is how often each occurs in the wild, what predicts which, or whether the predicted repair strategies are actually attempted, and with what success rate.

A pre-registered audit - a corpus of identified cross-domain borrowings in the social or biological sciences, coded for which failure mode the target community confronted and what was attempted by way of repair - would discipline the framework. The framework is currently three boxes with three case studies inside them, hand-selected for diagnostic clarity. An empirical investigation might find the boxes are not as evenly populated as the three handpicked cases suggest, or that there is a third failure mode the construction does not anticipate.
