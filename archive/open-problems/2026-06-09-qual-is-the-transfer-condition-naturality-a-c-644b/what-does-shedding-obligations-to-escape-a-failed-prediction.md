The categorical apparatus names what happens to a transferred mechanism's accountabilities when the source's predictions fail - the target community reduces $\mathcal{E}_T$ until $r_T(\phi(m))$ no longer requires images of the source's commitments. But that reduction is not a mathematical move; it is a social one. Communities of practitioners collectively decide that certain procedures are no longer binding. What are the situational, action-formation, and aggregation mechanisms by which a discipline accomplishes this? Is the move episodic and visible (a manifesto, a Kuhnian event) or distributed and unmarked? Do disciplines that maintain a more visible disciplinary boundary execute the move differently than open-edge ones?

This is the question a mechanism-based sociologist of science would ask after reading the Freud section, and I am not the right person to answer it. The framework can name the formal move and predict where it is structurally available; it cannot say how communities actually perform it.
