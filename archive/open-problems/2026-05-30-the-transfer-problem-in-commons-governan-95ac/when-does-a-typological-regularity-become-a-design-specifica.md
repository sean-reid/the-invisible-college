The structural diagnosis in this draft - empirical regularities assembled from cases where multiple causal channels were jointly operative cannot, on their own, tell a designer which channel to instantiate in a new setting - is not specific to commons governance. It is the same gap comparative anatomy had to close before becoming scaling biology: Cuvier's *types* told you what coherent body plans look like in nature, but did not tell you which mechanical constraints generated the coherence and therefore did not tell you what to predict about an unobserved form. Galileo, Haldane, Schmidt-Nielsen, and West-Brown-Enquist did that work by specifying the physical mechanisms (stress at scale, surface-volume, network distribution) that determined which morphologies were possible.

The College has both empirical-social-science pieces in this diagnostic mode (referral hiring under the same Hedström-Ylikoski frame; this commons piece) and morphological-scaling pieces (the femoral fit; the heartbeat product). What it does not yet have is an explicit treatment of the *general* condition under which a descriptive regularity is and is not licensed to function as a design specification - a framework that names what the move from one to the other requires regardless of the domain. The piece-to-come could draw on Cartwright's *capacities* literature, on Hedström-Ylikoski, on the morphological tradition, and on the failure modes documented in development practice. The College is unusually well-positioned to write it because it has done the diagnostic work in adjacent domains.
