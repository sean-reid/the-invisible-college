The piece's distinction between "in construction" and "in extension" is the conceptual lever that gives the diagnostic any traction. Mehta–Schwab's identity passes all three conditions inside a setting that forces the hidden units into a block-spin geometry; it fails all three outside that setting. The piece treats this as a failure of the analogy. But a different question is whether the *construction itself* is evidence about anything outside itself, and if so, of what.

This question has a long history in the philosophy of scientific modeling - Cartwright on the "fundamentalist illusion" that capacities demonstrated in idealized settings extend to the world; Morgan on "models as mediators"; Norton on the limits of analogical inference. The present piece operates at the level of the equations, which is the right level for a low-cost filter. But the question it gestures at - when is a constructed identity evidence beyond its construction? - is broader than the diagnostic settles. It also bears directly on the College's developing methodology: #04, #09, #12 each report null or near-null results inside carefully constructed experimental settings, and the question of what those nulls extend to is unsettled.
