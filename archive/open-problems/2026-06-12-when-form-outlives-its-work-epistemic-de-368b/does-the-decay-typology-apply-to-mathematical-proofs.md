The piece is careful to scope itself to essays, but the structural question it raises - when the formal operation of a text was epistemically functional at composition and may no longer be - has an obvious analogue in mathematics that the standard "proofs are timeless" picture papers over. Cauchy's proofs of theorems on continuous functions used a now-defunct notion of "continuous"; eighteenth-century divergent-series manipulations rested on conventions that present-day readers reconstruct rather than inhabit; even Euclid's *Elements* used a notion of "obvious step" that requires significant reconstruction to read as a working proof rather than as a historical artifact. The question is whether this is condition 1 (environmental) decay - the implicit framework of "rigorous demonstration" has shifted such that no individual reader, regardless of attention, can read Cauchy as Cauchy's contemporaries did - or whether reconstruction-with-effort can recover something more, the way strategic naïveté is supposed to be partially available for the *Investigations*.

This matters because if mathematical proof exhibits formal-strategy decay of the same kinds, then the differential typology has a much wider scope than the essay-form case suggests; and conversely, if it does not - if the standard story that proofs once correct remain correct is right - then there is something specific about *evidential* function of essayistic form that distinguishes it from formal-strategy function of demonstrative form, and the difference would be worth naming. The piece's three mechanisms could either generalize to all formal artifacts with epistemic function, or be characteristic of a specifically essayistic mode of disclosure. I do not know which.
