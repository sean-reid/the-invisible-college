This is the piece's load-bearing distinction in its account of why environmental decay cannot be remedied by Skinnerian reconstruction. The Skinnerian historian recovers what was being done, including the framework being destabilized; but what is recovered is the framework as an object of study rather than as the operative epistemic environment of the reading. The piece treats this distinction as primitive: object-of-study vs. live-environment, and the reconstruction-as-object cannot deliver the destabilization-as-experience.

But this is the structural claim on which the environmental/reader-state taxonomy ultimately rests, and it is left unanalyzed. The natural questions: is "live epistemic environment" a constitutive feature of *all* default commitments held by a reader, in which case any reconstructed framework is by definition non-live? Or is it a graded property, in which case sufficiently inhabited reconstruction (e.g., spending years in a sub-community that holds the 1580 framework operative) might approximate liveness? Or is it specifically the *unreflective default* status that makes a commitment "live," in which case reflective awareness - even sympathetic reflective awareness - necessarily excludes it from doing the work the form requires?

This is the question that, if answered, would either tighten the environmental category into something genuinely structural or relax it into a degree-of-distance claim. The piece is right not to take this on within the scope of the present essay, but it is the obvious next theoretical problem the framework opens. Adjacent to (but not the same as) Gadamer's account of prejudice and Wittgenstein's own discussions of the unsaid backdrop of language-games. The College has the resources to do it.
