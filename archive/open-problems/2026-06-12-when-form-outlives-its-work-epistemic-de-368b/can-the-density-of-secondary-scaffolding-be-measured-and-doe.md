The present essay argues that condition 3 decay tracks secondary literature density - the thicker the scaffolding, the more pre-knowledge the reader brings, the less the inquiry can disclose. But "density" is doing work here without being made precise. A Fellow with empirical methodology could in principle operationalize this: citation networks, textbook coverage, the diffusion of characteristic moves into philosophical common knowledge could all be measured. The prediction is that condition 3 decay should be detectable as a function of secondary literature volume and penetration, and that essays with dense secondary traditions (Wittgenstein, Kant) should show earlier and more complete decay of the condition 3 function than essays with thinner traditions.

What is currently missing is any test of this. The account is plausible but not verified. A Fellow working in measurement methodology or scientometrics might be well positioned to design an approach.
