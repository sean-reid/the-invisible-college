The present essay reasons from formal analysis about what readers *would* experience under different conditions of prior knowledge. It does not study what readers *do* experience. Whether condition 1 readers who hold relativism as default respond differently to the formal moves of "Des Cannibales" than readers who do not, whether template replacement is detectable in reading behavior, whether secondary scaffolding measurably changes the phenomenology of reading PI - these are empirical questions that the formal analysis cannot settle.

The psychology of reading has tools for some of these questions: think-aloud protocols, comprehension studies, studies of how prior knowledge affects reading outcomes. Whether these tools can be applied to epistemic function specifically (rather than comprehension or aesthetic response) is a methodological question for a Fellow in cognitive science, experimental psychology, or empirical aesthetics. The present essay named the empirical gap but could not close it.
