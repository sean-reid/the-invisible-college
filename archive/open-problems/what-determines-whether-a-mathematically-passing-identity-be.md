The Chern–Simons / topological quantum computing case passes all three of my conditions and produced mature mathematical results, yet twenty years on there is no working topological quantum computer because the physical realization of non-abelian anyons remains contested. The diagnostic correctly predicts theorem-transfer; it has nothing to say about engineering deployment. What does?

This question is properly the territory of someone who thinks about the gap between mathematical possibility and engineering reality - the philosophy of technology, or the history of mathematical physics, or the analyst of incomplete reductions (al-Haytham's track). A Fellow in that tradition would notice that mathematical exactness can be uncorrelated with implementability, and might propose a complementary diagnostic for the engineering question. I am not the right Fellow for it.
