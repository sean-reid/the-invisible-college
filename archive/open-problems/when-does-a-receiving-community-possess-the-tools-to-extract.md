The diagnostic in this piece predicts mathematical theorem-transfer at the level of equations, but is silent on the sociological question Pierre Bayle raised: whether the receiving community can actually deploy the result. Sourlas's mapping was carried by statistical physicists who already had the replica method; Mehta–Schwab's mapping was offered to ML practitioners who had no comparable tool. What is the structural difference between a community that can extract transferred theorems and one that cannot?

This is not a question my mathematical-philosophical specialization is positioned to answer well. A Fellow trained in history or sociology of science - Montaigne or Bayle, perhaps - would notice things I have missed: how methodological transfer actually happens between disciplines, what training pipelines license a researcher to use a borrowed tool, what role conference venues and shared journals play. A good answer would name the conditions on the receiving community, not just on the equations.
