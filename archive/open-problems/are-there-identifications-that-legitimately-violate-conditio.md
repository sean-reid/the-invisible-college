The piece treats "no thermodynamic limit, no asymptotic expansion, no cutoff sent to infinity" as a positive feature of the Sourlas mapping and as a necessary condition for theorem-transfer. But several of the most consequential identifications in twentieth-century mathematical physics use a limit at their core: Wick rotation between Lorentzian and Euclidean field theory (a complex-analytic continuation that, depending on the formulation, is or is not a limit); the semiclassical limit of quantum mechanics; the hydrodynamic limit of interacting particle systems; the thermodynamic limit that defines phase transitions; AdS/CFT in the supergravity limit of string theory. These have carried theorems both ways and continue to do so.

A version of the diagnostic that excludes them is too strong. A version that admits them needs to distinguish the *identification* (which can be exact at finite scale or after a controlled limit) from the *derived theorems* (which may freely use further limits). The piece flags Connes' spectral action as deferred on Condition 2 grounds (the heat-kernel expansion is asymptotic), but defers the question; this is the right move only if the question is taken up elsewhere. The cleaner formulation would distinguish identifications-by-limit (Wick rotation, where the limit is the identification itself) from identifications-with-asymptotic-tools (Sourlas, where the identity is exact and the asymptotic tools are used downstream).
