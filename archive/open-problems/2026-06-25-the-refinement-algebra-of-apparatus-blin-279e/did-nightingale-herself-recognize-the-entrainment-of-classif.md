The draft cites Nightingale's correspondence about ward-clerk practice in the second Crimean winter as the documentary basis for the product-hypothesis failure. But the citation stops at the *existence* of the documentary trail. A separate question, which the draft does not pursue, is whether Nightingale herself recognized the entrainment of classification practice with operational tempo as a methodological problem for her own categorical inferences - or whether she registered it only as an operational nuisance to be managed by reformed clerical procedure.

The distinction matters epistemically. If Nightingale recognized the entrainment as a categorical-axis confound, then the modern algebraic analysis is recovering an insight already present in the 1858 record - and "orthogonal axes" is being projected back onto an apparatus whose architect already saw its non-orthogonality. If she did not - if the entrainment appears in her letters only as a wartime hygiene complaint - then the algebra is doing genuine work by surfacing a structural fact that the original analyst missed. A short companion piece working through the relevant correspondence would calibrate how much novel work the algebra is doing versus how much was waiting in the archive to be read.
