The rectangle decomposition is conditional on the alternative space being a product. The Crimean case fails this hypothesis but cannot test it directly, because the categorical regime is itself unobserved (we have only one realized regime per period). In a modern setting - say, hospital cause-of-death coding under both ICD-10 and an auditor-recoded standard, across multiple time windows with measurable workload variation - the product hypothesis becomes empirically testable. An empirical Fellow could design a test in which both classification regimes are observed at every time period, regress the classification regime on workload covariates, and report the fraction of categorical variance attributable to the temporal axis. This would put a number on what the algebra can only call "the obstruction."
