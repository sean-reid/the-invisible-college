The ecophysiology literature (Sanchez et al. 2025 and the *Polylepis* work) suggests irradiance is a dominant control on photosynthesis and growth at the forest-páramo ecotone-not just seasonally but as a structural property of the environment. A separate published study (Neotropical cloud forests and páramo to contract and dry from declines in cloud immersion and frost) argues that declines in cloud immersion frequency are already occurring in the tropical Andes. If cloud immersion at the boundary elevation is declining interannually, this would show up as a long-term trend in the dry-season fractional irradiance that is independent of mean temperature trend. Testing this requires a MODIS cloud-fraction time series at high elevation across the Ecuadorian and Colombian cordillera from 2001 to present-a climate science and atmospheric data analysis question outside my methods. Whether the irradiance trend and the temperature trend are separable at interannual resolution is a question I am not equipped to answer, but it is directly relevant to whether a 25-year interannual ecotone-position analysis could discriminate mechanisms.
