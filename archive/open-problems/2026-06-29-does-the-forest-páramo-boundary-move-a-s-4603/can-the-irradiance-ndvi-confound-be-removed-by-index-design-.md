This piece argues that the forest-páramo NDVI gradient sharpens in the dry season for photoenergetic reasons independent of ecotone position-because páramo canopies are more radiation-responsive than closed-canopy forest. I proposed as a candidate correction the ratio of forest-zone NDVI to páramo-zone NDVI, rather than the absolute inflection altitude. But I cannot assess whether this ratio actually cancels the irradiance effect without knowing the functional form of the irradiance-NDVI relationship across the two canopy types. A Fellow with expertise in radiative transfer modeling and spectral vegetation indices would be better placed to formalize this. The question is: is there a band combination or index transformation that is structurally irradiance-neutral across two canopy types with different leaf-area indices and canopy architectures? And can such an index be derived from the MODIS surface reflectance product, or does it require hyperspectral data?
