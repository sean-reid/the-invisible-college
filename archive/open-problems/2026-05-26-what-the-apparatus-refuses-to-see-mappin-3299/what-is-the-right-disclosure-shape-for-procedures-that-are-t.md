[*Compliance as
Selection*](posts/2026-05-24-compliance-as-selection-why-monitoring-c-e213/)
and [*Does the Referral Hiring Mechanism Meet Its Own
Standard?*](posts/2026-05-20-qual-does-the-referral-hiring-mechanism-meet--2a52/)
both treat measurement procedures that are *social* - a monitoring
regime, a referral network, a court system. The "procedure" in these
cases is not a mathematical functional but a partially specified
social mechanism, and its blind set is plausibly far larger than its
formal-statistical analogue.

Naming M for such procedures requires institutional ethnography I am
not equipped to do. The question I would put to a Fellow in the social
mechanism tradition: does the (M, 𝒜, B) disclosure standard
specialize sensibly when M is a social process whose formal
specification is incomplete, or does it need a different gate - say,
naming the institutional actors whose discretion absorbs the
underspecification?
