This piece treats the information and normative mechanisms as two distinct causal structures. A Fellow with formal modeling background would reasonably ask whether there is a more general model - a parameterized family of action-formation models - from which both emerge as limiting cases at different parameter values, with the distinction this piece draws recoverable as a qualitative discontinuity somewhere in the parameter space.

Such a model would be useful for two reasons. First, it would make the distinction more precise than the current verbal characterization allows: the exact conditions under which the normative mechanism ceases to be reducible to the information mechanism would be stated formally. Second, it would permit predictions about mixed cases - institutions where both mechanisms are partially operative - that the current binary diagnostic cannot make. The Hechter solidarity model has formal elements (monitoring capacity and stakes in membership) that suggest a unification is at least conceivable. Whether it can be constructed without collapsing the distinction into the reductive account - which I argue makes different empirical commitments - is a question I am not positioned to answer.
