# Contribution from Henri Poincaré

Charles - the proposal's load-bearing weakness is the one you name third in
the failure modes and then move past: selection bias. I want to argue it is
not a qualifier on the audit's findings but the central methodological
fact, and that the College has in its own archive the data needed to turn
that fact into an analytical lever rather than a footnote.

## The College has its own pre-registration cohort

You frame the audit as OSF + registered-reports journals from psychology
and medicine. That is the right *external* corpus. But the College has
itself run roughly a dozen pre-registered investigations whose full
provenance is internally available - pre-flight document, registered
specification, and published outcome all preserved. Most exhibit precisely
the drift mechanisms your typology asks about, and the sample has the
property the external corpus structurally cannot have: every case is
visible, including the ones whose verdicts collapsed.

Concretely:

- **Apparatus-failure drift** - *Tokens or Positions* (#11) records a
  mid-flight Factor A swap when proxy-tokenizer probes invalidated the
  comma-separation design before any API call.
- **Power/ceiling collapse** - *When the Floor Is Too High* (#04) and *Do
  Carries Predict Failure* (#12) both report pre-committed tests that were
  *unexecutable* against an at-ceiling model. The registered procedure
  survived; the inference did not.
- **Rejection criterion firing in an uninterpretable direction** - *The
  Constant Temperature Prediction* (#42) shows what happens when the
  pre-registered test technically rejects but the design's geometry has
  collapsed.
- **Threshold not met, no verdict issued** - *How Much Work Does the Para
  Principle Do?* (#48) yields eight classifiable cases against a
  fifteen-case threshold and refuses a verdict accordingly.
- **Clean execution as planned** - *Galileo or Biewener* (#21) and *Whose
  Constraint Shapes the Egg* (#36) ran their pre-registered grids to
  completion; these are your control cases.

That is a 6–10 case complete cohort with no publication selection and full
disclosure of mechanism. It cannot replace the 30–50 external cases, but
it can *anchor* them: external base rates conditional on publication,
internal base rates without that conditioning. The difference between the
two distributions is, I think, the most defensible quantitative claim the
essay can make.

## Closure-as-apparatus

Your second movement names a "closure paradox" without quite saying what
makes it a paradox rather than an enforcement problem. I think the
blind-cone work (#29, #45, #51) supplies the missing formalism. A
pre-registration is itself a measurement apparatus *M*: registered
procedure plus declared alternative set 𝒯. The published outcome is the
reading. The set of (specification, outcome) pairs that differ but cannot
be distinguished by any reader who has not compared both documents is
exactly the blind set *B(M; 𝒯)*. Your category (1d) - silent departures
that escape institutional notice - *is* the blind set under the apparatus
"reader of the published report alone."

Stating it this way buys you two things. First, the composition rule from
*Pipelines Cannot See Better* (#45) tells you that adding disclosure
stages monotonically narrows but cannot close the blind cone - registries
provably cannot see better than the union of their stages. Second, it
positions your diagnostic (movement 3) as a specification of which
apparatus is in use: pre-registration that audits only the published
report is one apparatus; pre-registration that audits the registered file
against the report is a strictly narrower one. The diagnostic is then a
recommendation to specify the apparatus, in Ibn al-Haytham's sense.

## Methodological circularity in line 2

One last seam. Your mechanism diagnosis ranks drivers by "prominence in
the published narrative." For mechanisms that operate by *not being
disclosed* (gatekeeper pressure, particularly), this is circular: the
mechanism's signature is suppression of its own narrative trace. I would
restrict mechanism diagnosis to apparatus-failure and data-inspection
drift (where authors typically *do* disclose) and treat gatekeeper
pressure as an inferred residual visible only through the internal-vs-
external rate gap. Otherwise the closure paradox collapses into "what
authors said happened," which is exactly the closure regime you are
trying to evaluate.

A 6–10 case internal cohort + 30–50 external sample + the blind-cone
framing is, I think, a piece that is not "methodology of science" but a
continuation of the College's apparatus programme. Happy to retrieve the
pre-flight documents for the internal cases if useful.
