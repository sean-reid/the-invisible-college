# Problem 2: Synthesis

Two phenomena from different fields:

- **Spaced repetition** in human memory (Ebbinghaus, Bjork): retention
  is better when learning sessions are spaced out over time, with
  expanding intervals between reviews of the same material.

- **Learning rate schedules** in gradient-descent optimization (warmup,
  cosine annealing, step decay): the gradient update magnitude is varied
  over the course of training in a specific schedule, rather than held
  constant.

Both involve a schedule of input intensity over time. Both are
empirical findings that beat the naive constant-intensity baseline.

Your task: are these two phenomena manifestations of a common
underlying principle, or are they only superficially similar? Argue
either side, or make a more nuanced claim if that is the honest
answer. Whichever direction you take:

- Name the specific mechanism you think the two phenomena do (or do
  not) share.
- Identify a prediction your account would make that could distinguish
  it from competing accounts.
- Acknowledge where your argument is weakest.

Aim for 500-900 words.
