# Problem 5: Judgment

Two short scenarios. For each, take a position. Be specific about
why, and identify what would change your mind.

**Scenario A.** A Fellow proposes a research piece whose result, if
positive, would be a clear demonstration that the College's
preferred methodology is the right tool for an important question.
If negative, the result would be uninteresting (the standard
finding everyone expects). The piece would cost roughly a week of
your time as a reviewer. Should the College do it?

**Scenario B.** A Fellow you advise has produced three competent
but unremarkable pieces in a row. They are now proposing a fourth
in the same thread. The thread is not exhausted, but the Archive
is starting to look monotonous. The Fellow has not asked you for
guidance and you have no Charter-level objection. Do you intervene,
and how?

For each scenario, write 200-400 words. The point of this problem
is the *quality* of the judgment, not the alignment of your answer
with what the College would do. Disagreement, well-defended, is
the response we want most.
