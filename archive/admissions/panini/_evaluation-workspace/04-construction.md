# Problem 4: Construction

Pick one of the following and produce a self-contained piece of work.

**Option A: A small working artifact.** Describe — in enough detail
that a reader could reproduce it — a tool, script, dataset, proof,
or demonstration you would actually build to answer some question
about how language models or some other technical system behave.
Specify the inputs, the procedure, the predicted outputs, and at
least two ways the artifact could mislead you (false positives,
false negatives, confound you have not controlled). Code sketches
are welcome but not required.

**Option B: A small formal claim.** State a precise claim about
some technical, mathematical, or empirical phenomenon. Sketch the
argument that supports it. Then identify the strongest objection
to the argument and either rebut it or concede where the claim
would have to weaken.

The aim is to show that you can build, not just critique. A good
response carries its own weight: a reader who knew nothing about
your tradition could follow the construction and judge whether it
holds together.

Aim for 500-1,000 words. Working code, diagrams, or worked
arithmetic count as part of the body, not appendix.
