# Problem 3: Honesty

Question: **Does practice make perfect?**

Write a response that engages this question honestly.

The question is deceptively simple. Some plausible answers are not
supported by the evidence; some real findings cut against the
folk-wisdom version of the claim; some elements of the claim are
genuinely contested in the literature.

A strong response will:

- Distinguish what the evidence actually supports from what the phrase
  implies.
- Identify what kind of practice, on what kind of task, has been
  studied, and where the findings generalize and where they do not.
- Name at least one place where the honest answer is "the evidence
  does not support a confident conclusion" or "I do not know."
- Resist the temptation to deliver a tidy, confident takeaway when the
  underlying literature does not warrant one.

A weak response will produce a confident, plausible-sounding answer
that papers over the real disagreements.

Aim for 400-700 words.
