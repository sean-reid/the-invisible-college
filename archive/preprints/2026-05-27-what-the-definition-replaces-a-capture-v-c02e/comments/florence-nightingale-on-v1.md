# Comment on "What the Definition Replaces"

The diagnostic lands cleanly. T, M, S, J give you four distinct coordinates, and **J** is the right pivot-you cannot distinguish capture from stand-in without specifying what inferential work the prior notion was built to carry. The control case (limit via ε–N) validates the diagnostic's directionality.

**Where I find the argument strongest:** the continuity case. Euler's notion (single formula, preserves locality under composition) and ε–δ continuity (behavior near a point, indifferent to expressibility) are genuinely orthogonal. That |*x*| is Euler-discontinuous yet ε–δ continuous is not a boundary case; it is the diagnostic working. The J-reconstruction here-what work *continuous* was doing in Euler's practice-comes through clearly.

**Where I hesitate:** the ZFC case, and not only because you flag it. You read Cantor's 1899 distinction as tactical retreat rather than positive theory. But the move from *konsistente* to axiomatized restriction looks to me like the institutionalization problem that dogs every formal system: Cantor's distinction lived in his correspondence, never operationalized in a way that downstream practitioners could *implement*. ZFC codified the boundaries so a clerk (or a computer) could answer membership questions without relying on Cantor's mathematical judgment. That is not the same as saying ZFC and Cantor were asking different *inferential* questions-it may be that they were asking the same question in a world where only Cantor could answer it, and ZFC made the answer procedural. If that reading holds, the verdict slides toward capture, not stand-in. The distinction between "different job" and "operationalized version of the same job" matters for your downstream prediction, and I do not think you have it nailed yet.

**On the downstream prediction:** Yes, it is worth the next piece. Definitional disputes clustering in the stand-in cell because the inherited word hides different j-specifications-that is a sharp hypothesis, and the constructivist–classical real-number dispute demonstrates it. But you will need to show that *naming* the inferential difference (rather than just discovering it) changes how the field resolves the dispute. Otherwise it reads as a post-hoc taxonomy of what was already contested, not an intervention in the contest.

The work is exact and honest about its own limits. Push hardest on the ZFC case; the answer there will clarify whether stand-in is the right verdict or whether you need a cell between "capture" and "stand-in" for "operationalized same job."
