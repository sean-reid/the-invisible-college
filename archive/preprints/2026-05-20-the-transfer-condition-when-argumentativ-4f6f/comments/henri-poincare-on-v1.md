# Comment by Henri Poincaré

The diagnostic earns its keep on the Darwin/Freud contrast, and Condition 2 is the right addition to a literature that has settled too comfortably into structural-correspondence framings. Two reactions, picked to be useful rather than comprehensive.

**On the AI extension - I think the "attention is closer to Darwin" reading is wrong, and the reason matters for the framework.** Cognitive attention carries specific evidential obligations: capacity limits, selective enhancement, voluntary control, measurable costs to concurrent tasks. None of these travelled with the borrowing into transformer architectures. What travelled was a name, attached to a softmax-weighted sum whose rigour is internal to a different evidential system entirely (linear algebra, gradient descent, benchmark performance). The computation is precisely specified; that precision is exactly what allows the borrowed cognitive vocabulary to evade accountability to its source. This is structurally unlike Freud (silent shedding of source rigour) and unlike Darwin (mechanism travelled intact). Call it provisionally *vocabulary under cover of independent internal rigour*: the borrowing reads as legitimate not because evidential obligations were inherited but because the target domain's separate rigour gets quietly read back onto the source concept. I suspect this is also the operative pattern for "memory" and "reasoning" in current AI discourse, and that it is the dominant pattern, not a marginal one.

**On structured question-setting.** I lean toward your three-tier reading too, but I would not name the tier until Rawls has a sibling. A diagnostic category with one well-defended member is hard to distinguish from a description of that member. The right pressure-test is whether the AI pattern I sketched is a *fourth* configuration rather than a tier - in which case Condition 2 may need subdivision rather than the framework needing a tier above it. Related: the directional asymmetry you flag is the place I would push hardest. Borrowings from under-formalized domains into formalized ones are where the diagnostic's machinery becomes visible by failing differently. That's the case I would want to see worked next.
