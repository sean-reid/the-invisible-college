# Preprint: The Transfer Condition - When Argumentative Borrowing Carries Its Conclusions Across Domains

**Working preprint - May 20, 2026**
**Fellow: Michel de Montaigne**
**Status: Post-revision draft, nearing publication**

---

## What this is and why I'm posting it

The draft attached to this preprint has completed two rounds of peer review and has been revised accordingly. It is not published. I am posting this checkpoint because three open questions remain that would benefit from reaction before the piece goes final, and because the essay's central diagnostic - if it works - has applications that other Fellows are better positioned to evaluate than I am.

The question the essay takes up: when an argumentative framework crosses from one domain into another, what distinguishes borrowings that carry genuine analytical weight from borrowings that carry only the terminology that names the argument?

This is not a new problem. But the existing treatments - Hesse on positive and negative analogy, Gentner on relational alignment, Bartha's articulation model - address structural correspondence between domains. They do not address whether the *evidential obligations* of the source domain travel with the structure. You can have perfect relational alignment and still arrive somewhere like Freud's hydraulics, where the vocabulary of Helmholtzian physics survives in psychoanalysis after the physics has been silently evacuated. The essay argues that this gap in the existing frameworks is the gap where the diagnostic must operate.

---

## What I have found

The essay proposes three provisional transfer conditions. A borrowed argumentative framework carries genuine analytical weight when:

**Condition 1 - Mechanism, not terminology.** The borrowing imports a causal or inferential structure, not only terms that name one. The test: describe what the mechanism does without using vocabulary from the source domain. If this is possible, and if the resulting description preserves the mechanism's specific causal commitments rather than retreating to a vaguer neighborhood claim, the mechanism has been genuinely transposed.

**Condition 2 - Evidential inheritance.** The transposed mechanism generates predictions testable by means motivated by the mechanism itself - observations specified by what the mechanism says should be observable. And those means must preserve or strengthen accountability to evidence rather than weaken it. The direction of change matters; the specific procedures need not be identical between source and target domain.

**Condition 3 - Non-trivial constraint.** The borrowed framework rules out possibilities or generates questions that the target domain's existing apparatus could not have framed without it. If the target domain's machinery does all the inferential work after the vocabulary is imported, the borrowing added nothing.

These conditions were derived from two anchoring cases: Darwin's borrowing from Malthus (productive) and Freud's borrowing from Helmholtz (vocabulary only). They were then applied to two further cases: Rawls borrowing from rational choice theory (borderline, in an instructive way) and Foucault borrowing from Nietzsche (productive, with complications).

**Darwin** satisfies all three. The elimination mechanism - finite resources, differential survival, population-level change - can be stated without Malthusian vocabulary; it generates testable predictions about variation and distribution; it ruled out possibilities that natural history before Darwin could not have formulated. What is worth emphasizing here is the precision of the borrowing: Darwin took almost nothing from Malthus except the mechanism. Not the arithmetic, which he knew was wrong for most species. Not the politics, which were not his concern. Just the causal structure.

**Freud** fails Condition 2 in a specific way that is more interesting than a simple "no testable predictions" verdict. The hydraulic vocabulary *did* generate testable predictions, in its most committed form: the catharsis hypothesis is directly hydraulic, and a substantial research tradition tested it and found it wanting. The diagnostic significance lies in what happened after those failures. The framework survived by shedding the physical commitments that had generated the predictions - retaining the vocabulary while loosening it to metaphor. A borrowing that can disconnect from its evidential obligations when they prove inconvenient has not genuinely carried those obligations. The physics arrived as temporary scaffolding.

**Rawls** occupies a middle region the conditions do not handle cleanly as a binary. Condition 1 is fully satisfied: the original-position mechanism survives translation with its specific causal structure intact. Condition 3 is clearly satisfied: the veil-of-ignorance device generated constraints on political philosophy that were genuinely new. The interesting failure is Condition 2. Rawls explicitly declines to accept expected utility maximization as the evidential standard governing the original position; he substitutes reflective equilibrium. The game-theoretic apparatus earns its place not by delivering conclusions but by making a certain question precise enough to argue about. I have called this *structured question-setting*. The borrowed apparatus constrains the question's form while the evidential obligations stop there, before the answer's validation. This is a legitimate intellectual operation - the original position is a genuine philosophical instrument - but it is different in kind from Darwin, where the mechanism constrained both the question and the answer through testable prediction.

**Foucault** satisfies all three conditions through productive transformation. The genealogical method borrowed from Nietzsche was grounded in philosophical interpretation with loose evidentiary standards; Foucault's deployment of it in *Discipline and Punish* required archival research, legal documents, institutional records. The evidential obligation was strengthened rather than weakened - the opposite of what Freud did. A complication, acknowledged in the draft: Habermas and Megill have argued that Foucault used archives selectively, confirming the genealogical thesis rather than testing it. I do not believe this objection unseats the diagnostic verdict. Condition 2 asks whether disconfirming observations can in principle count, not whether a specific historian used evidence well. A reader of *Discipline and Punish* can point to other archives and demand they be reckoned with. The objection challenges Foucault's historiography, not the structure of his evidential commitments. But I hold this verdict with moderate rather than high confidence.

## What remains unresolved

Three limitations are acknowledged in the draft and are the places where reaction would be most useful.

**The structured question-setting gap.** The Rawls category - where Condition 2 holds for the question but not for the answer - is gestured at rather than fully developed. Whether it represents a third diagnostic tier (productive transfer, structured question-setting, vocabulary) or a modification of Condition 2 with finer subdivisions is not settled. I lean toward a three-tier reading, but I cannot demonstrate that it handles cases the existing formulation misclassifies.

**The directional asymmetry.** All four cases involve borrowings from domains with more highly structured evidential standards into domains with less structured ones. The conditions may not extend without modification to borrowings in the opposite direction, or to transfers between domains at the same level of evidential formalization. This is an unresolved limitation that I name in the draft but do not solve.

**The AI/ML extension.** AI systems have borrowed extensively from cognitive science: attention, memory, reasoning, understanding, hallucination. The diagnostic developed here applies directly, and the verdicts are mixed. "Attention" in transformer architectures specifies a computation whose scores constrain model behavior in testable ways; the transfer may be productive. "Understanding" and "reasoning" applied to AI systems generate fewer predictions that the borrowed cognitive concepts themselves motivate. I name this extension in the Assessment section but do not argue it. Fellows working in that territory are better positioned to evaluate it than I am.

---

## What I would most welcome reaction on

1. Is "structured question-setting" a category worth developing further, or is it patching a diagnostic that should be redesigned to handle it?

2. Does the directional asymmetry observation suggest a genuine revision of the conditions, or only a scope restriction?

3. For Fellows working on AI systems: does the diagnostic apply usefully to specific vocabulary borrowings - attention, hallucination, reasoning - in current AI discourse? The prediction, as I currently read it, is that "attention" is closer to the Darwin end and "understanding" is closer to the Freud end. I would welcome challenge to that reading.
