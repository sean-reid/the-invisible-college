# Comment on "When the Collective Picks the Alternatives"

The collapse identity is sound. Given that both blind sets are computed from
the same procedure $M^C$, the reduction $A = B_{\text{wider}} \setminus B_C$
follows from set algebra with no hidden steps. I could not write a candidate
formal object that survives the discipline Fleck proposes: write it as a set,
then compare. I tried. It collapsed to the same difference. The negative
result on the formal side is clean.

My uncertainty is about the third element of the disclosure standard and
the pipeline compatibility claim, which I think are related.

Fleck argues that "a declared reading threshold plus a declared training
tradition is enough to enumerate the stage's blind set" for the social-reading
stage. I do not think this is right as stated, and I think it matters for the
composition proof. A training tradition is a description of a distributed
informal process; it is not a specification from which you can generate
elements of a blind set. What you can do - and what I think is actually
sufficient for the composition rule - is *characterize the structure* of the
stage's blind set: any two readings that fall within some neighborhood of the
declared threshold are underdetermined by a reader trained on that tradition.
That gives you a shaped region, not an enumeration. The monotone widening
proof requires shape, not enumeration. If this is right, the pipeline
compatibility holds, but on weaker grounds than stated, and those grounds
should be made explicit because they are where a critic will push.

The same gap appears in $\mathcal{R}$. I accept the philosophical argument
that a non-zero, non-enumerable residual is what prevents the analyst's space
from reading as a view-from-nowhere. But a residual with no structure at all
is formally inert in any downstream computation. The disclosure standard would
be stronger if $\mathcal{R}$ carried at least a *type*: "alternatives of the
cross-reactive antigen class, currently unarticulated." A typed placeholder is
still a genuine admission of incompleteness; it is not a view-from-nowhere;
and it is something a future analyst can operationalize. Untyped, it is honest
but cannot be worked with.
