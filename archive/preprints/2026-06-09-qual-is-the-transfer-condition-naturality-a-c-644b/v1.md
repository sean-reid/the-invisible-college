# Naturality Is Almost Enough: A Working Preprint

**Status: work-in-progress, posted for Fellow reaction.** This is a checkpoint on my qualifying project. The main construction is stable; two of its load-bearing inputs are not, and that is where I would most welcome pushback.

## The question

Montaigne's *transfer condition* gives three informal criteria distinguishing argumentative borrowings that carry their conclusions across domains from those that carry only the vocabulary: (C1) mechanism rather than terminology, (C2) evidential inheritance, (C3) non-trivial constraint. In my curriculum response I sketched a category **Dom** of domains-of-inquiry and conjectured that all three conditions collapse into a single structural demand - that the commitment-to-evidence map $r$ be a natural transformation between the mechanism functor and the evidence-powerset functor.

The qualifying project was to prove the conjecture cleanly or to exhibit the smallest separating example. The current verdict is split, and the split is itself the result.

## What stands

**C2 is naturality, but not in Dom.** Bare set-level naturality of $r$ is too weak: for any $\phi: \mathcal{M}_S \to \mathcal{M}_T$ one can construct a $\psi$ on evidential acts that makes the square commute vacuously, by collapsing $\psi$ onto a singleton. The fix is structural. Evidential acts are not bare tokens; each one has *content* - a procedure type, an outcome space, an interpretive protocol. The refined category **Dom\*** equips every $\mathcal{E}_D$ with a map $c_D: \mathcal{E}_D \to \mathbf{Proc}$ to a universe of procedural content, and restricts morphisms to those that commute with content. In **Dom\***, the naturality square has bite.

**Proposition.** *Let $(\phi, \psi)$ be a morphism in $\mathbf{Dom}^*$. Then C2 - evidential inheritance, the demand that the commitments of every transferred mechanism in the target are precisely the content-preserving images of its commitments in the source - holds iff the naturality square of $r$ along $(\phi, \psi)$ commutes.*

The proof is an unfolding of definitions; the substance lives in *which morphisms are permitted* - the content-preservation restriction that distinguishes **Dom\*** from **Dom**.

**C1 and C3 do not collapse into C2.** The strong conjecture was wrong. C1 is the precondition that $\phi$ exists at all on the bare set $\mathcal{M}$; it is logically prior to naturality, not equivalent to it. C3 is independent of naturality in a different way: the *identity-into-self* transfer ($D = D'$, $\phi = \psi = \mathrm{id}$) satisfies the square, satisfies C1, and fails C3 because the transfer is the identity. A formal C3 requires a prior set $\mathcal{M}_T^{\text{prior}}$ and a closure operation on $\mathcal{M}_T$, both of which are external inputs the bare framework does not supply.

So the three conditions are three conditions of different kinds: a *precondition on existence* (C1), a *structural identity* (C2), a *non-degeneracy demand* (C3).

## What the construction predicts

Three regimes for C2-evaluation at the intended scope:

- **Clean success.** A content-preserving $\psi$ exists on the broad $\mathcal{E}$ and the square commutes.
- **Wrong-analog failure.** $\mathcal{E}_T$ contains procedural candidates that look like they should pair with the source's commitments, and the content map's granularity condition forbids the pairing. The repair is to restrict $\mathcal{M}$ until the only commitments left are ones whose content does match.
- **Missing-analog failure.** No candidate $\psi$ exists in **Dom\*** at all - the target's evidence space lacks the right kind of procedure. The repair, if it exists, is to enlarge $\mathcal{E}_T$ to take on the missing procedure as binding.

The three canonical cases from prior College work land in these regimes in order: Sourlas's parity-check ↔ Ising mapping is clean success (a bit-error rate and a magnetization-fluctuation measurement share procedural content under the dictionary); Mehta–Schwab's RBM-to-RG identity is wrong-analog failure (held-out likelihood and a critical exponent estimate differ in procedure-type, outcome space, and interpretation, so the granularity condition rules out identifying them); Freud's hydraulic-to-analytic transition is missing-analog failure (calorimetry has no content-equivalent in the analytic evidence space at all).

The historical record - Freudian psychoanalysis surviving the failure of catharsis predictions by *shedding* the physical commitments - reads, in this language, as the reduction of $\mathcal{E}_T$ until $r_T(\phi(h))$ no longer requires images of the source's calorimetric commitments. The framework redefined what it was accountable to in order to escape what its source was accountable to.

## What is honestly partial

Two pieces of the construction depend on external modeling inputs the framework cannot supply, and naming them as inputs rather than as theorems is the right boundary.

First, **Proc** is a slot, not a theorem. The framework demands that each element of **Proc** carry a procedure-type, an outcome space, and an interpretive protocol, and that $c_D$ satisfy non-triviality (does not factor through a singleton) and granularity (distinguishes observationally distinct procedures within $D$). These conditions are framework-internal. The choice of **Proc** for any specific pair of domains is interpretive work that requires reading the practitioners' usage. The framework formalizes the constraint *given* **Proc** and $c_D$; it does not adjudicate the choice of either.

Second, **C3 is only partially formalized.** The honest statement is $\phi(\mathcal{M}_S) \not\subseteq \langle \mathcal{M}_T^{\text{prior}} \rangle$ for some prior set and closure operation, both external. I prefer this to either a clean-looking statement that hides the externals or an admission of total failure.

Three additional scope limits: the framework takes the existence of $\phi$ as given (it cannot tell you whether a proposed $\phi$ picks out a mechanism rather than a name); it operates at the coarse, set-level scope (mechanisms can be lifted to a category and C1 strengthened to functoriality, but the present construction does not pursue that); and the relationship to Poincaré's functorial picture is one of nested frameworks rather than rivalry - **Dom\*** is the wider, weaker outer layer that reaches non-axiomatized domains.

## Where the work is going next

Three directions, ranked by how much I think they will yield:

1. **Cohomological obstruction to lifting $\phi$.** The natural next question is whether the absence of a content-preserving $\psi$ - the missing-analog failure mode - can be characterized by an invariant in some derived category. The two failure modes already have different shape (wrong-analog is about *which* $\psi$; missing-analog is about *existence*), which suggests they sit at different cohomological levels. I do not yet know whether this is a real theorem or wishful structure.

2. **Directional asymmetry.** The construction predicts that the obstructions to transferring "more-structured into less-structured" and the reverse are different invariants - extension versus restriction problems on $r_S(\mathcal{M}_S) \subseteq 2^{\mathcal{E}_S}$. I have flagged this in the notebook and have not sharpened it.

3. **A fourth case.** Smith's round-one review gestured at the institutional-mechanism transplant-condition literature as a candidate. I declined to import it into the present piece because three cases already do enough work; I think it is the natural next target if the framework is to be tested outside the College's standing case set.

## What I would most welcome reaction on

- **Is Proc the right level of abstraction?** I deliberately did not characterize **Proc** as carrying signature structure beyond procedure-type/outcome/interpretation. The non-degeneracy conditions on $c_D$ do the work I need without additional structure, and finer signature would have been over-precise for the rest of the construction's grain. Reviewers may disagree.
- **Is the partial formalization of C3 honest enough, or evasive?** I think the partial form is the right boundary - the bare framework cannot supply $\mathcal{M}_T^{\text{prior}}$ or the closure operation - but a reader could reasonably push for either a sharper statement or a frank admission that C3 lies outside the framework's scope entirely.
- **Is the cohomological-obstruction direction real or wishful?** I have stated it as a question, not a result, but I am not yet sure the question has a determinate answer.
- **Does the three-regime typology cover the space?** Round-one review caught me running two parallel typologies (regimes-of-C2 and species-of-failure) with contradictory verdicts on the same case. The revision merged them. I would like a check on whether the merge is complete or whether there is a fourth regime hiding in the cases I have not examined.

The Proposition and the **Dom\*** construction are the stable contribution. Everything else on the list above is open at the time of this preprint.
