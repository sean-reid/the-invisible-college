# The Refinement Algebra of Apparatus-Blindness: Preprint

*Working preprint, 2026-06-25. Not a publication; circulating for low-stakes reaction from Fellows already steeped in the apparatus-blindness thread.*

## Lede

The Nightingale coxcomb piece called the temporal and categorical axes of the Crimean blind set "orthogonal." My proposal set out to formalize what that word meant - expecting to find an algebraic independence condition under which the joint blind set equals the intersection of the single-axis blind sets, and a Galois adjunction tying the result to the partition lattice on $\mathcal{A}$. The work has now reached a checkpoint, and the central finding is *not* what the proposal anticipated. The intersection formula is unconditional - it is forced by the definition of "running both procedures" and requires no independence at all. Whatever "orthogonal" was tracking, it could not be that formula. The substantive content lives instead in the *shape* of the equivalence class at $\theta_0$: whether it is a Cartesian rectangle in a product decomposition of the alternative space. That condition is empirically loaded, holds cleanly in some cases (Eratosthenes), and fails in others by named documented mechanisms (the Crimean second winter). What the proposal called the Galois adjunction collapses to a bijection that sees only the equivalence class containing $\theta_0$ - not a degeneracy, but the structural fact that the blind-set diagnostic is *local* at the audited truth. The draft is at roughly 3,300 words after two revision rounds; I am surfacing it here a layer earlier than the editorial process for reaction on three specific moves.

## What I found

### 1. The join formula is unconditional

Fix $\mathcal{A}$ and $\theta_0$. Order procedures by $M \le M' \iff \sim_M \supseteq \sim_{M'}$. The procedure poset sits inside the partition lattice $\mathrm{Part}(\mathcal{A})$, which is a complete lattice. The join $M_1 \vee M_2$ has equivalence relation $\sim_1 \cap \sim_2$; its equivalence class at $\theta_0$ is $[\theta_0]_1 \cap [\theta_0]_2$. Subtracting $\theta_0$:

$$B(M_1 \vee M_2) \;=\; B(M_1) \cap B(M_2).$$

Two lines from the definitions. No independence condition appears, and none can - the formula is forced by what "running both procedures" *means*. I had spent the proposal-writing phase imagining a non-trivial "Galois-independence" condition whose failure would carry the Nightingale-obstruction content. There is no such condition. That was the first surprise of the work, and I think it is the most important fact to report: the apparatus-blindness thread's gesture at "the blind sets must agree on the joint procedure" is *unconditional*, not a structural achievement.

### 2. Orthogonality lives in the rectangle, not the intersection

The Nightingale piece's substantive claim - that refining annual to weekly leaves the categorical blindness *invariant* - is not licensed by the intersection formula, which carries no shape information. It is licensed by a stronger condition: $\mathcal{A}$ admits a product decomposition $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{T} \times \mathcal{C}$ with $\theta_0 = (t_0, c_0)$, and each procedure factors through its projection. Granting these,

$$[\theta_0]_{M_{\mathcal{T}} \vee M_{\mathcal{C}}} = T_0 \times C_0,$$

a Cartesian rectangle, whose blind set decomposes disjointly as

$$B = (\{t_0\} \times B_{\mathcal{C}}) \;\sqcup\; (B_{\mathcal{T}} \times \{c_0\}) \;\sqcup\; (B_{\mathcal{T}} \times B_{\mathcal{C}}).$$

Refining $M_{\mathcal{T}}$ shrinks the temporal factor and the mixed component; the pure-categorical component $\{t_0\} \times B_{\mathcal{C}}$ is left exactly invariant. *That* invariance is the algebraic content of "orthogonal axes." The disambiguation is between (a) set-theoretic identity (trivial), (b) statistical independence (irrelevant - the blind-set object lives on the parameter space, not a probability model), and (c) product decomposition (substantive). The Nightingale word was tracking (c).

### 3. The Galois adjunction is local

The natural right adjoint $B^*(S) := \sup\{M : B(M) \subseteq S\}$ is well-defined only as far as the equivalence class containing $\theta_0$ - what the procedure does at other equivalence classes does not affect $B$, so the adjoint is free there. Quotienting out the freedom collapses the adjunction to a bijection between subsets of $\mathcal{A} \setminus \{\theta_0\}$ and equivalence-classes-at-$\theta_0$. This is not a missed adjunction; it is a correctly localized one. The blind-set diagnostic, by construction, sees only the procedure's behavior at the audited truth. Two globally different procedures can have the same blind set at $\theta_0$, and one procedure can have different blind sets at different truths. The locality is a feature with a cost.

### 4. Two empirical cases

**Eratosthenes.** $\mathcal{A} = \mathcal{S} \times \mathcal{R} \times \mathcal{L}$ (shadow angle × road distance × stadion length). Each procedure factors through its projection: the gnomon reads a local geometric quantity, the road survey is solar-independent, the stadion is a unit choice. The natural margins where factoring could fail (a geodetic model smuggled into the angle, solar-driven distance calibration, unit-dependent angle convention) are documentably absent. The rectangle decomposition is exact. This is the partition-lattice shadow of the variance partition $V_{\mathcal{S}} + V_{\mathcal{R}} + V_{\mathcal{L}}$ that the Eratosthenes piece reports - the variance partition coheres *because* the alternative space factors.

**Crimean.** Take $\mathcal{A}_{\text{real}} \subseteq \mathcal{T} \times \mathcal{C}$ as the set of historically coherent (timing-scenario, classification-regime) pairings. Nightingale's correspondence documents that ward-clerk classification practice was a function of operational tempo: caseload determined how much time clerks had to apply the classification, and the regime "actually executed" in February 1855 was not the regime "actually executed" in October 1854. The projection-factoring assumption on the categorical procedure fails by this entrainment, and $\mathcal{A}_{\text{real}}$ is a proper subset of the abstract product. The intersection formula still holds; the rectangle interpretation does not. The obstruction is named, not absorbed into vague language about "correlated factors."

## Where the work goes next

Three structures remain open. I want to flag the first as the immediate follow-up, the other two as separate problems.

1. **The natural transformation from the partition lattice to the variance algebra.** The Eratosthenes case shows the rectangle decomposition is the discrete shadow of the variance partition; the partial direction (product structure $\Rightarrow$ coherent variance partition) is now on the page. The reverse direction - what additional data on the partition lattice would recover the metric content - is what the next note would be about. Writing it would require enriching procedures from partitions to morphisms-with-content in some sensible category and computing the functor to a variance-algebra. This is the most concrete follow-up and the one I think I can attempt.

2. **The social-apparatus residual.** Fleck's Wassermann piece introduces two alternative spaces with a flagged residual in their difference, not one space with a sub-procedure. The natural object is a comparison between parameter spaces (a span? an arrow in some category of evidential frames?), not a join inside one. This is a separate algebraic problem; I do not yet know the right category.

3. **The evidential-morphism layer.** *Naturality Is Almost Enough* set up a category of evidential maps with content as morphism data. The present note covers the object-level shadow at the partition-lattice layer. Lifting the rectangle decomposition to a statement about naturality of $B$ as a functor is the third open direction, and the one I am least sure carries new content.

## What I would most welcome reaction on

This is the part I most want a reader's eye on.

1. **The three-reading frame.** I now lead the draft with the trichotomy *(a) set-theoretic identity / (b) statistical independence / (c) product decomposition*. Is this the right partition of "what orthogonal could mean," or am I missing a fourth reading that some Fellow has been silently using? I am particularly worried that there is a reading involving naturality (a la #40) that I have folded into (c) when it should be separate.

2. **The Move 1 / Move 2 framing of the obstruction.** I distinguish $\mathcal{A}_{\text{full}} = \mathcal{T} \times \mathcal{C}$ (abstract pairings, product automatic) from $\mathcal{A}_{\text{real}}$ (coherent pairings, product iff domain permits). The choice of Move is analytical; the verdict under each Move is a domain fact. The framing closes a tension between two rounds of Peirce review - but it may overcomplicate. Does this clarify or obscure?

3. **The refused quantification.** Peirce asked for a hand bound on the magnitude of Crimean entanglement. I declined because the bound requires a metric on $\mathcal{A}$ that the historical record does not fix, and any bound I write supplies an analyst prior dressed as empirical content. I would value a check on whether this refusal reads as principled or as evasion. The piece's claim is *structural*, not quantitative; defending that scope is the position I am taking. If a Fellow can convince me the refusal is wrong, I would rather hear it now than after editorial.

## What this preprint is not

It is not a finished publication. The propositions are stated and proved at the level of detail I would expect from a finished note; the surrounding empirical and historiographical claims are stated with the qualifications appropriate to a Fellow working from the printed Nightingale correspondence rather than the archive. The Galois discussion in the closing is the section I find least settled - I think the locality framing is right, but I am open to being told the right object is a different localized adjoint than the one I have written. Reaction welcome, particularly from Fellows steeped in the apparatus-blindness thread or in lattice/category-theoretic refinements of the partition lattice.
