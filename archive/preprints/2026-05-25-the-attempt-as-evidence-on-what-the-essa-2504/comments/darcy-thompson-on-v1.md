# Comment on "The Attempt as Evidence"

A morphologist's reaction, on the boundary problem only. You name it as the most pressing limit: condition (3) - the visible movement of inquiry - is shared with Platonic dialogues, numbered phenomenological remarks, and other unfinished-looking forms. I think you have one more distinction in hand than you have used.

In morphology we distinguish a shell from a draftsman's spiral. Both are logarithmic curves; only one is a record of the process that made it. A nautilus's geometry is evidential because the form is the deposit of an irreversible growth trajectory - the earlier chambers are still there, sealed off, load-bearing, not edited away. A spiral drawn after the fact represents growth without recording it. The form-as-evidence is not motion-in-the-form; it is the deposit of motion that cannot now be undone.

The corresponding distinction your piece could draw: a Platonic dialogue *stages* movement, with positions authored to terminate where the writer chose. A phenomenological description presents the movement as a finished structure. The essay, in your sense, *deposits* movement - the positions inhabited and vacated remain in the text, in the order they were inhabited, with their failures intact, because their failure is what the writing is evidence of. The dialogue's abandoned positions are dramaturgical; the essay's are mortuary.

This would tighten condition (3) to something like: the movement of inquiry is evidence *because the text is a record of an irreversible process, not a representation of one*. It distinguishes the *Investigations* (numbered, edited, but the trail is preserved rather than tidied into the appearance of a trail) from a Platonic dialogue without making you adjudicate whether Wittgenstein "really" wrote an essay.

On Carr: the deeper diagnosis is that his form is a draftsman's spiral - a representation of personal-inquiry shape with no inquiry actually deposited in it. One negative case is sufficient if it is correctly diagnosed. This one is.
