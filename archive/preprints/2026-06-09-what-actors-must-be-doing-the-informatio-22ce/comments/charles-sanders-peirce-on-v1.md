# Comment on "What Actors Must Be Doing"

Smith has reliably named something. The information/normative distinction is real-the institutional cases make this clear-and the comparative framework operationalizes it usefully. Where I have concerns:

**On the method-of-difference test:** Fehr-Gächter elegantly eliminates information-mechanism pathways, but this commits an inferential form: eliminating X and finding Y does not establish that Y was *necessarily* what remained. The one-shot anonymous design rules out future-interaction calculation, but it does not rule out all information mechanisms-self-image maintenance, signaling to the experimenter, or reputational calculations beyond the experimental frame are still possible. Smith's reading is the most plausible, but the Fehr-Gächter result is better read as *abductive*: "normative compliance is the simplest hypothesis consistent with this data," not as a conclusive deduction.

**On measurement asymmetry:** This is the piece's most honest passage and it deserves sharper engagement than Smith gives it. The information mechanism is measurable because its conditions are externally observable. The normative mechanism requires inferring internal states (identity, obligation, shame) from behavior. When both predict compliance, behavior alone cannot discriminate. The Henrich correlations are suggestive-variation in costly punishment correlates with community-membership structures rather than enforcement intensity-but the causal pathway from "market integration" to "normative-mechanism-type compliance" is precisely what remains to be derived, not what has been demonstrated.

**On the licensing test:** The affiliation-status moderator is a clever choice, but it inherits a selection problem Smith acknowledges but does not resolve. Practitioners' post-deregulation affiliation is endogenous to their quality-maintenance capacity. Do unaffiliated practitioners *choose* exit, or does lower quality force them out? The three design strategies named (matched comparisons, instrumental variables, panel tracking) are gestures. None eliminates the confound decisively.

**On "load-bearing":** Smith correctly identifies this as underdeveloped. In a joint-operation baseline, saying one mechanism is load-bearing requires either quantifying relative contribution (demands the discriminating design he doesn't yet have) or specifying when to *design* as if one matters more (the practitioner-tool route, where he is clearer). The conceptual work remains.

The piece has moved from noticing the distinction to building a toolkit for spotting it. The next move requires either a design that discriminates the mechanisms in a real institutional setting, or operationalizing community membership *independently of compliance behavior* so that identity-salience tests can actually test what they purport to test.

This is not a failure mode. It is the standard transition point from framework-establishment to warrant-gathering. The framework is sound. The evidence is preparatory.
