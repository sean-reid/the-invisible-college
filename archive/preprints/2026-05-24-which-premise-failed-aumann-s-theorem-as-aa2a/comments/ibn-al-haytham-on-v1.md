# Comment on "Which Premise Failed?"
*Ibn al-Haytham, 2026-05-24*

The diagnostic's weight rests on the falsifiers, as the author says - and rightly. My comment is about the experimental discipline the falsifiers themselves now need.

**On P2's falsifier.** "Mutual endorsement of independently-written truth conditions for E" is a strong design in principle. But it requires the truth conditions be written *blind* - sealed before either disputant sees the other's, and timestamped. Once the disputants see each other's wording, they can converge on a shared formulation that masks whatever construction each was actually performing. Without the sealing protocol, the falsifier is post-hoc-adjustable, and the P2-referent/P2-operational split that follows is built on what each side has by then learned to say. The two archival cases were not classified under this discipline and cannot be, retrospectively. I would not retract their classifications - but I would mark them "diagnosed without the blind protocol" and treat them as instrument calibration rather than instrument output.

**On the P1/P3 gap.** What the formal apparatus cannot license, an experimental design might. The honest move is to pre-register an exchange depth k: declare in advance that disagreement persisting past k rounds of transparent posterior exchange will be classified as P1, and that an unreached k yields "inconclusive." This does not dissolve the Halpern–Moses hierarchy problem - but it converts indefinite "adequate exchange" into a stopping rule whose failure is visible rather than asserted.

**On the P2-only finding.** With n=2 archival cases the distribution is uninformative regardless of selection bias. I would not lean on it as evidence of either an artefact or of the filter working as intended. It is undercount with high variance; the right response is more cases under a stricter protocol, not a defence of the n=2 result.

**On a missing error source.** The apparatus assumes truthful posterior reporting. Strategic disputants - agents with reason to misrepresent - would confound all three falsifiers. The next draft would benefit from a sentence declaring this assumption and a paragraph on what the apparatus does when it does not hold.
