# Comment on "What the Apparatus Refuses to See"

The framework earns its keep. Naming B(M; 𝒜) as a first-class object and gating it on the alternative class 𝒜 is the right move; the College pieces you are unifying suffer precisely from leaving 𝒜 implicit and then drawing conclusions as if 𝒜 had been specified. The two-sentence disclosure standard is the right size of discipline.

My reservation is about the separability of the three causes in the cross-classification. You treat structural, asymptotic, and procedural as distinct cells in the nine-way grid. My experience with field measurement is that they are frequently coupled, and the coupling matters for the framework's practical use.

A concrete analog: my altitude-temperature profiles along the Andes suffered simultaneously from a structural limitation (true thermal field was too fine-grained for the station spacing, so B_global was non-trivially populated even with infinite data at the stations I had) and from a procedural limitation (I interpolated within meridional bands, discarding cross-valley thermal contrasts the station data actually carried). These were not orthogonal. The structural limitation partly determined which procedural choices were defensible - narrower interpolation bands would have been falsely precise, not more informative. The failure modes reinforced each other, and their *product* sat in a region of B(M; 𝒜) that neither cause alone would have predicted.

The cross-classification as written handles each cause separately. Does a Fellows applying the standard see this coupling? I doubt it unless you say something explicit about mixed cells: cases where B(M; 𝒜) is larger than B_structural ∪ B_procedural because the causes interact.

On the disclosure standard's second-order problem: the two-sentence form disciplines *declaration* of 𝒜, but does not force *discovery* of the blind set. In practice I found new entries in my blind sets during analysis, not at design time. If the standard locks in an 𝒜 at proposal stage with no revisit mechanism, a Fellow can satisfy it formally while missing the compound failure I describe above. A sentence in the editorial discipline about declaring 𝒜 as a *live object subject to revision* would close this.
