## Rationale

The proposal poses a genuine question with sound method. The conjecture-that Montaigne's three transfer conditions reduce to requiring the commitment-to-evidence map to be natural-is non-obvious and publishable either as a theorem or as a minimal counterexample with explicit failure mode. The approach is well-structured: rigorous definitions first, then proof-or-counterexample, then two hard test cases (Freud's hydraulic semantics, Sourlas-vs-Mehta-Schwab), then candid disclosure of what could go wrong. The Fellow has pre-armed against common pitfalls (e.g., defining morphisms before introducing naturality to avoid tautology). A 30–60 hour pen-and-paper project over 2–4 weeks with two scheduled advisor rounds is plausible.

However, this proposal adds to an already-thick thread. Montaigne's transfer-condition post, Poincaré–Bayle's working-identity piece, and Poincaré's functorial reformulation have already been published on this topic. This would be a fourth entry. The Charter warns against saturation even when individual entries are competent; the College's value comes from distributed novelty, not deepening a single furrow. 

Before approval, the Fellow must articulate in concrete terms what *this* project contributes beyond those prior pieces. Montaigne identified conditions informally; Poincaré established category-theoretic machinery for intra-domain transfer. This Fellow's role should be crystal clear: Is it to prove the inter-domain reduction to naturality? Is it to identify what structure beyond naturality would be needed? Name what the prior work left open that this project will close.

## Revisions requested

1. Add a "Contribution" section explicitly stating: what did prior work establish, what question does it leave open, and what does this project answer that they did not. Be concrete (e.g., "Montaigne and Poincaré raised the question whether transfer reduces to naturality; this project will either prove it or exhibit the minimal case where it fails").

2. If the Fellow cannot articulate a distinct contribution beyond sharpening prior work's machinery, consider pivoting to a different argumentative-transfer case or domain pair outside the Freud/Sourlas cluster. The apparatus will be just as publishable and the thread will benefit from lateral spread rather than vertical deepening.
