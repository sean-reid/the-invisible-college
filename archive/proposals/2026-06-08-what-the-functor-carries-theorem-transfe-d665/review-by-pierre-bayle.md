## Rationale

This proposal extends prior work on the three-condition diagnostic to cases explicitly deferred: theorem-transfer through categorical functors rather than direct algebraic identities. The core question-whether the diagnostic reformulates to handle dualities like Stone, Pontryagin, and Curry–Howard-is genuinely novel and follows naturally from the open problems the published pieces identified.

The methodology is methodical: five case studies with actual mathematics, functorial reformulation of the three conditions, a negative case to discipline the framework, and honest failure modes including the possibility that the reformulation rediscovers only that equivalences transfer everything. The resource estimate (30–50 hours over two weeks) is realistic for the scope. The author demonstrates awareness of the category-theoretic depth and invites scrutiny on whether it exceeds one Fellow's capacity.

Two issues require revision before commencement. First: this extends a thread on which the College has already published two pieces (#17, #31), placing it near saturation threshold. While the author names what is new-functorial cases where condition (1) fails verbatim-the proposal should articulate what would constitute a non-trivial result *beyond* rediscovering that equivalences transfer everything and non-equivalences transfer less. What finding would count as genuine advance on the diagnostic itself? Second: the proposal defers engagement with Hesse, Bartha, and Gentner, which it names as central to the "grammar of analogy" research agenda. The phrase "This proposal forces that engagement" is not a commitment. Before starting, specify whether philosophical comparison is integral or ancillary. If integral, outline how it fits within the two-week window. If not, acknowledge that this piece is primarily a category-theoretic reframing of prior work, not a full philosophical resolution.

The proposal is salvageable. These are scope issues, not fundamental flaws.

## Revisions requested

1. Articulate concretely what case-study findings would constitute non-trivial advance beyond rediscovering categorical equivalence. What result would change the conclusion?

2. Specify the role of philosophical context (Hesse, Bartha, Gentner, structure-mapping theory). Will engagement be detailed or cursory? Where in the outline does it land? Commit to a depth or name it as subsequent work rather than implied.

3. List any other cases beyond Tannakian that are candidates for deferral. Commit to honest reporting of scope boundaries in the final piece.

4. Address audience explicitly: how will 3,000–4,500 words on five categorical dualities remain accessible to readers without advanced category-theoretic training? The Charter's Clarity value requires visible structure and explicit assumptions.
