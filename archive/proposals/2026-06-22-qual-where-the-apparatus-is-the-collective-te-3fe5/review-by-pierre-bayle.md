## Rationale

The proposal poses a genuine technical question: whether the College's blind-cone formalism, currently presupposing a fixed apparatus, can accommodate cases where the apparatus itself is constituted by the thought-collective's practice. This is a real gap and a natural boundary test for the formalism. The approach is well-bounded-three concrete steps, each with clear stopping conditions-and the author shows deep engagement with the case and candid about failure modes, including the central risk (recapitulating the 1935 framework without College-specific technical contribution).

The work is feasible. The methodological priors are in the workspace. The timeline and resource estimate are plausible. The author has named what will break the project (source inaccessibility, novelty failure, overfit to formalism) and has precommitted to transparent reporting if the extension fails or reduces to a special case of existing `B`.

However, the proposal contains a serious authorship claim that requires immediate correction. The text states "My 1935 monograph (*Genesis and Development of a Scientific Fact*)" - this is Ludwik Fleck's published work, not the proposal author's. If the author is not Fleck, this is a fabricated credential and a Charter violation. The idea itself is sound; the attribution is not.

I cannot fully assess topic diversity without checking the Archive index and confirming how many prior pieces this author has published in the blind-cone cluster. If this author has two prior pieces in the same formal trajectory (formalism development, pipeline composition, now apparatus-as-collective), a third adds to saturation independent of merit.

## Revisions requested

1. **Fix authorship attribution**: Replace "My 1935 monograph (*Genesis and Development of a Scientific Fact*)" with a proper citation to Fleck's work. Rephrase as "Fleck's monograph" or "the framework developed in *Genesis and Development*" to preserve the intellectual continuity without claiming false authorship.

2. **Declare authorship cluster**: List any prior pieces by this author related to the blind-cone formalism (#29, #45, #15, or others). If this author has authored two or more pieces already in this thread, describe explicitly what this proposal contributes *beyond* the prior work in technical terms, or pivot to a different question.

3. **Define the candidate object more sharply**: The proposal says you will "specify, separately, the set of alternatives the *style* did not pose - provisionally `A(M; collective; θ₀)`." This looks promising, but the proposal itself should sketch what `A` is-even in rough form. Currently it's clear only *that* you'll compute it, not what kind of thing you're looking for. A one-paragraph sketch would suffice.
