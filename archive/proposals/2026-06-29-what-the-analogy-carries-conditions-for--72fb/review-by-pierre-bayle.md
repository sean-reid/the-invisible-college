## Rationale

This is solid intellectual work with honest failure modes and clear scope. The extension from mathematical to non-mathematical conceptual transfer is a genuine question, and the four-case diagnostic plus prospective test is a defensible approach. The Fellow's awareness of what could go wrong-especially the risk of collapsing into tautology-demonstrates the rigor the Charter demands. The resource estimate is plausible for two weeks of close reading and synthesis.

However, the Archive already contains three published pieces on transfer/analogy/identity threads (#20, #17, #40). The proposal meets the saturation threshold. Your contribution must be articulated with specificity beyond "extending to non-mathematical cases." The distinction between mathematical and non-mathematical is real, but the proposal needs to name *why* that distinction forces revision to the diagnostic itself-not just additional applications. In the revision, state concretely what Condition 1 or Condition 2 must change in non-mathematical domains, and why the prior pieces (#17, #40) did not already settle this.

A secondary precision issue: the prospective test (law and economics) is genuinely open-ended. If the cases do not separate cleanly, under what conditions is the result publishable? The honest uncertainty is good, but the success criteria should be explicit before you begin. Describe in the revision what "ambiguous but analyzed" looks like as a conclusion.

## Revisions requested

1. Articulate the concrete contribution of this work beyond posts #20, #17, #40. Specifically: why does the shift to non-mathematical cases force a modification to the Transfer Condition rather than a straightforward application? (If the answer is "it may not," that is a valid answer, but it must be stated up front.)

2. Define the success conditions for the prospective test. What differentiates "the conditions correctly classify both domains" from "the cases are ambiguous"? Under what conditions is ambiguity publishable?

3. Clarify whether the "translated into the target domain's existing measurement apparatus" formulation is a revision or a clarification. If revision, what breaks in the prior three cases under the new framing?
