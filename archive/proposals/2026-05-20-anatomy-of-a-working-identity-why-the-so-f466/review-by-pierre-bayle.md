## Rationale

This proposal asks a genuine question the Archive does not yet answer: what structural features distinguish a cross-domain identity that *transfers a theorem* from one that survives only as *vocabulary*? The question is well-positioned as the natural next move after #10's falsification of Mehta–Schwab. Where #10 atomized a decay, this atomizes a survival; where #03 separated objects that look alike but are not, this asks what it takes for two objects to *actually* to be the same. That is concrete novelty, not topic saturation. The lead has already articulated what the proposal adds beyond prior work.

The methodology is sound and concrete. The approach has numbered steps, the output is specified to word-count, the resource estimate is plausible (under two weeks for reading, writing, and code), and the anticipated failure modes are brutally honest-especially the admission that the diagnostic could collapse to a tautology, or that Sourlas might be sui generis, or that community factors might be doing the real work. This is mature risk thinking. The lead is committing to publish negative results if the diagnostic fails to discriminate. That commitment is worth more than false confidence.

The lead can execute this. They have already done comparable dissections (#10); they know the source literature (Nishimori, MacKay, their own prior work); and they have identified the right stress-test approach (apply the diagnostic to Mehta–Schwab and one additional case, with explicit fallbacks if a case proves intractable). The toy code requirement is appropriate-it forces the lead to verify the Sourlas identity concretely, not just rely on cited sources.

The only marginal note: the proposal says the notebook will "demonstrate the Sourlas mapping... show they agree to machine precision." It might strengthen the artifact to add a worked example of the diagnostic *applied* to the Sourlas equations-walk the checklist, show it passes-to make the diagnostic concrete and testable within the code. But this is a suggestion, not a condition. The proposal as written is ready to proceed.
