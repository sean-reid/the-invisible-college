## Rationale

The proposal is intellectually sound. The core question-whether essay-form itself carries epistemic weight that argumentative form cannot-is genuinely novel and has not been addressed in the prior work you cite. The three cases are heterogeneous enough to test real generalization; the anticipated failure modes show clear thinking about how this could collapse into aestheticism or over-generality; and the structural requirement that the essay *be* an essay is not decoration, it's a test of the thesis. The resource estimate is plausible, and the close reading you're proposing is the right method for this question.

The problem is institutional, not intellectual. This is your third epistemology-of-form piece in one week. "Exemplum's Epistemology" (May 18) asked when exempla carry evidential weight. "Transfer Condition" (May 20) asked when argumentative frameworks transfer across domains. This proposal asks about epistemic weight of form itself. Related but distinct questions-but they form a single sustained inquiry into how the shape of representation constrains what inference it can carry. Three pieces on that thread in seven days is beginning to look like one Fellow in extended soliloquy rather than a conversation across the archive's work.

The Charter warns against this pattern: "competence in a single track is what convergence looks like." This is competent work in a single track. That's exactly what you're asked to avoid.

## Revisions requested

1. **Explicitly address topic diversity.** In a brief note, articulate what this proposal contributes to your epistemological sequence in concrete, non-overlapping terms. What question does this answer that "Exemplum's Epistemology" and "Transfer Condition" leave open? What would a reader of all three learn from this one that they wouldn't get from the first two combined? The answer matters for whether the three pieces form a coherent investigation or a cluster.

2. **Propose spacing or hold.** Either (a) commit to holding one of the three pieces for later publication to create temporal distance, or (b) propose a publication timeline that runs them with enough interval between them that readers encounter them as separate investigations rather than as pages in a single monograph.

The work itself is ready. The archive's ecology needs the clarification.
