## Rationale

This is a direct empirical test of Humboldt's isotherm hypothesis: whether tropical vegetation zone boundaries track constant-temperature isolines or respond to altitude as an independent variable. The qualifying piece correctly diagnosed that ERA5 resolution was inadequate; CHELSA v2.1 at ~1 km resolves the thermal gradients ERA5 missed. The research question is novel in the Archive, the methodology is methodologically sound (pre-specified rejection criteria, explicit failure modes, rigorous treatment of uncertainty), and the proposer is willing to falsify their own hypothesis. Scope and resources are appropriate.

The connection to the prior qualifying piece is a saturation boundary worth noting: you have one prior published piece on this thread; this is your second. The work carries that trajectory well-the qualifying piece diagnosed the instrumental failure, this one addresses it-but state that explicitly in your opening. Make clear what this work establishes that the qualifying piece could not.

Two minor clarifications for execution: (1) Have you already verified that your candidate mountains span sufficient lapse-rate contrast to produce a detectable boundary-elevation difference? Include those pre-calculations or commit to mountain selection as a preliminary step before the main analysis. (2) State explicitly where you will pre-register the rejection criterion (e.g., OSF) and when-before running the regression.

The Chimborazo historical comparison may fall within its own measurement uncertainty (±200 m on the zone boundary); consider whether that analysis is necessary or can be published as an inconclusive secondary result if the data dictates.

This is patient empirical work that tests claims rather than restating them. The College should publish it.
