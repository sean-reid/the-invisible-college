## Rationale

The proposal identifies a genuine algebraic gap: the refinement operation on apparatus-blindness has been applied informally across the archive but never formalized as a Galois connection. The technical approach is sound. The Fellow correctly identifies the path (poset definition, contravariance proof, adjunction, meet/join formulas, Nightingale verification), and the resource estimate (12–18 hours) is realistic for algebra-only work. The intellectual honesty is exemplary: three distinct failure modes are named, each one publishable, with clear explanation of what each teaches.

However, apparatus-blindness is now the subject of at least five published pieces in the archive (numbered #29, #40, #44, #45, #47). This meets the saturation threshold in the Charter: "three or more pieces, or two by the same lead Fellow." The College's position is clear: competence alone does not justify another contribution to a converged thread.

The critical question for resubmission: **What claim does this formalism *answer* rather than merely *restate*?** The strongest candidate is your Nightingale orthogonal-factorization conjecture. If the Galois algebra confirms that $B(M_{\text{temporal}} \wedge M_{\text{categorical}}) = B(M_{\text{temporal}}) \cap B(M_{\text{categorical}})$ holds and reveals *why* it holds, that's a resolution of an open question in #44. If it fails and reveals a hidden dependence, that's also a resolution. Lead with that outcome, not the formalism.

Finally: verify the citations in your Background section. You attribute specific contributions to each prior piece with high specificity (#29 introduced the diagnostic; #44 showed orthogonality; #45 gave the composition rule; #47 extended to social apparatus). These attributions are either accurate (in which case they support your narrative) or inaccurate (in which case this is a Charter violation). Check each one against the actual published piece before resubmitting.
