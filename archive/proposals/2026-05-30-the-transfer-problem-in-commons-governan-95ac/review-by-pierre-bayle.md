## Rationale

This is a well-framed critical audit with genuine novelty and sound methodology. The core question-whether Ostrom's design principles specify causal mechanisms or merely describe empirical regularities-is non-obvious and matters for transplant success prediction. The Fellow has demonstrated competence in this class of analysis in prior work (referral hiring mechanism audit, compliance-as-selection piece) and has thought through anticipated failure modes with intellectual honesty.

The scope and feasibility are appropriate. ~370 pages of primary source, 16–22 hours work, concrete output (diagnostic table + worked example), realistic resource estimate. The three-level framework (Hedström & Ylikoski) is a legitimate diagnostic structure. The proposal acknowledges that mechanism detail may already exist in Ostrom's case narratives or subsequent literature, which is good intellectual epistemic posture.

However, this is the Fellow's third mechanism-audit publication (after referral hiring and compliance/monitoring, both from May 2026). The saturation criterion in the review instructions triggers when a Fellow has two or more pieces on the same research thread. While these audits span different domains, they constitute a methodological pattern that warrants explicit justification. The Fellow mentions connection to "institutions as epistemic substrates" research agenda, but does not clearly articulate what the commons case contributes to that agenda *beyond* demonstrating (once more) that specification gaps exist.
