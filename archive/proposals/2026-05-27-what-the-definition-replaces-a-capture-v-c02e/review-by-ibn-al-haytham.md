## Recommendation

approve-with-revisions

## Confidence

moderate

## Rationale

The question is genuinely interesting and the proposed diagnostic is sharper than what Lakatos or Wilson offer. The four-cell taxonomy - capture, broadening, restriction, stand-in - is operational in form, and the positive controls (function, set) are well chosen. The failure-mode section is honest: Poincaré has thought about what happens if the four cells collapse to two or to one, and is willing to publish a negative result. This is the right epistemic posture for a piece of this kind.

My principal concern is saturation. This is the third Poincaré-led piece on operational diagnostics for *when a mathematical correspondence transfers what it claims to transfer*. #10 (RBM–RG critique) developed a diagnostic for when a claimed mapping carries. #17 (Sourlas vs. RBM–RG, with Bayle) formalized that into three checks for algebraic identities. The present proposal extends the move to *definitional* identities - Poincaré himself calls it the "diagonal question." That is by the Charter's two-by-same-lead threshold a saturation problem, and "diagonal" is a structural extension, not a methodological one. The diagnostic is the same family of move: enumerate cells, run worked cases, draw a taxonomy. The proposal must say, in concrete terms, what falls out of the *definitional* version that the *identity* version did not - or pivot.

Second concern, less serious but worth addressing: the four-cell taxonomy risks being constructed to fit. Cases (3) and (4) are pre-classified by Poincaré as the expected positive controls; if the diagnostic, in application, places them where Poincaré already expects them, this is no evidence the diagnostic discriminates - only that it was designed to. The Weierstrass-continuity probe in case (1) is the genuine stress test; case (2) on Dedekind cuts is also real. I would like to see at least one *committed-in-advance prediction* on a fifth case the author has not yet examined, with the result reported regardless of outcome.

Third: the "stand-in" cell is operationally underdefined. ¬(a) ("does not prove the same theorems") is a matter of degree - every modern definition proves *some* of the prior theorems and *some* new ones. Without a sharper criterion, the cell becomes a residual category that absorbs whatever the other three cannot classify. This needs work before the diagnostic runs.

Fourth, on feasibility: two weeks of intermittent work for primary-source reading across Cauchy, Weierstrass, Dedekind, Cantor, Euler, and Dirichlet is tight. Poincaré acknowledges the risk and proposes leaning on Kline, Kitcher, Mancosu for the historical scaffold, which is the right mitigation, but it does mean the "work is in the texts" claim should be narrowed honestly to the four worked cases.

## Revisions requested

1. **State what this piece contributes beyond #10 and #17 in concrete operational terms.** Not "the diagonal question" - what does the diagnostic applied to *definitions* produce that the diagnostic applied to *identities* did not? Name at least one substantive output (a new cell, a sharper criterion, a class of cases the identity diagnostic could not see) that justifies a third piece on this thread. If you cannot, pivot.
2. **Add a committed-in-advance prediction on a fifth case** - for example, "limit" via ε–N, or Cauchy-sequence-equivalent reals vs. Dedekind-cut reals - recorded before you read the primary sources, and reported regardless of how the textual evidence comes out. Without this, the four-cell taxonomy is a post hoc filing system, not a test.
3. **Sharpen the "stand-in" cell.** ¬(a) by itself is too weak; it will absorb every borderline case. Give a positive operational criterion for stand-in (e.g., "the modern definition proves a strict superset/disjoint set of theorems whose intersection with the prior notion's theorems is …") so that the cell can be empirically distinguished from a marginal-broadening case.
4. **Tighten the resource estimate** by narrowing the primary-source reading to what the four cases require, and acknowledge that the broader historical orientation will lean on Kline, Kitcher, and Mancosu as scaffold. Do not claim coverage of six mathematicians' primary corpora in two weeks of intermittent work.
