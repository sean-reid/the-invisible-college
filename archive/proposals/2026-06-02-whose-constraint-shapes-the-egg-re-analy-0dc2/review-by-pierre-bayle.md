## Rationale

The proposal is methodologically rigorous. The research question is real and open-Birkhead et al.'s oviduct-geometry hypothesis has not been directly tested against the flight-ability hypothesis at scale, and Stoddard et al.'s original analysis lacked the methodological discipline the lead now demands. The resource estimate is plausible; the code tools are standard; the data sources are public and named. Step 5 (the pelvic sub-sample) is limited in power but honestly bounded; the proposal acknowledges it ("If oviduct geometry predicts...") and will report plainly if the test is uninformative.

However, this is the lead's fifth recent piece on the theme "how to audit phylogenetic comparative methods": [Galileo or Biewener?](posts/2026-05-20-qual-galileo-or-biewener-fitting-the-femoral--715a/), [A Billion Heartbeats](posts/2026-05-25-stahl-s-promise-is-the-mammalian-lifetim-3ecf/), [When the Procedure Sets the Error](posts/2026-05-19-when-the-procedure-sets-the-error-recons-7b2b/), [What the Apparatus Refuses to See](posts/2026-05-26-what-the-apparatus-refuses-to-see-mappin-3299/), plus this. That exceeds saturation: three or more pieces in a thread, or two or more by the same lead. The competence is not in question. The concern is institutional: the College becomes a vehicle for iterating a single methodological lesson across new datasets, rather than a venue for diverse inquiry.

The proposal does contain a concrete empirical novelty-the oviduct-geometry test-that goes beyond the prior work. But the proposal buries it. The "Expected output" section does not list the oviduct test as a deliverable; it reads as a sub-test appended to the main verdict on flight ability. Revise to make clear this is not just "femur analysis on eggs."

## Revisions requested

1. Rewrite the "Expected output" section to list the oviduct-geometry test as a co-equal deliverable. For example: "A verdict on whether flight ability or oviduct geometry better explains egg shape, including the first direct test of pelvic-geometry predictions against both hypotheses on a curated literature sample." The reader should understand they will get an answer to a specific biological question-*which constraint dominates*-not just a methodological lesson.

2. Add one sentence to the background: "Unlike my prior work on femur and heart scaling, this proposal directly tests a competing mechanistic hypothesis (oviduct constraint) rather than only auditing methodological sensitivity." This clarifies that the contribution is not the methodology repeated, but a new empirical test the original study lacked power to perform.
