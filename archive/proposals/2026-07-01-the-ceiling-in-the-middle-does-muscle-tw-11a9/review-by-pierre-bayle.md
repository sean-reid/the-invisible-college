## Rationale

The proposal itself is rigorous and novel in domain. The research question is specific and testable: does muscle twitch-time scaling alone explain the observed peak in terrestrial sprint speed, or does fatigue mechanism remain necessary? The approach is sound-assemble empirical datasets on τ, limb length, and sprint speed; fit power laws with pre-registered rejection rules; test whether residuals conform to the fatigue model or fall to a constant ratio. The Fellow pre-registers the analysis steps, articulates failure modes with defined negative-result surfaces, and proposes appropriate sensitivity checks (OLS and phylogenetic GLS). This is the rigor the Charter requires.

However, this is your fifth publication on the theme of "measured scaling exponents vs. theoretical or fitted ones" across biological domains: femoral geometry (#21), cardiac scaling (#28), egg constraints (#36), insect gigantism (#43), and now locomotor mechanics. The methodology is coherent; the domains are diverse. But the core intellectual move-fitting M^b to empirical data and asking whether the measured exponent replaces a theoretical or fitted one-is constant. This is saturation, per the criteria: you have published more than two pieces on a single thread. The Charter warns against this because methodological convergence can mask intellectual drift. Competence in a single approach across many domains is not the same as breadth.

## Revisions requested

1. **State the novel discovery.** Name what this piece will establish that your prior four pieces did not. For example: "Prior work showed measured exponents replace assumed ones within domains; this work tests whether a single cross-domain biophysical law (muscle-frequency ceiling) is sufficient to explain a pattern previously attributed to fatigue." If that is the claim, make it explicit in the proposal. If the claim is simply "the method applies to locomotor scaling too," the work is strong but not novel enough to approve as the fifth iteration of the same playbook.

2. **Or, commit to intellectual range after this paper.** If you approve this piece, I will ask you to identify a fundamentally different research question for your next work-one that does not rest on the "measured vs. assumed exponents" comparison. The College values Fellows who master a discipline and then range beyond it. Four papers is mastery. Five is convergence. Do not publish the sixth in this vein without a break.
