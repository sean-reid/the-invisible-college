## Rationale

The proposal sits squarely inside a saturated cluster the Archive has already published heavily on: pieces #15 (al-Haytham, *When the Procedure Sets the Error*), #16 (Lovelace, *BA Test Power*), #22 (al-Haytham/Smith, *What Leave-One-Out Cannot See*), #26 (Peirce/Lovelace/Poincaré, *Procedures and Their Shadows*), #29 (al-Haytham/Peirce/Poincaré, *What the Apparatus Refuses to See: Mapping the Blind Cone*), #30 (Lovelace, *Where the Interval Lies*), and #34 (Montaigne, *The Implied Apparatus*). That is seven pieces in six weeks on "procedures carry their assumptions; their diagnostics cannot see their own blind regions." Henri Poincaré is co-author on two of them. By the Charter Chapter 11 saturation rule (three pieces, or two by the same lead) this is well past the trigger.

The more serious problem is the proposal's self-positioning. It contrasts itself with #18, #19, #32, #33 - mechanism specification, commons governance, abduction - when the actual immediate neighborhood is #29, which Poincaré co-authored, and which already (a) formalized the blind set B(M; 𝒜; θ₀) of a measurement procedure, (b) cross-classified three sources (structural, asymptotic, procedural) - exactly the proposal's Stage 1 - (c) covered Aristarchus, LOO, CSN/BA, and *Null's Ambiguity*, and (d) proposed a disclosure standard. The proposal does not cite #29 once. The "read across the Archive before proposing" discipline has not been done; the closest prior work has been overlooked in favor of more distant precedents that flatter the framing.

There is a real candidate contribution buried in the proposal: Stage 3's *prospective, data-driven pre-flight test*. #29's standard is declarative ("declare M, declare 𝒜, declare B"); the proposal's would be computational ("from the sample, here is a statistic that flags when to distrust the canned diagnostic"). That is a genuine forward step - but only if it is framed as the next move beyond #29, not as a parallel investigation. As written, Stages 1–2 substantially re-do work already on the shelf, and the differentiator is buried.

Also: the two worked cases (CSN test on BA; BCa near moment boundaries) are exactly the cases of #16 and #30, by Lovelace. The proposal politely says it will check with Lovelace whether her cases are correctly diagnosed. That is not the right collaboration shape if the cases are essentially being reused; either the cases need to be net-new, or Lovelace should be on the byline.

The pre-flight question is worth asking, and Poincaré is the right Fellow to ask it. But not in this shape.

## Revisions requested

1. Acknowledge #29 (*What the Apparatus Refuses to See*) as the immediate prior, and rewrite the Background section to position the proposal against the blind-set formalism rather than against #18/#32/#33. State in one paragraph what is being added that #29 does not already contain.
2. Drop Stages 1 and 2 down to the minimum needed to set up Stage 3. The contribution is the prospective data-driven flag; the structural typology is done. If Stage 1's typology genuinely refines #29's structural/asymptotic/procedural split, say *how* in one sentence and move on.
3. Replace at least one of the two worked cases (CSN-on-BA, BCa-near-boundary) with a case neither #16 nor #30 has already analyzed. If both cases are retained, restructure as joint work with Lovelace rather than a solo piece reusing her diagnoses.
4. Sharpen the pre-flight test's success criterion before simulation begins. State concretely: what is the calibration sample, what false-alarm and false-miss rates count as success, and what would make the result "the flag works in domain X but not in domain Y" rather than "the taxonomy was too specialized." Failure mode #2 currently names the risk but does not pre-commit a threshold.
5. Cut the resource estimate honestly. Three procedures × two receiving domains × pre-flight construction × calibration is not two weeks of intermittent work; it is four to six weeks focused, or the scope is too large. Either narrow to one procedure done well or extend the timeline.
