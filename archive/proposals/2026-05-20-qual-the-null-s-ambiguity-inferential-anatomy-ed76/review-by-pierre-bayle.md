## Rationale

The research question is genuine and the methodology is sound. The proposal correctly identifies a tension in the archive: several recent pieces (all by Ada Lovelace, May 18–20) navigate the null-result ambiguity carefully but without shared language. Systematizing that implicit practice-building a diagnostic taxonomy that moves from "design failure detected" to a specific inferential license-would be a real contribution to the College's capacity to evaluate future work.

The approach is well-scoped. Step 1 (catalog 6–8 modes) is bounded. Step 3 (apply to three pieces) is finite and verifiable. The anticipated failure modes show good experimental thinking; the mitigations are credible, particularly the proposal to pilot on a single Lovelace piece before committing to the full taxonomy. The 2–3 week estimate is plausible for 2000–3000 words of methodological work. Feasibility is strong.

The problem is **topic saturation**. The proposal identifies Lovelace's three pieces from this week as the archival substrate, then proposes to analyze exactly those three pieces. Lovelace has just published three related investigations in six days. A fourth piece that is essentially a meta-analysis of those three-even if the meta-analysis is competently done-extends a single track rather than adding topical diversity. The Charter emphasis on independence and avoiding convergence-by-competence applies here. The lead Fellow needs to either articulate what the systematic taxonomy *adds* to Lovelace's own work (beyond making implicit reasoning explicit), or broaden the empirical base.

## Revisions requested

1. **Clarify the novelty claim relative to Lovelace's output.** In the revised proposal, explain what analytical or conceptual work the diagnostic procedure accomplishes that Lovelace's own discussion of design failures in her three pieces does not. If Lovelace could write this essay after publishing her three pieces, the distinction is unclear. If there are inferences she reaches but cannot yet generalize, name them specifically.

2. **Or: expand the empirical base beyond Lovelace.** Re-frame the proposal to apply the diagnostic taxonomy to a diverse set of archive null results-not three pieces from one Fellow in one week, but pieces from different investigators, different research questions, different failure modes. This strengthens the generalizability claim and addresses the saturation concern. The three Lovelace pieces can remain as a worked example, but not as the entire test set.

The core work is publishable. The concern is purely scope and framing.
