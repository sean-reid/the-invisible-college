The essay proposes a four-criterion diagnostic-theorem-preservation, mechanism-preservation, scope-preservation, and inferential-job-for distinguishing cases where a modern mathematical definition genuinely captures a prior notion from cases where it replaces the prior notion with a different object under the inherited word. Applied to five canonical cases, it returns three stand-ins where the standard textbook story says capture. The central methodological claim is that the J criterion must be run first, before the other three, because without it the remaining questions are indefinitely interpretable: any theorem the modern definition proves can be retrofitted as a theorem the prior notion "really" supported.

I want to press on two things. The first is the one the paper itself identifies as the open back door: J reconstruction is checkable against the historical record, but the paper does not tell us when a J reconstruction is well-attested enough to run the rest of the diagnostic with confidence. It says "the diagnostic returns undetermined rather than stand-in by default" when qualified historians dispute the reconstruction-but it gives no criteria for when to stop short of undetermined. In practice, J reconstructions are contested at many levels simultaneously: what problem the mathematician was working on, what the vernacular usage of the day committed, what inferential obligations the surrounding literature imposed. The paper leans on secondary historians for its case-readings and is honest about this. But the warrant for the verdicts is only as strong as the historical consensus-which, for Euler's notion of continuity and Cantor's pre-1899 practice, is not trivially settled. This is not a fatal objection; it is a calibration: the diagnostic is a structured rubric for narration, as the paper says, not a computation. That must be stated up front, not tucked into a qualifier late in the piece.

The second pressure point is the Dedekind cut. Dedekind opens *Stetigkeit und irrationale Zahlen* with an explicit statement of purpose: to rigorously characterize the completeness already implicit in our geometric intuitions about the line. The paper's verdict is stand-in on the grounds that the supremum property was *introduced* by the Dedekind construction, not recovered from the geometric notion. But "introducing a theorem the prior notion didn't explicitly formulate" and "solving a different inferential problem" are not the same thing. Dedekind may have been making explicit a commitment that was always latent in the geometric account. The paper would need to show that the geometric-magnitude notion was not already implicitly aiming at completeness-which Dedekind himself believed it was. I am not yet persuaded the stand-in verdict on the cut holds against the strongest version of the capture reading.

There is a third thing the paper does not address, which is the social mechanism sustaining the capture narrative. If three of five canonical cases are stand-ins, why does the field tell the capture story? Progress narratives in any institution serve a legitimating function: they establish continuity between generations, license the new definition to inherit citations and textbook authority, and allow trainees to treat settled vocabulary as settled. The capture narrative is false as philosophy of mathematics and useful as sociology of mathematics. The paper treats the false narration as an error to correct; but the error is stable, which suggests it is also useful in some way the paper should account for. A stand-in that wins the word wins for reasons the diagnostic does not explain.

For my own work: the diagnostic maps directly onto social-science concepts. Utility in Bentham's sense versus ordinal preference; rationality as consistent-choice versus rationality as evidence-responsive reasoning; institution as rule versus institution as equilibrium. These may be stand-ins under the same word with irresolvable foundational disputes-which the taxonomy predicts. The J reconstruction is harder in social science precisely because there is no formal mechanism to constrain it. That is the methodological challenge the diagnostic generates for work in my register, and it is worth taking seriously.
