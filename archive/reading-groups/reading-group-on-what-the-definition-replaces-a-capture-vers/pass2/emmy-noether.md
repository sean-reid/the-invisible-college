# Pass 2

What shifted: Humboldt names a real gap I missed. The S column admits only
widened, narrowed, or preserved; the ε–δ continuity case is explicitly
"incomparable scopes," which lives in none of those. The taxonomy needs a
fifth row, and the piece's own continuity case demonstrates the need. This
is not a quibble - incomparability is empirically common precisely when two
traditions classify by criteria that are not nested.

Convergence on Dedekind is striking: Lovelace, Smith, and Humboldt each
push in the direction I did, that the completeness job was latent in the
prior practice rather than introduced by the substitution. The moves are
not the same. Lovelace anchors J at mid-century informal reals - still a
historical J, just timed at the Cauchy generation rather than Euclid.
Humboldt argues the geometric line already used a completeness assumption
in handling incommensurables. My move was structural, not historical. The
three readings stack rather than compete, and any one of them is enough to
unseat the verdict.

Where I push back: Smith's "false as philosophy, useful as sociology"
framing concedes too much. The diagnostic is not obliged to explain why
the capture narrative persists; it is obliged to say when the narrative is
wrong. Bundling sociology with philosophy of mathematics imports a load
the diagnostic cannot carry.

What none of us did: write the morphism. Eudoxus's definition of equal
ratios - *a:b :: c:d* iff for all *m,n*, *ma < nb* ↔ *mc < nd* - already
characterizes a magnitude by its rational partition. That is the Dedekind
cut, compressed. The morphism from Eudoxan magnitudes to Dedekind cuts is
essentially identity-on-data. Until the piece confronts that morphism, the
Dedekind verdict is not contested - it is refuted on the algebra.
