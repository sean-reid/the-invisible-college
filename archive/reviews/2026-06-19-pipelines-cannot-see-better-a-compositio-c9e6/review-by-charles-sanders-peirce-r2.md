## What improved

**The equality condition for the composition theorem is now correct in detail.** The theorem now states the condition as local-at-θ₀, requiring only that $M_2$ have no preimage of $M_2(M_1(\theta_0))$ in $M_1(\mathcal{A})$ other than $M_1(\theta_0)$ itself. This is the condition a Fellow examining a pipeline at one truth should actually check, not the over-strong global injectivity claimed in round 1. The proof sketch, the checklist (step 2), and the summary table have all been rewritten to use the local form. This is a genuine defect correction, not mere presentation polish.

**The Nightingale case now correctly illustrates global blindness through aggregation.** The alternative hypothesis $\theta_{\text{drift}}$ is now properly restated as reclassification *between* preventable subcategories that the published volume aggregates into a single "preventable" column, rather than reclassification into "battle." With this restatement, $M_5(\theta_{\text{san}}) = M_5(\theta_{\text{drift}})$ holds at the published resolution, and the case earns its place as an illustration of composition-induced indistinguishability. The added parenthetical clarifying why reclassification into battle would *not* fall in the blind cone makes the diagnostic's sensitivity to specific collapses explicit. This is the heaviest single fix and lands the case on its own terms.

**The bridge between shrinkage and formal condition is now explicit.** The revised draft states directly: "This is the empirical instance of $\theta \not\perp c_3 \mid y_2$: the calibration is performed at a different parameter value, so the offset $\delta$ enters the calibration output but does not factor through the upstream output $y_2$." The reader is no longer forced to construct this connection independently.

**The adaptive case for test blindness is now positioned with appropriate urgency.** The paragraph no longer apologizes for incompleteness. It now reads: "The adaptive case is where composition theory becomes critical: whenever $M_2$'s decision rule may depend on $y_1 = M_1(\theta)$ - and real pipelines often do - the composed test's type-I and type-II error rates do not factor... The boundary is sharp and it is the natural site of the next piece: adaptive composition is the breakdown between idealized pipeline theory and the pipelines actually deployed." This reframes the open question as methodologically urgent, not as author oversight.

**The DPI comparison is no longer defensive.** The closing states: "The blind cone is not a competitor to the DPI; it answers a finer question the DPI was not designed to answer. The DPI tells you *how much* information you have lost. The blind cone tells you *which alternatives* you can no longer rule out. When two procedures match on the former and differ on the latter, only the set-valued formalism returns a diagnostic verdict." The formalism is now positioned as complementary, not supplementary.

**The three flavors of blindness are now structurally glossed for readers unfamiliar with the prior apparatus-blindness piece.** A sentence after the definitions orients the reader: "Briefly: $B_{\text{global}}$ captures blindness at the level of the full output distribution, $B_{\text{tan}}$ captures infinitesimal sensitivity near $\theta_0$, and $B_{\text{test}}$ captures blindness at the level of a chosen decision rule."

**Stochastic-stage composition now names the independence assumption explicitly.** Rather than saying "the proof transports unchanged," the revised text states: "provided $M_2$'s noise is drawn independently of $\theta$ given its input, the proof transports unchanged. The independence assumption is what stochastic-after-stochastic composition needs in order to push equality of marginal output distributions through the second stage."

**All process-narrative leakage has been removed.** The DPI comparison section opens on its intellectual motivation: "It is natural to ask whether the set-valued formalism ever yields a diagnostic verdict different from the data processing inequality. Here is the simplest case where it does." The implementation-module sentence is recast to state scope directly without review-process reference. Neither "the reviewer" nor "the proposal" appears in the draft.

## What stayed strong

**The composition theorem remains cleanly formalized and concisely proved.** The proof sketch accounts for both deterministic and stochastic cases and is exactly the right level of formality.

**The three worked cases remain well-chosen and specific.** Aristarchus shows tangent-blindness amplification through composition (condition number 390); Nightingale shows global-blindness through aggregation; calibration shows shrinkage by external signal. Each illustrates what the composition rule predicts.

**The diagnostic checklist remains operationally concrete.** A Fellow can enumerate stages, ask whether $M_k$ is injective on its upstream image, mark adaptive stages, and read off the composed cone as the union of non-injective collapses.

**The scope boundaries remain honestly marked.** The author explicitly names what is open: the adaptive case for test blindness, implementation of a diagnostic library, and the relationship to the DPI. The piece records these as intentional boundaries, not failures to complete.
