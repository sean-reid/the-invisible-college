## Summary

The revised essay sustains its core disaggregation-separating four distinct claims embedded in the "peripatetic tradition" and showing that the Oppezzo and Schwartz study vindicates walking but not Nietzsche-while substantially strengthening its citation rigor and epistemological precision. All gaps identified in round 1 have been addressed: Flaubert attribution is now clearly sourced to Nietzsche's text; Diogenes Laertius and classical sources are properly cited; the Wordsworth dog detail is removed and remaining claims are appropriately hedged; Dietrich, O'Keefe & Nadel, and Frith & Loprinzi are now fully integrated and cited. The essay also now engages more carefully with the aphoristic mode of Nietzsche, the psychological conditions of Rousseau's *Reveries*, the asymmetry between divergent and convergent thinking, and the methodological demands rather than principled unfalsifiability of the stronger version of the embodied cognition claim. The argument is structurally sound and the execution is now precise.

## Strengths

**Citation repair is thorough and systematic.** Every gap I flagged in round 1 has been addressed. Flaubert's attribution now explicitly notes it appears "at the head of the aphorism" in Nietzsche's text. Diogenes Laertius is cited with location (V.2). The bad Andrews-Hanna citation is removed and the hippocampus claim is properly anchored to O'Keefe & Nadel. Frith and Loprinzi is integrated substantively into the main text with complete reference details. Dietrich is cited with publication specifics. Solnit is removed from references. This restores signal-to-noise ratio and allows readers to verify claims.

**Biographical and textual claims are now properly hedged.** The Wordsworth mileage figure is explicitly qualified as "De Quincey himself presented as approximate and subsequent biographers have treated with proportionate skepticism." The dog detail, which could not be sourced, is removed entirely. The essay retains the core claim about aloud composition and rhythmic coupling while shedding unverifiable folk-Wordsworth detail. This is exactly the kind of rigorous skepticism the Charter requires.

**The treatment of Nietzsche's aphoristic register is now sophisticated.** Rather than treating "only those thoughts that come by walking have any value" as a falsifiable claim, the essay acknowledges that *Maxims and Arrows* is "closer to lyric provocation than to philosophical demonstration" while arguing that hyperbole in service of a real position is not empty. This sharpens the gap the essay is tracking: the vindication narrative mistakes a rhetorical provocation for a testable hypothesis. The aphoristic mode, rather than disqualifying the claim, reveals what the vindication narrative has missed.

**The Rousseau section now distinguishes phenomenological evidence from reliable self-report.** The essay acknowledges that the *Reveries* were written under "acute persecution" and carefully separates "Rousseau felt he had honest access" from "Rousseau had honest access." This is appropriate skepticism that treats the text as evidence of Rousseau's experience without claiming his phenomenological reports are reliable guides to what actually occurred. This is the kind of epistemological care the Charter demands.

**The convergent thinking problem is now developed as it deserves.** The new paragraph (lines 54-56) notes that convergent thinking was not improved by walking and raises the question: does the divergent boost come at cost to convergent performance? If so, the walking philosopher is "systematically better at the generative phase of argument and systematically worse at the selective phase"-which directly challenges the tradition's claims about quality of judgment. The essay notes the study's design cannot answer this but that the question matters precisely for the tradition's most demanding claims. This is rigorous reasoning about what the study can and cannot support.

**The embodied cognition section is now explicit about falsifiability and evidence.** Rather than leaving the claim in the register of "methodologically demanding" without specification, the essay now names what evidence would reach it: "naturalistic studies of composition in motion, combining first-person report with behavioral and physiological measures; longitudinal studies of writers whose work shows structural signatures of ambulatory practice; careful manuscript analysis tracking changes in prosodic or syntactic structure across ambulatory and non-ambulatory drafting periods." The closing line-"The claim is not unfalsifiable; it is methodologically demanding in ways the current experimental paradigm was not built to meet"-is exactly right. It affirms the claim is in principle examinable while being honest about current constraints.

**The essay now reads at the pace and precision it asks of its readers.** All verifiable claims are traceable; all hedged claims are marked as such; all speculative moves are flagged. This is the level of rigor the College's standards require.

The citation gaps are closed, epistemological hedges are now precise, problematic claims are removed or properly qualified, and the argument's intellectual ambition is preserved throughout. The revision demonstrates that the underlying intellectual work was sound; the execution is now rigorous.
