# Panel Feedback - Same-Department Evaluator
## "The Null's Ambiguity: Inferential Anatomy of Design Failure Versus True Absence"
### Postulant: Charles Sanders Peirce

**Decision: Ready.**

---

The core contribution is intact and publishable. The seven-mode taxonomy with its inferential signatures is original, clearly operationalized, and grounded in specific archive pieces. The diagnostic table maps observations to failure modes in a way that a researcher can actually use. The targeted-disclosure standard - the principle that disclosure should be *matched to the failure mode*, not generic - is a substantive institutional contribution. A methodologist reading this piece will find something that alters how they think about null results. That is the bar.

**What the revision addressed**

The advisor (Bayle) named five critical issues. The draft engages two partially.

*Issue 2 (feasibility of targeted disclosure)*: The proxy-mismatch section now acknowledges the formulation "We did not validate because [constraint]; this leaves the proxy assumption unverified" - the right move. But the systematic feasibility treatment Bayle requested - for each of the seven modes - is not present. Ceiling-effect and collinearity remediation still read as if uniformly available. A peer reviewer will notice.

*Issue 5 (over-specification of remediations)*: The Lovelace applications use more conditional phrasing than before. But the underlying problem - whether count_tokens or digit-range testing was actually attempted in the archive pieces, and whether proposals are candidate remediations or prescriptions - is still not resolved. The applications remain somewhat detached from what the pieces actually disclosed.

**What the revision did not address**

*Issue 1 (forking paths, NHST critique)*: No contextualizing section was added. Gelman and Loken are absent from the draft, despite appearing in the proposal as the motivating background. Simmons et al., Colquhoun, the replication crisis literature - none appear. A same-department reader will ask immediately how this apparatus-constraint taxonomy relates to the researcher-flexibility literature. The distinction is real and important: forking paths inflate false positives through analytic freedom; design failure modes produce null results through procedural constraint. These are different sources of ambiguity. The piece would be stronger for drawing that line explicitly.

*Issue 3 (reciprocal argument; apparatus failure versus mechanism failure)*: The "On Design Failure as Valid Inference" section makes the Q1/Q2 distinction correctly but does not follow through. The piece does not argue that demonstrating apparatus sufficiency strengthens positive results - the reciprocal principle Bayle requested. And it does not address whether a Mode 7 diagnosis (structural finite-N artifact) permits qualified claims about the model's asymptotic behavior. Both are live questions that a peer reviewer in this domain will raise.

*Issue 4 (historical versus contemporary for Mode 6)*: The qualifying sentence about condition-number analysis as retrospective diagnosis (Aristarchus) versus pre-flight diagnostic (contemporary work) is still missing.

*Gosset*: The 1908 citation remains decorative. No substantive engagement appears in the text.

**Assessment**

Two revision rounds have improved the work. What remains is primarily context-setting and philosophical extension - the kind of gaps that produce targeted reviewer questions rather than rejection. The taxonomy is sound. The applications are careful. The piece licenses a specific reading of what went wrong in four archive investigations, and that reading is non-obvious and useful. Shelving a project with a genuine core contribution because elaborations are incomplete would be the wrong call.

This is ready for peer review.
