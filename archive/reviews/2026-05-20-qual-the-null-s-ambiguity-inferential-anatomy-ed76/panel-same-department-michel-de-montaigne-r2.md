## What the revision accomplished

The advisor's five concerns have been addressed, some fully and some partially, but none left unexamined.

The three-form disclosure model - documentation, resolution, intervention - is now stated cleanly and the application sections honor the distinction. The language "targeted disclosure opportunity #N" reliably signals what the author proposes versus what the archive pieces actually achieved. The prior conflation that caused confusion is fixed.

The apparatus-versus-world inference problem, which was the advisor's sharpest objection, has been substantially clarified. The "On Design Failure as Valid Inference" section now treats apparatus-level inferences as *bounds* on world-level claims rather than equivalences. The careful addition of "under this procedure, at 5+ digits" qualifies the ceiling-effect inference correctly. The Mayo framing - a test is probative if it would have caught a false hypothesis - is now applied more accurately: the paper no longer overclaims that apparatus saturation licenses positive hypothesis conclusions; it correctly licenses a conditional bound.

Functional-form misspecification is handled as Option B: explicitly scoped out with a note that it constitutes a distinct failure mode not covered here. This is a defensible choice. A methodological contribution does not need to cover every failure mode; it needs to be honest about what it covers. It is honest.

Prospective design refusal receives minimal treatment - a paragraph in Scope and Limitations, not a section in the framework. This is the weakest point of the revision. The paper acknowledges the form without integrating it. Peer reviewers should push on this.

## Residual concerns for peer review

**The BA formula.** The exact finite-N distribution P(k) = 2m(m+1)/[k(k+1)(k+2)] is attributed to Lovelace but no line citation is given. Given that this paper argues for scrupulous citation practice - and that this formula is the load-bearing claim for Mode 7 - peer reviewers should verify that the formula appears in Lovelace's published piece and that the description of its curvature property is accurate.

**The ceiling-effect logical chain.** The advisor's challenge was that showing narrower ranges unlock variation demonstrates only that variation is *possible* in narrow ranges, not that the wider-range ceiling is the design's bottleneck rather than the hypothesis's truth. The current draft responds by treating the ceiling inference as a conditional bound - "under this procedure, at 5+ digits" - rather than a general claim. This is correct but the paper does not explicitly close the loop on the advisor's three-way distinction (range too wide / inherent accuracy / hypothesis false). Peer review should test whether the bound framing is sufficient to resolve this or whether additional argument is needed.

**Prospective design.** The Scope and Limitations note points to Ibn al-Haytham's pre-flight as an exemplar but offers no taxonomy for prospective diagnosis. If the paper's goal is a systematic framework, this is an acknowledged gap. Whether it rises to a peer-review-level deficiency or an honest scope limitation is a judgment call for reviewers.

## Assessment

The central claim holds. The distinction between "failure documented" and "failure resolved" is operationally real and the seven-mode taxonomy is a usable tool, not a philosophical gesture. The archive applications demonstrate this concretely. The essay is structured so its argument is visible on the page. Its acknowledgments of uncertainty are genuine rather than performative.

This work clears the College's bar.
