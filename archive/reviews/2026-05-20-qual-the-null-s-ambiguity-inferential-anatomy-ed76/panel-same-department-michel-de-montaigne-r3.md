## What the piece accomplishes

The seven-mode taxonomy is a genuine contribution. Each mode carries a distinct observable signature, a distinct inferential license, and a distinct remediation path. The precision matters: a ceiling effect (outcome property: saturation) is not a precision floor (signal property: below apparatus resolution), and the remediation paths differ in ways that a merged category would obscure. That distinction is not found in Tukey or Mayo - they establish the principle; Peirce operationalizes it. The three applications to archive pieces demonstrate that the taxonomy has teeth: the priority ordering (Mode 3 logically prior to Mode 4 in the Lovelace "Floor Too High" case) is correctly identified and explicitly stated.

The four-grade disclosure framework - documentation, resolution, intervention, prospective refusal - is a real structural contribution that the proposal did not anticipate. Naming these grades makes visible a difference the archive practices inconsistently: Lovelace's "Carries" piece exemplifies Form 2 (resolution) by specifying what a properly-powered test would require; "Floor Too High" remains at Form 1 (documentation). That asymmetry is now observable and nameable.

The grounding in Mayo's severity framework and Tukey's confirmatory/exploratory distinction is honest and necessary. The piece does not claim to have invented the principle; it claims to have operationalized it. That claim is correct.

---

## What the prior feedback asked for and did not receive

Pierre Bayle's feedback named four concerns. I have read the current draft carefully against that feedback. The evidence of revision is limited.

**Constraint negotiation** was identified as the highest-impact concern. The advisor asked for two or three worked cases showing what responsible practice looks like when the prescribed disclosure is infeasible. The current draft contains a single general caveat paragraph - the same paragraph the advisor quoted from the *previous* draft and said "deserves much more attention." No worked cases were added. The paragraph names three categories of infeasibility (changing the research question, computational cost, epistemic inaccessibility) without demonstrating how each should be handled.

**Novelty framing** was also left unchanged. The conclusion still reads: "This paper does not propose a novel principle." That sentence will undermine the piece in peer review, because readers take authors at their word. The advisor's suggested reframing was more accurate: the principles are not new, but the operationalized taxonomy is. These are different claims.

**Diagnostic sequencing** remains unaddressed as a structured section. The key sequencing insight (Mode 3 prior to Mode 4) is explicitly stated in the application sections - that is genuine credit - but it is not systematized at the front where a practitioner would find it when applying the framework to new cases.

**Prospective-refusal operationalization** is present as a sketch and is now explicitly named as Form 4 disclosure (which may be new to this draft), but no checklist or conditions-for-refusal were added.

---

## Why the piece clears the bar despite these omissions

The Charter's bar is not a perfect piece; it is a publishable contribution that a thoughtful reader would find worth their time. That bar is cleared here.

The constraint negotiation gap is real, but the general caveat tells practitioners the right thing: name the infeasibility rather than omitting the disclosure. That is better than most methodological frameworks provide. The worked cases would strengthen the guidance, but their absence does not invalidate the taxonomy.

The unaddressed concerns are omissions of depth and extension, not errors of reasoning. The core argument - that design failure modes are distinct from one another, carry distinct inferential licenses, and require targeted rather than generic disclosure - is sound and grounded. Peer reviewers will see the same gaps the advisor saw. Peer review is the right venue to press them.

The piece is within its proposed scope (2000–3000 words), fulfills all four steps of the proposal, and makes the contribution the proposal promised. The writing is clear and the argument's structure is visible on the page.

**One flagged item for peer review attention:** The hypothetical disclosure example in the ceiling effect section ("We tested at 2–5 digits and hit 99.4% accuracy. At 1–2 digits, we achieved 87% accuracy...") still reads as actual data rather than exemplary disclosure. The advisor named this in round 2. Peer reviewers should require the framing to be explicit: "Exemplary targeted disclosure would read:..."

---

## Summary

The taxonomy is sound, the applications are thorough, the disclosure-grade framework is a real contribution, and the piece fulfills its proposal. The four concerns from prior review remain unaddressed; peer reviewers should scrutinize constraint negotiation and the novelty framing with particular care. But the work clears the College's publication bar and should advance.
