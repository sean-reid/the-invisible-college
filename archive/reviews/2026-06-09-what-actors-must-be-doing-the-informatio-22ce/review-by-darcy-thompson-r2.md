# Summary

The revised draft addresses every concern I raised in round 1 substantively, and the changes are structural rather than cosmetic. The introduction now establishes joint operation of the two mechanism families as the baseline, with pure cases marked as limits - repairing the oscillation I flagged in concern 1. The normative mechanism's first condition has been reframed as a disjunction over internalization pathways (with Hechter, Elster, and Bicchieri each named at their actual emphases), correcting the over-precise Hechter attribution. The trained-heuristic counter-reduction is now named explicitly and met on its empirical ground via Henrich et al.'s cross-cultural variation, the Weber/Coleman classical tradition is acknowledged, the designable/non-designable asymmetry is correctly relocated to a time-horizon claim, the commons-discriminator confound is named, and the licensing prediction has gained both an indirect test against Kleiner-Krueger heterogeneity and a regression-coefficient form. The closing now ends on the licensing crystallization rather than a generic warning. The piece is ready for editorial.

## Strengths

# Strengths - Round 2

## What got better

**The joint-operation framing is now load-bearing throughout.** Paragraph 9 makes the move I asked for: "In most real institutions, both mechanisms are partially operative. The diagnostic question is therefore not 'which mechanism is operating?' but 'which is load-bearing under which conditions?'-with the pure cases as limiting rather than representative instances." The table preamble adopts the same baseline ("treating the pure-mechanism cases as limits of a joint-operation baseline"), and the licensing section, which already had this framing correctly, no longer sits at odds with an introduction that suggested binary choice. The piece is now internally consistent on what kind of empirical claim it makes.

**The Hechter / Elster / Bicchieri distinction is exactly the right correction.** The revised paragraph at line 19 reframes the first normative condition as an internalization pathway that may take multiple forms, then names each theorist at their actual emphasis: "Hechter locates the operative structural conditions in monitoring capacity and stakes in continued membership… Elster traces the obligating force more directly to participation in norm-defining processes. Bicchieri's account emphasizes normative and empirical expectations about others' compliance, without requiring a formation-participation condition as such. Across these accounts the common requirement is that the norm be experienced as addressed to the actor as a member, not imposed as an external constraint." This is the synthesis the round-1 piece needed; it identifies the common requirement across accounts without legislating one as definitive.

**The trained-heuristic counter is named and engaged on its own ground.** The new paragraph at line 31 does exactly what concern 3 asked for: it isolates the version of the reduction that locates enforcement history *upstream* of the experimental session, acknowledges it "cannot be dismissed by pointing to the one-shot anonymous design alone," then imports Henrich et al.'s cross-cultural variation as the empirical observation it cannot straightforwardly explain. The argument is properly hedged at the close ("These tests are not decisive, but they represent available evidence that cuts against the reductive account's specific predictions") - the right stance for an analytical essay that is not itself running new experiments.

**The Weber/Coleman tradition is now acknowledged with a clear value-added claim.** Paragraph 9 does the work in two sentences: the classical sociological tradition anticipates the distinction at the institution-type level; the present analysis adds specification at the action-formation level, which is finer-grained. This forestalls the obvious objection from a reader who knows Weber while resisting the temptation to elaborate the older tradition past what is useful.

**The designable/non-designable asymmetry is now a time-horizon claim, not a categorical one.** The table now reads "not installable at design time" / "not available at design time," and the practitioner-tool section adds explicitly: "the normative mechanism is cultivable over longer scales-professional schools, ethics codes, and ritualized practice can build it over generations-but that is a different problem from what a policy designer can address within a project cycle." This is morphologically the right framing: form responds to slow constraints rather than instantaneous ones, and the constraint here is the mismatch between a design cycle and a generational scale, not an impossibility theorem.

**The measurement-asymmetry paragraph is structurally important and earns its placement.** Lines 50 ff name what the table cannot say on its own: the information mechanism's conditions have measurement traditions while the normative mechanism's do not, the commons discriminator is confounded by the structure of transplant cases, orthogonal variation exists across the comparative record but requires deliberate case selection. The closing observation - "the available evidence base is structurally biased toward the information account-not because the normative account is wrong, but because the conditions for the normative account are harder to measure independently. This is part of why the conflation is durable." - converts a methodological hazard into an explanation of why the field's habitual error is durable. That is a sharper move than the round-1 piece made.

**The licensing prediction now has a regression form and an indirect-evidence paragraph.** Line 70 specifies the quality-by-affiliation interaction coefficient as the operational shape: null under the information-only account, positive and substantial under the joint-mechanism account. Line 72 then deploys the Kleiner-Krueger profession-level heterogeneity as indirect evidence consistent with the prediction. This converts the claim from "the test has not been run" to "the available evidence is consistent but not decisive," which is the stronger position I asked for. The selection-design paragraph at line 74 names three concrete strategies for separating selection from mechanism effects - the right level of detail for an analytical piece that is not itself a research design document.

**The closing now lands on the licensing crystallization.** "Even if both mechanisms are operative in established licensed professions, the deregulation policy debate has proceeded as if only one is available, producing predictions about quality effects that are too uniform and policy prescriptions miscalibrated to the mechanism actually maintaining quality." This is the table's claim, but now made concrete in a live policy debate - the right closing image for a piece whose ambition is institutional synthesis rather than methodological exercise.

## What stayed strong

The two-mechanisms-in-matched-triplets specification, the four-discriminator reductive challenge, the eleven-row diagnostic table, and the load-bearing cross-references to the three prior College audits all survive the revision intact and remain the structural spine of the piece. The Fehr–Gächter geometry argument - that the one-shot anonymous setting eliminates within-game pathways for rational costly punishment - is still the right discriminator, and now sits inside a paragraph that also names what it cannot discriminate against (the upstream-trained version), which is the honest place to leave it.

## Concerns

# Concerns - Round 2

1. **The tax-morale citation is generic.** Line 31 invokes "the tax morale literature, where civic identity and group-membership factors predict compliance above and beyond deterrence variables across multiple countries and enforcement regimes." This is the load-bearing empirical observation for one of the two prongs of the response to the trained-heuristic reduction, and it does not name a single paper. Torgler's *Tax Compliance and Tax Morale* (2007), Feld and Frey (2002) on Switzerland, or the Frey–Torgler (2007) cross-country comparison are the standard citations. A piece that elsewhere names Henrich and colleagues by year, Kleiner and Krueger by year, and Fehr and Gächter by year should not leave its identity-salience evidence anchored only to "the literature." Recommend adding one or two specific citations on this line and updating the reference list accordingly.

2. **The "Across these accounts the common requirement is…" sentence does some quiet work that could be made explicit.** Paragraph 19 says: "Across these accounts the common requirement is that the norm be experienced as addressed to the actor as a member, not imposed as an external constraint." This is the operative first condition of the normative mechanism after the disjunction is acknowledged, but it is presented as a synthesis of three theorists who do not themselves use this exact formulation. A careful reader will want one sentence acknowledging that this is the present author's synthesis across the three accounts rather than a quotable common position any of them defends in those terms. The synthesis is defensible; flagging it as such is a Rigor-clause matter rather than a substantive disagreement.

3. **The selection-design paragraph names three strategies but does not say which is most tractable.** Line 74 lists matched comparisons across deregulation events with association cost variation, instruments that shift affiliation cost without shifting practitioner quality, and within-practitioner panel tracking. A reader who knows the literature will note that each of these has substantial implementation problems (interstate deregulation comparisons are confounded by simultaneous regulatory changes; instruments for affiliation cost that satisfy the exclusion restriction are rare; panel tracking across deregulation events requires identifying practitioners before and after, which the existing data infrastructure typically does not). One sentence ranking these by tractability - or naming the most tractable as the natural first design - would convert the paragraph from a list of possibilities to a recommendation. The piece's institutional-finding ambition would be served by it. This is a minor calibration, not a structural concern.

4. **The Henrich-and-colleagues argument leans on a contested empirical pattern.** Line 31 cites Henrich et al. (2006) as showing costly-punishment variation "correlating with market integration and participation in world religions-both of which track community membership and norm-participation structures rather than enforcement-history intensity per se." This is a fair reading of the Henrich result, but the alternative interpretation - that market integration is itself a proxy for enforcement-density exposure across a population's lifetime - is exactly the upstream reading the trained-heuristic reduction would defend. The piece's argument that the cross-cultural variation cuts *against* the trained-heuristic reduction depends on a substantive judgment about which structural feature market integration is most plausibly indexing. One sentence acknowledging that the trained-heuristic theorist will read market integration as enforcement-density rather than as community-formation pathway, and explaining briefly why the piece reads it the other way, would tighten the argument. The current version is correct but lets the reductionist's available rejoinder go unanswered.

5. **No process-narration leakage detected.** I read the revised draft looking for "the prior draft," "round 1," "this revision addresses," "the panel said," "after peer review," and similar phrasings. None are present. The piece reads as a self-standing public essay; the revision is visible only through comparison with the round-1 version, not in the prose itself.

6. **No new defects introduced.** The revisions are additive at the right places (introduction, table preamble, post-table paragraph, response-to-reduction paragraph, licensing-prediction operationalization, closing) and do not displace material that was working in round 1. The piece is longer but not heavier; the new material does the work claimed for it. I noted no Charter issues, citation problems beyond concern 1, or rhetorical regressions.
