# Strengths - Round 2

## What Got Better

**The eight-cell accounting is now complete and arithmetically closed.** The two previously unaccounted cells (MLM-Order and MLM-Family at the base covariate set) are now reported as marginal - excluding zero by between one and two standard errors, neither confidently positive nor confidently null. The full tally (3 confident exclude / 3 include zero / 2 marginal) is given in the text, and the fragility rule fires on the right evidence.

**The asymmetry scope is now properly bounded.** The "What the data are" section explicitly limits the reported analysis to ellipticity, explains that the Stoddard headline lives in the ellipticity result, acknowledges that asymmetry was inspected on the same grid and showed qualitatively similar fragility, and gives the reason for not developing it. The "eight whole-sample cells" phrase is now defined precisely as 4 fitting strategies × 2 covariate sets for ellipticity alone, eliminating the promise-scope mismatch in the prior draft.

**The aerial case subsection is a genuine intellectual improvement, not just a defensive addition.** The prior draft's treatment of the aerial result amounted to: "lifestyle does more work than HWI." The revised version states plainly that among the 111 aerial specialists the HWI slope is the largest and most precisely estimated in the lifestyle-stratified analysis - four times the pooled slope with $R^2 = 0.36$ - and that a reader committed to Stoddard can fairly say the dataset supports the flight-streamlining mechanism in exactly the class where the mechanism was hypothesised to operate. It then names the two interpretations consistent with that observation (genuine flight-streamlining versus pelvic-geometry covariation with HWI inside a narrow body-plan class) and concedes that the data cannot decide between them. The conclusion against the textbook reading - that the slope is not exportable, goes the wrong way in the largest class, and is therefore at best a body-plan force rather than a class-wide flight-streamlining signal - is correspondingly narrower and more defensible. The revision transformed the weakest part of the prior draft into one of the strongest.

**The lifestyle aliasing subsection correctly constrains the inferential reach.** The "what else could lifestyle proxy?" section names diet, nest-site geometry, clutch size, predation pressure on the egg, and incubation regime as the four or five other variables that travel with lifestyle, and then states explicitly that the "lifestyle does the work" claim is robust while the further inference "and the work it does is specifically pelvic" is not yet licensed by this evidence. The disambiguation measurement - species-level pelvic-canal width - is named. This is the right move: the piece does not overclaim what lifestyle explains, and it does not let the Birkhead hypothesis absorb unearned credit just because lifestyle outperforms HWI.

**The sign-test treatment is now epistemically honest.** The within-order section no longer reads the 3-vs-3 directional split as a test. It explicitly names the split as structurally uninformative at $n = 6$ and pivots to the genuine question, which is power. The back-of-envelope SE calculations (worked from the reported $p$-values) and the power estimates for each order are now in the text: essentially complete in Passeriformes, moderate in Charadriiformes, and below 0.20 in the three small orders. The within-order pattern is read as directional evidence about clade heterogeneity rather than as an adjudicated null, which is the epistemically appropriate reading.

**The locked-grid gloss paragraph makes the pre-registration logic accessible to a public reader.** Adding a brief explanation of what the grid is, what the rejection rule is, and why both are committed before fitting means the College's practice does not presuppose institutional familiarity. A reader who has not followed prior pieces can now understand the fragility claim without working back through the methodology section.

**The blind-set framework is integrated correctly.** The apparatus, the alternatives, and the blind set are named in the disclosure idiom established in *What the Apparatus Refuses to See*, with a direct cross-reference. The blind set - lifestyle aliasing pelvic geometry, nesting, and diet together, unable to be unbundled by a categorical descriptor - is correctly identified, and species-level pelvic-canal width is named as the disambiguating measurement. The cross-reference also serves to anchor the fragility claim within a framework the College has already published a standard for.

**The data-access barrier is now reproducible.** The revised text names PubMed Central's proof-of-work challenge as the specific barrier to automated download of the Anten-Houston et al. pelvic-dimension table, and states that manual browser-mediated download was not attempted. A future researcher now knows what kind of barrier they face and what the workaround is. The disclosure standard the College applies to instrument failures is met.

**The LRT model class is now explicit.** All three models (M1/M2/M3) are stated to be OLS fitted by maximum likelihood under a Gaussian error assumption, and the text notes that the MLM specifications appear elsewhere but are not the basis of this model comparison. The ML/REML hazard does not apply, and the reader can verify the LRT degrees of freedom.

**The marginal $R^2$ decomposition replaces the imprecise "most of its explanatory power" claim.** Lifestyle adding 0.042 and HWI adding 0.029 on top of each other - a ratio of roughly 1.4 - is the right unit for the comparison. The prior draft's "most" was an overstatement; "about 1.4 times" is a defensible and precise characterization.

**The closing sentence now qualifies its reach correctly.** "Form, in birds, is bounded by what the body that grew it could produce - at least as much, on the evidence currently available, as by any flight-streamlining pressure on the egg itself" carries the essay's momentum while tethering the conclusion to what this re-analysis can actually support. The revision preserved the rhetorical force while removing the overreach.

## What Stayed Strong

The within-lifestyle sign reversal (insessorial HWI $= -0.00167$, SE $0.00054$, $n = 435$) and the within-Passeriformes coefficient contraction from $+0.00054$ to $+0.00010$ when lifestyle is controlled remain the piece's core empirical contribution. Neither is a model artefact, and the reasoning against the textbook mechanism is correctly indexed to this result.

The order-level residual analysis - Strigiformes 17.2 SE below predicted, Falconiformes 16.4 SE below - continues to be the most structurally damaging finding for the flight-constraint interpretation, and it is still reported correctly: as description, not mechanism, with the four alternatives for interpreting the heteroscedasticity named.

The nested model comparison ($M_3$ preferred to both $M_1$ and $M_2$, with both lifestyle and HWI contributing unique variance) is preserved and correctly framed. Neither variable is allowed to absorb the other.

## Concerns

# Concerns - Round 2

Both remaining concerns are editorial-level. Neither affects the core analysis, the direction of any finding, or the conclusions. They are flagged for editorial cleanup before publication, not as grounds for another revision round.

1. **The locked-grid gloss paragraph states "four covariate-augmentation sets" where the eight-cell arithmetic requires two.** The new paragraph added to explain the locked-grid practice for a public reader (the second paragraph of "What the data are, what the analysis is, what it isn't") states: "I committed in writing to a fixed set of response variables, four fitting strategies, four covariate-augmentation sets, and a rejection rule." Four fitting strategies × four covariate-augmentation sets = 16 cells, not 8. The rest of the section explicitly defines eight whole-sample cells as "4 fitting strategies × 2 covariate sets for the ellipticity response alone." The paragraph should say "two covariate-augmentation sets." This inconsistency was introduced by the revision: the new gloss paragraph appears to reproduce language from the pre-registration that was not updated when the eight-cell math was clarified. A reader trying to verify the arithmetic of the locked grid will encounter 4 × 4 = 16 ≠ 8 before reaching the explicit "4 × 2" definition. Editorial should reconcile the two characterizations.

2. **Jetz et al. (2012) is missing a DOI.** Every other reference in the bibliography includes a DOI; Jetz et al. does not. The paper is "The global diversity of birds in space and time," *Nature* 491: 444–448, and its DOI is 10.1038/nature11631. This is a minor inconsistency but the reference is central to the piece - it is the phylogenetic tree Stoddard et al. used, and the reason the present re-analysis could not use it is that the tree is not available in the analysis environment. A reader tracing that lineage should be able to locate the source without searching by title.

No process-narrative leakage was detected in the revised draft. The cross-reference to the Galileo/Biewener piece ("The same substitution appears in [*Galileo or Biewener? Fitting the Mammalian Femur*], where it is named as a pre-registration deviation and discussed methodologically. The deviation is named here too.") is appropriately written in procedures voice, not response-to-reviewers voice. No other passages read as review-process artifacts.
