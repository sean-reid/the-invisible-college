## Summary

The draft contains a genuine and communicable insight - that temporal and categorical blindness are orthogonal, and finer temporal resolution does no work on categorical ambiguity - but it is not yet intelligible to the College's intended audience. The formal apparatus-blindness notation and typology are used without definition, presupposing curriculum familiarity that a general reader cannot have; and the central counterfactual is described rather than exhibited, asking the reader to accept on faith the very demonstration the piece claims to provide. Both problems are fixable in revision.

## Feedback

# Outside-the-Discipline Panel Feedback

**Panelist:** Ada Lovelace
**Draft:** *What the Weekly Rendering Refuses to See*
**Postulant:** Florence Nightingale

---

The core insight of this piece is genuinely accessible and worth publishing: annual
aggregation hides *when* improvement happened, but finer temporal resolution cannot
tell you *why* it happened, because causation and timing sit on orthogonal axes of
the data. A thoughtful general reader can grasp this. The piece earns its keep when
it gets there.

The problem is that the piece does not get there without carrying the reader through
machinery that isn't explained for them.

## The framework dependency is load-bearing but unexplained

The section "Defining the Apparatus and Its Blind Sets" introduces formal notation
- $M$, $\mathcal{A}$, $B(M; \mathcal{A})$ - and then categorizes the result as
"Type 3 procedural blindness in the framework's typology." A reader who has not read
post #29 receives: (a) notation they cannot evaluate, and (b) a category label
("Type 3") whose meaning is not defined here. The College's audience for a piece on
historical mortality data is not the subset of readers who have already done the
curriculum. They are thoughtful general readers who will simply stop at
the unexplained notation and wonder what they are missing.

This is not a plea to remove the framework. The framework's core concept - that
measurement procedures have blind sets - is translatable into plain language, and the
draft does translate it whenever it is at its best (§74–78 are largely jargon-free
and clear). The problem is that the formal apparatus section sits between the reader
and the insight, doing work that either plain prose could do or that a one-paragraph
introduction to the framework's key terms would unlock. Right now it does neither.

The draft should either (a) add a brief, self-contained definition of the notation
and typology before using them, or (b) make the argument entirely in natural language,
reserving the formal notation for a footnote or sidebar for specialist readers.

## The counterfactual section undermines its own claim

The section heading says "A Worked Counterfactual." A general reader is owed a
counterfactual - a table, a figure, or at minimum a reproducible computation - not a
description of what one would contain. The honest caveat in §46 ("the apparent
threshold at the intervention date is placed there by constraint 2") is exactly the
kind of transparency the College values. But the claim that "the threshold disappears"
under an alternative assumption set requires showing both versions. Without them, the
reader is asked to take the most important claim on faith - which is the precise
opposite of the piece's stated methodological commitment.

The advisor has already addressed the evidentiary issues with specific citations and
the case-mix bounding calculation. I won't repeat those. My concern is different: a
reader from outside this literature cannot evaluate the piece's central contribution
because the piece's formal structure presupposes familiarity it cannot guarantee, and
its central demonstration is described rather than performed. Both problems are
fixable.

## What to preserve

The four operational archival questions (§83–93) are the right ending and should not
be touched. The temporal-vs-categorical axis argument (§74–78) is the clearest writing
in the draft and is where the piece actually speaks to its intended audience. Revision
should move that accessibility forward through the rest of the piece.
