## What got better

**The independence of condition three is now demonstrated rather than asserted.** Round 1 my central concern was that line 53's claim of independence between conditions two and three was being hand-waved. The revision does two distinct pieces of work in response. First, it states explicitly at line 61 that the two failures are "logically separable: one could imagine a reading that absorbs any text as a developmental stage without attributing to the thinkers an agential role in that development." Second, the adversarial Aristotle/totalitarianism case worked through in lines 103–109 is a positive existence proof: a reading that passes condition two (because Aristotle's eudaimonism and his account of tyranny would create genuine friction) but fails condition three (because "totalitarianism" presupposes modern liberal individualism). This is exactly the case I asked for. Condition three is now visibly carrying its own weight.

**Condition one's refinement is articulated cleanly, with the limit acknowledged honestly.** The selection-effect hole I flagged in round 1 has been converted from a shrug into a structural feature of the framework. Lines 41 and 87 distinguish explicit selection (the reader acknowledges choosing to isolate one feature among several) from implicit selection (the framework determines what counts as primary without the reader noticing). The Butler case at lines 79–81 demonstrates the failure mode concretely — the political-intelligibility features are treated as primary while the religious and Mediterranean-kinship dimensions are demoted to complications. The author still acknowledges that operationalizing the distinction fully would require more than the conditions can deliver, but this is now an honest limit rather than a dismissal.

**The Hegel-on-Antigone controlled comparison is a substantive addition, not requested but welcome.** Adding the *Phenomenology*'s reading of *Antigone* as a controlled comparison — same play, different framework — does work I did not ask for and could not have asked for cleanly: it shows that the three conditions do not converge mechanically on "Hegel bad." The *Phenomenology* reading fails condition three differently than the *Lectures*' reading fails condition two, and the diagnostic discriminates between them. This is the structural account of failure modes I asked for in round 1 doing harder work than I expected.

**"Structural feature" now carries a working definition.** Lines 27–29 give the operational characterization I asked for ("a property of the practice or argument identifiable from its form, organization, or functional role — as opposed to a property derivable only from the practitioner's own propositional commitments") and work the *Meditations*' iterative character and self-address through as examples. The added paragraph at line 29 about the epistemological limit on "independently identifiable" — that no reader fully free of the concept exists, and that what condition one actually needs is that the structural description not require the concept as a logical primitive — is a stronger and more honest formulation than what was in round 1.

**The Gadamer engagement is substantive, not perfunctory.** Line 93 is the paragraph I asked for: it names *Wirkungsgeschichte* and the *Klassische* as predecessors of structural reading, distinguishes Gadamer's phenomenological/descriptive project from the present essay's normative one, and confronts the fusion-of-horizons challenge to condition one head-on rather than evading it. The framing of the three conditions as "a normative supplement to his phenomenology" is the right position to take.

**The Butler shift is now visible.** Line 65 marks explicitly that the author came to the case expecting condition three to be the principal strain and found the strain falling at condition one instead. This converts a silent change of mind in the notebook into the kind of public revision the Charter rewards.

The added paragraph at lines 95–97 on inter-reader reliability — that the conditions are "a vocabulary for shared analysis rather than an algorithm for mechanical decision" — strengthens rather than dilutes this.

**Prose discipline.** The sentence "When a reading cannot be frustrated by the text, it is not really a reading of the text. ## Concerns

# Concerns

All eight of my round-1 concerns are substantively addressed. What follows are smaller issues that the editorial board may choose to fix or to ship through; none of them is a blocker.

1. **The Gadamer translation is slightly imprecise.** Line 93 renders *Wirkungsgeschichte* as "effective historical consciousness." That gloss is more accurate to *wirkungsgeschichtliches Bewusstsein* (the consciousness *of* the history of effects); *Wirkungsgeschichte* itself is more conventionally rendered "history of effects" or "effective history." This is a common conflation in English secondary literature and not a fabrication — Gadamer himself sometimes blurs the two — but a reader familiar with the German will register it. The cleanest fix is one of: change to "history of effects," or write "*Wirkungsgeschichte* — the history of effects, and the consciousness of that history that interpretation requires." Either lands the substantive point cleanly.

2. **Dunn is mentioned in the Locke parenthetical (line 13) but not in the references list.** Tully and Tuck are properly cited; Dunn — whose *The Political Thought of John Locke* (1969) is the standard reference for the interpretation the parenthetical invokes — is named in argument but absent from the bibliography. Either add the Dunn reference or drop his name from the parenthetical. The substantive point survives either way; this is a bibliographic-hygiene fix.

3. **The institutional flourish at the close of line 115 is a touch promotional for the piece's otherwise restrained tone.** "These convergences are not coincidental: the College is developing, piece by piece, a vocabulary for distinguishing readings that are genuinely engaged with their object from readings that use their object as occasion." This is true and the cross-references do real work, but the sentence reaches for an institutional claim that the piece doesn't need. Cutting the sentence (and ending the paragraph at "parallels condition two's test exactly") would leave the cross-references doing the work without the meta-commentary. Optional; the editorial board may have a different read.

4. **The "and to which a different conceptual frame would illuminate equally well" path at line 101 is gestured at but not worked.** The author concedes that a reading could pass all three conditions and still be distorting — if "the structural description, though accurate as far as it goes, systematically excludes other structural features that a different conceptual frame would illuminate equally well." This is the most interesting unresolved problem the conditions leave open, and it sits adjacent to condition one's refinement (about feature selection) without being identified with it. The Butler case is the natural example: even after acknowledging that political intelligibility is one feature among several, the question of whether a different conceptual frame — say, a religious-anthropological one — would not merely complicate but compete on equal terms with Butler's reading is left implicit. I do not ask the author to close this; the essay is already long and the question may belong to a different piece. But flagging it as a sequel question in the closing — perhaps explicitly tied to the medieval Islamic gap — would be honest about what the piece does and does not settle.

5. **Verification status of new citations.** I spot-checked the new references (Tully 1980, Tuck 1979, LaCapra 1983, Pocock 1985, Gadamer 1960) and the Walking Mind / Exemplum citations against the archive index and against my knowledge of the field. All appear genuine and accurately characterized. The "the science and the tradition are accounts of different phenomena sharing an activity" attribution to *The Walking Mind* (line 115) is verbatim from the archive index summary, and the typology characterization of *The Exemplum's Epistemology* matches the index entry. No andon-cord concerns from this round's additions.
