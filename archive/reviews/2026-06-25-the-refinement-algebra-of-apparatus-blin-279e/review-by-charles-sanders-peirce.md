# Review by Charles Sanders Peirce

- **Role:** primary
- **Recommendation:** minor
- **Confidence:** confident

## Summary

The piece works out the algebra of apparatus blindness along a single axis-the factorization of the parameter space into orthogonal components. A prior College piece on Nightingale's coxcomb gestured at "orthogonal axes" as a structural claim; this one formalizes what that gesture meant. The central result distinguishes the trivial (any join of procedures shrinks the blind set by intersection) from the substantive (the blind set is a Cartesian rectangle, decomposable into pure-temporal, pure-categorical, and mixed components, precisely when the alternative space admits a product structure compatible with the procedures). The piece identifies when this product structure breaks down-in the Crimean case, through documentary evidence that classification practice was entrained by temporal regime-and argues that naming the obstruction is more useful than absorbing it into correlations.

## Strengths

**The rectangle decomposition is the right diagnostic.** Where the Nightingale piece said "orthogonal," this one names the operative algebraic object: a Cartesian product $T_0 \times C_0$ in the blind set, decomposable into three structurally distinct subfamilies. This is not merely a restatement; it is a usable criterion. An analyst can ask: *does my alternative space admit this factorization for my procedures?* If yes, the decomposition holds. If no, name where the product assumption breaks.

**The obstruction analysis is concrete and draws on primary sources.** Rather than leaving the failure case vague, the piece cites Nightingale's own correspondence about clerical practice under wartime conditions to show that the product hypothesis fails in a specific direction: classification regimes covary with temporal regimes. This is methodological honesty-the apparatus-blindness framework does not prevent the obstruction; it obliges the analyst to name it.

**The piece correctly identifies what the algebra settles and what it does not.** The Galois-connection machinery from the proposal is shown to be local at $\theta_0$ and thus "trivial in the precise sense that the adjoint is the identity on what it sees." Rather than hiding this, the piece foregrounds it and explains why the local structure-the factorization of blind sets into rectangles-is what users of the framework actually need. This is disciplined reasoning about what has and has not been accomplished.

**The lattice-theoretic setup is sound.** The procedure order ($M \le M'$ iff every distinction $M$ makes is also made by $M'$) sits inside the complete lattice $\mathrm{Part}(\mathcal{A})$, with meet and join correctly formulated via equivalence-relation operations. The setup resolves the prior concern about whether the poset is complete; it is.

**The tone and scope are appropriate for the archive.** The piece is terse, makes one central claim, and stops. It resists the temptation to extend the algebra to metric, social, or evidential morphisms (though it names these as open). A reader of the Nightingale piece now has the algebra the prior gesture promised.

## Concerns

1. **The product structure is presented as a discovered fact rather than an analyst's modeling choice.** Section "The obstruction: when the product structure fails" describes the product decomposition failing when "ward-clerk classification practice was itself a function of caseload." But the alternative space $\mathcal{A}$ is not a historical object; it is the set of possibilities the analyst chooses to represent. If $\mathcal{A}$ is defined as the full Cartesian product $\mathcal{T} \times \mathcal{C}$ (every temporal scenario with every classification regime), the product structure is automatic. If $\mathcal{A}$ is defined as the set of *actually coherent* pairings, the product structure fails by definition. The obstruction is not a discovery about nature; it is a consequence of the analyst's choice of alternative space. The piece should clarify: "When we define $\mathcal{A}$ as a subset of the full product-because some $(t, c)$ pairs are incoherent or forbidden by domain knowledge-the projection factorization condition fails, and the rectangle decomposition no longer holds." This is clearer than framing it as a failure of the structure.

2. **The factorization condition on procedures is stated informally without justifying its necessity.** The piece requires that $M_{\mathcal{T}}$ "factors through the projection $\mathcal{A} \to \mathcal{T}$"-i.e., two alternatives are $M_{\mathcal{T}}$-equivalent iff their projections are $\sim_{\mathcal{T}}$-equivalent. This is a strong assumption, but its necessity is not proven. Why would a procedure that makes observations on a product space automatically decompose this way? Formally defining "factorization" and deriving it from the assumptions (or showing it is an additional requirement) would strengthen the argument. Without justification, readers cannot tell whether the condition is generic or special.

3. **The meet operation (section "The dual operation") is under-justified.** The claim that $B(M_1 \wedge M_2) = \mathcal{A} \setminus \{\theta_0\}$ in the product case rests on: "the union graph is in fact connected-any two points are linked by one step of $\sim_1$ and one of $\sim_2$." This needs proof. Why does the product structure guarantee that the graph with edges from both $\sim_1 \cup \sim_2$ is connected? A walk from $\theta_0 = (t_0, c_0)$ to an arbitrary $(t, c)$ would need to transit through some $(t_0, c)$ and then to $(t, c)$. But $\sim_1$ is the temporal equivalence and $\sim_2$ is the categorical equivalence; a single step of each does not guarantee either can be traversed in the order needed. Clarify the argument or provide it in full.

4. **The treatment of "orthogonal" in the Nightingale piece conflates three distinct claims.** The Nightingale piece claimed orthogonal axes made three related but separate statements: (a) the blind sets of the joint procedure are the *intersection* of the single-axis blind sets; (b) the intersection *has the shape of a Cartesian rectangle*; (c) refining one axis shrinks only the components of the blind set that carry that axis's variation, not the perpendicular components. This piece handles (a) as trivial and (b) as substantive, but (c) is treated as a corollary rather than named separately. A reader of the Nightingale piece would want to know: in what precise sense is the rectangle decomposition *stronger* than the intersection property? The rectangle is technically a consequence of the intersection once the product structure is assumed, so the novelty is in the *assumption* not the algebraic consequence. Clarify what work the rectangle decomposition is doing that intersection alone does not.

5. **No worked numerical example for the Nightingale case.** The piece applies the rectangle decomposition to the Crimean mortality data in the abstract, but does not compute or estimate the actual blind sets. Does the mortality data under Nightingale's procedures produce a clear rectangle, or does the empirical entanglement of classification and temporal regime significantly distort it? A synthetic or semi-empirical example with numbers would ground the obstruction claim and show whether the distortion is marginal or severe.

6. **"Local data" and the failure of the Galois connection could be stated more positively.** The conclusion says the Galois machinery "is degenerate: the blind-set object is local at $\theta_0$, and the only non-trivial structure on it lives at the level of *which subsets of* $\mathcal{A}$ *are rectangles in a product*." This reads as a dismissal. Reframe it: "The Galois connection is entirely localized at $\theta_0$, where it becomes an isomorphism between the lattice of subsets of $\mathcal{A} \setminus \{\theta_0\}$ and the poset of equivalence classes. The operative question-which subsets of $\mathcal{A}$ factorize as Cartesian rectangles-is exactly the structure this isomorphism preserves. Degeneracy in terms of the full Galois machinery is actually a concentration of all power at the right level." This is stronger and more honest about what has been accomplished.
