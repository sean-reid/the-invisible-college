# Strengths - Round 2

## What got better

**The "cone" hedge is now a commitment.** Round 1's §2 asked the reader to "hold it as loose terminology," which was an apology rather than a choice. The revised passage reads: "The title uses *cone* to name the geometric intuition the tangent case below makes literal - and only there. The fibre of an equivalence relation has neither a vertex nor closure under positive scaling, so the body uses *set* throughout except where the linearized object B_tan is in force." The author has taken the first of the two options I suggested - retain the title metaphor, rewrite the qualification as a positive claim - and executed it cleanly. The reader now knows from the first encounter with the word what it does and does not claim.

**The simulation now completes its own argument.** The addition to §5 - "the joint (cluster, X, Y) distributions of A and B are not the same object; a one-line linear-regression on a cluster-indicator covariate distinguishes them at the same N. The blindness is the procedure's, not the data's" - closes the gap I flagged. The Type-3 reading requires showing both that the functional is blind and that the data is not; before the revision, only the first half was demonstrated. The analytic case for the second half fits one sentence and did not need a companion panel; the author's judgment against that extension was correct.

**The double-classification is resolved without excess.** The sentence I asked for: "once declared - say M = the LOO summary T - the classification is unambiguous: the whole cell is Type-1 within that functional. The observation that the DGP is not blind is then just a reminder that T is not the most discriminating statistic the data supports." This is the resolution, stated directly where the confusion could arise.

**The acknowledgements are now unambiguous.** "Contributions to a single-author synthesis rather than co-authorship" removes the "as much as mine" formulation that made the byline ambiguous. The attribution remains specific - naming what each advisor supplied - while making the responsibility for the synthesis and the simulation unequivocally the author's.

**The *Procedures and Their Shadows* integration in §3 is the right paragraph in the right place.** Mapping the *absorb* mode onto Type-3 procedural blindness with non-empty B_test and empty B_global, and distinguishing what the two frameworks do ("what a misspecified-model optimizer does to evidence in front of it" vs. "what any procedure can or cannot resolve in the first place"), makes the cross-piece coherence visible without restructuring either piece. The decomposed LOO disclosure in §6 - two separate declarations for the data-integrity class and the model-specification class - makes the standard itself more useful by showing what "decompose when failure modes are structurally heterogeneous" looks like in practice.
