## Strengths

# Strengths - Round 2

## What got better

**The information-selection sub-mechanisms are now rule-separated.** Entry screening and consumer signaling now appear as formally distinct processes with distinct operative conditions (a–b for screening, c–d for signaling) and failure modes that the round-1 draft conflated. The key addition - that when screening has already collapsed via near-universal pass rates while signaling persists, deregulation removes the consumer-information function rather than average pool quality - is exactly the precision the typology required. The prediction is preserved but now supported by a derivation.

**Operative condition (b) converts a hidden meta-rule into a named precondition.** The round-1 draft's deregulation prediction rested on the tacit assumption that mandatory training is the *necessary* vehicle for professional socialization. The revision states condition (b) explicitly - mandatory training must constitute the *dominant* socialization pathway - and names the consequence for the timing prediction if alternative pathways persist. A tacit *paribhāṣā* that shaped the prediction invisibly is now stated as a checkable condition.

**The formal expression earns its entry.** $[t_0,\; t_0 + L]$ with midpoint near $t_0 + L/2$ converts the "fifteen to twenty-five years" claim from qualitative comparison into a prediction with explicit parameter dependence. The expression is brief enough to be Pāṇinian and precise enough to be tested.

**The within-career drift variant is cleanly separated.** The durable-socialization form (flat quality through each incumbent's career, then turnover-driven decline) and the drift variant (gradual within-period erosion before cohort replacement) now have distinct temporal signatures and the same tests identify them without a dedicated fourth test. This is the right structural move: the discrimination comes from the pattern of change, not from a new instrument.

**The measurement-apparatus confound is now named and grounded.** The "On measurement-apparatus changes" subsection in Test 1 applies the blind-cone formalism to a specific instantiation: deregulation removing the inspection infrastructure that generated the measured outcome. The citation of the prior apparatus-blindness and composition-rule work is earned, not decorative - it anchors a domain-specific observation in a general College result.

**The introduction no longer overclaims.** "The tests are specifiable at this level, requiring data and study horizons existing deregulation studies do not provide" is the accurate description of what a design-level piece can deliver. The prior claim that the tests were *feasible* was in tension with Test 2's acknowledgment that individual-level data linked to credential timing does not currently exist in any regulatory system; that tension is resolved.

## What stayed strong

The audit table remains the piece's most efficient diagnostic instrument. Five texts, three columns, one visible pattern. The sociology of professions section continues to do structural work rather than ornamental citation - Larson, Freidson, and Abbott are placed in relationship to each other and to the economics literature's selective reading, not merely listed. The discrimination tests are still operationalized honestly: each names the data required, the observable implication under each mechanism, and the current-status assessment that separates analysis constraints from data-collection constraints.

## Concerns

# Concerns - Round 2

Both items below are organizational, not substantive. Neither blocks acceptance.

1. **The Abbott scope disclosure and the mechanism-space scope statement are separated without a connecting reference.** The Abbott bounding - "the discrimination tests are designed to distinguish information-selection from the identity-normative family broadly construed...rather than to disaggregate that family internally" - appears at the end of the sociology section. The separate declaration that the tests do not address reputation effects, liability, and third-party credentialing appears in the "On the scope of these tests" subsection after Test 3. A reader who encounters Test 3's observable implication under identity-socialization ("practitioners affiliated with such associations should maintain quality after deregulation") without the sociology section's Abbott caveat in active memory may read the test as discriminating between Freidson and information-selection specifically, rather than between the identity-normative family broadly and information-selection. A single clause connecting the two scope statements - "as bounded in the sociology section above, these tests do not disaggregate the identity-normative family internally" - at the opening of the discrimination tests section would close the gap. The current text is accurate; it is not maximally navigable.

2. **The "On the scope of these tests" paragraph slightly understates what the tests accomplish.** The paragraph concludes: "A complete mechanism discrimination would require tests targeting these alternatives." This is correct, but "complete mechanism discrimination" sets a standard the tests were never intended to meet and that no three-test design could. The tests do accomplish something specific and nontrivial: they isolate the information-normative split, which is the piece's declared contribution. A more precise formulation - "a complete discrimination across the full space of quality-maintenance mechanisms would require tests targeting these alternatives" - would avoid implying the tests are incomplete relative to a standard they never claimed to meet, while still correctly marking the boundary of the contribution.
