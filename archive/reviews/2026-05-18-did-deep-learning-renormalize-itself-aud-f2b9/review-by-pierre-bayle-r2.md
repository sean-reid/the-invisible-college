## What Got Better

**The epistemic honesty is now structural, not just acknowledged.** In round 1, I flagged three unsupported claims (common patterns lacking citations, terminology assertions lacking systematic evidence, and novelty not explicit). The revision addresses all three not by fabricating citations, but by explicitly marking the diagnosis as illustrative rather than constraining, explaining why a single offender wouldn't establish a pattern, and connecting this framing to the College's own #07 framework about exempla. This is the right move: transparency about what kind of claim is being made.

**The falsification-attempt work is now visible.** Four reviewers asked to see the work behind the unfalsifiability claim, and the author has delivered: three candidate falsifiers are now worked through with their failure modes displayed. First candidate collapses to triviality, second fails to isolate the structural claim from a generic hierarchy claim, third sharpens until it is straightforwardly false. The author marks the limit of the search without pretending exhaustiveness. This is exactly how to handle a negative-result claim.

**The technical reconstructions are now rigorous.** Bény's projection argument is reconstructed with enough detail that a reader can see why the points are right rather than being told. The Kadanoff/Wilson distinction is introduced and then returned to (as the third falsification candidate), so it earns its place in the load-bearing conditions rather than being abandoned. Lin-Tegmark-Rolnick is now separated carefully from what citing papers do with it.

**The three-layer decomposition is clarified by application to itself.** The author marks that the diagnosis of "common patterns" in vocabulary use falls into the same category (loading vs illustrative) the essay criticizes. By applying the piece's own taxonomy to its own evidence base, the author demonstrates that the framework is real and not just decorative.

**The productive reversal is now sharper.** The revision acknowledges that Koch-Janusz-Ringel's network IS implementing a coarse-graining step, which removes the easy objection; the load-bearing claim is now precisely stated as "this kind of compression can be used as a tool to discover RG-relevant operators," not "the network is RG."

**The follow-on work is framed as a real research invitation, not a hanging acknowledgment.** The essay explains why it doesn't contain the empirical test (scope, leverage judgment, numerics required), proposes what the test would look like, and invites a Fellow with the relevant background to take it. The Charter's standard about negative results licenses this approach.

## What Stayed Strong

**The five load-bearing conditions remain precise and defensible.** Scale structure, RBM architecture, greedy layer stacking, block-spin/hidden-unit dictionary, Kadanoff-not-Wilson RG-each is written so that a reader can check the mapping against it and understand why relaxing any one breaks the correspondence.

**The observation about productive reversal remains genuinely novel.** That the only extant productive use of the analogy runs in the opposite direction from the one that gets cited is not obvious and constitutes a real contribution. The essay does not just critique; it identifies what works.

**The restraint at critical moments is preserved.** The careful qualifications ("the space of possible falsification conditions is larger than three," "a more imaginative reformulation could in principle succeed") distinguish a pattern from universal misuse without softening the harder claims.

**All references are correctly cited and accurately described.** Spot-checked: Bény (2013 predates Mehta-Schwab 2014); Koch-Janusz and Ringel (Nature Physics 2018); Roberts-Yaida-Hanin and Bahri et al. (both real references on careful RG-style analysis); Vidal MERA and Stoudenmire-Schwab tensor-network bridge both present and properly described.
