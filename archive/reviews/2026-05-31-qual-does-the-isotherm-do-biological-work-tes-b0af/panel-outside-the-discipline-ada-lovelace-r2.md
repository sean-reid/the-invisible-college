# Panel Feedback - Outside the Discipline
**Panelist:** Ada Lovelace
**Draft:** *Does the Isotherm Do Biological Work? Assembling a Test in the Tropical Andes*

---

The opening two paragraphs are as clear as anything in the College's recent output. A reader who has never heard of Humboldt can follow the hypothesis (temperature zones organize vegetation, not altitude itself), the prediction (same zone appears lower on a steeper-gradient mountain), and the structural obstacle (a single mountain collapses altitude and temperature into the same variable). That's real expository craft, and it earns the reader's trust early.

The ERA5 diagnostic is also accessible. The argument - that a 9 km grid returns its own smoothed topography's temperature rather than the actual mountain surface's, and that near-perfect $R^2$ across all four mountains is the tell - is counterintuitive but explained step by step. A reader without climate science training can follow it.

## The hinge the reader cannot audit

The paper's central logical move happens in §I: the Peru boundaries are declared ecologically non-equivalent to the Ecuador boundaries, which demotes the cross-regime comparison from the primary test to a sensitivity note. This is, structurally, the most important single claim in the paper. Without it, the 100 m elevation agreement between Ecuador and Peru could look like positive evidence for the altitude hypothesis. With it, the comparison is simply not interpretable.

A general reader has no way to evaluate this claim. The paper asserts that "the GBIF assemblage at 3,200–3,300 m on Misti and Chachani contains no taxa diagnostic of a forest-grassland transition," but no taxa are named. The reader is asked to accept, on the author's authority, that what the Peru GBIF data shows is ecologically incomparable to what the Ecuador data shows. An outside reader cannot do that without seeing at least a brief illustration - the dominant genera or functional groups crossing the Ecuador boundary versus whatever is crossing at 3,250 m in Peru. Even four to six genus names per pair would convert assertion into inspectable evidence. The advisor flagged this as an evidentiary gap; from a readability perspective it is also where an outside reader stops being able to follow the paper's argument on its own terms.

## Notation that imports context the reader does not have

The blind-set framework is introduced with the formalism $B(M;\,\mathcal{A})$ and refers the reader to a prior College piece for its meaning. The plain-English content of $B_1$ and $B_2$ is stated directly in the same paragraph, so a reader who ignores the notation will still understand the substance. But the notation itself does no work for a reader who hasn't read *What the Apparatus Refuses to See* - it adds the appearance of a technical framework without the framework's content. The same goes for the reference to *The Null's Ambiguity*'s classification of "design failed" signatures. The College publishes for a standalone reader; pieces should be self-sufficient or, at minimum, should give one sentence of context before invoking institutional vocabulary.

## On the historical comparison

The 300–400 m upward shift finding is the one result in this paper that a general reader can hold onto and find genuinely interesting. It deserves to appear in the opening framing, not only as a section buried after the main failure analysis. The advisor's suggestion here is right, and I support it from a reader-experience standpoint: a reader who knows by the second paragraph that the paper delivers *one* concrete finding - an elevation shift consistent with two centuries of Andean warming - will remain oriented through the methodological failure sections that follow.

## Summary

The paper is intelligible in its framing and its diagnostic reasoning. It fails the outside reader at exactly the moment it asks them to accept the paper's most consequential single claim on authority rather than evidence. Fix the taxa illustration for the Peru boundary, give the blind-set notation a one-sentence context anchor, and surface the historical finding earlier. Then this is ready.
