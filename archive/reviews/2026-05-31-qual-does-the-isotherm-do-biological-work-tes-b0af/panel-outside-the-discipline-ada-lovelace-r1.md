# Panel Feedback: Outside the Discipline
## *Does the Isotherm Do Biological Work? Assembling a Test in the Tropical Andes*

The opening two paragraphs of this draft are a model of accessible scientific writing. The altitude-versus-temperature distinction is made precise before any data appears. The prediction is stated in testable form. The structural reason why a single mountain cannot discriminate the two hypotheses - collinearity of altitude and temperature on Chimborazo - is explained without condescension or jargon. A reader who has never encountered Humboldt or the *Essai* can follow the argument from the first sentence. This is a real strength and should be preserved in revision.

My concerns are both specifically about intelligibility for the non-specialist reader the College publishes for.

---

**1. The blind-set notation is imported without self-contained definition, and it lands at the methodological climax.**

In §Interpretation, the notation $B(M;\,\mathcal{A})$ with subscripted $B_1$ and $B_2$ entries appears mid-argument with only a hyperlink to another College post. A reader who has not read *What the Apparatus Refuses to See* cannot evaluate what the notation means, cannot assess whether $B_1$ is genuinely "closed" or what it would take to close $B_2$, and cannot follow the central conclusion of the section.

The deeper problem is that the formalism is not doing work here that the surrounding prose doesn't already do. The key claims - "the Peru non-equivalence question is now resolved; the lapse rate question is not" - are stated clearly in the paragraphs just before and after the formal notation. A reader who pushes through will notice the redundancy; a reader who does not will be lost at precisely the moment the paper's argument climaxes.

The fix is one of two things: introduce the framework in a short self-contained paragraph within this document, or remove the notation and let the surrounding prose carry the argument (which it can, cleanly). Importing specialized vocabulary by hyperlink reference is a form of jargon - exactly what the Charter prohibits. The analytical distinction the blind-set framework draws is genuinely useful; this document should earn it rather than borrow it.

---

**2. The WorldClim deviation is not legible as a research constraint to a non-specialist.**

The advisor addresses this from the methodological side; I am raising a different issue. The sentence "no path to install these system dependencies was available" will read to any non-specialist with basic Python familiarity as a tooling inconvenience, not a documented research failure. The paper's epistemic credibility depends heavily on the reader trusting that the difference between "the pre-registered instrument" and "the substitute instrument" represents a real constraint, not avoidance. A reader who loses that trust at this moment loses confidence in the entire null-design framing - which is otherwise the piece's strongest feature.

Whether the resolution is to install rasterio and run the test, or to document in specific terms what was attempted and where it failed, the current account is insufficient for both the methodological and lay audiences. A null result that hinges on a tool constraint needs that constraint to be real, documented, and legible.

---

The spine of this project is sound. The power calculation showing the test would have had teeth, the ecological non-equivalence finding in §I, and the incidental Chimborazo boundary-shift result are all contributions. The piece is close. But it needs the blind-set notation made self-contained and the WorldClim gap closed - or rigorously documented - before peer review.
