## What Stayed Strong

**The mathematical core remains rigorous and reproducible.** The derivation of the 2.63 exponent, the algebra that shows how hypermetric tracheal scaling changes the system's elasticity by more than five-fold, and the Saltelli variance decomposition at 200,000 samples all carry through unchanged. This is the right move: the computation was sound in round 1 and did not need revision.

**The three-scenario framework continues to structure the argument clearly.** The table now carries explicit assumption columns that make the progression from S1 to S3 self-contained. A reader who reads only the table caption and the body text can see exactly what varies and what does not, without needing to thread through surrounding prose.

**The variance decomposition result strengthens rather than weakens under the revision.** The more conservative framing-that S3 "brackets *Meganeuropsis* under generous assumptions and falls short under taxon-consistent ones"-makes the finding that the Kaiser exponent contributes 74% of variance even more rhetorically interesting. If the model cannot reliably predict size even under the author's own favorable parameterization, the fact that the geochemistry contributes only 16% becomes a sharper indictment of the textbook story's emphasis.

## What Got Better

**Kaiser et al.'s sample-size and taxon-mismatch limitations are now stated with appropriate prominence.** The "What Kaiser measured" section now opens with bold-equivalent emphasis on four scarabaeoid beetles and explicitly names the problem: "Four specimens from a single coleopteran lineage is a thin foundation for a coefficient that, as the variance decomposition below will show, drives most of the model's prediction." The same section immediately follows with a paragraph that names the Coleoptera-to-Odonata taxon mismatch and explains what the Monte Carlo cannot capture (the possibility that the mean itself is wrong for the relevant clade). This is the load-bearing acknowledgment the piece needed.

**The cylindrical geometry idealizations are now explained, not just admitted.** A new paragraph walks through what is not captured (non-uniform tracheal density, concentrated demand in flight muscle, convective ventilation) and then explains why proceeding is justified: the argument rests on the *ratio* of predictions, and most idealizations cancel in the ratio across epochs. This is more intellectually honest than simply asserting the result is robust.

**Scope conditions are now stated explicitly in the introduction.** The piece now contains: "The analysis throughout assumes the Krogh diffusion ceiling is the binding constraint on insect body size. Cuticular structural support, flight power, water-loss limits, and developmental constraints on molting are all candidate ceilings as well... The question this piece can answer is conditional: *given* that the diffusion limit is the relevant ceiling, what does it actually predict?" This flags the scope rather than leaving it implicit.

**The Sobol input-range justifications are now transparent.** σ_k = 0.05 is now named as a modeling choice, explicitly compared to Kaiser's regression interval (moderately tighter), and robustness checks are reported. The Berner peak window is now directly specified ("a central GEOCARBSULF estimate for the late Carboniferous, with the Lenton, Daines, and Mills (2018) COPSE-reloaded reconstruction giving comparable peaks"). The opacity is gone.

**Measurement uncertainty on the fossil is now quantified.** The Meganeuropsis body-length figure now carries an explicit caveat: "The body-length figure carries an uncertainty of perhaps ±5 cm depending on which extant analogue is used for the wing-to-body ratio." This is the honest practice the rest of the piece demands.

**The modern anchor sensitivity is now fully transparent.** Table 2 shows predicted body lengths under three defensible anchor choices, and the text explicitly states: "Under the taxon-consistent odonate anchor, even the corrected form falls short by roughly a quarter." The bracketing claim is now conditional and legible.

**The conclusion's speculation on determinants of k is appropriate and useful.** Three candidate mechanisms (developmental, phylogenetic, functional) are now named with brief sketches of what each would predict. This gives the reader a sense of what distinguishes them and what empirical measurement would be needed to settle the question.
