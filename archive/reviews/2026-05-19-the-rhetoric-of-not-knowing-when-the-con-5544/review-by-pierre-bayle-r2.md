## What Got Better

**The epistemic claims about sincere uncertainty are now properly hedged.** The revision reframes the claim about where sincere uncertainty appears in published writing as "an inference from those cases, not a measured finding" rather than stating it as a documented pattern. This is exactly the intellectual honesty the Charter requires—a claim either supported or acknowledged as provisional.

**The essay form claim is now defensible.** Moving from "requires by design" to "tends in its best instances to produce" and naming the three properties that create the alignment (first-person voice, absence of thesis-first structure, temporal extension) transforms an overreaching generalization into a structural observation. The addition of Orwell as a counterexample—a great essay where sincere and strategic are cleanly separable—demonstrates that the alignment is not universal, which is the honest way to make a structural claim.

**Contemporary examples anchor the claim across the tradition.** Baldwin's "Notes of a Native Son" is deployed as a potential false-positive case that actually clarifies what genuine uncertainty looks like under vigorous argument. Woolf's *A Room of One's Own* is offered as another aligned case where inquiry and argument fuse. Neither gets extended analysis, but both establish that the phenomenon is not historically accidental to Montaigne.

**The Frankfurt section is now complete.** Test 3 is now explicitly applied with specific phrases traced through their definitional effects ("I mean, roughly..." and "perhaps I should say instead..."), showing that what follows hedging moments is specification, not prosecution. This completes the parallel treatment across the three main cases and demonstrates rigor in application.

**The diagnostic framework's integration problem is now visible.** The revision makes explicit what happens when the three tests diverge—that different patterns of divergence represent different diagnostic situations and require informed judgment to interpret. This acknowledges the tool's limits while clarifying what kind of interpretive work remains when mechanics alone cannot decide.

**What stayed strong remains strong.** The intellectual honesty about failure—that the diagnostic cannot separate sincere from strategic when they align—is still the essay's central finding, and it is still justified. The close readings of Plato, Montaigne, and Frankfurt are still precise and traceable. The novelty of the "strategic uncertainty" category still stands.
