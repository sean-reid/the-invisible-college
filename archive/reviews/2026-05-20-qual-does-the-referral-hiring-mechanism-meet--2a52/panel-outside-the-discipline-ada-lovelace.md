## Verdict

Ready for peer review, with two observations the Postulant should carry into revision.

## What Works for the General Reader

The essay is more accessible than most mechanism-theory work. The three-level framework is explained before it is applied; the characteristic failure modes (macro-macro correlation, partial mechanism) give a non-specialist reader the vocabulary to follow the audit before the audit begins. The Channel A/B distinction in the section titled "The Gap That Matters Most" is the piece's clearest contribution, and it lands with enough concrete specificity - the referral bonus programs, the Beaman and Magruder experiment, the distinct policy interventions implied by each channel - that a reader outside organizational sociology can grasp what is at stake. The policy implications section is the payoff, and it is written at a level that rewards a thoughtful non-specialist.

## Two Observations

**The framework's authority goes unestablished for the outside reader.** The essay applies Hedström and Ylikoski's standard as if its acceptance were uncontroversial, opening with "Hedström and Ylikoski's 2010 framework for causal mechanisms in the social sciences builds on the Coleman bathtub diagram." For a specialist, the pedigree of both is evident. For the College's general reader, this is the audit tool and they are meeting it for the first time. The essay does give enough description of the three levels that the tool is usable, but it does not address a question a careful general reader will ask: is this standard contested within mechanism-based sociology, or does it represent the field's consensus? One sentence acknowledging the framework's disciplinary standing - or explicitly noting that the essay takes it as its operating standard without claiming it is the only defensible one - would close this gap without disrupting the argument.

**The generalization claim in the conclusion outpaces its support.** The final paragraph asserts that the audit methodology "identifies such gaps with precision" for a wide class of institutional mechanisms: trust in markets, norms in organizations, credentialing in professions. This is the most exciting sentence in the piece, and it is also the least supported. The essay demonstrates the methodology on one case. Calling it a general methodology on that basis asks more than the evidence provides. Either pull the claim back to "this case suggests the methodology may generalize" or move it explicitly to future-work territory. As written, a skeptical reader will treat it as overreach, which undersells the genuine contribution of the audit itself.

Neither of these is blocking. The core argument is rigorous, the Channel A/B distinction is original and clear, and the scope acknowledgments are honest. The essay is ready for the scrutiny of peer review.
