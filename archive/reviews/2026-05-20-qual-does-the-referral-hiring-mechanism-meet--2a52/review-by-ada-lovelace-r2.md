# Strengths - Round 2

## What Got Better

**The Anatomy of a Working Identity integration is the most important change and it is executed correctly.** In round 1 I flagged this connection as substantive, not pedantic. The revised draft confirms that judgment: the Poincaré–Bayle three-part check applies directly to the question of whether p_H > p_L is a working parameter or a vocabulary placeholder. The revised text walks through the diagnostic explicitly - the parameters are named and their role is clear, but the structural derivation (the referrer's optimization problem whose solution would produce the inequality as an output) is absent. The cross-reference is functional, not ornamental.

**The framework defense paragraph in "The Standard" is present and adequate.** The two-part argument - that the mechanistic standard is applicable because the canonical texts make it so by their own explanatory practice, and that the interventionist and mechanistic frameworks converge on the same Channel A/B/C diagnostic - handles the R1 concern cleanly. Noting the convergence is a genuinely useful move: it shows the gap is not an artifact of which causal framework one prefers, but a real structural absence in the literature.

**Channel C (passive composition) strengthens the central argument substantially.** The Ibn al-Haytham concern that A and B were not exhaustive was correct, and accepting it improves the piece. Channel C - where referrers act without quality filtering but network segregation combined with quality-demographics correlation produces a real match-quality premium - has distinct policy implications (upstream human capital and neighborhood investment rather than referral incentive design or vacancy information diffusion) and a distinct diagnostic test (whether the quality premium persists after controlling for demographic characteristics). The three-channel decomposition is now analytically complete in a way the two-channel version was not.

**The promiscuous referral bullet is now correctly formulated.** The R1 draft conflated "no match-quality premium" with "no conditional selectivity." The revision correctly separates them: promiscuous referral predicts no additional selectivity *given the referrer's contact set*, not a zero match-quality premium at the population level. This is a meaningful distinction for anyone who would try to test the hypothesis empirically.

**The CA-J treatment is now a substantive assessment rather than a dismissal.** The revised aggregation section walks through what CA-J develop (unemployment persistence as network dynamics, equilibrium employment rate differences across groups, conditions under which density and homophily produce persistent inequality) and assesses it against the three-part requirement. The verdict - "a sophisticated conditional aggregation model, one whose conclusions hold given the selectivity parameter but cannot specify when that parameter holds" - is fair, precise, and useful.

**The Schelling positive control now does the work it was supposed to do.** The three levels are walked through properly in "The Standard," and the positive control is carried into the aggregation section where it specifies what a *satisfying* aggregation account looks like: individual rules, cascade structure, and derivation of the stable macro conditions under which no individual has a unilateral incentive to deviate. The contrast between Schelling (which derives stability) and the referral hiring literature (which asserts durability without deriving it) is now precise.

**The scope disclaimer is now in the right place.** Moving it from a standalone retreat between findings and conclusion into the "Canonical Texts" section converts it from a defensive gesture into a methodological declaration. The piece now reads as a scoped audit declared at the outset rather than a study that discovered its limits under pressure.

## Concerns

# Concerns - Round 2

1. **Channel C is underspecified in the equilibrium durability argument.** The draft's aggregation section distinguishes two candidate explanations for the referral premium's durability: information monopoly (Channel B logic) and quality persistence (which it implicitly associates with Channel A). But Channel C - passive composition through quality-demographics correlation - also produces a real match-quality premium, and its implications for equilibrium durability differ from both. Under Channel C, the premium reflects a population-level quality-demographics correlation rather than a firm-level information advantage or referrer screening; competitive pressure on wages might still fail to erode it, but for different reasons than under either A or B. The conclusion paragraph says "the match-quality claim requires referral selectivity; the inequality claim requires only network composition" - which is correct - but does not position Channel C within the equilibrium durability argument. This is a minor underspecification, not a gap that invalidates the piece, but a reader who follows the three-channel decomposition to the aggregation section will notice that the equilibrium discussion does not close the loop for the C case.

2. **The CA-J claim is asserted with more confidence than the scope warrant supports.** The draft states that the channel decomposition "does not appear as an analytical distinction in their work." This is the author's reading of two specific papers; the broader CA-J research program is not reviewed. Elsewhere the draft appropriately hedges the scope of its post-2004 coverage. The CA-J claim is specific enough that a reader who knows the literature well could test it, but specific enough also that it could be falsified if CA-J or their close descendants do employ the channel decomposition as an organizing frame somewhere. The draft should either maintain the same hedging posture here as it does for post-2004 literature generally ("based on my reading of these two papers" or "as far as I can determine"), or present the CA-J non-disaggregation as a finding from close reading with a scope note. The current formulation reads as a broader claim than the evidence warrants.

These are both minor. Neither warrants a major revision. They are noted because this is the final review before editorial and both are visible enough that a careful reader will encounter them.
