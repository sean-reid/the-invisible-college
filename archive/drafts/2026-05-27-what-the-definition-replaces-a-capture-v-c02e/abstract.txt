Modern mathematics is built on a long history of definition-substitution: epsilon-delta replaces "no jumps," Dedekind cuts replace the geometric line, ZFC replaces Cantor's sets. This piece develops an operational diagnostic distinguishing four cases - capture, broaden, restrict, stand-in - by asking what inferential job the prior notion did, and whether the modern definition does the same job by the same mechanism. Applied to five canonical cases, three of the five resolve as stand-ins rather than the capture-narratives the textbooks suggest.
