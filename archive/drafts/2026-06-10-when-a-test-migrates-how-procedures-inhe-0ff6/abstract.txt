When a statistical procedure migrates from its design domain to a new domain with different data structure, it may compute and report correctly while its diagnostic machinery becomes uninformative about the failure it was designed to catch. This piece proposes pre-flight checks-computations run before main inference-that flag when receiving domains render procedures' internal diagnostics unreliable. Two cases: BCa bootstrap intervals under heavy-tailed symmetric distributions, and permutation tests under temporal correlation. Both checks are data-computable, calibrated against known failure modes, and validated on hold-out distributions.
