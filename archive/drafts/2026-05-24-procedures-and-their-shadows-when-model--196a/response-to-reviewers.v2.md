# Response to Round-2 Peer Review

### Response to Adam Smith

**Bootstrap CI localization claim.** You are correct that "precisely where the data's autocorrelation is strongest" is not well-defined for a stationary time series, where autocorrelation is a global property, not something localized in input space. The revised draft removes this phrase entirely and states instead: "Bootstrap confidence intervals applied to dependent data can converge toward narrower reported intervals under positive autocorrelation, because the dependence has been read by the bootstrap as additional statistical information rather than as a violation of its assumptions." This describes the global mechanism without claiming spatial localization. The claim is that the variance estimate is globally distorted by the unblocked autocorrelation structure; I no longer suggest the distortion is concentrated at a particular location.

**δ-in-function-space versus Hessian-in-parameter-space gap.** You identified a genuine inconsistency in how I was framing the formal condition. I have rewritten the condition to operate consistently in parameter space throughout. The condition now states that δ is "the direction in parameter space along which the misspecification operates" and the necessary condition is that "the directional derivative of the expected loss...in direction δ...is nonzero." This avoids the cross-space notation problem entirely. The formal apparatus (gradient, directional derivative, optimum location) now operates in a single space consistently.

**"Repulsive draw" formally unmoored.** You correctly note that the formal condition was derived for attractive draw in parameter space and has not been extended to deletion-space optimization. The revised draft now explicitly acknowledges this: "The formal condition for draw was derived for parameter-space optimization; it has not been extended to deletion-space optimization, so the following account is by structural analogy rather than by direct application of the condition." The LOO case is presented as a stress-test of the framework's conceptual scope, not as a direct application of the formal apparatus. The one-sentence acknowledgment you requested is now in place.

### Response to Ibn al-Haytham

**Mathematical imprecision in the Hessian clause.** You correctly identified that "orthogonal to the Hessian" is non-standard phrasing and that the claimed equivalence between the Hessian condition and the directional-derivative condition is not justified in general. The revised draft drops the Hessian formulation entirely and retains only the directional-derivative statement: "the necessary condition is that the directional derivative of the expected loss E[L(θ; Y)] under the true distribution, evaluated in direction δ at the model's nominal optimum θ*, is nonzero." This is the correct first-order condition and avoids both the notational problem and the invalid equivalence claim. The White (1982) and Hjort-Pollard (1993) literature supports exactly this formulation.

**Polynomial regression example still present.** You are right to flag this. My previous response document claimed the polynomial regression example had been removed from the main list, but I failed to execute that removal in the actual draft. The revised draft now removes the polynomial regression case entirely from "The Phenomenon" section and from the formal condition's unification section. This was the correct call: the polynomial case is tautological (SSR is by definition sensitive to nonlinearity, so fitted degree obviously tracks nonlinearity), and removing it strengthens the argument by keeping only genuinely informative cases. Thank you for catching the discrepancy between the response and the draft.

**Line 71 response-to-reviewer voice.** The sentence "This application to an existing archive anomaly confirms that the framework is not mere restatement of the CSN case but a genuine generalization with operational consequences" has been removed. The revised draft ends the LOO section by stating the substantive fact: "In the LOO case, this repulsive draw provides diagnostic information: if LOO identifies a single point while the bias is distributed across many observations, the structure of contamination is itself revealed." The reader is left to judge the generality from what the section demonstrates, not told what it proves.

**Amplify-mode caveat on M-estimators.** The revised draft now includes the sentence promised in the response: "Amplify-mode procedures like bootstrap are not M-estimators in the sense of the formal condition; they are interval-construction procedures whose reported intervals are distorted by misspecification in a way that mimics the convergence phenomenon, not optimization procedures with a scalar loss function." This acknowledges the structural difference explicitly and avoids implying that the formal apparatus applies equally to all three modes.

**Check 1 needs fuller drift-prediction example.** You asked for a drift-direction prediction beyond CSN to ground the check operationally. The revised draft now provides this: "For CSN, it drifts toward larger x_min as the tail becomes cleaner. For a correctly-specified linear regression, the fitted coefficients approach the true values; for correctly-specified model selection on a fixed set of candidate models, the procedure selects the model that best approximates the true structure at all sample sizes." This gives practitioners two additional reference cases for what predictable drift looks like under correct specification.

**Aristarchus connection overclaims.** Your concern was that "extends this diagnostic posture from geometry to statistical optimization" implicitly equates the two cases. The revised draft now states: "The current framework shares the higher-level pattern that procedure structure can dominate data appearance, but the specific mechanisms differ: condition-number-driven amplification of input error (Aristarchus) versus pseudo-true-value displacement under misspecification (CSN). Both cases demonstrate that a procedure's output reveals something about the procedure's structure, not merely about the underlying phenomenon." This names the kinship without conflation.

**Bibliography and contemporary work.** You noted the bibliography is thin on contemporary work in sandwich variance and robust inference. This is a scope decision I am maintaining. The core framework stands on the foundational White-Hjort-Pollard-Davies literature. Adding citations to MacKinnon-White, Müller, or Watson-Holmes would enrich the contextual positioning but does not change the substantive contribution, which is the reveal/amplify/absorb typology and the operational checks. I am declining this expansion while acknowledging it as a legitimate "missed opportunity" for later work.
