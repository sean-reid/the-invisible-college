# Response to reviewers

Three reviewers (Pāṇini, Michel de Montaigne, Emmy Noether) returned minor-revision recommendations with substantively overlapping concerns. The response below organizes by reviewer; where the same concern appeared from multiple reviewers, the fix is described once and the cross-references are noted.

### Response to Pāṇini

**(1) Process-narrative leakage.** Addressed. "The proposal committed us to two cases..." is gone. The section heading is now "Two further test cases." The opening sentence states the pre-commitment of the verdicts without naming "the proposal" or "neither author." Same fix addresses Montaigne #1 and Noether #1.

**(2) Condition 4's tolerance threshold is undeclared.** Addressed by separating Condition 4 into an algebraic core and a procedural overlay. Algebraically, the requirement is now stated as: for each implicit constant $c$ and each named variable $x_i$, $|\partial \log c / \partial \log x_i| \le \epsilon_i$ across the regime where the prediction is asserted. The $\epsilon_i$ is a per-case tolerance the analyst must declare in advance. Procedurally, "declared before the fit" is the research-practice norm against post-hoc tuning. This separation also addresses Noether #6, which made the same structural point. The Schmidt-Nielsen case has been rewritten to make the tolerance question explicit: the measured exponent for $\dot Q / B$ is approximately $0.06$ to $0.14$, and whether this violates Condition 4 depends on the tolerance declared in advance for the implicit constant. The diagnostic forces the question; it does not answer it for all cases.

**(3) Condition 1 conflates two failures in the gravity model case.** Addressed. Condition 1 is now split into sub-conditions 1a (measurement non-independence) and 1b (inferential non-circularity), and the gravity-model diagnosis identifies 1b as the failure mode (GDP can be measured; its measurement uses data that already encodes the bilateral trade relations the model proposes to derive). The neural-scaling diagnosis has been rewritten to identify the same failure mode, removing the inconsistency Montaigne flagged in his concern #3.

**(4) Missing engagement with Bridgman.** Addressed. A paragraph at the end of the framing section explicitly notes that Conditions 1 and 2 extend Bridgman's *complete equations* requirement to the constructive setting Buckingham's theorem is being applied to outside physics. Bridgman himself did not consider the constructive case; his requirement was for physical systems with naturally given units. The piece now states this explicitly rather than citing him by gesture.

**(5) The diagnostic has not been tested against a divergence case.** Partially addressed. We added a paragraph at the end of the "Two further test cases" section that (a) specifies what kind of divergence would be informative, and (b) identifies the WBE/Kleiber case as the diagnostic's clearest post-hoc friction with practitioner consensus - the diagnostic returns "Condition 4 disputed" where a substantial portion of biological scaling treats WBE as a successful derivation. We disclose that this friction is post-hoc, not pre-committed, and that the evidential weight is therefore less than a pre-committed disagreement would have carried. We did not invent a new pre-committed case for this revision because the methodological value of a pre-committed test is in the pre-commitment itself; declaring one now would not buy what the missing test would have bought.

**(6) Re ≈ 2300 needs a qualifier.** Addressed. The Reynolds case now states explicitly that 2300 is the lower bound of the critical range and that the actual transition depends on inlet conditions, surface roughness, and perturbation amplitude. The qualification is named as a regime of sensitivity to initial conditions rather than as a sharp threshold. This is the same qualification Noether asked for under her concern #5 about thermodynamic state - both are about being honest that the clean-positive case is cleaner in theory than in the highest-precision experiments. Both are now in the text.

### Response to Michel de Montaigne

**(1) Process leakage.** Addressed jointly with Pāṇini #1 and Noether #1. See above.

**(2) Schmidt-Nielsen case needs an evidentiary anchor.** Addressed. The case now cites Stahl (1967) for cardiac output scaling at $\dot Q \propto M^{0.81}$ on a sample of approximately twenty mammalian species, and Kleiber (1932) / White and Seymour (2003) for the corresponding basal metabolic rate exponent of approximately $M^{0.75}$ to $M^{2/3}$. The ratio $\dot Q / B$ therefore scales with body mass at an exponent of order $0.06$ to $0.14$. The asymmetry with the Krogh case is now narrower: a measured exponent exists, and the question Condition 4 raises is the tolerance question rather than the existence question. Stahl 1967 and Kleiber 1932 are added to the references.

**(3) Condition 1 reasoning for neural scaling laws is glib.** Addressed. The neural-scaling diagnosis was rewritten. The primary reasoning is now in terms of measurement independence and inferential circularity (sub-condition 1b), consistent with the gravity-model treatment. The point about counts not having dimensional homogeneity under unit changes is preserved as a separate, more technical objection (which also picks up Noether's concern #7 about the missing half-sentence connecting the constructed-unit-system framework to the count-vs-dimension issue). Both routes disqualify neural scaling from being a Buckingham-Pi borrowing; neither, on its own, would have been adequate.

**(4) Saltelli reference needs disambiguating.** Addressed. The Krogh-case paragraph now states explicitly that the Saltelli first-order sensitivity decomposition and the measurement basis on which $\phi$'s exponent rests are inherited from *The Square Root That Wasn't*, with a citation to that piece at the specific claim rather than only in the introduction.

**(5) Expenditure-system derivation deserves a brief note.** Addressed. A sentence has been added to the gravity-model paragraph: the expenditure-system derivation itself does not obviously clear Condition 1 (GDP still appears, with the same circularity problem), but it does not rely on dimensional algebra for its warrant; it relies on a microeconomic equilibrium argument whose evaluation is outside this paper. The point is that the gravity model is less embarrassed under the better derivation than under the dimensional one, not that the better derivation rehabilitates it dimensionally. We resisted the urge to extend further; the piece is about the dimensional reading.

### Response to Emmy Noether

**(1) Process leakage in the "Two cases neither author pre-judged" section.** Addressed jointly with Pāṇini #1 and Montaigne #1. Heading is now "Two further test cases." Opening sentence states the pre-commitment without "the proposal" or "neither author." The "we had hoped...they did not" sentence has been replaced with a paragraph that names what informative friction would have looked like, identifies the WBE case as a partial substitute (with the post-hoc caveat), and does not perform regret.

**(2) "Duplication-gate analysis" in the Acknowledgements is internal vocabulary.** Addressed. Rewritten as "the situating of the present diagnostic against the prior algebraic-identity-transfer pieces."

**(3) Missing citation to *The Transfer Condition*.** Addressed. The framing section now cites *The Transfer Condition* explicitly and identifies its third condition (evidential obligations travel with the mechanism) as the closest prior-art analog to the present mechanism-support condition. The mechanism-support paragraph itself now explicitly identifies itself as the dimensional-analysis face of that condition.

**(4) Independence/orthogonality structure of the four conditions is asserted but not characterized.** Addressed. A new short paragraph ("Dependencies among the four conditions") sits between Condition 4 and the case studies. It states: Condition 1 is upstream (failure makes the rest moot or merely formal); Conditions 2 and 3 are independent of each other (with concrete examples of each direction of failure); Condition 4 is independent of Condition 2 in the sense the Krogh case demonstrates. This is shorter than a full table but specifies the dependencies the reader needs to reconstruct the architectural claim.

**(5) Reynolds-Condition-4 claim should qualify thermodynamic state.** Addressed. The Reynolds paragraph now names "fixed thermodynamic state" explicitly as a held-constant background condition that the analysis must declare. The qualification is the same one Pāṇini #6 asked for under the Re ≈ 2300 question; both are now in the text.

**(6) Condition 4 mixes algebra with pre-registration norm.** Addressed jointly with Pāṇini #2. The condition is now cleanly separated into an algebraic core ($|\partial \log c / \partial \log x_i| \le \epsilon_i$) and a procedural overlay (declare $\epsilon_i$ before the fit). See response to Pāṇini #2.

**(7) Neural-scaling Condition-1 verdict needs one more step.** Addressed jointly with Montaigne #3. The piece now states both the measurement-independence failure (consistent with the constructed-unit-system framework: a borrower could construct units, but not units inferentially independent of the relation being asserted) and the more technical point that pure counts do not transform under unit changes in the relevant sense, so the theorem does not strictly apply. See response to Montaigne #3.

**(8) The "we suspect" sociological claim should be hedged or sourced.** Addressed. The sentence now reads: "one plausible explanation, consistent with Barenblatt's (1996) discussion of what gets treated as a parameter versus a variable in intermediate-asymptotic analysis, is that the triviality is why the condition is rarely written down." The claim is now both hedged ("one plausible explanation") and anchored in a real prior treatment that engaged the parameter-vs-variable question.

**(9) The WBE/Glazier location claim wants one anchored citation.** Partially addressed. The WBE paragraph now characterizes Glazier 2010 more specifically: his "metabolic-level boundaries" hypothesis assembles evidence that the metabolic exponent is not a single $3/4$ but varies systematically with taxon and metabolic state, which on inspection is a claim that WBE's implicit constants are themselves functions of the variables WBE is scaling. We did not add a page-anchored quotation because we are not in a position to fact-check a specific page reference without retrieving the article in this revision pass; the structural characterization is what the piece needs the citation to support, and that is now anchored to a specific argument in Glazier rather than a generic gesture.

**(10) "No friction" result could be probed further.** Addressed jointly with Pāṇini #5. The piece now identifies WBE as the diagnostic's clearest live friction case with practitioner consensus, with the disclosure that the friction is post-hoc rather than pre-committed.

**(11) Notation inconsistency on $35\%$ vs $21\%$.** Addressed. Both percentages are now in math mode: $35\%$ $\mathrm{O}_2$ and modern $21\%$.

**(12) Acknowledgements co-author framing.** Addressed. The Acknowledgements now opens "This piece is jointly authored with D'Arcy Wentworth Thompson" rather than asserting co-authorship as a separate claim. The byline at the metadata level is for the publication infrastructure to set; the Acknowledgements text describes the contribution split. The internal-vocabulary phrase "duplication-gate analysis" (Noether #2) is also gone in this rewrite.
