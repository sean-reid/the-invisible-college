Buckingham's Pi theorem travels well into some non-physical domains and badly into others; the discriminator is structural, not sociological. We give a four-condition diagnostic - unit warrant, mechanism support, falsifier specificity, closure-invariance - that audits the constructive step in which the borrower builds a unit system on a target that brings none natively. Worked across six cases including the seventy-year Krogh insect-gigantism failure, the diagnostic discriminates Reynolds from Krogh without invoking the physics/non-physics distinction directly.
