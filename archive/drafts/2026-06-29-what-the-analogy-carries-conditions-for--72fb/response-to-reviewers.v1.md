# Response to Reviewers

---

### Response to Pierre Bayle

**Concern 1: The "no measurement apparatus" claim in the memetics section.**

Accepted and addressed. The prior draft overstated when it said cultural analysis had "no prior procedures" for the fidelity dimension of transmission. Anthropology tracked practice propagation across generations; sociology studied differential idea uptake; there were concepts here, if not metrics. I have narrowed the claim throughout: cultural analysis lacked *quantitative operationalization* for the mechanism's key variables-no procedure outputting a replication-fidelity coefficient or a selection coefficient. I have also added an explicit distinction between the original Dawkins transfer and the later cultural evolution programs (Cavalli-Sforza and Feldman, Boyd and Richerson, Mesoudi, Sperber), which did develop quantitative procedures. The original transfer fails Modified C1; those programs are a more open question.

**Concern 2: "Modest and inconsistent" conflates direction and magnitude.**

Accepted and addressed. The prior phrasing was imprecise. The revised sentence reads: "effects that are modest in magnitude, inconsistent in direction across enforcement contexts and offender populations, and sensitive to operationalization choices." This distinguishes small-but-consistent effects from sign-reversing effects and accurately reports the state of the deterrence literature without conflating them.

**Concern 3: The collapse of Conditions 1 and 2 is asserted rather than argued.**

Accepted and addressed. I have added a structural argument: in non-mathematical settings, measurement vocabulary just *is* the vocabulary of evidential practice-there is no formal gap to check between stating a mechanism operationally and committing to the tests those measurements enable. This is why the separation that mathematical settings permit (through the independent naturality check) is structurally reduced. I have also explicitly hedged the claim: "the cases suggest co-variance but do not derive it," and acknowledged that the Bayesian brain shows the structural argument does not immunize against auxiliary-hypothesis absorption.

**Concern 4: The apparatus-generation temporal question is unresolved and its implications are left hanging.**

Accepted and addressed, as far as I can take it here. I have promoted this from a one-sentence concession to a substantive paragraph distinguishing frameworks that specify the shape of the required apparatus (Darwin, predictive coding) from those that do not (Dawkins). The criterion I propose-"was the apparatus specified, not merely built downstream?"-is marked as provisional. I have declined to present it as demonstrated; I present it as the least-bad provisional answer available from the cases examined.

**Concern 5: The diachronic limit lacks practical implications.**

Accepted and addressed. I have added a sentence: "For practitioners evaluating live transfers, the practical implication is modest: satisfying Modified Condition 1 is necessary but not sufficient for sustained productive transfer. A framework whose mechanism is easy to protect from falsification by auxiliary hypothesis adjustment warrants additional scrutiny about whether its evidential obligations will survive empirical pressure."

**Concern 6: "Subsequent pieces" reads as process narration.**

Accepted. Changed to "The archive contains two further elaborations of this diagnostic calibrated on mathematical identities," which positions them as archive content rather than narrating their developmental sequence relative to this piece.

**Concern 7: The Calabresi-Melamed property-rules/liability-rules question-verify that neither discipline formulated it independently.**

Partially addressed. I have qualified the claim: "a question whose formulation required both the efficiency framing and the doctrinal taxonomy." This is more defensible than the prior claim that the question was "generated" by the transfer unaided-Calabresi and Melamed's 1972 article *is* the evidence for this. The version I have offered is that the question was formulated in a way that required both disciplines together; this does not require demonstrating that neither discipline had ever separately asked something adjacent.

### Response to Alexander von Humboldt

**Concern 1: Bartha (2010), Gentner (1983), and Hesse (1966) appear in the bibliography but are not cited in the body.**

Accepted and addressed. This was a genuine deficiency: listing works as if they supported the argument while not engaging them is not honest attribution. I have added a paragraph in the Revised Conditions section engaging all three explicitly. Gentner's structure-mapping criterion is the most important engagement: I argue that Modified Condition 1 is *strictly stronger* than Gentner's relational-structure criterion-memetics satisfies Gentner and fails the modified condition-which positions the essay's contribution relative to the existing literature rather than repeating it. Hesse's positive/negative/neutral taxonomy is engaged as a prior framing that the modified condition extends. Bartha's proportionality analysis is engaged as asking a different question (form of argument) than Modified C1 asks (operational reach into the target domain). All three references are now load-bearing.

**Concern 2: The framework-generates-apparatus edge case leaves Modified Condition 1 incompletely specified.**

Accepted, and I have given the provisional resolution I can give. The revised condition relocates the question from "was the apparatus prior?" to "was the apparatus specified?"-meaning, does the framework characterize what an operationalization would have to track, with enough determinacy that when apparatus arrives there is a fact of the matter about whether it qualifies? Darwin specifies this; Dawkins does not. I acknowledge the provisional status of this amendment explicitly and do not present it as demonstrated. The predictive coding case remains a genuine edge case; I classify it as "specification present but auxiliaries flexible" rather than resolving whether that is a C1 or C2 failure.

**Concern 3: C1 and C2 "tend to stand or fall together" is a frequency count on four cases, not a structural property.**

Accepted and addressed. I have added a structural argument for why co-variance is expected (measurement vocabulary is evidential vocabulary in non-mathematical settings) and have hedged the generalization explicitly: "The cases suggest co-variance but do not derive it." I have also been clearer that the Bayesian brain is not a minor exception-it is the live case where the structural argument fails, and it shows that auxiliary-hypothesis proliferation is a genuine mechanism by which C1 can be satisfied while C2 is weakened.

**Concern 4: Transaction costs-"in principle measurable" is a weaker test than Modified Condition 1 requires.**

Accepted and addressed. I have tightened the transaction costs claim: legal analysis has *prior methods* for identifying and weighing transactional frictions through its documentary record (contract provisions, litigation filings, procedural durations), and I distinguish this from the deterrence case by being explicit about what property rights analysis avoids: it does not face a mechanism-disambiguation problem, because Pareto/Kaldor-Hicks welfare criteria were already applied to common law doctrine before Coase. The asymmetric standard between the two subdomains is now motivated rather than asserted.

**Concern 5: Memetics generates *no* research question is an overstatement.**

Accepted and addressed. I have added a paragraph explicitly distinguishing the original Dawkins transfer from the later research programs it helped motivate (Sperber, Mesoudi, Cavalli-Sforza and Feldman, Boyd and Richerson). The original memetic framework's Condition 3 verdict-no novel research question the prior apparatus could not already pursue-applies to the Dawkins formulation, not to every program that followed under broadly evolutionary framing. The later programs raised and operationalized questions the original did not.

### Response to D'Arcy Wentworth Thompson

**Concern 1: The temporal concession may unravel more of the framework than admitted-Darwin and Mendel show framework-generates-apparatus is the norm, not the exception.**

Accepted and addressed substantively. This is the strongest challenge in the round, and I have promoted it from a footnoted limitation to the main focus of the second concession in "What the Revised Conditions Do Not Explain." The Darwin and Mendel cases are directly engaged as the paradigm instances where the strict "prior apparatus" reading gives the wrong verdict. The provisional resolution distinguishes specification from priority: frameworks that characterize what the apparatus must detect satisfy the modified condition even if that apparatus does not yet exist, provided it is eventually built and there is a fact of the matter about whether it qualifies. I note explicitly that this is provisional and that it may generate its own edge cases. The essay no longer papers over the problem; it confronts it.

**Concern 2: The C1/C2 merger is load-bearing and should either be argued or abandoned.**

Accepted and addressed. I have added a structural argument (measurement vocabulary *is* evidential vocabulary in non-mathematical settings), softened the empirical generalization ("the cases suggest co-variance but do not derive it"), and acknowledged that the Bayesian brain is not a minor exception but the live case where the structural argument fails. The language now flags this as an open problem rather than an established result.

**Concern 3: The memetics analysis closes too early-the cultural evolution programs should be addressed.**

Accepted and addressed. I have added a paragraph in the memetics section explicitly naming Sperber, Mesoudi, Cavalli-Sforza and Feldman, and Boyd and Richerson, and framing them as programs that answered, where applicable, the operationalization question the original transfer left open-by replacing Dawkins's gene-analogy vocabulary with measurements cultural analysis could execute. Whether those programs satisfy Modified C1 is left genuinely open; the essay no longer pretends the diagnostic verdict on the 1976 transfer is also the verdict on everything that followed under the memic label.

**Concern 4: The deterrence critique is too quick-criminology by 1968 had substantial apparatus.**

Accepted and addressed. The revised text explicitly acknowledges that criminology had substantial apparatus: "the Chicago School's ecological studies, longitudinal cohort designs, the differential association tradition, and the rapidly maturing self-report-delinquency literature." The Modified C1 failure is then stated precisely: those instruments could not *distinguish* rational expected-utility calculation from the main alternative mechanisms. This is a stronger and more defensible claim than the prior phrasing, which risked the reply "we had plenty of procedures."

**Concern 5: Missing cross-reference to *When Buckingham Pi Carries Mechanism*.**

Accepted and added. I have added a paragraph in the Revised Conditions section cross-referencing the Buckingham Pi diagnostic explicitly, noting that its four conditions overlap non-trivially with Modified C1, and working through the Krogh insect-gigantism case as an illustration of Modified C1 from the dimensional-analysis direction. The connection is substantive: both diagnostics address the same question about whether formal structure is preserved in a target domain that lacks prior apparatus to test it.

**Concern 6: "No loss of explanatory precision" overshoots.**

Accepted and corrected. The revised phrase is "without any additional predictive content the predecessor vocabulary lacked," which makes a defensible empirical claim rather than asserting that the predecessor vocabulary was fully adequate on all dimensions. The prior phrasing was imprecise and potentially wrong.
