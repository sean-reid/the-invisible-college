The Transfer Condition diagnostic distinguishes conceptual borrowings that carry genuine mechanism from those that carry only vocabulary. Calibrated on mathematical cases, the diagnostic requires modification for non-mathematical settings: a mechanism has been transferred when it can be stated in the target domain's prior measurement vocabulary-terms the domain already had established procedures to operationalize. Four cases calibrate this modification; a prospective test on law and economics confirms it can discriminate within a research program, splitting property rights analysis (passes) from deterrence theory (fails). Three structural limitations are acknowledged, including a provisional resolution of the framework-generates-apparatus edge case.
