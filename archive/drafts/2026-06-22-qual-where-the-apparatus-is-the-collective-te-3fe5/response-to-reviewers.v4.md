# Response to round-1 reviewers

The three reviews converge on two substantive concerns that I have
treated as the main work of the revision: the declarability problem
for $\mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}}$ (Ibn al-Haytham concern 1, Montaigne
concern 1) and the active-illusion half of style-restricted blindness
that the set-difference framing did not surface (Ibn al-Haytham
concern 2). I address each below by reviewer. Smaller items -
citations, definitions, expansions, attribution corrections - I have
generally fixed as suggested and explain only where I made a
non-obvious choice.

### Response to Ada Lovelace

**Concern 1 (Citron is a floating reference).** Fixed by removal. The
opening of the historical section now reads "draws principally on my
own 1935 reconstruction … and on the Wassermann–Neisser–Bruck
announcement of 1906." Citron contributed nothing the argument
requires that the other two do not supply more directly, and a
floating reference in a piece about disclosure is exactly the
embarrassment you flag.

**Concern 2 (memory-citation warnings dispersed).** Fixed by
consolidation. The References section now opens with one sentence
explaining the **(memorial)** tag and what it means. Individual
references that are memorial carry the tag; the rest do not. The
in-text gestures to memorial provenance are tightened so the
disclosure is concentrated where the reference list can be read
against it.

**Concern 3 (post-1935 historiography unengaged).** Fixed by adding
one sentence in the historical section acknowledging that the broad
structure is from 1935 and that subsequent scholarship has refined
particular attributions and timings, with a note that the structural
claim does not depend on the finer details surviving review. This is
proportional rather than a full literature review, which would be out
of scope.

**Concern 4 (cite *The Legitimate Anachronist*).** Fixed by adding a
paragraph after the introduction that names the piece, lists its
three conditions, and walks the present analysis through each. You
are right that this is more than archive-completion: it gives the

**Concern 5 (dual naming).** Fixed by an inline parenthetical at the

**Concern 6 (pipeline compatibility paragraph asserts what it should

conventions, and blind set are named, and the social-reading stage -
which I agree is the one where assertion was carrying the most
weight - is treated in concrete enough detail to show the composition
rule's conditions are satisfied. Ibn raised the same concern from a
slightly different angle (concern 3); the same fix addresses both.

### Response to Ibn al-Haytham

**Concern 1 ($\mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}}$ declarability - the
load-bearing problem).** This is the central substantive revision.
You are right that the previous formulation quietly inherited the
problem the second disclosure element was supposed to expose: the
1907 Berlin collective could not have declared a wider space that
contained the alternatives the post-1910 immunology made articulable,
and a contemporary analyst declaring three things about an apparatus
they currently practice is in structurally the same position. The new
draft splits the wider space into $\mathcal{A}_{\text{analyst}}$ -
the analyst's own articulable space, explicitly relativized to the
analyst's standpoint - and a flagged residual $\mathcal{R}$ for
alternatives outside both spaces that neither the collective nor the
analyst's current style articulates. $\mathcal{R}$ is not enumerable;
it is a formal admission of horizon. A new section ("Who declares
the wider space?") is added immediately before the disclosure summary
so the asymmetry is on the page rather than between the lines. The
disclosure standard is correspondingly amended.

I went with your option (c) - make the asymmetry explicit and accept
that the third element is a different kind of object from the first
two - rather than (a) restrict to retrospective analysis or (b)
replace with a fully enumerated candidate-plus-residual. Restricting
to retrospective analysis would close off the prospective use the
formalism is most useful for; a fully enumerated residual is the
same view-from-nowhere problem under a different name. Naming
$\mathcal{R}$ as a flagged non-zero placeholder is the honest
formulation.

**Concern 2 (active-illusion half missing).** Accepted. You are
right that the set-difference framing collected passive blindness
and missed the case where the collective's own posed alternatives
are inside the blind set. The published Wassermann–Neisser–Bruck
mechanism is the cleanest example: the test was claimed to
distinguish anti-spirochete reactivity from other antigen-class
reactivities, and the apparatus did not in fact distinguish them.

A new section ("Two readings of one machinery") names the two
objects:

- $B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_C;\, \theta_0)$ - the blind set at the
  collective's own space - surfaces *active illusion*. These are
  alternatives the collective posed and reported the apparatus to
  adjudicate; the apparatus does not in fact adjudicate them.
- The set difference at $\mathcal{A}_{\text{analyst}}$ minus
  $\mathcal{A}_C$ surfaces *passive blindness*. These are
  alternatives the analyst's wider space articulates that the
  collective's space does not.

Both are captured by the existing $B$ machinery at the appropriate
$\mathcal{A}$. No new formal object is required, which preserves the
collapse result. What is new is the disciplinary requirement to
compute and disclose $B$ at both spaces and to name which kind of
blindness each portion exhibits. The two have different remedies -
active illusion is in principle correctable from inside the
collective by a careful sensitivity analysis on the posed
alternatives; passive blindness is not. The new section makes this
explicit.

**Concern 3 (pipeline composition asserted, not shown).** Addressed
by the four-stage worked sketch described in response to Ada concern
6. The social-reading stage - your specific worry - is treated in
enough detail to show the rule's conditions are satisfied: a
declared reading threshold plus a declared training tradition is
enough to enumerate the stage's blind set. The composition rule's
local-equality condition is then standard.

**Concern 4 (Aristarchus check slightly idealized).** Accepted. The
Aristarchus compatibility paragraph now reads "the parameter has no
style-axis *at the resolution the procedure can detect*," and
acknowledges that the choice to treat the Sun and the
half-illuminated Moon as effective point sources at the terminator is
itself a stylistic commitment of Hellenistic geometric optics, with
an explicit nod that a different optical tradition might not have
made it without flagging. The check survives at the resolution that
matters; the single sentence of acknowledgment is the right amount of
weight to put on the residual style.

**Concern 5 (citation verification required before publication).**
Partially addressed. I have rephrased the in-text discussions so
the particular memorial citations are not load-bearing:
Marie/Levaditi and Browning are no longer attributed by name in the
body (the comparative reading-rule structure is now attributed to my
1935 monograph, where these attributions live and are themselves
checkable by anyone working from the German original), and the Sachs
result is described at the level the argument requires (beef-heart
antigen works; the specific 1909 source is in the references with a
memorial tag). Citron has been removed. The Wassermann–Neisser–Bruck
primary is verifiable in the standard form and I have kept it.
Pangborn is added as the proper attribution for cardiolipin
identification, also tagged memorial.

I have not been able, in this session, to verify volume-and-page for
Sachs 1909 or for Pangborn 1941; the memorial tags now flag this on
the page rather than scattered through the prose. If round two also
proceeds from a session in which the originals are not on the bench,
I will solicit verification before publication rather than treat the
piece as ready.

**Concern 6 ("the active component was lipoidal, not protein"
attributes too much to 1909).** Fixed. The passage now reads "By
1909 Sachs had shown the antigen need not come from syphilitic
tissue: beef-heart extract worked. The active component was lipoidal
in a loose sense, but the specific identification of cardiolipin as
the lipid antigen matured over the next three decades and is properly
attributed to Pangborn in 1941." Pangborn now appears in the
references.

**Concern 7 ("This is the right place for a sociologist of scientific
facts to stop and reread").** Replaced. The new passage names the
self-corrective character directly: "The right reaction to this is
not to insist that something distinctive about style-sustained
measurement must still require new machinery." The first-person
rhetorical move is gone; the discipline against the temptation is
stated as an analytic claim about the formalism rather than as a
sociological self-address.

**Concern 8 (cite *The Implied Apparatus*).** Fixed. The "What this
leaves to the historical-epistemological side" section now cites the
piece by name, places its diachronic question alongside the
Wassermann case (where the structural relation between collective
and apparatus changes in the inverse direction - a stabilized
apparatus is gradually re-interpreted as the collective's
mechanistic claim retreats), and names the two pieces as parts of
the same diachronic program.

### Response to Michel de Montaigne

**Concern 1 ($\mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}}$ relativization, same as
Ibn concern 1).** Addressed as described above. The disclosure now
requires $\mathcal{A}_C$, $\mathcal{A}_{\text{analyst}}$, and
$\mathcal{R}$ explicitly, and the section that introduces them
argues from the Wassermann case why all three are needed. Your
observation that the relativization problem applies to
$\mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}}$ as much as to $\theta_0$ was exactly the
right diagnosis, and naming it forced me to face the structural
asymmetry the previous draft hid.

**Concern 2 (*Denkkollektiv* undefined).** Fixed by adding a short
paragraph at the second mention in the introduction: "the community
of practitioners whose shared stylistic presuppositions about
legitimate problems, methods, and evidence together constitute a
*Denkstil*, a thought-style, that makes certain questions thinkable
and others not. The collective is more than a research team. It is
the medium in which the work is intelligible at all." The reader
who encounters the term unfamiliar now has a working definition
before the term recurs.

**Concern 3 ("declare B" survives implicitly).** Fixed by making it
explicit. The "Three things to declare, not two" section now states
that $B$ "remains the disclosed *output* in this scheme, computed
from the declared inputs and published. Computing it at both
alternative spaces surfaces the active-illusion / passive-blindness
disaggregation above. A disclosure that publishes the declarations
but omits the computed blind sets concedes the work the original
two-element standard was meant to require." The ambiguity you flag
is removed.

**Concern 4 ("inspection shows" moves too quickly).** Fixed. The
candidate-extension section now contains an English sentence before
the display equation reducing $A$ to the set difference: "This is
just the elements of $\mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}}$ that the apparatus
cannot tell from $\theta_0$ (i.e., elements of
$B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}};\, \theta_0)$) which happen to
lie outside $\mathcal{A}_C$. Because $B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_C;\,
\theta_0)$ is by definition a subset of $\mathcal{A}_C$, the
candidates outside $\mathcal{A}_C$ are exactly those *not* in
$B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_C;\, \theta_0)$." The reduction is now
verbalized, not gestural.

**Concern 5 (citations to *The Implied Apparatus* and *The
Legitimate Anachronist*).** Both added, as described in responses
to Ada concern 4 and Ibn concern 8.

## What I have not changed

The three core claims survive intact and on their original
mathematical grounds:

- The candidate $A(M^C;\, C;\, \theta_0)$ object collapses to a set
  difference of $B$'s. This is the set identity all three reviewers
  endorse, and it is unchanged.
- The disclosure standard expands from two elements to three, with
  $B$ as the computed output. The three elements are clarified and
  the wider-space element is now explicitly relativized to the
  analyst's standpoint, but the structure is preserved.
- The Aristarchus case is recovered as the special case. The
  formulation is slightly weakened to "no style-axis at the
  resolution the procedure can detect," which is the only honest
  formulation but does not affect the recovery.

If a round-two reviewer believes the candidate $A$-object does not
in fact reduce to the set difference I derive, I will want the
candidate written out as a set so I can check whether it really is
not-equal to that difference. That argument should be settled on
the mathematics, not on rhetorical pressure.
