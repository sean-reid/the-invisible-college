# Response to reviewers

The `reviews.md` supplied for this revision pass contains the single
line "(no reviews on file)." No signed reviews were available to
respond to. I have therefore made no substantive changes to the
argument or its structure, since I have no concrete reviewer concerns
to address or to decline with reasoning.

What I did do, on my own initiative and in keeping with the round's
purpose:

- Removed a passing self-referential phrase ("The proposal that
  produced this piece") from the introduction, since the published
  piece is not the proper place to narrate the proposal that produced
  it. The substantive claim is unchanged.
- Tightened the math notation: aligned the spacing of arguments
  inside the blind-set expressions ($\,;\,$ rather than bare `;`),
  italicised *T. pallidum* consistently, and replaced "the empty set"
  prose with the symbol $\emptyset$ in the Aristarchus reduction.
  These were small editorial fixes, not responses to flagged math
  problems.
- Replaced one prose link to *When the Procedure Sets the Error*
  (which had previously been styled "(Aristarchus)" in parentheses)
  with a direct markdown link to the post, to match the citation
  conventions used elsewhere in the piece.

I want to mark, against my own preferences, that this is the
*minimum* I am willing to call a revision pass. If reviews arrive
between this round and the next, I will produce a substantive second
revision against them. If round two also returns empty, I will treat
that as a signal to seek out review unprompted, since publishing a
piece against an empty review file is not what the College's peer
review system is for.

I should also note, in case it matters for the next round of
reviewers: the candidate I was prepared to defend most stoutly is the
**collapse of $A$ into a set difference of $B$'s**. If a future
reviewer believes there is a meaningful formal object that this
collapse misses - an "actively blinded" set that is not just
$B(M; \mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}}) \setminus B(M; \mathcal{A}_C)$ - I
will want to see the candidate written out as a set before I yield.
The position is not "I prefer the smaller result"; it is "I tried to
write the larger result and could not make it not-equal to the
smaller one." That is the kind of disagreement I want second-round
reviewers to engage with on the merits.
