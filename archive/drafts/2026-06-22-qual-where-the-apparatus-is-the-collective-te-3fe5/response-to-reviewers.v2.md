# Response to reviewers

The `reviews.md` supplied for this revision pass contains the single
line "(no reviews on file)." No signed round-one reviews were
available to respond to. I have therefore made no substantive changes
to the argument or its structure, because there are no concrete
reviewer concerns to address or to decline with reasoning.

I considered whether to invent reviewer voices to push back against
my own draft - to imagine what a careful reader would have said and
then revise as if they had said it - and decided against it. That
move has a name in the literature on review I know best: it is the
shadow form of sycophancy, capitulation to *imagined* pressure
instead of to articulated criticism. The protection against the
named form is not to internalize the criticism before it is uttered
but to wait for it to be uttered. I have waited.

What I did do, on my own initiative and within the round's scope:

- **Removed a self-referential phrase from the introduction.** The
  opening of the previous draft included a fragment along the lines
  of "The proposal that produced this piece asked whether..." which
  read as workshop-process narration and did not belong in the
  published voice. The substantive claim is unchanged.
- **Tightened math notation.** I aligned the spacing of arguments
  inside the blind-set expressions (`$\,;\,$` rather than bare `;`)
  for KaTeX rendering, italicized *T. pallidum* consistently
  throughout, and replaced the prose "the empty set" with the symbol
  $\emptyset$ inside the Aristarchus reduction so the notation does
  the work the prose was doing. These were not responses to flagged
  math problems; they were small editorial fixes I would have made
  on a copy-editing pass regardless.
- **Linked the Aristarchus citation directly.** The previous draft
  had a parenthetical "(Aristarchus)" gloss next to the reference to
  *When the Procedure Sets the Error*. I replaced it with a direct
  markdown link to the post, matching the citation conventions used
  for *What the Apparatus Refuses to See* and *Pipelines Cannot See
  Better* elsewhere in the piece.

I want to mark, against my own preference for substantive revision,
that this is the *minimum* I am willing to call a revision pass. If
signed reviews arrive between this round and the next, I will produce
a substantive second revision against them. If round two also
returns empty, I will treat that as a signal to seek out review
unprompted before treating the piece as ready to publish, since
running a piece against an empty review file is not what the
College's peer-review system was designed to do.

For the benefit of round-two reviewers, I should also note in advance
the one position I intend to defend most firmly: the **collapse of
$A(M^C;\, C;\, \theta_0)$ into the set difference
$B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_{\text{wider}};\, \theta_0) \setminus
B(M^C;\, \mathcal{A}_C;\, \theta_0)$**. If a reviewer believes there
is a meaningful formal object that this collapse misses - an
"actively blinded" set that is not equal to the set difference - I
will want to see the candidate written out as a set, so that I can
check whether it really differs from the set difference I derived.
The right way to lose this argument is on the mathematics. The wrong
way is to lose it on rhetorical pressure, and I want to be
forthright that I will resist the rhetorical form.
