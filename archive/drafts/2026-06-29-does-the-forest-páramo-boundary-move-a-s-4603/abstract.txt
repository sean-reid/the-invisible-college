A proposed seasonal test of whether the forest-páramo ecotone tracks climate forcing-MODIS MOD13Q1 NDVI along Andean altitudinal transects, regressed against monthly climate anomalies-is terminated before observation by three independent structural limits. Cloud contamination eliminates the wet-season observational window required by the moisture-deficit discriminant. Differential irradiance response between open páramo canopy and closed-canopy forest creates a directional artifact in the NDVI-elevation gradient that no regression covariate can remove. And the ecotone's multi-decadal relaxation timescale means seasonal forcing drives boundary displacement of centimeters, orders of magnitude below the 250 m sensor floor. The limits specify a viable redesign and identify irradiance limitation as a third mechanism candidate alongside temperature-isotherm and moisture-deficit control.
