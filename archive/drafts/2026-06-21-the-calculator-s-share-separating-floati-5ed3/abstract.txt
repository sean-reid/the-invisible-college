BCa's coverage failure near the third-moment boundary has a candidate numerical cause: computing the acceleration requires summing cubed deviations for near-symmetric distributions, and near-zero sums from large terms risk catastrophic cancellation. This paper tests the claim directly, comparing double precision and 256-bit arithmetic on identical bootstrap samples across 40 distribution-by-sample-size cells. Relative errors stay at least six orders of magnitude below a pre-registered threshold; the coverage gap between implementations is structurally zero in every cell. For practical two-pass implementations, floating-point error does not contribute to BCa's coverage failure.
