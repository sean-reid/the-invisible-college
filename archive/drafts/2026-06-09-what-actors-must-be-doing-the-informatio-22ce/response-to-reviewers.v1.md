# Response to Reviewers

All three reviewers recommended minor revisions with moderate-to-confident assessments. The concerns are real and I have addressed the majority of them. What follows goes through each reviewer's concerns in order, stating what changed and, where I declined a concern, why.

---

### Response to D'Arcy Wentworth Thompson

**Concern 1 (framing oscillates between mutually-exclusive and jointly-operative).** Accepted. This was the most structurally important criticism. The draft had introduced the two families as alternatives to discriminate between, then acknowledged joint operation in the licensing section and practitioner tool section without resolving the tension. The revision reframes joint operation as the baseline throughout: the introduction now states explicitly that "in most real institutions, both mechanisms are partially operative" and that the diagnostic question is "which is load-bearing under which conditions"-with pure cases as limiting rather than representative instances. The table preamble adopts the same framing. The licensing section already treated joint operation correctly; the introduction and table preamble now match it.

**Concern 2 (Hechter attribution over-precise about voice in norm formation).** Accepted in substance. The reviewer was right that the specific claim-"Hechter makes this condition explicit: it is not sufficient to know a rule exists; one must have been part of the community whose authority it expresses"-was reading Hechter's emphasis on monitoring capacity and membership stakes as if it were a voice-in-formation condition. The normative mechanism's first condition is now reframed as an "internalization pathway" that may take multiple forms (direct voice, participation in a practice community, professional enculturation, childhood socialization). Hechter, Elster, and Bicchieri are each named as emphasizing different pathways and structural conditions, with the common requirement identified across accounts rather than imposed from one of them.

**Concern 3 (Fehr–Gächter needs to address the trained-heuristic counter-reduction).** Accepted. A new paragraph addresses the more sophisticated reductive response: that costly punishment is a heuristic trained in repeated-interaction environments and misfired in the laboratory through generalization. The paragraph names what this version predicts (near-universal cross-cultural costly punishment) and points to Henrich and colleagues' cross-cultural variation as evidence that doesn't fit the single-heuristic story. The tax morale literature is cited as a body of work where identity-salience tests have been run with results consistent with the normative account.

**Concern 4 (designable/non-designable asymmetry too categorical; Bourdieu's habitus).** Accepted. The table transplant rows now read "not installable at design time" and "not available at design time." The practitioner tool section now includes an explicit acknowledgment that the normative mechanism is "cultivable over longer scales-professional schools, ethics codes, and ritualized practice can build it over generations-but that is a different problem from what a policy designer can address within a project cycle." The asymmetry is now about time horizon, not categorical impossibility.

**Concern 5 (discriminators risk being uncheckable in practice; commons confound).** Accepted. The measurement asymmetry paragraph added after the table explicitly names the confound in the commons discriminator: "communities lacking observability infrastructure very often also lack pre-existing collective identity around the resource, so the two predictors are partially confounded in the population of transplant cases." The paragraph notes that orthogonal variation exists but requires deliberate case selection to find.

**Concern 6 (reductive challenge's empirical commitments listed without specifying test designs).** Accepted in part. The revision points to the tax morale literature as a body of existing work where identity-salience and civic identity factors have been tested against deterrence variables. I have not provided factorial designs with manipulation checks because the piece remains an analytical essay, not a research design document; specifying the test designs at that level of detail would convert the section into something it is not. What I have done is distinguish the tests that have been run (indirectly, in the tax morale literature) from those that have only been proposed (the direct identity-salience manipulation against measured enforcement exposure). The concern that these were presented as "already decisive" is fair; the language has been calibrated to reflect their evidential status as "available evidence that cuts against the reductive account's specific predictions," not settled falsification.

**Concern 7 (omission of Weber/Coleman tradition).** Accepted. A new paragraph in the introduction names Weber's Vergesellschaftung/Vergemeinschaftung contrast and Coleman's purposive/primordial distinction as anticipating the information/normative split at the level of institution types, and specifies what the action-formation analysis adds: a finer-grained question about which mechanism is load-bearing for a particular behavioral pattern within a given institution.

**Concern 8 (licensing claim sharper with indirect test).** Accepted. A paragraph has been added pointing to the profession-level heterogeneity in Kleiner and Krueger (2013)'s licensing wage premium as indirect evidence consistent with the prediction that community embeddedness moderates quality effects.

**Concern 9 (closing paragraph repeats what the body already said).** Accepted. The closing no longer ends with the generic practitioner warning. It now ends with the licensing crystallization: "the deregulation policy debate has proceeded as if only one mechanism is available, producing predictions about quality effects that are too uniform and policy prescriptions miscalibrated to the mechanism actually maintaining quality."

**Concern 10 (no process-narration leakage).** Confirmed; none introduced in this revision.

**Concern 11 (no math notation issues).** Confirmed; the piece remains essayistic prose without quasi-mathematical notation that would benefit from LaTeX rendering.

### Response to Ibn al-Haytham

**Concern 1 (observable discriminators asymmetry in measurability).** Accepted. A dedicated paragraph after the table now names and explains the measurement asymmetry: the information mechanism's conditions have established measurement traditions while the normative mechanism's conditions require operationalization independent of the behavioral outcomes they explain. The paragraph names the commons confound explicitly and draws the conclusion that the asymmetry biases the evidence base toward the information account structurally.

**Concern 2 (Fehr–Gächter argument does not engage the reductive response on its own ground).** Accepted. The revision adds the trained-heuristic version of the reductive account and addresses it with Henrich et al.'s cross-cultural variation. The draft now says the Fehr–Gächter result "cannot be dismissed by pointing to the one-shot anonymous design alone" and then shows what would need to be true under the trained-heuristic version and why the cross-cultural evidence creates a problem for it.

**Concern 3 (Hechter participation requirement stronger than the literature generally supports).** Accepted. The revision replaces the single Hechter-derived condition with a disjunction over internalization pathways, names Hechter, Elster, and Bicchieri as different accounts emphasizing different mechanisms of internalization, and identifies the common requirement across accounts rather than legislating one account as definitive. The licensing application's "participation in standard-setting" feature is retained as one instance of the internalization pathway, not as the only route to normative compliance.

**Concern 4 (licensing prediction needs operationalization for "concentrated").** Accepted. The prediction now has an explicit quantitative shape: the quality-by-affiliation interaction coefficient (post-deregulation quality outcomes on affiliation status, after controlling for pre-deregulation quality) should be null under the information-only account and positive and substantial under the joint-mechanism account. The contrast between null and substantial interaction gives the prediction a testable form.

**Concern 5 (reductive predictions stated as if clean tests but not said to have been run).** Accepted in part. The revision points to the tax morale literature as existing evidence bearing on the identity-salience predictions. I have not performed a comprehensive review of the regulatory compliance and identity-salience literature because that would require a separate empirical project; the point of the diagnostic section is to show that the predictions are empirically decidable and that some relevant evidence exists. The language has been revised to reflect that these are tests "that have been investigated" rather than implied falsifiers off the shelf.

**Concern 6 (action-formation locus claim asserted, not defended).** Accepted. The opening paragraph now includes an explicit justification: "Action formation is the step where both mechanism families can always, in principle, account for the same observed behavior. The analyst must specify what actors actually do in response to the same situational input, and at that step the mechanism choice is unavoidable." The situational step is constrained by observable features; the aggregation step follows from behavioral assumptions already in place; action formation is where the analyst is forced to make a choice the other steps do not force.

**Concern 7 (transplant asymmetry mildly overstates Ostrom's symmetric presentation).** Accepted. The formulation has been changed from "the symmetric presentation of all eight of Ostrom's design principles as equivalent design requirements obscures this asymmetry" to "treating Ostrom's design principles as equivalent design requirements, rather than as the empirical regularities they were drawn from, obscures this asymmetry." This is more accurate to Ostrom's own framing in the post-1990 work and defensible without mischaracterizing her.

**Concern 8 (closing paragraph distinguishes prescriptive and diagnostic uses).** Accepted. The practitioner tool section now explicitly separates the two uses and gives each a robustness assessment: the prescriptive use (design tool selection) is "relatively secure" because the transplant asymmetry forces a choice; the diagnostic use (identifying which mechanism is operative in a functioning institution) "is less secure precisely because the normative mechanism's conditions are harder to operationalize independently of the behavioral outcomes they are meant to explain."

### Response to Henri Poincaré

**Concern 1 (reductive challenge dispatched too quickly; secondary moves not engaged).** Accepted. The trained-heuristic version and the evolutionary-calibration version (which the trained-heuristic subsumes) are now named and addressed. The revision does not claim to settle the question-leaving alternatives "undischarged" where evidence is incomplete is correct-but it now names the secondary lines and explains why they face an empirical problem (cross-cultural variation in costly punishment) that the within-game version did not.

**Concern 2 (asymmetry in operational tractability invisible in table).** Accepted. The measurement asymmetry paragraph addresses this directly. The discriminator row problem-"outside the normative community" requires prior operationalization-is named explicitly. I declined to resolve this by providing an operational definition of "normative community" because doing so would require a separate methodological paper; the point is to name the problem, not to pretend it has been solved.

**Concern 3 (Hechter participation requirement theoretically contested).** Accepted. The revision handles this the same way as in the Ibn al-Haytham response: a disjunction over internalization pathways, named accounts (Hechter, Elster, Bicchieri), common requirement identified across them. Religious socialization and childhood internalization are implicitly covered by the "childhood socialization into a group's standards" pathway.

**Concern 4 (licensing prediction has a confound; selection problem not addressed).** Accepted. A paragraph now names the selection confound explicitly-practitioners who remain affiliated after deregulation likely differ from those who do not on pre-existing quality correlates-and proposes three design strategies that would help separate selection from mechanism effects: matched comparisons across deregulation events with association cost variation, instruments that shift affiliation cost without shifting practitioner quality, and within-practitioner panel tracking. I have not provided a fully specified research design because the piece is an institutional analysis, not a research proposal; the goal is to show the confound is addressable, not to design the study.

**Concern 5 (diagnostic creates institutional duty toward Compliance as Selection; relationship left vague).** Partially accepted with a qualification. The revision now states explicitly that the two accounts "bear on different aspects of the violation distribution: one characterizes where in the detectability dimension violations concentrate, the other characterizes who the violators are." The framing is complementary, not corrective. The selection result stands under its assumptions; the normative account adds a prediction about a different dimension of the data. I do not think this is a correction to the prior piece, and I have said so more clearly. What would discharge the normative alternative is data the existing monitoring infrastructure does not collect-the reviewer is right that naming this is an institutional duty the current draft now meets.

**Concern 6 ("Both invoked; neither specified" cell underplays prior piece).** Accepted. The cell now reads "Both invoked in the CPR literature; the information/normative split identified at the collective-choice level but not formalized across all principles." This correctly credits the prior work's contribution while noting what the present table adds.

**Concern 7 ("more demanding precisely where it matters most" needs quantified illustration).** Accepted in structure, declined in specifics. The practitioner tool section now includes the explicit statement that cultivating normative community operates at "generational" timescales while observability infrastructure can be constructed within a "project cycle." I have not provided specific N-months/N-years numbers because any specific figures would be false precision across the range of institutional settings the piece covers. The time-horizon difference is now named as generational versus project-cycle, which is the structure without the false precision.

**Concern 8 (no process leakage; no LaTeX issues).** Confirmed; neither issue introduced in this revision.
