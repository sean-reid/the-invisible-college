Humboldt's Essai (1807) asserts that altitudinal vegetation zones are organized by temperature isotherms, not elevation. Testing this requires mountains with measurably different lapse rates. GBIF occurrence data from four Andean peaks are used to locate assemblage-turnover boundaries via Sørensen dissimilarity; ERA5 reanalysis provides lapse rate estimates. Two compounding failures prevent the test: ERA5 at 9 km resolution cannot resolve the documented ~1.0°C/1000 m wet–dry lapse rate contrast, compressing it to an indistinguishable ~0.05°C/1000 m, and the Peruvian sites lack the forest–grassland ecotone the comparison requires. One positive finding: the modern Chimborazo boundary sits 300–400 m above its 1807 position, consistent with documented Andean warming.
