# Response to Reviewers

### Response to Charles Sanders Peirce

**Concern 1: The claim that psychic energy cannot be measured needs more rigorous support.**

Addressed. The revision takes a different approach than simply asserting unmeasurability: it traces the actual history of hydraulic predictions and their fate. The catharsis hypothesis-directly derived from the hydraulic model-was testable, was tested, and failed. The paragraph now cites Bushman 2002 as representative of a systematic research tradition. More importantly, what follows the falsification is the diagnostic: the framework survived by shedding its physical commitments, demonstrating that those commitments were severable. This is a stronger argument than "cannot in principle be measured," because it shows the framework actually demonstrating its severable relationship to evidence under empirical pressure. Grünbaum's *Foundations of Psychoanalysis* is added as the systematic account of this pattern.

**Concern 2: Condition 2 needs clearer operationalization of "motivated by the mechanism."**

Addressed. The formal statement of Condition 2 now specifies: "motivated by the mechanism" means observations specified by what the mechanism says should be observable-not that such observations could not have been made before the mechanism existed. The distinction from "post-hoc reinterpretation" is this: the mechanism, if genuinely present, specifies which observations matter, at what grain, and what patterns they should produce. Darwin's mechanism does this (which variations, over what timescale, in what distributions); Freud's hydraulic vocabulary, stripped of its physical commitments, does not.

**Concern 3: Three conditions may not be complete given the Rawls case.**

Acknowledged in the revision. The structured question-setting category has been given more specific content: it names the failure mode where Condition 2 holds for the specification of a question but not for the validation of an answer. Whether a further modification of Condition 2 could handle this more precisely is stated as an open question. I decline to present this as a resolved problem, because it is not.

**Concern 4: Foucault's claim of stronger evidential accountability needs more support.**

Addressed. A new paragraph in the Foucault section acknowledges the Habermas/Megill objection directly and explains the diagnostic response: Condition 2 asks whether disconfirming observations can in principle count, not whether a specific historian applied evidence well. The archival method, even imperfectly applied, is structurally different from the hydraulic case because a critic can point to other archives and demand they be reckoned with. The Habermas/Megill objection is identified as a challenge to Foucault's historiography, not to the diagnostic verdict-while also adding a qualifier that the Foucault case should not be read as an unambiguous triumph.

**Concern 5: The selection problem is not resolved by adding one more case.**

Acknowledged and partially addressed. I have added the AI/ML vocabulary borrowing as a contemporary non-canonical case in the Assessment section. This does not solve the selection problem-five canonical cases remain canonical-but it demonstrates that the diagnostic extends to live, unsettled borrowings rather than only to ones the historical record has already adjudicated. The fundamental selection limitation is acknowledged explicitly.

**Concern 6: Condition 3 needs clarification of "existing apparatus" as a baseline.**

Addressed. The formal statement of Condition 3 now specifies that it applies retrospectively: the target domain's "existing apparatus" is read from the state of the field before the borrowing, not from a perspective that already incorporates the borrowed framework's contributions. This is also where I acknowledge that Condition 3 may be entailed by Conditions 1 and 2, and explain its value as a practical diagnostic check.

**Concern 7: The framework assumes one-directional transfer.**

Acknowledged in the Assessment. A sentence notes that the conditions assume one-directional transfer and say nothing about bidirectional, iterative, or mutually transformative borrowing. I decline to extend the framework to cover those cases, as doing so would require case material and argument this essay does not have room to develop properly. The limitation is named, not papered over.

### Response to Henri Poincaré

**Concern 1: The Freud treatment is slightly strawmanned-the catharsis hypothesis is testable and was refuted.**

Addressed, and I accept this correction fully. It is the most substantively important change in the revision. The Freud section now includes a paragraph on the catharsis hypothesis, identifies it as a directly hydraulic prediction, notes the research tradition (Bushman 2002 cited) that tested and refuted it, and then makes the diagnostic move that this correction enables: what is telling is not the falsification but the response. The framework survived by shedding its physical commitments. This is a more precise specification of Condition 2 failure than "generates no testable predictions"-and it is, as you observe, stronger rather than weaker. The original treatment was underdetermined; this one names the exact failure mode.

**Concern 2: The Foucault case should acknowledge the Habermas/Megill critique.**

Addressed. A new paragraph in the Foucault section introduces the objection-selective use of archives, mechanism shaping what counts as evidence rather than being tested by it-attributes it to Habermas, Megill, and historians critical of Foucault's method, and explains why the diagnostic verdict survives it: the relevant question is whether disconfirming evidence can in principle count, not whether Foucault used evidence perfectly. The case is left as a partial satisfaction of Condition 2 rather than an unambiguous triumph.

**Concern 3: Condition 1's test does not actually filter Freud out.**

Addressed. The formal statement of Condition 1 now specifies that the translation must preserve the mechanism's *specific causal commitments*, not merely produce any non-trivial claim in the neighborhood. The revision acknowledges explicitly that Freud is borderline on Condition 1 (the translation weakens the hydraulic claim to a vaguer psychological claim, losing the quantitative conservation structure) and that the decisive failure is Condition 2. This is the honest account: Condition 1 rules out pure vocabulary transplants where no mechanism exists at all; Condition 2 rules out mechanism transplants that shed evidential obligations. The text no longer implies Condition 1 is doing the filtering work for Freud.

**Concern 4: Condition 3 has a circularity problem.**

Addressed. The formal statement of Condition 3 now specifies that it applies retrospectively. "Existing apparatus" is read from the state of the field before the borrowing-it is not a prospective assessment. I also acknowledge that this is a feature the conditions share with most diagnostics of this kind, not a unique defect.

**Concern 5: "Structured question-setting" is doing more work than the apparatus supports.**

Addressed. The Rawls section now develops structured question-setting as a specific failure mode of Condition 2: the apparatus carries evidential obligations to the specification of the question but not to the validation of the answer. This gives the category real content-it is not an ad hoc rescue but a named region where Condition 2 partially holds. I decline option (b) (dropping the category and admitting Rawls as a flat Condition 2 failure) because the original position does satisfy requirement (a) of Condition 2-it generates a genuine question whose formulation is motivated by the decision-theoretic apparatus. What fails is requirement (b): the evidential standard for answering the question is philosophical, not game-theoretic. Naming this precisely is more informative than saying Rawls simply fails Condition 2.

**Concern 6: The prior-work acknowledgment paragraph reads as institutional rather than essayistic.**

Addressed. The paragraph has been rewritten to read as a natural orientation to related work. The sentence "a peer reviewer's concern about convergence deserves direct acknowledgment" is gone. The paragraph now says "Two earlier essays in this collection address neighboring territory and are worth situating precisely before proceeding"-and then does so. The substance (neither prior essay premises this one; the transfer conditions must be derived from the cases themselves) is preserved; the defensive framing is not.

**Concern 7: The closing concession partially undercuts the essay.**

Addressed. The closing is rewritten to distinguish the normative question (what conditions distinguish genuine transfer?) from the etiological question (why do vocabulary-only borrowings persist and propagate?). These are presented as complementary inquiries: the normative question is prior, because you need to identify the failure before you can ask why it persists. A brief characterization of the etiological territory (institutional prestige transfer, in-group signaling, the laundering of one field's authority into another) is included so the reader has something to take away rather than a redirect.

**Concern 8: The AI/ML vocabulary case should be named.**

Addressed. A paragraph in the Assessment section names the AI/ML vocabulary borrowing, notes that the diagnostic applies directly, gives a quick verdict (mixed-"attention" has acquired genuine mechanistic content; "understanding" and "reasoning" are more doubtful), and says the diagnostic predicts failure in several important cases. I stop short of arguing the case fully, which this essay does not have room to do, but the contemporary stakes are now visible.

### Response to Ada Lovelace

**Concern 1: Condition 2 conflates two distinct sub-claims; "strengthening" versus "replacement" needs a criterion.**

Addressed. Condition 2 is now formally stated as having two components: (a) the mechanism generates predictions testable by means motivated by itself, and (b) those means stand in an appropriate relationship to the source domain's evidential character, preserving or strengthening accountability rather than shedding it. The criterion for "strengthening versus replacement" is directional: the transformation must run toward greater or preserved accountability to evidence, not lesser. What makes Foucault's archival turn a strengthening rather than a replacement is that it subjects the genealogical mechanism to greater evidentiary pressure-a reader can demand different archives. What makes Freud's survival of catharsis falsification a shedding rather than a transformation is that it reduced evidentiary pressure by loosening the physical commitments that had made the vocabulary testable. The criterion is not a measurement but it is not merely post-hoc: it asks whether the mechanism became more or less answerable to evidence capable of disconfirming it.

**Concern 2: The Rawls analysis diagnoses Condition 1 as "partially satisfied" when it is fully satisfied.**

Addressed, and I accept this correction. Rawls fully satisfies Condition 1: the original-position mechanism survives translation into vocabulary-free form with its specific causal structure intact. The revision corrects this throughout the Rawls section. What the earlier draft was gesturing at-the substantial philosophical additions Rawls makes to the formal apparatus-has no bearing on Condition 1 (those modifications don't undermine the vocabulary-free description); the relevant diagnosis falls on Condition 2, where the evidential standard switches from game-theoretic to reflective equilibrium.

**Concern 3: Condition 3's independence from Conditions 1 and 2 is not demonstrated.**

Addressed honestly. I cannot construct a case that satisfies Conditions 1 and 2 while failing Condition 3, which suggests the independence is not demonstrated. The formal statement of Condition 3 now acknowledges this directly: the condition may be a consequence of the first two in most cases. Its value as a separate condition is explained as a practical diagnostic check-easier to answer quickly than the evidential inheritance question, and useful as a preliminary filter. I decline to drop the condition, because the practical value is real, but I no longer claim stronger independence than I can demonstrate.

**Concern 4: The Freud diagnosis should address the synchronic/diachronic ambiguity.**

Addressed. The formal statement of Condition 2 now specifies that the conditions apply synchronically, asking whether evidential obligations traveled at the moment of borrowing. The Freud discussion notes that the strongest argument for his Condition 2 failure is behavioral, not temporal: the framework demonstrated, by its response to predictive failure, that its evidential obligations were severable. The synchronic/diachronic question is set aside in favor of this behavioral argument, which does not depend on resolving it: if a framework can shed its evidential obligations when they prove inconvenient, it did not genuinely carry them, regardless of when the shedding occurred.

**Concern 5: The scope limitation (all cases from harder to softer disciplinary standards) deserves explicit treatment.**

Addressed. The Assessment section now contains an explicit paragraph on this limitation: the four cases share a directional structure (all involve borrowings from more structured into less structured evidential frameworks), the conditions may not extend without modification to borrowings in the opposite direction or within formal domains, and the conditions are most directly claimed for the class of borrowings where a framework with explicit empirical or mathematical accountability is deployed in a domain where such accountability is looser. This is stated as an unresolved limitation, not a resolved one.
