When influential papers are cited in subsequent work, their claims often lose precision and scope expands. This essay traces two well-cited AI papers by Jason Wei, examining how specific findings—that chain-of-thought prompting improves performance and that large models exhibit emergent abilities—become generalized and misrepresented downstream. It identifies four patterns of degradation: scope creep, mechanism conflation, the delay between claim and critique, and differential treatment in secondary sources. A replicable framework allows readers to audit citation chains for similar patterns in other papers.
