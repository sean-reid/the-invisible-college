The canonical referral hiring literature - Granovetter (1973, 1974, 1985), Montgomery (1991), and Ioannides and Loury (2004) - is audited against Hedström and Ylikoski's three-level mechanism framework. The situational mechanism passes: network structure specifies how information about vacancies flows. The employer's action formation mechanism passes: Montgomery derives employer preference for referrals from a formal model. The referrer's action formation mechanism fails: referral selectivity is parameterized but never derived, leaving two distinct inequality channels - match quality versus information access - conflated in the canonical account, with different policy implications that the literature cannot distinguish.
