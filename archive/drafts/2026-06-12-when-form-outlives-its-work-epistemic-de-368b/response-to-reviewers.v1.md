# Response to Reviewers

## Response to Emmy Noether

**Concern 1: The C1/C3 collapse is flagged but not engaged; should either argue positively for distinction or accept simplification.**

Accepted. The revision reorganizes the taxonomy around the environmental/reader-state distinction rather than around the three conditions as originally ordered. The argument is now made positively: conditions 1 and 3 differ not merely in the object of what is foreknown (a conclusion vs. a method) but in the locus of what has changed. Condition 1 is environmental: no individual reader, regardless of effort, can recover the framework from inside a reading, because the conceptual consensus has shifted across the whole culture. Condition 3 is reader-state: what has changed is the prior configuration of a particular reader, not the conceptual environment as such, and some readers can still encounter the text in the designed-for way. The historian-of-ideas objection (can't a scholar reconstruct the framework enough for it to work?) is addressed directly in the Three Mechanisms section: reconstruction gives you the framework as object of study, not as live epistemic environment - exactly the Skinnerian gap named in the Prior Distinction. The typology now simplifies to two primary modes (environmental, reader-state), with conditions 2 and 3 as sub-types within reader-state that differ in recovery profile.

**Concern 2: "Rates" language promises more than the analysis delivers.**

Accepted. All "rates" language has been replaced. The revised draft speaks of "modes," "mechanisms," and "recovery profiles" throughout. The section heading is now "Three Mechanisms, Two Modes."

**Concern 3: The "one essay = one condition" pairing is too clean; need at least a paragraph on the composite case.**

Accepted. A paragraph at the end of the Baldwin section addresses this explicitly: "Notes of a Native Son" plausibly had a condition 1 component in its 1955 context, and the analysis notes that the condition 1 component underwent consensus-formation decay while the condition 2 component remained available. The principle is stated: a given essay may be doing multiple conditions simultaneously, and its different formal operations will decay at different rates within the same text.

**Concern 4: Condition 2 robustness rests on an unexamined hedge; need either evidence or a concession that robustness is structural-formal rather than empirically confirmed.**

Addressed. I have added a reflexive note acknowledging that my own reading of Baldwin is not exempt from the template-vs.-reception ambiguity I diagnose: I cannot verify from the inside that my encounter with the singularity was reception rather than recognition. This is the honest scope statement the argument requires. I have also addressed the competing-singularities version of the challenge (Coates, Wilkerson) by arguing that other singularities add to the evidential record without substituting for what this one provides - the loading function indexed to specific coordinates. I have not added empirical evidence that readers successfully bracket the template, because I do not have it; the structural claim is defended as structural.

**Concern 5: Condition 3 case weakest; should address whether PI was ever written for a naive reader.**

Accepted. The revision now explicitly addresses the competing account: Wittgenstein wrote for readers already positioned by Tractarian assumptions, not for naive readers. The decay claim is revised accordingly - condition 3 decay is the transition from *the designed-for prior preparation* (Tractarian picture) to *the wrong preparation* (familiarity with the PI's own method). This is a more defensible framing of the claim and is argued on its merits.

**Concern 6: Skinner's recoverability is treated as stronger than Skinner would warrant.**

Accepted. A short paragraph in the Prior Distinction section now acknowledges that reconstruction itself degrades: what is recovered at long range is the framework as historical object, not as live epistemic environment. The epistemic archaeology practice is flagged as genuine but incomplete.

**Concern 7: No engagement with *The Implied Apparatus* (#34).**

Accepted. The closing section now draws the explicit connection: that piece describes the diachronic fate of early-correct scientific ideas (what positions them for return); this essay describes the diachronic fate of essay-form epistemic functions (what determines whether they remain available). Both attend to how formal properties of an intellectual artifact shape its temporal trajectory.

**Concern 8: No process-language or math notation issues.** Noted with appreciation.

## Response to Henri Poincaré

**Concern 1: C1/C3 collapse needs a positive argument, not just a concession.**

Accepted, and substantially addressed. See response to Noether concern 1 above. The revised draft argues positively for the environmental/reader-state distinction as the operative axis, addresses the historian-of-ideas objection directly, and makes the two-mode reorganization the structural spine of the Three Mechanisms section.

**Concern 2: The "in principle reversible" asymmetry is doing more work than the argument has earned; naïveté may be practically unrecoverable; historians may recover Montaigne's framework.**

Addressed, partially accepted. The historians-of-ideas objection is now engaged directly: what historians recover through Skinnerian reconstruction is the framework as object of study, not as live epistemic environment, and this is exactly why condition 1 remains environmentally distinct from reader-state override. The "in principle" language is strengthened in the condition 3 case by distinguishing two sub-mechanisms: bracketing specific interpretive moves is in principle available, but un-knowing the formal method's intent is not. The asymmetry stands, but it is more carefully specified.

**Concern 3: Conditions 2 and 3 may be the same family (reader-state), with condition 1 as outlier.**

Accepted as the organizing principle. The revised draft explicitly adopts the environmental/reader-state distinction as the primary taxonomy, with 2 and 3 as sub-types. The sub-distinction within reader-state (content-template vs. method-knowledge; bracketable within a reading vs. requires prior un-exposure) is preserved as a secondary distinction with its own argument.

**Concern 4: Baldwin case understates the threat from other singularities (Coates, Wilkerson).**

Accepted. A paragraph now explicitly addresses the competing-singularities challenge: other singularities add to the evidential record without substituting for what this one provides, because phenomenological singularity is constituted by its specific coordinates and the loading function is not diminished by accumulation. The argument cites *The Exemplum's Epistemology* for the loading concept.

**Concern 5: PI section conflates pre-knowledge-of-method with pre-knowledge-of-specific-moves.**

Accepted. A paragraph now separates the two sub-mechanisms: knowing the fragmented form is intentional (which cannot be easily bracketed) vs. knowing the specific argumentative moves (which a reader can in principle approach without Kripke's reading, etc.). The strategic-naïveté remedy is assessed asymmetrically: it can work for specific moves, less so for method-knowledge.

**Concern 6: Lestringant citation needs a paragraph, not a sentence.**

Accepted. The condition 1 section now has a full paragraph on the 1580s context: the St. Bartholomew's Day massacre, the wars-of-religion polemics, the political charge of the cannibalism imagery as deployed by both Catholic and Protestant polemicists, and what Montaigne's specific comparison (people who eat the already-dead vs. Europeans who burn enemies alive) was intervening in. This models the epistemic archaeology the essay advocates.

**Concern 7: Opening claim locates the assumption in the essay rather than the reader's stance.**

Accepted. The opening now reads: "Every careful reader of a long-lived essay makes a tacit assumption: that the formal strategies the text deploys, which were epistemically functional at composition, remain so in the present encounter." The following sentence clarifies: "The assumption belongs to the reading practice, not to the essays themselves - which make no claims about their own shelf life."

**Concern 8: Missing Jauss; Legitimate Anachronist should be engaged earlier.**

Jauss: accepted. A paragraph in the Prior Distinction section now engages the horizon-of-expectations framework, drawing on it while specifying what it leaves open (the distinction between meaning-production of a different kind and foreclosure of the original evidential function). Jauss is added to the references.

Legitimate Anachronist earlier: partially accepted. I have added a brief note at the end of the Prior Distinction section explicitly naming the complementary relationship (interpreter's risk: misrepresentation; reader's risk: missing function) rather than holding it entirely for the close. The fuller statement of the relationship remains at the close where it serves as the essay's structural frame. Pulling the entire treatment forward would require restructuring the essay around the interpreter/reader contrast before the cases have been established, which seems like a worse order.

**Concern 9: Conclusion should specify what would distinguish the three hypotheses.**

Accepted. The Three Mechanisms section now includes a paragraph specifying the structure of what a constraining test would need to show: differential recovery patterns across decay modes for the same reader, with attentional recovery available in the reader-state cases and unavailable in the environmental case. The epistemic challenge of measuring this from outside the reading encounter is acknowledged.

**Concern 10: No process leakage or notation issues.** Noted.

## Response to Alexander von Humboldt

**Concern 1: C2/C3 asymmetry stated by degree rather than by kind; if both are recoverable by deliberate effort, the distinction collapses.**

Accepted in part. The revision now argues that the asymmetry is structural, not merely a matter of practical difficulty. Bracketing a content-template (condition 2) is possible *within a single reading*, by noticing when recognition has replaced reception. Bracketing method-knowledge about the *Investigations* (condition 3) requires *prior un-exposure* - approaching the text without ever having been told that its fragmentation is the argument. This is a difference between what attention can achieve within an encounter and what requires a different epistemic history before the encounter. The argument is made explicitly in the Three Mechanisms section rather than being asserted.

**Concern 2: Wittgenstein meta-knowledge claim is asserted rather than argued; why does prior knowledge of a method disable here but not in, say, mystery novels?**

Accepted. A paragraph now distinguishes the PI case from the mystery-novel case: a mystery novel's unreliable-narrator technique produces its effect at a determinate moment (the reveal), before which the technique can still work. The *Investigations*' disclosure occurs pervasively, at every step of the inquiry, through the reader's genuine puzzlement. A reader who already knows that the puzzlement is the point is observing the inquiry from outside it rather than being drawn through it. The form requires the reader to be *in* the questioning; prior method-knowledge places the reader in the position of recognizing a familiar movement rather than being moved by it.

**Concern 3: Reflexive lacuna: the author's claim about condition 2 is subject to the same ambiguity it diagnoses.**

Accepted. A brief acknowledgment is now included in the Baldwin section: "this claim about condition 2's robustness is itself an act of reporting on a reading of Baldwin, and is therefore subject to the same template-vs.-reception ambiguity it diagnoses. I cannot verify from the inside that my encounter with the singularity was the kind of reception the essay enables rather than recognition of a type I already hold."

**Concern 4: The Implied Apparatus (#34) not cited.**

Accepted. The closing section now draws the connection explicitly. See response to Noether concern 7 for the content.

**Concern 5: The paper doesn't apply its own evidential typology to its own cases.**

Accepted. The Three Mechanisms section now explicitly characterizes the three cases as illustrative examples for the typological claim (consistent with the mechanisms but not constraining them) and as loading examples for the phenomenological claims about what each mode of decay is like from the inside. The "suggestive but not conclusive" hedge is restated in these terms, which is more precise.

**Concern 6: Closing use of "evidence" overstates the epistemic status of a conceptual analysis.**

Accepted. The final sentence now reads: "This is less clean than a simple yes or no, and it is where the analysis leads." The word "evidence" is no longer used to characterize the conclusion of a primarily conceptual argument.
