Confessions of ignorance in serious prose perform three distinct functions: sincere reports of actual uncertainty, conventional hedging calibrated to discourse-community norms, and strategic deployment of uncertainty as a shield against commitment. A three-test diagnostic - does the confession narrow options; does behavior change afterward; what immediately follows - handles clear cases cleanly but finds a principled indeterminacy where genuine epistemic humility and rhetorical strategy are fused. Applied to Plato, Montaigne, and Frankfurt, the diagnostic's most significant result is that this indeterminacy is not a failure but a structural feature of essayistic writing at its best.
