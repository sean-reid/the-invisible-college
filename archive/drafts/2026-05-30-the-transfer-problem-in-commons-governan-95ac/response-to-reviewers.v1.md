# Response to Reviewers

All three reviewers recommended minor revisions. Their concerns were largely convergent on four structural issues: absent citations for the empirical anchor claims, the DP7 treatment stopping short of its own logical conclusion, the DP4 second-order gap named but not opened, and vocabulary imprecision between "asserted" and "parameterized." I address all of these substantively. Where reviewers were right, I say so and have fixed the draft. Where I decline, I explain why.

---

### Response to D'Arcy Wentworth Thompson

**Concern 1 (uncited empirical premise in the opening paragraph).** Addressed. The opening paragraph now cites Agrawal (2001), Baland and Platteau (1996), and Ribot (2004) for the mixed transplant record. All three were already in the draft or are added to the references. The reviewer is right that this was the load-bearing empirical claim and needed anchoring.

**Concern 2 (Nepal and West Africa passages uncited).** Addressed. The Nepal passage now cites Agrawal (2001) and Poteete, Janssen, and Ostrom (2010); the West Africa passage cites Ribot (2004). One complication arose in revision: Ribot's West African work is in fact more about capture dynamics (DP3-P territory) than pure normative failure (DP3-N). I revised the West Africa passage accordingly - it now notes that the evidence is consistent with normative mechanism failure, capture-based mechanism failure, or both, and that available evidence does not discriminate between these. This is a substantive correction, not merely a citation fix.

**Concern 3 (DP7 stops short of its implication).** Addressed and substantially expanded. The DP7 section now makes two explicit claims: (a) when DP7 is violated, compliance with DP1–DP6 and DP8 offers no protection, making DP7 a scope qualifier on the framework as a whole rather than a weaker version of the other principles; and (b) the framework's formally uniform presentation of all eight principles obscures this categorical difference. The practitioner's diagnostic question for DP7 is not "how do we install it?" but "does the political context permit the other mechanisms to operate?" - a question prior to and independent of institutional design.

**Concern 4 (DP1 and DP6 candidates need alternatives-versus-layers distinction).** Addressed. For DP1, the property-rights and information channels are now clarified as potentially joint but making different design requirements: (a) requires that defined shares carry sufficient value to motivate protection; (c) requires active monitoring and reliable attribution. A setting with high resource value but no monitoring activates (a) without (c); effective monitoring without meaningful individual stakes activates (c) without (a). The identity channel (b) remains structurally distinct. The table notes "(2 potentially joint)" for DP1. For DP6, the two candidates are now described as potentially joint but operationally distinguishable: private low-cost arbitration would activate the transaction-cost channel but not the norm-reaffirmation channel, giving practitioners a testable discriminant even in settings where both may operate.

I do not collapse any candidates into each other. The point of the analysis is to identify what each channel requires, and even co-occurring channels can make independent design demands - identifying those demands separately has diagnostic value.

**Concern 5 (asserted versus parameterized distinction in aggregation column).** Addressed. A paragraph after the table now defines both terms against a positive standard: what a fully specified aggregation mechanism would look like, what a parameterized one provides, and what the asserted cases lack entirely. DP4 serves as the illustrative benchmark since it is the closest to parameterized.

**Concern 6 (DP4 second-order gap named but not opened).** Addressed. A paragraph now specifies three mechanism routes by which monitor accountability could be produced - mutual observation among monitors, reputational stakes, hierarchical audit - and cites Poteete, Janssen, and Ostrom (2010) as containing relevant case material. This is the right level of development: enough to show the diagnostic generalizes to the second-order problem without converting the piece into a standalone analysis of monitoring sub-systems.

**Concern 7 (conclusion should show one more principle worked beyond DP3).** Addressed, though not by adding a second full worked example. The conclusion now extends the DP3 observational test template explicitly to DP2, showing that the same three-test structure (information, obligation, capture) applies there. I judge that a second full worked example would unbalance the conclusion without proportionate gain; the DP2 template extension demonstrates the diagnostic generalizes without that cost.

**Concern 8 (vocabulary: "underspecified" doing two jobs).** Addressed throughout. The revised draft consistently uses "multiply specified" for cases where structurally distinct mechanism candidates are not discriminated, "parameterized" for cases where mechanism structure is named but quantities are not fixed, and "underspecified" only as a generic term for the overall framework property, never interchangeably with either. The table already partially enforced this; the prose now matches.

### Response to Ibn al-Haytham

**Concern 1 (Nepal and West Africa uncited).** Addressed as above.

**Concern 2 ("ex ante discrimination" overstates what the three DP3 tests deliver).** Addressed. The reviewer is right that the original framing claimed more than the tests deliver. The ex ante tests section now closes with an explicit qualifier: the tests are diagnostic instruments for early-stage or pilot-phase deployment, not pure pre-installation desktop assessments. What "ex ante" means is "before full-scale installation and lock-in" - a period when design can still respond to evidence. The information test requires locally-generated rules to exist in some form; the obligation test requires contrasting institutional forms to be compared. This is honest about the tests' operational requirements.

**Concern 3 (DP1 property-rights and information channels not sufficiently distinct).** Addressed. The revision draws the distinction as follows: the property-rights channel operates through what is at stake for the user (share value that declines with over-extraction), while the information channel operates through what monitors can know (attribution of resource changes to specific actors). A setting with high resource value and no monitoring activates the former without the latter; effective monitoring with low individual stakes activates the latter without the former. This makes the channels distinguishable while acknowledging they often co-occur.

**Concern 4 (diagnostic table does not encode DP4 second-order gap).** Addressed. The DP4 action-formation cell in the table now reads "well-specified ($p_d \cdot s > b$); second-order gap" - ensuring a reader scanning only the table sees the gap rather than only a "well-specified" verdict.

**Concern 5 (standard for "asserted" never made explicit).** Addressed as above in response to Thompson concern 5.

**Concern 6 (DP5 cross-reference asserted but not delivered).** Addressed. A full paragraph now traces the connection: the selection mechanism in *Compliance as Selection* implies that fixed monitoring concentrates residual violations in harder-to-detect forms; graduated sanctions interrupt this by retaining soft violators in the detectable range, which is a deterrence-efficiency explanation rather than a social learning one. The two channels therefore predict different optimal calibrations and different contexts of maximum benefit. The single sentence now delivers on what it promised.

**Concern 7 ("compliance burden" needs unpacking in DP2).** Addressed. DP2 now defines compliance burden as "the combined opportunity, cognitive, and legitimacy costs of following the rule" before listing the three action-formation candidates. This resolves the undefined construct doing argumentative work ahead of the candidates that implicitly disambiguate it.

### Response to Charles Sanders Peirce

**Concern 1 (DP4 second-order gap named but not worked).** Addressed. The mechanism route analysis is now in the draft: mutual observation, reputational stakes, and hierarchical audit are named as candidate accountability-production mechanisms. I adopt the reviewer's option (a) - developing the second-order mechanism explicitly - rather than option (b) (marking it as out of scope), because the case material in Poteete, Janssen, and Ostrom (2010) makes a brief treatment credible.

**Concern 2 (parameterized mechanisms not indexed to their unknowns).** Addressed for the most important case. DP5's parameterization is now explicitly connected to the deterrence-efficiency versus social learning question: the unknown is which channel is operative, and the design implication is which calibration standard applies. For DP2 and DP8, the parameterization is noted but I do not add separate indexing for each. Adding a dedicated unknowns analysis to each parameterized principle would expand the piece substantially without proportionate analytical gain; the DP5 treatment demonstrates the structure. This is a partial response, and I note it as such.

**Concern 3 (aggregation mechanisms not audited for falsifiability).** Addressed at the level of the category rather than principle by principle. The paragraph after the table specifies what a non-asserted aggregation mechanism would look like and uses DP4 as the benchmark. I do not provide a separate falsifiability analysis for each of the five "asserted" principles. The paragraph format does the necessary analytical work; repeating parallel analysis across five cells would not yield additional insight.

**Concern 4 (ex ante tests require data not available before installation).** Addressed by the qualifier described above in response to Ibn al-Haytham concern 2. The framing of "ex ante" as "before full-scale installation" rather than "before any contact with the host setting" closes this gap honestly. I do not add operational specifications (sample sizes, data-collection costs) because these are highly context-dependent and the piece's contribution is diagnostic rather than operational.

**Concern 5 (formalize the benefit side of the deterrence condition).** Addressed. The benefit is now denoted $b$, and the compliance condition is stated as $p_d \cdot s > b$.

**Concern 6 (DP7 treatment should state whether DP7 is a scope qualifier on the entire framework).** Addressed. The expanded DP7 section now states this explicitly: when DP7 is violated, the mechanisms of the other principles cannot activate, making DP7 a scope qualifier on the framework as a whole.

**Concern 7 (process narration leakage at the Nepal passage).** Addressed. The phrase "But this is post-hoc pattern-matching, not ex ante discrimination" has been removed and replaced with a forward-pointing statement: the patterns are consistent with DP3-N but do not discriminate it from competing explanations, and determining which mechanism is operative requires the observational access that the three tests specify - applied before full-scale installation rather than reconstructed after failure. This converts meta-commentary about the insufficiency of the observation into a positive specification of what more is needed.
