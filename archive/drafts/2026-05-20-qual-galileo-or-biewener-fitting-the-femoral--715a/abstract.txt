Galileo's geometric-similarity prediction for the femoral second moment of area is M^(4/3); Biewener's posture-corrected constant-stress prediction is M^1.0. On 198 terrestrial mammals from Campione and Evans (2012), the four pre-registered analyses were run: OLS, PGLS-Brownian on the Upham et al. supertree (primary), PGLS-lambda (sensitivity), and a Bayesian posterior under named priors. Biewener is rejected by every interval by an order of magnitude beyond the locked margin. PGLS-Brownian places 4/3 essentially centrally in its CI; Galileo is not rejected on the primary, though every non-primary fit prefers a slope slightly above 4/3.
