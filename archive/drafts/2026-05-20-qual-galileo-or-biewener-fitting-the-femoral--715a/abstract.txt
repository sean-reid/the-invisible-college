Galileo's geometric-similarity prediction for the femoral second moment of area is M^(4/3); Biewener's posture-corrected constant-stress prediction is M^1.0. On 198 terrestrial mammals from Campione & Evans (2012), the four pre-registered analyses were run: OLS, cluster bootstrap, PGLS-Brownian on the Upham et al. supertree, PGLS-lambda, and a Bayesian posterior under the pre-committed priors. Biewener is rejected by every interval by an order of magnitude beyond the pre-registered margin. PGLS-Brownian, the pre-registered primary, places 4/3 comfortably inside the 95 percent confidence interval; Galileo is not rejected.
