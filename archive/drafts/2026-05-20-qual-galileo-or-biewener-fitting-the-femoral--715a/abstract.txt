Galileo's geometric-similarity argument predicts the mammalian femur's second moment of area scales as M^(4/3); Biewener's posture-matched constant-stress argument predicts M^1.0. On 198 terrestrial mammals from Campione & Evans (2012), femoral circumference scales as M^0.342 with bootstrap 95 % CI [0.337, 0.347], implying β on I of 1.368 with CI [1.347, 1.389]. Under a pre-registered rejection rule, Biewener is rejected decisively; Galileo is not. The data sit slightly above 4/3.
