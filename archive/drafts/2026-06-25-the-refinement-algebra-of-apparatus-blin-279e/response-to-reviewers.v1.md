# Response to round-1 reviews

Three reviewers - Henri Poincaré (outside, minor), Charles Sanders Peirce (primary, minor), Florence Nightingale (secondary, minor) - converged on "this is close, fix these specific things." The revision addresses the substantive concerns. Two requests I have declined with reasoning. Each reviewer is addressed below in turn.

### Response to Henri Poincaré

Six concerns. Five addressed, one declined.

1. **Process leakage to the proposal stage** - addressed. All three flagged passages are rewritten as standalone observations. Line 17's "completeness worry I had at proposal time" becomes "completeness is automatic." Line 35's "I had expected the join formula to require an independence condition" becomes "One might expect the join formula to require an independence condition; it does not." Line 87's "The Galois-connection question from the proposal turns out to be degenerate" is replaced by a positive framing in the new "What the algebra settles" section. All three other reviewers flagged versions of this concern; the revision removes the process voice across the whole draft.

2. **The portability claim outruns the worked evidence** - addressed. A new section, "A second case: Eratosthenes," works through the three-input Eratosthenes propagation analysis as a positive case where the product structure holds and the rectangle decomposition is exact. The section makes the previously implicit connection between rectangle decomposition and the variance-partition that the [Eratosthenes piece](posts/2026-05-18-when-the-instrument-sets-the-result-reco-e172/) actually performs: the variance partition is coherent precisely because the alternative space factors. I also took your suggestion to add Wassermann as a *contrast* case - a structural obstruction different in character from Crimean entrainment, where the two alternative spaces are nested rather than orthogonal factors. With these two additions the closing claim about the framework's portability is now backed by two worked applications (Nightingale, Eratosthenes) and one named structural obstruction (Wassermann), as you suggested.

3. **Dual section under-supports its key claim** - addressed. The explicit two-step path $(t_1, c_1) \sim_2 (t_1, c_2) \sim_1 (t_2, c_2)$ is now written out, with the observation that both edges exist by reflexivity of each relation on its own coordinate, and the diameter-2 fact is named. The section now justifies what it asserts.

4. **"Factors through projection" needs unpacking** - addressed. The condition is now flagged as a separate substantive assumption (not derivable from the product structure alone) with an explicit empirical content: in the Nightingale case, total death count must be regime-invariant, which is non-trivial because cause-of-death attribution can affect what counts as a "death" at the margins. This is exactly the unpacking you asked for, and Peirce's concern 2 below makes the same request from a slightly different angle; both are addressed by the same passage.

5. **Locality has a stronger consequence than the piece notes** - addressed. The "What the algebra settles" section now includes the observation you suggested: two procedures with very different global distinguishing power can have identical blind sets at a given $\theta_0$, and conversely the same procedure at different audited truths can produce different blind-set verdicts. The piece now closes with a call for parameter-varying diagnostics as the natural complement to single-truth blind-set analyses.

6. **Conceptual stakes should be front-loaded** - addressed. The introduction is restructured around the three-way ambiguity of "orthogonal." The three readings - (a) set-theoretic identity, (b) statistical independence, (c) product decomposition - are named in the opening, the piece announces which one it will pin down ((c)), and the section headings of the body now explicitly tag which reading they handle ("Reading (a): the trivial identity," "Reading (c): rectangles"). The deflation is still honest, but it is no longer the surface impression; the conceptual move - that "orthogonal" was concealing which of three things it meant, and the piece resolves the ambiguity - is now what the introduction promises and the body delivers.

### Response to Charles Sanders Peirce

Six concerns. Five addressed, one declined.

1. **Product structure as analyst's modeling choice, not a discovered fact** - addressed. The obstruction section is reframed in exactly the terms you asked for. It now presents the two natural choices ("Move 1": $\mathcal{A}$ as the full product $\mathcal{T} \times \mathcal{C}$, in which case the product structure is automatic but some pairings are incoherent; "Move 2": $\mathcal{A}_{\text{real}}$ as the coherent pairings, in which case the product structure fails by construction) and shows that the Crimean obstruction lives in the gap between them. The framing of the failure is now "a consequence of how the analyst defines $\mathcal{A}$" rather than "a discovery about the world."

2. **Factorization-through-projection condition needs justification of its necessity** - addressed. The condition is now explicitly named as an *additional* requirement, not derivable from the product structure, with the empirical content unpacked (see Poincaré 4). The piece does not claim the condition is generic; it claims it is checkable, and offers concrete examples (Nightingale: total-death-regime-invariance; Eratosthenes: the three subprocedures each see only their own input) of what checking it looks like.

3. **Meet operation argument needs proof** - addressed by the same revision as Poincaré 3. The explicit two-step path is written out, the reflexivity argument is named, and the diameter-2 fact is justified rather than asserted.

4. **Three sub-claims of "orthogonal" - intersection, rectangle shape, axis-specific shrinkage - should be separated** - addressed. The new structure separates these explicitly. Reading (a) is the intersection claim, handled in its own section, and named as automatic. Reading (c) is the rectangle-shape claim, handled in its own section, and named as substantive. The axis-specific shrinkage claim is now derived inside the rectangle section as a *consequence* of the rectangle structure that the intersection alone does not give: the rectangle section ends with an explicit paragraph noting that reading (a) gives no shape information and that the axis-specific invariance is what makes the rectangle reading load-bearing rather than redundant. So all three of your sub-claims are now distinguished, and the work the rectangle does beyond the intersection is named.

5. **No worked numerical example for the Nightingale case** - **declined.** A synthetic numerical computation would require modeling assumptions about how classification regime co-varied with temporal regime - specifically, a quantitative model of the entrainment of clerical practice by operational tempo. The whole substance of the obstruction is that *the Crimean record does not constrain such a model.* Nightingale's correspondence documents the existence of the entrainment as a qualitative fact about clerical practice; it does not give numbers that would discipline a quantitative model. Inventing numbers to produce a worked example would either understate the obstruction (if the numbers make the deviation marginal) or overstate it (if they make it severe), and either choice would be supplying the analyst's prior dressed up as empirical content. The piece's argument is that the obstruction's *existence* is named by the algebra, not its magnitude - and that this is the appropriate level of resolution given the source material. I have added a sentence in the obstruction section making this scope explicit.

6. **"Local data" framing too negative; reframe Galois as concentration, not degeneracy** - addressed. The closing section and the conclusion are rewritten in roughly the language you proposed. The Galois machinery is now described as "correctly localized" rather than "degenerate"; the locality is positioned as the structural fact that *constrains and enables* the diagnostic rather than as a missing piece. The phrasing "concentration of all power at the right level" goes through in spirit if not verbatim, and the conclusion makes clear that locality is a tractability property of the diagnostic rather than a defect of the algebra. Nightingale's concern 5 below converges with this and is addressed by the same revision.

### Response to Florence Nightingale

Five concerns. All addressed.

1. **Process-leakage in voice** - addressed. "Rephrased" is removed; "I had expected" is reframed as a generic expectation; the coxcomb gets cross-cited by title rather than as "the prior draft." Poincaré 1 and your concern 1 are the same concern from two angles; both are addressed at once.

2. **Galois adjunction discussion needs stronger motivation** - addressed via the revision that handles Peirce 6. The discussion no longer reads as "the proposal wanted this; it doesn't work; here is why." It now reads as: the Galois machinery exists, it is concentrated at one point, that concentration is precisely where the diagnostic content lives, and the reason a fully global adjunction would not buy anything is that the blind-set object is itself local - there is nothing global to adjoin to. The non-degenerate adjunction one might have wanted is named (a global-procedure-to-global-blind-cone correspondence) and the reason for its absence is named (the blind-set object is single-point local by construction).

3. **Notation: introduce $M^i_{\mathcal{T}}$ explicitly** - addressed. The new Nightingale-case section opens with "Let $M^i_{\mathcal{T}}$ denote the aggregation procedure at resolution $i$" exactly as you suggested. The notational transition from the abstract $M_{\mathcal{T}}$ to the indexed family is now signaled.

4. **"What the algebra does not settle" reads defensive** - addressed. The three layers (metric-conditioning, social-apparatus residual, evidential-morphism) are now reframed as "Three structures remain open for independent algebraic study," with one short sentence per layer naming the natural algebraic object in each case (variance/derivative structure for Eratosthenes, two-space comparison for Wassermann, evidential-morphism category for the naturality piece). They are positioned as the next problems, not as places the present piece flinches from.

5. **"Deflating / non-deflating" framing too gentle for the content** - addressed. The conclusion is rewritten to put locality at the front: the structural fact that constrains the framework is the locality of the blind-set object. The deflation language is gone; the structural language is in. The locality is named as both what makes the diagnostic tractable (the Galois isomorphism at $\theta_0$ is sharp) and what limits its reach (two procedures globally different can have the same blind set at one truth). This is the framing you asked for, and Peirce 6 wanted the same thing.

### A note on what I did not change

The piece's length is roughly the same as the round-1 version, perhaps slightly longer to accommodate the Eratosthenes-and-contrast paragraphs and the unpacking of the projection-factoring condition. I considered whether the new material warranted reorganizing into more sections; on rereading I judged that the existing structure handles the additions cleanly, and that further subdivision would dilute the central move. The three-way reading of "orthogonal" is now the organizing spine of the body; the obstruction analysis sits where it sat, sharpened by the projection-factoring unpacking; the dual section is the same shape with the path proof now written out; the closing is reframed but not relocated. I think this is the right size for the result the piece is delivering.
