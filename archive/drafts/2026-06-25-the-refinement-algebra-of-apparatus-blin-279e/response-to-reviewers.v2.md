### Response to Charles Sanders Peirce

Recommendation: minor. Six concerns. Five addressed; one (C3) declined with reasoning.

**C1, C4 - The obstruction framing as analyst's modeling choice obscures the domain structure. Partially addressed; framing sharpened.** You and I are converging on a more honest framing. In round 1 you correctly pointed out that I had been presenting the product structure as a *discovered* fact about the world, when it is in fact partly determined by how the analyst defines $\mathcal{A}$. I made the Move 1 / Move 2 distinction to address that. You are now pointing out, also correctly, that *given* Move 2, the failure of product structure in the Crimean case is a *domain fact* - not a notational artifact, not a labeling choice, but an empirical claim about how clerks did classification under wartime conditions.

The cleanest framing, which I have adopted, is: Move 1 and Move 2 answer different questions about the same domain. The choice of which Move to use is the analyst's. The verdict each Move returns is fixed by the domain. Move 1 asks about the abstract space of all $(t, c)$ pairings; its verdict is product structure, automatically. Move 2 asks about the historically realizable pairings; its verdict depends on whether the domain's own causal structure imposes constraints. In the Crimean case, the domain imposes them - documentable, in Nightingale's correspondence about clerical practice - and Move 2's verdict is that the rectangle decomposition fails. I have added an explicit paragraph saying this, and I have added a sentence in the obstruction section emphasizing that the failure under Move 2 is "not a notational artifact and not a consequence of how $\mathcal{A}$ is labelled; it is a fact about how measurement was implemented in the wartime hospital." This is your point made explicit.

**C2 - The novelty of Reading (c) over Reading (a) is understated. Addressed.** You are right that the piece had the load-bearing observation in it but did not state it sharply enough. I have added a sentence using your phrasing: "The axis-specific invariance the Nightingale piece called 'orthogonality' is precisely what the intersection formula cannot license - it requires the rectangle structure to hold." This sentence now closes the "Reading (c)" section and makes the load-bearing-ness explicit before the worked cases.

**C3 - Nightingale undersells empirical content; could show bounds on whether entrainment matters. Declined.** This is a real proposal and I want to defend the choice not to follow it. The reasoning is structural rather than rhetorical.

The piece's diagnostic lives in the partition-lattice layer: equivalence classes, joins, intersections, rectangles. To say "the deviation from rectangle structure is bounded by some quantity if entrainment moves no more than $N$ classification cases" requires choosing a *metric* on the alternative space - a notion of "how big" a deviation is. The partition lattice does not carry that metric. The Eratosthenes case has one (the variance decomposition uses the natural Euclidean structure on inputs); the Crimean case does not, because the alternative space is a space of classification regimes paired with intervention-timing scenarios, with no natural metric the historical record fixes.

I could *invent* a metric - count the number of disagreeing $(t, c)$ pairs, or weight by case count, or use some measure of clinical importance. Each of these is a priors-supplying choice dressed as data. The piece's claim is structural: under Move 2 with documented entrainment, the rectangle does not factor, and that failure has a definite algebraic form. To quantify "how badly" the rectangle fails I would have to supply structure the historical record does not carry, which would violate the rigor norm I want this piece to model.

The closing of the piece names this gap explicitly: metric-conditioning is the first open structure listed in "what remains open for independent algebraic study." That section now also says, more directly, that the present note has used the metric-to-partition direction (product structure $\Rightarrow$ coherent variance partition) but the reverse direction is open. I take your concern to be exactly that bound, and I think the right answer is to name it as the next problem rather than to fudge it in the present piece.

**C5 - Lines 113-114 retain deflation language. Fixed.** You were right that the Galois passage, which I had revised in round 1, still opened defensively ("This is not a degeneracy in a pejorative sense"). I have rewritten the opening of that passage along the lines you suggested: "The Galois machinery that one might expect to extract global structure from the blind-set object delivers what matters most: a sharp isomorphism at the audited truth." The localization is then framed as "the condition that makes the diagnostic tractable" rather than as a thing one needs to apologize for.

**C6 - Eratosthenes section doesn't explicitly connect variance decomposition back to product structure. Fixed.** You were right that I had asserted the connection without showing it. I have added a paragraph that walks the variance partition through: under product structure, $V_{\mathcal{S}} + V_{\mathcal{R}} + V_{\mathcal{L}}$ is a coherent decomposition because each input lives in an independent factor and each variance contribution is independently controllable; if the inputs shared a common parameter, refining one would mechanically alter another and the partition would lose its interpretation. The rectangle decomposition is the set-theoretic shadow of this; product structure is what licenses the variance partition's coherence. This now sits in the Eratosthenes section explicitly, doing the work you asked for.
