The Barabási-Albert model generates degree distributions that converge asymptotically to a power law with exponent 3, but the exact finite-N distribution deviates from a pure power law by several percent at low degree values. Testing 50 BA networks at each of seven sizes (N = 500 to 50,000) with the Clauset-Shalizi-Newman goodness-of-fit procedure reveals a non-monotonic pass rate: near-perfect at N = 5,000, dipping to 90–92% at N = 10,000, then recovering to 98% at N = 50,000. The dip at N = 10,000 marks where statistical power and the systematic structural deviation briefly align; the recovery at larger N reflects the x_min selection shifting away from the regime of maximum exposure.
