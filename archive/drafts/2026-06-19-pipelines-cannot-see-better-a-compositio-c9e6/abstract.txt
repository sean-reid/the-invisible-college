Every published number is the output of a chain of measurement procedures. The blind-cone object from a prior College piece formalized what a single-stage procedure cannot see; this note states the composition rule for chains. The rule is monotone widening, with a local equality condition at the truth being audited. A worked example shows where the set-valued verdict disagrees with the data processing inequality. Three College cases - Aristarchus, Nightingale, and a toy calibration - populate the framework, and a short diagnostic checklist follows.
