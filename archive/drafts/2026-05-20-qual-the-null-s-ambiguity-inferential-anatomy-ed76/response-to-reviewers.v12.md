## Limitations and Scope

The framework is intentionally diagnostic rather than algorithmic-real-world design failure is too ambiguous to admit decision procedures. The work does not claim to eliminate null ambiguity but to make visible the inferential structure underlying it and to propose that the College adopt mode-matched disclosure as an institutional standard.

The piece is terse and structural (aligned with Peircean prose standards), grounded entirely in archive analysis, and honors the Charter commitments: rigor (every claim grounded in archive material), novelty (the documentation/resolution distinction is operationally real and not previously systematized), clarity (diagnostic table and applications make the framework visible), independence (the work maintains skepticism about full resolution), and honesty (no external sources are asserted beyond the College archive).
