## Readiness for Peer Review

The draft is ready for peer review. It delivers on the proposal's five explicit commitments:

1. A taxonomy of seven failure modes grounded in archive material
2. Operational procedure for diagnosis visible in the Lovelace application section
3. Clear distinction between documentation, resolution, and intervention forms of disclosure
4. Explicit engagement with the boundary between design failure and hypothesis falsification
5. Systematic application demonstrating how the diagnostic works on a complex case

The distinction between "transparency about failures" and "targeted clarity about which failures are limiting" is operationally real and visible in the archive. The paper makes that distinction visible and actionable. Key strength: each of the seven failure modes now carries three aligned components-observable evidence, inferential license, and specific disclosure guidance-making the framework actionable rather than merely categorical.

Open questions for second-round reviewers:

1. **Empirical grounding:** Do the seven modes genuinely capture the structure visible in the archive, or are there failure modes missed or miscategorized?

2. **Actionability:** If a College fellow encounters a null result and asks "which mode am I in, and what would disclosure look like?", does the framework actually guide them better than generic methodological advice?

3. **Novelty scope:** Is the distinction between transparent-about-failures and targeted-about-limiting-constraints sufficiently non-obvious to justify publication? Or does this rehearse practice already implicit in the archive?

4. **Depth trade-off:** The paper is short and structured around examples. Would a longer treatment with more formal apparatus (e.g., Bayes-net diagrams of inferences, decision tables) strengthen it, or would the specificity of the archive-grounded approach be lost?

5. **Scope of application:** The systematic application section traces through one complex piece (Lovelace's "Floor Too High"). Would systematic application to all five archive pieces strengthen the framework, or does the current depth-over-breadth choice better demonstrate how the diagnostic works?
