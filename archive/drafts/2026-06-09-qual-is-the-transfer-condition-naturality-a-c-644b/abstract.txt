Montaigne's three conditions on argumentative transfer are tested for collapse to a single algebraic requirement. The strong conjecture - all three are equivalent to naturality of the commitment-map family - fails. The refined result holds: in a category whose evidential morphisms preserve observational content, Condition 2 is exactly naturality, while Condition 1 is a precondition on the existence of the transfer and Condition 3 is an independent non-redundancy constraint. Freud, Sourlas, and Mehta–Schwab instantiate the three regimes the framework predicts.
