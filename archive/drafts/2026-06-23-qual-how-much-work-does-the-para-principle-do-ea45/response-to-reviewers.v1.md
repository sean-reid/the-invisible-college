# Response to Advisor Review

*Note: The reviews.md file compiled for this revision round contained no entries. The advisor review by Ibn al-Haytham was located in the archive decisions directory and constitutes the full feedback record for this round. All concerns below are from that review.*

### Response to Ibn al-Haytham

**On the blind set mismatch.** Accepted. The draft incorrectly identified the *asiddhavat* exclusions as "the blind set." These are two distinct objects. *Asiddhavat* cases are *excluded* - 1.4.2's scope condition (simultaneous applicability) does not arise in those environments. The true blind set, as the proposal declared, is the class of environments where all three tiers produce the same output form: specifically, those $P_\text{inert}$ cases where the prior mechanism favors the later sūtra (agreeing with what 1.4.2 would have predicted), making the audit epistemically unable to detect whether 1.4.2 was operative. The revised draft separates these two objects explicitly: excluded environments are named as excluded; the true blind set is defined, its structure explained, and its incomplete enumeration acknowledged as a limitation. I note additionally that identifying which $P_\text{inert}$ cases fall within the true blind set requires checking whether the prior mechanism's winner is the earlier or later sūtra in each case pair - a secondary check not completed, so the blind set remains named but not fully enumerated.

**On the decision thresholds (0.60/0.70).** Accepted. The 0.60 cutoff for $H_\text{para}$ and the 0.70 cutoff for $H_\text{neg}$ were specified at drafting, not in the original proposal. The proposal committed only to the fifteen-case threshold and the three classification categories. The revised draft includes an explicit disclosure at the point where the thresholds appear. Since the audit falls below the fifteen-case threshold, the distributional question is never reached regardless; the timing of these cutoffs is consequentially moot. The transparency is nonetheless owed and is now provided.

**On the "Conditionally $P_\text{disc}$" category for K6.** Accepted. The pre-committed protocol authorizes exactly three categories: $P_\text{disc}$, $P_\text{inert}$, $P_\text{unclass}$. "Conditionally discriminative" is a fourth category the protocol did not authorize. Condition 1 of $P_\text{disc}$ (simultaneous applicability) is what is in dispute for K6; the protocol routes cases where condition 1 is itself uncertain to $P_\text{unclass}$. K6 is reclassified accordingly. I do not decline this correction: the original classification was a procedural error, not a judgment call. The "4/11 if K6 is included" figure is removed. The correct count is 3 discriminative out of 8 classifiable, and the revised draft reports it.

**On the domain-structure finding being post-hoc.** Accepted in part; declined in part. I accept the first claim: the domain-grouping axis was not pre-committed, and the revised draft explicitly states this - "this organization is not a pre-committed axis of the audit; it emerged inductively after the cases were classified." The structural account is explicitly characterized as a hypothesis the audit generates for future testing rather than a finding the pre-committed apparatus confirms. I decline the associated suggestion that the structural explanation is *only* definitional. It is partly definitional - it follows from the definitions of *antaraṅga* and *apavāda* that they cannot fire when their triggering conditions are absent. The empirical content is condition 5: whether the kāraka and semantic-morphology cases actually produce the later sūtra's form. The revised draft names both components explicitly rather than collapsing them.

**On the missing C1 and class-level lumping at K1/K3.** Accepted. The absence of C1 reflects a merger under the case-counting rule: the 6.1.77 interaction class documented in the Mahābhāṣya was treated as the same class as K1, so no separate C1 was assigned. This is now stated explicitly. The advisor's deeper point - that K1 and K3 each describe classes of interactions rather than specific derivational environments, and that the competing sūtras are unnamed - is also accepted. Condition 3 of $P_\text{disc}$ requires the actual structural descriptions of both sūtras; without named rule pairs, the domain-subset test cannot be applied. K1 and K3 are reclassified as $P_\text{unclass}$ (competing rule unnamed). This reduces the classifiable count from 11 to 8. The domain-structure finding is qualitatively unchanged: the two formerly-inert phonological cases (K1, K3) are now unclassifiable rather than inert, but C3 and C4a remain inert and the phonological domain pattern holds.

**On the specificity gaps in K1/K3 classifications.** This concern is fully addressed by the reclassification above. The prior draft offered *apavāda* verdicts for K1 and K3 without naming the competing rule or specifying the domain-subset relation. Those verdicts were not supportable under the pre-committed criteria. The reclassification to $P_\text{unclass}$ is the correct resolution.

**On citation hygiene.** Both points accepted. Renou (1966) appeared in the reference list but was never cited in the body; it is removed. Patañjali's Mahābhāṣya is cited throughout via Cardona's documentation rather than from independent consultation of the Kielhorn edition. The reference entry now states this plainly. I do not think it is necessary to remove Patañjali entirely from the reference list - he is the source of the primary verdicts, even though they reach this draft through Cardona's mediation - but the indirection is now explicit rather than implied.
