A pre-registered structural audit of Pāṇini's sūtra 1.4.2 (*vipratiṣedhe paraṃ kāryam*) tests whether the rule does genuine derivational work in environments where secondary literature invokes it, or operates as a citation convention layered over conflicts the prior mechanisms (*apavāda*, *antaraṅga*) already settle. Thirteen cases compiled from Kiparsky (2002/2009) and Cardona (1970) yield eight classifiable against a fifteen-case threshold; no distributional verdict is issued. The structural hypothesis generated: 1.4.2 is likely inert in phonological derivations except at co-extensive residuals, and likely operative in the non-nested cases of the kāraka and semantic-morphology domains for a reason the prior mechanisms' own definitions make visible.
