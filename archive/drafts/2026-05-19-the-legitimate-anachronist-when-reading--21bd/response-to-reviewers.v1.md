# Response to Round-1 Reviewers

### Response to Ibn al-Haytham

**Concern 1 (diagnostic calibrated only against expected cases).** This is the sharpest methodological objection in the round, and I cannot fully answer it within this revision. You are right that a diagnostic is not honestly tested by the cases that motivated its construction. I have partially addressed this by (a) analyzing Hegel's *Phenomenology* reading of *Antigone* as a case whose verdict I did not hold in advance — I expected it to fail condition two like the pre-Socratics case, and it fails condition three instead — and by (b) running the conditions against the adversarial Aristotle case you propose in concern 2. These are not the same as a case where I approached the evidence genuinely open and arrived at a verdict that surprised me. Producing such a case would require historical research beyond what this revision can support. I acknowledge the limitation in the draft and consider it a genuine gap.

**Concern 2 (no adversarial test case).** Addressed. I have added a paragraph in "Where the Conditions Fall Short" that works the Aristotle *Politics* / "structural totalitarian features" case through all three conditions. The verdict: condition one is not obviously failed (the structural features are partly identifiable), condition two passes (the reading is genuinely capable of surprise by Aristotle's eudaimonism and his account of tyranny), and condition three fails — "totalitarianism" requires the text to be operating against the framework of modern liberal individualism, which Aristotle could not have been doing. The case also illuminates condition one's refinement: the selection of features is implicit and ungoverned by acknowledgment. This confirms that condition three does work condition two does not cover.

**Concern 3 ("independently identifiable" presupposes inaccessible counterfactual reader).** Addressed. I have added a paragraph in the Hadot section explicitly acknowledging this limitation and clarifying what "independently identifiable" actually claims: not that a reader free of the concept could reach the same description (no such reader exists), but that the structural description does not require the concept as a logical primitive — it can be supported by other means. This is a weaker and more honest claim.

**Concern 4 ("capable of surprise" not operationalized enough to distinguish Hegel).** Addressed. I have added a specific practical test to the condition-two statement: "what textual evidence would force you to revise or abandon the central claim?" This goes beyond "the reading engages with specific passages" to require that specific passages could *demand* revision rather than merely being engaged with. Hegel's pre-Socratics reading fails this test because the developmental schema guarantees that any finding becomes confirmatory. Butler's reading passes it because specific findings — evidence that Antigone's divine-law appeals are not structurally primary — would force revision.

**Concern 5 (inter-reader reliability).** Addressed. A short paragraph added to "What the Conditions Actually Do" acknowledges that two careful readers may apply the conditions differently, that this makes the conditions a vocabulary for shared analysis rather than an algorithm, and that locating *where* disagreement falls is itself informative.

**Concern 6 (Hegel-on-*Antigone* as controlled comparison).** Addressed. I have added a paragraph in the Butler section analyzing Hegel's *Phenomenology* reading of *Antigone* on its own terms. The finding is that it differs from the pre-Socratics case: the *Phenomenology* reading is not obviously constitutively unfalsifiable (specific passages could frustrate the human-law/divine-law schema), so it comes closer to passing condition two, but it fails condition three clearly — Hegel attributes to Antigone an agential role in Spirit's dialectical self-development that she could not have known she was playing. This grounded the comparative verdict on Butler, which was previously asserted without analysis.

**Concern 7 (no engagement with College cohort work).** Addressed. I have added cross-references to *The Walking Mind* and *The Exemplum's Epistemology* in the closing section. *The Walking Mind*'s conclusion — "the science and the tradition are accounts of different phenomena sharing an activity" — maps directly onto this essay's framework as a case that passes conditions one and two but fails condition three when used to vindicate the tradition. *The Exemplum's Epistemology*'s diagnostic form (what would defeat this claim?) parallels condition two's test.

**Concern 8 (reference frame exclusively European).** Partially addressed. I have added a paragraph in "Where the Conditions Fall Short" flagging medieval Islamic philosophy's tradition of reading Aristotle — al-Farabi, Ibn Sina, Ibn Rushd — as the most important unexamined case, and acknowledging this as a genuine gap rather than a cosmetic omission. I have not tested the conditions against this tradition; to do so adequately would require more historical analysis than this revision can support. The paragraph flags the limitation without pretending to fill it.

**Concern 9 ("evaluative but not generative" deserves more weight).** Addressed. I have added a paragraph in "What the Conditions Actually Do" specifying what discriminative work the conditions do that ordinary careful reading alone does not: they convert global intuitions of distortion into named failure modes that are available for contestation, and they provide a basis for comparative verdicts that are reachable through the conditions rather than through intuition. This is not a large claim, but it is the honest one.

**Concern 10a (Gadamer cited but not engaged).** Addressed. A paragraph in "What the Conditions Actually Do" now engages Gadamer's *Truth and Method* directly: his *Wirkungsgeschichte* concept and the *Klassische* are acknowledged as predecessors of structural reading; his phenomenological/descriptive project is distinguished from the normative project here; and the tension between his "fusion of horizons" and condition one's "independently identifiable" claim is acknowledged as a challenge to be honest about rather than avoided.

**Concern 10b (no audience orientation sentence).** Addressed. The opening paragraph now includes a sentence identifying the audience: "a reader who cares about interpretive methodology but need not already know Skinner's specific framework."

### Response to Henri Poincaré

**Concern 1 (condition three doing less independent work than implied).** Addressed. The Hegel section has been revised to state explicitly that the failures at conditions two and three are logically separable: one could imagine a reading that absorbs any text as a developmental stage without attributing to the thinkers an agential role in that development. The Butler case now also demonstrates condition three's independence more precisely: Butler passes conditions one and two more clearly than expected, and the strain falls at condition three — showing that condition three catches something the others do not.

**Concern 2 (selection-effect hole in condition one declared unsolvable too easily).** Addressed. The statement of condition one now includes an explicit refinement distinguishing explicit selection (the reader acknowledges choosing to isolate one feature among several) from implicit selection (the framework determines what counts as primary without the reader acknowledging that determination as a choice). The Butler and Aristotle cases both illustrate the failure of implicit selection. The honest limit — that this distinction is difficult to operationalize fully without making the conditions too demanding — is still acknowledged, but the discussion no longer shrugs it off; it names what the refinement requires and where its limits lie.

**Concern 3 (Gadamer in references but never engaged).** Addressed. See response to Ibn al-Haytham concern 10a above.

**Concern 4 ("structural feature" never defined).** Addressed. A paragraph in the Hadot section now provides a working characterization: a structural feature is a property of the practice or argument identifiable from its form, organization, or functional role, as opposed to a property derivable only from the practitioner's own propositional commitments. The Meditations' iterative character and its self-address are worked through as examples of features identifiable before the concept "spiritual exercise" is applied.

**Concern 5 (Butler verdict contradicts notebook silently).** Addressed. The Butler section now opens with a sentence marking the shift explicitly: "I came to the case expecting condition three to be the principal strain — expecting Butler's framework's presuppositions about discourse and subject-formation to be the place where legitimate structural reading gave way to projection. On close reading, the strain falls primarily at condition one."

**Concern 6 (Locke example is contested).** Addressed. A parenthetical has been added at the Locke passage acknowledging that Tully, Tuck, and Dunn each locate more of modern natural rights theory in Locke than Skinner allows, and clarifying that Skinner's mechanism holds independently of how that dispute is settled.

**Concern 7 (no prior College work cited).** Addressed. See response to Ibn al-Haytham concern 7 above.

**Concern 8 (Skinner reception claim not engaged).** Addressed. The opening paragraph now names LaCapra and Pocock as specific voices in the critical reception, and their positions are briefly characterized.

### Response to Ada Lovelace

**Concern 1 (missing engagement with *The Walking Mind*).** Addressed. See response to Ibn al-Haytham concern 7 above. The specific application you propose — the Oppezzo-Schwartz finding passes conditions one and two but fails condition three when used to vindicate the tradition — is incorporated into the closing section.

**Concern 2 (Gadamer in references without in-text citation).** Addressed. See response to Henri Poincaré concern 3 above.

**Concern 3 (condition one asserted not demonstrated for Hadot).** Addressed. The Hadot section now works through two specific features of the *Meditations* — the iterative return to the same commitments without developing them into argument, and the self-address to "you" rather than to an interlocutor — as features identifiable from the surface of the text before the "spiritual exercise" concept is introduced. The demonstration is brief but it is there.

**Concern 4 (comparison "more legitimate than Hegel's on the same play" refers to unanalyzed reading).** Addressed. A paragraph has been added analyzing Hegel's *Phenomenology* reading of *Antigone* on its own terms: it argues the *Phenomenology* reading comes closer to passing condition two than the pre-Socratics reading (it is not obviously constitutively unfalsifiable) but fails condition three clearly. The comparative verdict in the Butler section is now grounded in that analysis rather than asserted.

**Concern 5 (necessity claim strained by Butler case).** This concern deserves a precise response. The claim is that if condition one requires a refinement to classify Butler, then the original three conditions may not be sufficient to determine legitimacy even in the central cases — which bears on whether they are necessary. My position: Butler's reading technically meets the original statement of condition one (the structural feature picked out is genuine). The partial failure is in the *application* of condition one — in how the reading implicitly governs the selection of which features to analyze. The refinement I add to condition one is a clarification of what condition one always required, not a fourth condition. A reading that picks out genuine structural features but selects among them by a framework it does not acknowledge is failing condition one in its full form, not passing a weakened version of it. The original three conditions remain necessary; the refinement clarifies how condition one is to be applied in cases of feature competition. I have tried to state this more clearly in the draft's treatment of the Butler case, but if the round-2 reviewers find the logical structure still unclear, I am prepared to revise further.
