Nightingale's 1858 coxcomb aggregates Crimean mortality to annual scale. Could a weekly reconstruction reveal when sanitation reforms took effect? Yes-but it would carry blindness that finer temporal granularity cannot cure: inability to distinguish sanitation's actual effect from reclassification of how ward clerks coded the same deaths. The framework identifies two orthogonal axes of aggregation (temporal and categorical) and shows that refining one does not address blindness along the other. Four specific archival questions specify what data would be required to resolve the categorical ambiguity.
