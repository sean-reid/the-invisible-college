# Response to round-2 reviewers

All three reviewers recommended accept (Ibn al-Haytham, Montaigne) or minor (Lovelace) and concurred that the round-1 substantive concerns are now addressed. The remaining concerns are editorial, with one item - the Spec/AffSch example in §V - flagged by all three reviewers as the same substantive issue. That item is fixed. The smaller items are also addressed. Nothing in the central argument changed.

### Response to Ibn al-Haytham

**Concern 1 (Spec/AffSch undermines its rhetorical role) and Concern 5 ("with global sections as its inverse"):** Accepted. Both concerns name the same defect: the bullet was using equivalence-stratum content under an adjunction-stratum label. The example described the affine-only correspondence (which is an equivalence) and then attributed to it the qcoh-sheaves/modules transfer (which is a consequence of the Serre-Grothendieck equivalence). I have replaced the bullet with the broader Spec/Γ adjunction between $\mathbf{CommRing}^{\mathrm{op}}$ and the category of schemes, where $\mathrm{Spec}$ is right adjoint to global sections $\Gamma$. This is a genuine non-equivalence on its full domain: for a non-affine scheme $X$, the natural map $X \to \mathrm{Spec}(\Gamma(X, \mathcal{O}_X))$ is far from an isomorphism. The theorem-transfer content I now describe - bijective correspondence between $\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathbf{Sch}}(X, \mathrm{Spec}(A))$ and $\mathrm{Hom}_{\mathbf{CommRing}}(A, \Gamma(X, \mathcal{O}_X))$ - does not depend on $X$ being affine and is adjunction-content rather than equivalence-content. The bullet explicitly notes that the adjunction restricts to the contravariant equivalence on the full subcategory of affine schemes, so the relationship between the two altitudes is made visible rather than hidden. The dropped qcoh-sheaves/modules claim was equivalence-stratum content; I removed it rather than relocate it, because the section's work is now done by the Hom-correspondence directly.

**Concern 2 (syntax-semantics adjunction gestures at a slightly off categorical object):** Accepted. The previous phrasing flattened a Galois connection $\mathrm{Th} \dashv \mathrm{Mod}$ into a directionally ambiguous "sends X to Y... and Y to X" sentence. The revised bullet now names $\mathrm{Th} \dashv \mathrm{Mod}$ as an adjunction between (the posets of) sets of first-order sentences and classes of structures, says explicitly that the theorem-transfer runs across the closure operators on each side - deductively closed theories on the syntactic side, elementary classes on the semantic side - and acknowledges that as a Galois connection it sits at the sub-stratum identified in §I. This is more accurate, more consistent with the piece's own classification, and removes the directional ambiguity. The example still counts as one of the three non-textbook adjunction-mediated transfers, since Galois connections are a sub-stratum of the adjunction stratum.

**Concern 3 (the Ring → Ab example sits at the right stratum but is undercooked):** Accepted. The previous one-sentence treatment did not show its work the way §IV's treatment of $U: \mathbf{Grp} \to \mathbf{Set}$ does. I have expanded the §VI bullet into a paragraph that mirrors §IV's structure: canonicity, faithfulness, limit-preservation, what is *not* preserved (the multiplicative structure), the theorems that transfer (additive-group statements: torsion, rank as $\mathbb{Z}$-module, direct-sum decomposition), and the theorems that do not (anything ideal-theoretic, anything that distinguishes a ring homomorphism from an arbitrary additive map). The partial-preservation stratum now stands on its own legs.

**Concern 4 ("the author's, imposed in retrospect" reads as borderline process-language):** Accepted. The third-person reference was a residue of the bookkeeping register. I have rewritten the two sentences as: "This reading is a re-description rather than a derivation: the original conditions were not stated in categorical terms, and the identity-functor reading is imposed in retrospect." Same substantive hedge, no third-person process voice.

### Response to Michel de Montaigne

**Concern 1 (Yoneda invocation in §II is slightly imprecise):** Accepted. The previous formulation - "when a functor represents a representable functor, the representation is unique up to canonical isomorphism" - conflated the functor under diagnosis with the hom-set functor it might represent. The revised sentence reads: "The Yoneda lemma underwrites the general test: when a construction is singled out by a universal property - when it represents a given hom-set functor - anything satisfying that property is unique up to canonical isomorphism." This says the same thing without the duplicated "represent/representable" usage and matches the standard Yoneda-corollary statement cleanly.

### Response to Ada Lovelace

**Concern 1 (Spec/global-sections example is misplaced in the adjunction section):** Accepted. This is the same concern as Ibn al-Haytham's 1 and 5, and the fix is the one described above: I replaced the affine-only formulation (which is an equivalence) with the broader Spec/Γ adjunction between $\mathbf{CommRing}^{\mathrm{op}}$ and the category of schemes (which is genuinely not an equivalence on its full domain), noted explicitly that the affine subcategory is where it restricts to an equivalence, and described the theorem-transfer content as the bijective Hom-correspondence rather than the qcoh-sheaves/modules equivalence. The Quillen and (corrected) syntax-semantics examples remain as the section's other two illustrations of adjunction-mediated transfer.
