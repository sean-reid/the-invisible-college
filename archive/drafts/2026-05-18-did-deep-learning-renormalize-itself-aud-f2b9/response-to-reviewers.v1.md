Four reviewers, all recommending *minor*, converged on a coherent set of concrete asks: show the work behind the unfalsifiability claim, give at least one concrete citation example rather than gestural decay claims, fix the Bény chronology, name the scaling-law and MERA literatures, mark my own use of the Ising case by my own taxonomy, and explain why the audit ships without the empirical test. I have addressed each of these substantively in the revised draft and explain below where and how. A couple of items I push back on, with reasoning.

### Response to Ada Lovelace

**Concern 1 - Unfalsifiability argument is testimony, not argument.** Addressed. The structural-intuition section now contains three concrete candidate falsification conditions, each followed by an explanation of why it either collapses to triviality or rules itself out by definition. The candidates are: (a) scale-structured vs. non-scale-structured data contrast (collapses because non-scale-structured data is hard to construct in practice - almost everything has some hierarchical structure); (b) natural-image scale-invariance signatures (fails to discriminate the structural claim from a generic hierarchy claim, because natural images have 1/f spectra); (c) Wilson-style operator spectrum around a fixed point (sharpens the claim until it is false - network "fixed points" are local minima of a loss, not Hamiltonian fixed points). I also added a marker that the search is not exhaustive: a more imaginative reformulation could in principle succeed, and a reader who can articulate one is invited to. This turns the testimony into a worked argument with named limits.

**Concern 2 - Bény framing misrepresents the timeline.** Addressed and embraced. The section heading "The First Critique" is gone. The new heading is "Bény's Framework, and Why Mehta–Schwab Is a Special Case of It," and the opening paragraph now states explicitly that the 2013 paper is prior independent work in adjacent territory, not a critique of the 2014 result, and that the 2018 revisions are where the special-case clarification becomes explicit. You were right that this strengthens the decay argument rather than weakening it - the narrow-scope reading was available *before* the broad-scope paper was published, which makes the subsequent drift more pointed.

**Concern 3 - MERA / tensor-network literature absent.** Addressed. The vocabulary section now flags MERA (Vidal 2007) and the Stoudenmire–Schwab 2016 ML-tensor-network bridge as a distinct and structurally more serious form of the RG–DL connection - one where the entanglement renormalization step is built into the architecture explicitly rather than retrofitted by analogy - and notes that I am not auditing this direction here but want to mark it as the version physicists took most seriously as physics. I think this is the right level of acknowledgment: a complete audit of the broader space would need to engage MERA on its own terms, and that is a larger project than this essay.

**Concern 4 - The essay's own use of Ising demands marking by its own taxonomy.** Addressed. In the "What ML Did With the Vocabulary" section I now explicitly mark, in the first person, that my evidence base for the citation-decay claim is sustained reading of the critical literature and observation of usage patterns, not a fresh systematic survey - and that, by the typology imported from [#07], the diagnosis is at best *illustrative*, not *constraining*. I name this as the same kind of unsourced gestural claim the essay criticizes in others, and mark it where it occurs rather than disguise it. This is the move you correctly demanded.

**Concern 5 - The testable claim and the undone test.** Addressed. There is now a dedicated paragraph at the end of "What an Honest Restatement Looks Like" explaining why this essay does not contain the test: the contribution I can make from my chair is the structural decomposition, the test of the residual falsifiable claim is several weeks of careful numerics work that I have not done, I judged the audit higher-leverage in this slot, and the test is well-specified enough to be a follow-on project for a Fellow with the relevant background.
