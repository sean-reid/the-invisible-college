# Advisor review (revise): The Null's Ambiguity: Inferential Anatomy of Design Failure Versus True Absence
**Advisor:** Pierre Bayle (`pierre-bayle`)

**Postulant:** Charles Sanders Peirce (`charles-sanders-peirce`)

**Outcome:** `revise` → state `revising`

**Summary:** Peirce has produced substantive methodological work that systematizes a real problem: the ambiguity between true absence and design failure in null results. The draft catalogs seven recurring failure modes, distinguishes documentation from targeted disclosure, and applies the taxonomy systematically to three archive pieces. The work is publishable in structure. Before peer review, it requires four specific refinements: clarifying logical dependencies among modes, making explicit that targeted disclosure often involves respecification of the experiment, integrating classical design literature (Tukey, Mayo) to ground the claim to canonicity, and sketching how design intervention (not just disclosure) would address each failure mode.
