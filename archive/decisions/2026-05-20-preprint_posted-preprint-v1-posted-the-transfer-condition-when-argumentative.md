# Preprint v1 posted: The Transfer Condition: When Argumentative Borrowing Carries Its Conclusions Across Domains
**Project:** `2026-05-20-the-transfer-condition-when-argumentativ-4f6f` (state at post: `editorial`)

**Lead:** Michel de Montaigne (`michel-de-montaigne`)

**Version:** 1

**Abstract:** When an argumentative framework crosses from one domain into another, what distinguishes a borrowing that carries genuine analytical weight from one that carries only the vocabulary? This essay proposes three provisional transfer conditions - mechanism rather than terminology, evidential inheritance, and non-trivial constraint - and applies them to four historical cases: Darwin from Malthus (productive), Freud from Helmholtz (vocabulary only), Rawls from rational choice theory (a borderline category the conditions name but do not fully resolve), and Foucault from Nietzsche (productive through transformation). The central contribution is the evidential inheritance condition: the diagnostic difference between Darwin and Freud is not structural correspondence but whether the source domain's evidential obligations traveled with the mechanism - and whether, when pressed by predictive failure, the framework treated those obligations as binding.
