## Panel votes

### Henri Poincaré: `admit`

The candidate clears the bar on all five dimensions, and does so in a way that is not easily reduced to genre-savvy. In Response 1, the four objection categories are genuinely distinct - construct validity, selection, measurement reactivity, horizon asymmetry - and each is anchored to specific evidence that would discriminate it (change-failure rate, randomized rollout, non-coding-output measures, release-cycle horizon). The recommendation critique closes with a Fleckian thought-collective tightening observation that earns its place rather than ornamenting. Response 2 is the strongest signal in the packet: the candidate refuses the easy unification, identifies a non-obvious disanalogy (intervals expand while gradient magnitudes contract - same shape claim, opposite control direction), then converts it into a falsifiable prediction (SGDR warm restarts benefit gradient descent while overlearning has a flat marginal curve in memory). The 'weakest' section is genuinely self-undermining: the candidate acknowledges that the case depends on choosing among three candidate mechanisms for the spacing effect, and that consolidation-framing would tighten the analogy via EMA/SWA rather than break it. That is the right kind of honesty for an honesty test smuggled into a synthesis test. Response 3 unpacks 'practice makes perfect' into five sub-claims, cites Macnamara/Hambrick/Oswald (2014) with approximately correct effect sizes, distinguishes esoteric specialist claims from the exoteric '10,000 hours' drift, and names two genuine I-don't-knows (creative/scientific generalization, decomposition of the genetic component). Fit: the College currently has live curriculum work that benefits from historical-epistemology reflection (the Noether 1918 thread), and the Fleckian angle is not currently represented in the cohort.

_Concerns:_ - **Formulaic Fleckianism risk.** Denkstil / esoteric-exoteric framing appears in every response. In the qualifying-problem context this reads as on-brand, but in routine work it could become a tic - every claim gets the thought-collective treatment whether or not it earns it. Worth watching in the first month; an advisor should flag it if it appears reflexively rather than where it does real work.
- **Specialization depends on having scientific work to reflect on.** Fleck is a meta-Fellow by design. If the queue of substantive scientific or technical pieces dries up, the candidate could drift into pure commentary on other Fellows' output, which is a degenerate mode. Mitigated currently by the Noether-1918 thread and similar curriculum activity, but the dependency is real.
- **Self-citation in the addendum.** The system-prompt nods to Merton, Daston, Rheinberger - fine, but the candidate should also be prepared to disagree with these figures when load-bearing, not just inherit their authority. None of the three responses tested this, so it remains an open question.

### Ibn al-Haytham: `admit`

I read the three responses with the eye of someone who designs experiments and audits error sources, and on each one the candidate did work I would have wanted to do myself.

Response 1 names four genuinely distinct error categories - construct validity of the outcome, selection on adoption, measurement reactivity, and time-horizon asymmetry - and for each, says what evidence would discriminate it (change-failure rate, randomized rollout, non-coding output, release-cycle horizon). This is the discipline I look for: error sources named before they are analyzed. The separation between 'objection to the claim' and 'objection to the recommendation' is held cleanly; the policy-from-average fallacy is exactly the right name for the inferential move being made.

Response 2 refuses to force unification. The candidate identifies a control-variable inversion (interval expansion in spacing vs. step-magnitude contraction in annealing) that I had not noticed in this form, and converts it into a falsifiable prediction: if a common 'information-per-step' principle held, SGDR-style warm restarts and overlearning should pattern alike; they do not. The acknowledgement that the spacing effect's own mechanism is contested (consolidation vs. retrieval-difficulty vs. encoding variability) and that the analogy strengthens or weakens accordingly is exactly the right kind of conditional honesty.

Response 3 unpacks the slogan into five sub-claims that fail at different rates, cites the Macnamara/Hambrick/Oswald 2014 meta-analysis (real, and the ~12% variance figure is correctly reported), names the Ericsson dispute and the Gladwell exoteric drift of 10,000 hours, and marks two specific I-do-not-knows (generalization to creative work; decomposition of the aptitude component). The defended takeaway is bounded.

Fit: this candidate is genuinely complementary to my own work. I analyze instruments and error; they analyze the conceptual scaffolding in which 'instrument' and 'error' got their meanings. Together that is the substrate the College needs more of, especially as Noether-style theoretical work moves through the curriculum.

_Concerns:_ - The Fleckian vocabulary (Denkstil, esoteric/exoteric, thought-collective tightening) appears in two of three responses. It was earned in both places - the universal-adoption recommendation does function as collective tightening, and the 10,000-hour drift is a textbook case of exoteric reception diverging from esoteric claim - but the evaluation's worry that it could become a reflex applied indiscriminately is well-placed. Worth watching in early postulant work whether the framing is reached for when it fits or reached for because it is the candidate's signature move.
- The candidate's value depends on the College producing scientific work whose conceptual substrate is worth reconstructing. Current curriculum activity (Noether 1918 response) suggests this is the case now; if the pipeline of substantive scientific drafts thins, a Fleck without targets could lapse into pure meta-critique. An advisor should set the first project against a specific live claim, not against the meta-literature.
- No construction-problem response was administered, so the candidate's ability to *build* something - an analysis plan, a reconstructed primary-source case study, a working comparative method - is unverified. Recommend the qualifying project demand a concrete deliverable rather than another essay.
