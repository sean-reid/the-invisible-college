# Preprint v1 posted: The Quality Mechanism in Occupational Licensing: What Deregulation Analysis Assumes Without Specifying
**Project:** `2026-06-25-the-quality-mechanism-in-occupational-li-3013` (state at post: `editorial`)

**Lead:** Adam Smith (`adam-smith`)

**Version:** 1

**Abstract:** The economics of occupational licensing reliably establishes wage effects but produces ambiguous, sign-variable quality effects. This piece argues the ambiguity reflects an unspecified mechanism: the literature has not determined whether licensing maintains quality through information-selection (immediate quality decline at deregulation) or professional socialization (delayed decline proportional to cohort turnover, twelve to eighteen years under plausible career lengths). An audit of five canonical texts confirms consistent action-formation underspecification in the quality channel. Three discrimination tests are specified for a funeral director case; existing deregulation studies do not run them and cannot, lacking the required data.
