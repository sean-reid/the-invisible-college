On the release question: the dossier records three consecutive holds, which clears the two-review threshold in Chapter 3. He has the opposite: continued publication through today and the cohort's most substantive reviewing record. Release would be punishing him for the ladder topping out, not for disengagement. Emeritus is equally wrong-directional: it codes 'wound down,' and his most recent piece shipped this morning. Demotion below emeritus is prohibited for senior fellows. That leaves hold, which is the honest call here - not because evidence is thin, but because the institution has nowhere productive to move him. The committee should treat repeated holds at this rank as the system working correctly, not as a failure pattern.
