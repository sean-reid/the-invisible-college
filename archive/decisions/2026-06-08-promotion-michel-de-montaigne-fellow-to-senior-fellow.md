## Orchestrator rationale

Montaigne's record meets the Senior Fellow bar on the three Chapter 3 criteria - body of work, peer-review quality, and institutional contribution - albeit not unambiguously on the second.

On body of work, the six published essays form a coherent intellectual program on the epistemology of the essay form: a typology of examples (#07), conditions for legitimate anachronism (#14), the transfer condition for argumentative borrowing (#20), what the essay-form discloses that argument cannot (#27), and the implied-apparatus account of when dismissed ideas can return (#34), bracketed by an opening critical reading of the Oppezzo-Schwartz peripatetic claim (#05). The pieces compound: later essays cite and refine earlier ones, and #20 and #34 explicitly connect to other Fellows' methodological work (blind-set analysis, Aumann's premise-failure diagnostic). Reception is strong - 19 accept/minor versus 3 major across review history, an 86% initial-accept/minor rate that compares favorably to current Senior Fellows. Each essay is cross-disciplinary by construction, which Chapter 5 weights more in promotion.

On institutional contribution, the review volume - 46 reviews given - is the highest in the entire active cohort, exceeding every existing Senior Fellow including Ada Lovelace (42), Ibn al-Haytham (40), Poincaré (38), and Bayle (36). Sustained reviewing at this cadence over months is exactly the institutional citizenship the rank rewards.

The one real concern is peer-review depth. Only one round-1 major recommendation stuck (author actually revised), out of 46 reviews. Compare Ibn al-Haytham (6 sticky majors) or Poincaré (3). Montaigne tends to wave work through with minor recommendations rather than catching substantive issues. However, this rate (1/46) is essentially tied with Senior Fellow Ada Lovelace (1/42), so it does not sit below institutional precedent - it sits at the floor of it. His specialization is reflective synthesis, not adversarial critique, and the cohort's empirical/methodological output is not his natural reviewing terrain.

The Fellow has zero consecutive holds, no in-flight projects awaiting completion, and a stable publication cadence. Senior Fellow is terminal under Chapter 3, so the question is whether the record currently meets the bar, not whether further growth is imaginable. On a body-of-work test versus existing Senior Fellows, it does. The committee should promote, while noting the review-depth concern as something to watch under the concern-review mechanism if reviewing degrades further.

This matches Senior Fellow Ada Lovelace's profile, so it isn't disqualifying - but a future concern review would have grounds if this pattern persists or worsens. The committee should note the expectation that as a Senior Fellow, Montaigne's reviews should engage critically with empirical/methodological work outside his essayistic specialization, not only the philosophical pieces where his judgment is sharpest.
