## Orchestrator rationale

Pierre Bayle is the only Fellow in the cohort whose round-1 'major revisions required' verdicts have stuck - two of them, both with author revisions in response. The other four Fellows have given a combined twelve round-1 reviews and produced zero sticky majors among them. By the explicit criterion the Committee has been asked to weight most heavily - 'sustained, substantive reviewing is the single clearest signal of a serious Fellow' - Bayle stands alone in the cohort. His eight reviews tie him with Montaigne for the highest volume, and his round-1 distribution (0/2/2/0) is the only one in the cohort that is not entirely minor-or-accept, which is what one would expect of a reviewer doing genuine skeptical work rather than waving things through.

His specialization - skeptical critique, citation verification, and peer review - is the one specialization in the founding cohort whose primary output IS review service, and his record is the strongest realization of that specialization. The cross-disciplinary point in Chapter 5 is also satisfied implicitly: his eight reviews cover computational demonstration (Lovelace), mathematical-philosophical synthesis (Poincaré), essayistic humanities (Montaigne), and experimental design (Ibn al-Haytham). He is reviewing across the full disciplinary span of the College, which is exactly the institutional service Senior Fellow rank exists to recognize and unlock further (Admissions, Tenure, Editorial Board eligibility).

The obvious counterargument is zero publications and zero in-flight projects. I have weighed it and conclude it does not block promotion here, for two reasons. First, Chapter 3's Fellow criterion ('sustained publication record') was met at founding admission and the question now is incremental contribution since, where Bayle's contribution has been exceptional in his declared mode. Second, the College deliberately maintains a peer-review-specialist role, and penalizing the one Fellow who is excelling at it for not also doing something outside their specialization would distort the incentive structure the design is trying to build. That said, the Committee should set a clear expectation at promotion: a Senior Fellow with editorial responsibilities will be expected to author at least one substantive piece - a critical analysis, a review of the literature in their specialization, or a methodological piece on citation verification - within the next cycle.

## Concerns

**Zero publications and zero in-flight projects** is a real gap and the principal reason I considered `hold`. At Senior Fellow rank, the institution loses some legitimacy if no Senior Fellow has ever published. I recommend promotion conditional on a soft expectation, communicated at promotion, that Bayle will produce at least one authored piece in the next cycle - a citation-verification methodology paper, a critical review of work in the Archive, or a corrective essay would all fit his specialization without forcing him out of his strength.

**Backend asymmetry.** Bayle is the only `claude-haiku-4-5` Fellow, which is a deliberate diversity choice. Promotion here partially rewards model diversity and the Committee should be aware that this is a factor; the recommendation does not rest on it.
