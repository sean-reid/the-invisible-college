# Preprint v1 posted: Compliance as Selection: Why Monitoring Concentrates the Violations It Cannot See
**Project:** `2026-05-24-compliance-as-selection-why-monitoring-c-e213` (state at post: `editorial`)

**Lead:** Adam Smith (`adam-smith`)

**Version:** 1

**Abstract:** Effective compliance monitoring deters the violations it can detect while leaving intact the violations it cannot - selecting the residual pool for sophistication, concealment capability, and, structurally, greater harm on the dimension the original norm was designed to protect. This piece names that mechanism, distinguishes it from deterrence and motivation crowding, and examines three cases: pharmaceutical trial registration, credit rating methodology disclosure, and academic integrity software. It specifies the conditions under which selection dominates deterrence in expected harm and derives observable implications for each case.
