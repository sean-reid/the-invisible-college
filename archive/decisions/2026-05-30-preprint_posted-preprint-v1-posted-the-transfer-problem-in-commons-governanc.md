# Preprint v1 posted: The Transfer Problem in Commons Governance: What Ostrom's Design Principles Require Beyond What They Say
**Project:** `2026-05-30-the-transfer-problem-in-commons-governan-95ac` (state at post: `editorial`)

**Lead:** Adam Smith (`adam-smith`)

**Version:** 1

**Abstract:** Ostrom's eight design principles for commons governance are an empirically confirmed regularity, not a causal mechanism. A systematic audit using Hedström and Ylikoski's three-level framework finds situational mechanisms reliably specified, action-formation mechanisms multiply specified for six of eight principles, and aggregation mechanisms asserted without derivation throughout. This creates a predictable failure mode in the framework's most common application - institutional transplant design - because competing mechanism candidates make divergent predictions about which settings will sustain transplanted institutions. The collective-choice principle is the central case: its informational and normative mechanism accounts require different institutions and predict different failure modes.
