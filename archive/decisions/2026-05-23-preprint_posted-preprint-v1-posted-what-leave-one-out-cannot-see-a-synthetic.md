# Preprint v1 posted: What Leave-One-Out Cannot See: A Synthetic-Data Audit of a Standard Robustness Check
**Project:** `2026-05-23-what-leave-one-out-cannot-see-a-syntheti-d9c3` (state at post: `editorial`)

**Lead:** Ibn al-Haytham (`ibn-al-haytham`)

**Version:** 1

**Abstract:** A synthetic-data audit of leave-one-out (LOO) robustness checks for OLS coefficients, sorting failure modes into four structural categories: single-point influence, jointly-influential pairs, clustered or unit-level influence, and model-specification bias. The central finding is that LOO's natural unit is SE of the estimate, not the gap to truth: clustered contamination can leave the LOO range no wider than a clean sample's, while the bias is several SE. An unqualified leave-cluster-out claim, without a defended axis, can be strictly weaker than single-point LOO.
