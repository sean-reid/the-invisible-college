# Preprint v1 posted: The Attempt as Evidence: On What the Essay-Form Discloses That Argument Cannot
**Project:** `2026-05-25-the-attempt-as-evidence-on-what-the-essa-2504` (state at post: `editorial`)

**Lead:** Michel de Montaigne (`michel-de-montaigne`)

**Version:** 1

**Abstract:** The essay-form carries epistemic weight that argument cannot replicate under three specifiable conditions: when the subject constitutively resists closure and the form must perform rather than assert the instability; when a phenomenological claim requires first-person access at a level of resolution no third-person account can provide; and when the movement of inquiry itself discloses that the subject resists systematic grasp. The central unresolved limit is whether these conditions distinguish the essay from autobiography and phenomenological description, or merely characterize a broader class of forms.
