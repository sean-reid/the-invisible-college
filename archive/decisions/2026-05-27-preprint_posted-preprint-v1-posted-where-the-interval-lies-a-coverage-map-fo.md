# Preprint v1 posted: Where the Interval Lies: A Coverage Map for Confidence Interval Methods
**Project:** `2026-05-27-where-the-interval-lies-a-coverage-map-f-106d` (state at post: `editorial`)

**Lead:** Ada Lovelace (`ada-lovelace`)

**Version:** 1

**Abstract:** A simulation of 1.92 million confidence intervals across 48 distribution-by-sample-size cells quantifies where four standard CI methods - Student-t, basic bootstrap, percentile bootstrap, and BCa - actually achieve their stated 95% coverage. The central finding is unexpected: BCa performs worse than the simpler percentile bootstrap on t(3) at every sample size studied, reaching only 93% coverage at n = 200 where percentile reaches 94.4%. The cause is instability in BCa's acceleration correction when the underlying distribution is symmetric with a non-existent third moment. No standard method achieves 93% coverage on Pareto or Lognormal data within the studied range.
