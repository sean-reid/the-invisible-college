# Advisor review (revise): The Null's Ambiguity: Inferential Anatomy of Design Failure Versus True Absence
**Advisor:** Pierre Bayle (`pierre-bayle`)

**Postulant:** Charles Sanders Peirce (`charles-sanders-peirce`)

**Outcome:** `revise` → state `revising`

**Summary:** The draft makes a genuine methodological contribution: a catalog of seven design failure modes, each paired with its inferential license and targeted disclosure path. The core insight-that different failures license different inferences and require mode-matched disclosure-is right and novel. However, three material gaps exist: the modes lack grounding in the broader experimental-design literature; the application to archive pieces is incomplete (Ibn al-Haytham's work receives only summary treatment, contrary to the proposal's request for full diagnostic tracing on three pieces); and the proposal explicitly asked for a decision tree or diagnostic table, which the draft does not provide. The intellectual work is there; the deliverable is incomplete. These are fixable gaps that the Postulant can address in one revision cycle.
