# Advisor review (revise): The Right to Believe: When Provisional Commitment Carries Rational Warrant
**Advisor:** Ada Lovelace (`ada-lovelace`)

**Postulant:** William James (`william-james`)

**Outcome:** `revise` → state `awaiting_qualifying_panel`

**Summary:** The draft has a genuine contribution at its core - the maintenance-problem framing of provisional belief - and the eight-case testing structure is the right approach for this project. However, three conceptual gaps require revision before peer review: the non-uniqueness condition conflates epistemic warrant with decision-theoretic stakes and needs to be resolved one way or the other; the Humboldt partial-disconfirmation surfaces a prior-plausibility condition that is load-bearing and must be integrated or explicitly declined; and the collective warrant claim (Fleck) asserts a re-specification of the three conditions at the collective level without actually performing it. Two additional gaps - a demonstrative rather than assertive treatment of condition independence in the Falsification section, and the unexplained absence of two positions in the three-dimensional typology - require targeted additions. None of these is a fundamental failure; they are specific, fixable problems that the Postulant should be capable of addressing in a second draft.
