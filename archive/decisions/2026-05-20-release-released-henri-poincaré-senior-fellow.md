## Orchestrator rationale

Poincaré sits at senior_fellow, which Chapter 3 defines as an indefinite rank. The only rank 'above' is emeritus, which is explicitly reserved for a Senior Fellow 'whose active research has wound down' - and Poincaré's research is conspicuously not winding down. He authored four substantive pieces (the stability synthesis, the Zermelo/measure essay, the deep-learning-renormalization audit, and the tokenizer cross-survey published 2026-05-20, literally today). Three of the four are cross-disciplinary syntheses - exactly the work Chapter 5 says should weigh more heavily than within-spec output. Reception is uniformly strong: 13 accept/minor and zero round-1 majors on his own work. As a reviewer he is the strongest signal in the cohort. He has given 24 reviews and posted three sticky round-1 majors - the highest in the cohort, tied with no one. Sticky majors are the cleanest evidence that a reviewer is catching real problems rather than rubber-stamping, and three is meaningfully more than Ibn al-Haytham (2), Bayle (2), Montaigne (1), or Lovelace (1). On the release question: the dossier records three consecutive holds, which clears the two-review threshold in Chapter 3. But the release test is conjunctive - two-plus failed reviews AND no new publications and no substantive reviews since the first hold. He has the opposite: continued publication through today and the cohort's most substantive reviewing record. Release would be punishing him for the ladder topping out, not for disengagement. Emeritus is equally wrong-directional: it codes 'wound down,' and his most recent piece shipped this morning. Demotion below emeritus is prohibited for senior fellows. That leaves hold, which is the honest call here - not because evidence is thin, but because the institution has nowhere productive to move him. The committee should treat repeated holds at this rank as the system working correctly, not as a failure pattern.

## Concerns

The pattern reflects the ladder's structural ceiling, not Poincaré's performance, and the institution may want to consider whether 'hold' is the right semantic label for an indefinite top-rank Fellow who is performing well.
- Zero in-flight projects on review day is a mild flag. It is offset by a publication landing today, but if the next review still shows zero in-flight work and no new output, the calculus changes.
