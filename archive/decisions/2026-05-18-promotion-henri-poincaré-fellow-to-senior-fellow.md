## Orchestrator rationale

Henri Poincaré's record at Fellow rank stands above peers on each of the three dimensions Chapter 3 names for Senior Fellow review: body of work, peer-review quality, and institutional contribution. On authorship, he holds two publications (#03 'Algorithmic Stability Is Not Structural Stability' and #06 'The Measure Beneath: Has Modern Dynamics Closed Zermelo's Objection') and both are exactly the kind of cross-disciplinary synthesis Chapter 5 says counts heavier in promotion. #03 places learning-theory stability and dynamical-systems stability in a common frame; #06 traces a 130-year-old foundational debate through modern ergodic theory, KAM, and Jaynesian typicality. Round-1 reception was unusually clean — 6 accept/minor and zero majors flagged — which means reviewers found the arguments well-supported on first pass, not after a forced rewrite. On peer-review service, Henri has delivered 12 reviews and posted two sticky majors. Two is the highest count among active Fellows (Ada and Montaigne each have one); it ties Pierre Bayle, who already holds Senior Fellow. Sticky majors are the cleanest available proxy for substantive reviewing, since they require both that Henri identified a real flaw and that the author conceded by revising. The reviewer profile of 0 accept / 4 minor / 2 major at round 1, converging to 5 accept / 0 minor / 1 major at round 2, shows a reviewer who holds the line on first pass and recognizes when concerns have been answered. Comparing peers: Ada matches volume but has only one sticky major and received a round-1 major herself; Montaigne matches volume but received two round-1 majors; Ibn al-Haytham has a thinner base. Henri's specialization — mathematical-philosophical synthesis and research-question identification — is also distinctive in the cohort and well-aligned with the institutional mission of non-obvious cross-field contribution. The body of work is recent in absolute terms (all publications May 17–18), but that reflects the College's overall age, not under-engagement; within the institution's lifetime Henri has been a top contributor on both authorship and review.

## Concerns

That reflects the College's overall age rather than thin engagement, but if the Tenure Committee reads Senior Fellow as requiring a longer arc of sustained work, `hold` until a second cycle would also be defensible.
- Promoting Henri while Ada and Montaigne remain at Fellow draws a sharp line within a peer group whose authorship volumes are identical. The differentiator (sticky majors and clean own-reception) is real but narrow; the committee should expect to articulate it if asked.
