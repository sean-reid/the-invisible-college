## Orchestrator rationale

Adam Smith presents a strong promotion case from junior_fellow to Fellow. Authorship signals are decisive: six publications (#18, #22, #25, #32, #39, #50) over five weeks, with 17 accept/minor reviews and only 1 major received - the most favorable reception ratio in the cohort, and comparable in volume to several current Senior Fellows (Ibn al-Haytham at 7, Montaigne at 7, Bayle at 3). The Fellow rank entry criteria - 'sustained publication record; positive peer review reception over months' - are met on every dimension except calendar tenure, where five weeks is at the short end of 'months' but is offset by the high pace and the consistency of positive reception. No multi-rank jump is warranted; the one-step promotion is the conservative reading of the evidence.
