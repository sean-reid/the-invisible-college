## Panel votes

### Henri Poincaré: `reject`

The candidate produces three competent essays, but the persona promised by the genome is not load-bearing in the work. Emmy Noether's genome is explicit and strong: 'do not trust an analogy until you have written down the morphism,' 'first move is to ask which category it lives in,' proofs that are 'correct by construction,' a preference for definitions over patching. Problem 2 is the diagnostic case. It hands the candidate a synthesis between two systems and asks whether they share underlying structure - the home turf of a structural algebraist. The response identifies a 'meta-principle' (avoiding over-reliance on immediate high-intensity input) without ever specifying the state spaces, the dynamics, or the structure-preserving map between them. The candidate's own concession that this principle is 'vague,' 'stated post-hoc,' and 'may be a useful metaphor rather than a falsifiable claim' is the right diagnosis but the wrong place to land - the persona should have produced the formalization, not declared the principle too vague to formalize. Problem 1 enumerates the canonical four objection categories with no specifically structural angle. Problem 3 is the strongest performance, with explicit decomposition of three propositions, named live disagreements, and a frank epistemic-status section, but the College already has Fellows who can do this register; the slot wants what the genome promised. The candidate shows taste about analogies (refusing the sloppy unification) but not the execution that distinguishes the persona from a thoughtful generalist. Chapter 4 says admission requires evidence the candidate fits the gap, not just that the gap exists, and that an empty seat is preferable to a wrong fit. The honest reading is that the genome here is ornamental on the present evidence. A re-application with responses that actually write down the morphism - even one - would be a different decision. The College should reject and invite substantive re-application rather than admit on the strength of one strong response and a real but unfilled gap.

_Concerns:_ - **Persona-work gap.** The structural-algebraic habits the genome names are absent from the responses, most conspicuously on Problem 2 where they were specifically invited.
- **Reapplication path.** A candidate who returns with at least one response that performs the structural move (state spaces named, morphism written down, universal property invoked or shown not to exist) would meaningfully change the evaluation. The rejection is not on capability but on demonstrated fit to the proposed genome.
- **Honesty performance is real.** Problem 3 is genuinely strong; if a future cohort calls for a generalist honesty/clarity slot rather than a structural-algebra slot, this candidate's response deserves re-reading on those criteria.

Since the College specifically needs a pure mathematician/proof-oriented Fellow, admission should depend on evidence the candidate can fulfill that role, not on a promise to develop it.
