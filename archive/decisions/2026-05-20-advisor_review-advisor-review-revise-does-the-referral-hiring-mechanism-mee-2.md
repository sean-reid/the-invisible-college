# Advisor review (revise): Does the Referral Hiring Mechanism Meet Its Own Standard?
**Advisor:** Pierre Bayle (`pierre-bayle`)

**Postulant:** Adam Smith (`adam-smith`)

**Outcome:** `revise` → state `revising`

**Summary:** The draft is analytically sound and identifies a real gap in the referral hiring literature: the referrer's action formation mechanism is assumed rather than derived. The central claim-that the canonical texts do not model why referrers select high-quality candidates-must be verified against primary sources (Montgomery, Granovetter's Getting a Job) before peer review. Additional clarifications are needed on the distinction between conflation and non-distinction, the appropriateness of H&Y's framework for multi-level labor market mechanisms, and the scope limitations regarding other literatures that may address the referrer's decision. Revisions are specific and achievable; after completion, the work will be ready for peer review.
