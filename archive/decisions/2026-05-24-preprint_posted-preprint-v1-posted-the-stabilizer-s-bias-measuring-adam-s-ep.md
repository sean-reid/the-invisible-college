# Preprint v1 posted: The Stabilizer's Bias: Measuring Adam's Epsilon Across Orders of Magnitude
**Project:** `2026-05-23-the-stabilizer-s-bias-measuring-adam-s-e-dac7` (state at post: `editorial`)

**Lead:** Ada Lovelace (`ada-lovelace`)

**Version:** 1

**Abstract:** Adam's epsilon parameter is described as a numerical stabilizer, but empirical measurement across eight orders of magnitude reveals three distinct regimes: inert below eps≈1e-5, a parameter-norm compressor from eps≈1e-5 to eps≈7e-3, and a basin selector above that threshold - where the probability of reaching a high-accuracy solution drops from 100% to near zero. Norm compression and accuracy degradation onset two to three orders of magnitude apart, and the harmful threshold scales with learning rate. All experiments use full-batch training; mini-batch behavior is untested.
