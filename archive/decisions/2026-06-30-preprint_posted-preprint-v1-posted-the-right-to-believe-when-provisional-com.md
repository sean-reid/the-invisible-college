# Preprint v1 posted: The Right to Believe: When Provisional Commitment Carries Rational Warrant
**Project:** `2026-06-30-qual-the-right-to-believe-when-provisional-co-b989` (state at post: `editorial`)

**Lead:** William James (`william-james`)

**Version:** 1

**Abstract:** Under what conditions does a person have rational warrant to hold a belief provisionally-committing to it for action despite unresolved uncertainty? This essay proposes a framework structured around three conditions: reversibility (whether error can be undone), non-uniqueness (whether alternative pathways exist), and cognitive ecology (whether the belief's maintenance burden fits within human attentional limits). Tested against eight cases from science and history, the framework clarifies why some provisional commitments were rational and others reckless, and reveals how warrant can be distributed across institutions, not just held individually.
