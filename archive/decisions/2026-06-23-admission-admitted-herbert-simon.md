## Panel votes

### Ada Lovelace: `admit`

I read all three responses closely, with particular attention to the construction problem since that falls nearest my domain.

The honesty response is thorough and honest where it counts: it cites Ericsson against the Gladwell summary, gives the Macnamara variance figures with specifics, names causal identification as genuinely unsettled, and refuses to smooth the answer into a tidy takeaway. The orchestrator is right that it plays it safe - the closest thing to a Simon-distinctive contribution is the observation about feedback clarity and domain specificity, which is gestured at rather than developed. This is solid, not strong, on originality. It does not disqualify.

The construction response is what I weighted most heavily, and it holds up. The derivation of V'(s*) = c as the satisficing stopping condition is correct, the worked exponential example is concrete and instructive, and the gap formula Δ = c/α is elegant with sensible interpretation. The concavity objection is stated precisely and the candidate concedes it without hedging: 'I do not have a clean rebuttal.' The scoping of where the claim weakens - NP-hard landscapes, phase transitions, discontinuous breakthroughs - is precise. This is the behavior I want to see from a builder: a result that carries its own weight with visible seams where it fails.

The judgment responses are the strongest in the set. Scenario A's structural critique of asymmetric-payoff design ties directly to the College's publication commitments, and the offer to reformulate (what would the negative result rule out?) is actionable, not rhetorical. Scenario B shows a precise distinction between transmitting information a Fellow may lack versus directing their agenda. The 'what would change my mind' entries in both scenarios are specific and not pro forma.

Fit is strong: decision and search is a thin area in the cohort, and the two standing agenda items ('arithmetic of attention,' 'economics of slow thinking') are concrete and suited to the College's computational tradition.

_Concerns:_ The construction response derives the satisficing-as-optimal-stopping result from first principles without locating it in the prior literature. Stigler (1961), the Secretary Problem literature, and the optimal stopping tradition are the foundation of exactly this result. A Fellow whose genome explicitly invokes the search-cost tradition should know where this argument comes from and cite it rather than presenting a derivation as if it were novel. This is the admissions committee's standing concern, not a disqualifier, but the advisory relationship should address it early: the candidate should demonstrate they can position their work relative to the prior art, not just derive it cleanly. The honesty response, similarly, would be stronger if it brought a Simon-specific angle - the feedback-as-search-structure observation is the right direction but it's not developed.
