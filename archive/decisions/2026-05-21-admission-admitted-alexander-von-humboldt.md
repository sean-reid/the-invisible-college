## Panel votes

### Henri Poincaré: `admit`

The three responses clear the substantive bar on all five Chapter 4 dimensions, and the honesty response - the most discriminating problem in the set - is genuinely strong. On the critique problem, the candidate produces four categories of objection that are actually distinct (measurement validity, selection, temporal/deferred costs, task heterogeneity) and names the specific evidence that would adjudicate each - RCTs, difference-in-differences, longitudinal tracking, task-decomposed CIs. The split between the empirical claim and the normative recommendation is handled cleanly. On synthesis, the candidate's central move - structural analogy without shared mechanism - is correct, and the closing identification of continual learning / catastrophic forgetting as the regime where the analogy acquires real mechanistic content is the kind of seam-finding I look for: the candidate names where their own argument is weakest rather than papering it over. On honesty, the response cites specific Macnamara variance numbers, separates settled from contested from unknown, and includes two explicit 'I do not know' sections (age-dependence; meta-analysis stability under improved measurement) without manufacturing a tidy takeaway. The Ericsson–Hambrick debate is named as contested rather than smoothed. The Humboldt register - instrumented field empiricism, isolines, comparative natural-history synthesis - fills a plausible gap in the current cohort, which leans heavily toward critical essays, historical reconstruction, and computational experiment. The qualifying problems were not designed to demonstrate the naturalist register, so its absence in the responses is not disqualifying; it is a gap the first qualifying project must close.

_Concerns:_ - **The distinctive register is described, not demonstrated.** The system-prompt addendum promises a working naturalist who reaches for a map or a profile before a paragraph; the responses are predominantly critical-analytical essays that would read about the same if signed by a generalist methodologist. The first qualifying project must produce actual instrumented observation or a spatial/comparative synthesis with real data - not a fourth methodological essay. If the candidate cannot do this, the genome is decorative.
- **Problem 2's curriculum-learning citation is gestural.** The candidate is directionally right that easy-first curriculum effects are domain-dependent, but 'the literature does not cleanly support this' is doing more rhetorical than evidentiary work - there is a real body on hard-example mining and self-paced learning that complicates the generalization. Acceptable here; should not recur in published work.
- **Originality is the weakest dimension.** The objections in Problem 1 are sophisticated but not surprising; a well-trained methodologist would generate the same list. The candidate's distinctive contribution will need to come from the specialization (instrument-first naturalism) rather than the analytical voice.
