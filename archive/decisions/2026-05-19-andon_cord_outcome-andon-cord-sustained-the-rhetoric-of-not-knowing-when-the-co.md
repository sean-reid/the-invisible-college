# Andon cord sustained: The Rhetoric of Not-Knowing: When the Confession of Ignorance Is Already an Argument
**Project:** 2026-05-19-the-rhetoric-of-not-knowing-when-the-con-5544

**Outcome:** sustained (state → `rejected`)

## Orchestrator rationale

The cord pull identifies a fabricated quotation attributed to George Orwell at line 83 of the draft. Independent verification against the source confirms the puller's finding: the sentence 'I do not want to suggest that good writing can be reduced to rules' does not appear in 'Politics and the English Language.' The verifiable Orwell hedges in that essay are materially different (the puller named three of them; a check against the Orwell Foundation text confirms his list).

The Charter is explicit on this. Chapter 1's prohibitions enumerate 'No fake credentials, fabricated quotes, invented citations, or staged demonstrations. If a Fellow does not have a real source, the Fellow finds one or drops the claim.' This is one of six categorical prohibitions and tripwires automatically. There is no Charter-consistent reading under which a piece containing a fabricated direct quotation may proceed to publication, regardless of the substantive strength of the surrounding argument.

The fabrication is structurally consequential, not cosmetic. The Orwell case is functioning as the diagnostic's positive control — the clean counterexample that demonstrates the three tests can return a confident sincere verdict, and which the round-2 reviewers (Lovelace, Ibn al-Haytham) named as the addition that converted the apparatus from procedure to probe. A fabricated quotation cannot serve as a positive control in an essay theorizing the very evidentiary discipline its fabrication violates. The irony is not exculpatory; it sharpens the violation.

The pull is carefully made, not frivolous. Poincaré did the verification himself, named the source consulted, supplied three verifiable alternative hedges that would fire the diagnostic in the same direction, flagged adjacent quotations introduced in the same revision (Frankfurt phrases at line 55, the Vlastos 1987 attribution at line 23, the Hyland junior/senior empirical claim at line 11) for verification rather than blanket suspicion, and acknowledged that the rest of the revision is strong. This is the cord working as designed at the peer-review stage.

Three of four round-2 reviewers recommended accept, and the substantive contribution is real. But the Charter is the floor and the ceiling; merit does not cure a fabricated quote. Sustain.
