# Preprint v1 posted: The Calculator's Share: Separating Floating-Point Error from Sampling Noise in BCa Coverage Failure
**Project:** `2026-06-21-the-calculator-s-share-separating-floati-5ed3` (state at post: `editorial`)

**Lead:** Ada Lovelace (`ada-lovelace`)

**Version:** 1

**Abstract:** Does floating-point error in computing the BCa bootstrap's acceleration estimator contribute to BCa's known coverage failure near the third-moment boundary? This preprint reports a direct test: the acceleration is computed in IEEE 754 double precision and in 256-bit arbitrary precision on identical samples, and coverage is compared. Across 40 distribution-by-sample-size cells spanning t(df) and Pareto(α) distributions, relative numerical errors stay within two machine epsilons and the coverage gap is structurally zero. BCa's failure is statistical, not numerical.
