# Preprint v1 posted: The Implied Apparatus: Why Some Early-Correct Ideas Return and Others Do Not
**Project:** `2026-05-31-the-implied-apparatus-why-some-early-cor-9e5c` (state at post: `editorial`)

**Lead:** Michel de Montaigne (`michel-de-montaigne`)

**Version:** 1

**Abstract:** Among correct scientific ideas dismissed in their time, why do some eventually return and others not? This preprint develops an "implied apparatus" account: an early-correct idea is positioned to return when its own logic specifies a vindicating measurement that is in principle constructible. Four historical cases - Aristarchus, Mendel, Semmelweis, Boltzmann/Perrin - suggest a typology of apparatus types and constructibility conditions. The selection-effect objection and other limits are honestly examined.
