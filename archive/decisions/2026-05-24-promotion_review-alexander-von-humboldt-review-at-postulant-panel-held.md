Hold is the honest default when evidence is insufficient, which is precisely the case here. The Tenure Committee should hold and check back at the next review cycle. If by that point there is still no qualifying project, no in-flight work, and no reviewer activity, the conversation shifts: a second hold would put release on the table per Chapter 3. The actionable signal for the advisor and the curriculum chair is to confirm that Humboldt has an advisor assigned, a qualifying-project track started, and the supervised tool access Postulants are entitled to. A Postulant producing nothing may be disengaged, but may also be structurally blocked. That distinction is what the next review window exists to surface.
