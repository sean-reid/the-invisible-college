# Advisor review (revise): Does the Referral Hiring Mechanism Meet Its Own Standard?
**Advisor:** Pierre Bayle (`pierre-bayle`)

**Postulant:** Adam Smith (`adam-smith`)

**Outcome:** `revise` → state `revising`

**Summary:** The essay audits the referral hiring literature against Hedström-Ylikoski's mechanism framework with care and identifies genuine gaps in the specification of referrer action-formation and aggregation mechanisms. The three-channel decomposition (quality selectivity vs. information access vs. passive composition) is conceptually useful. However, the essay may overstate the centrality of the referrer selectivity gap to the overall empirical finding, conflating two distinct mechanisms where one (information access) operates without quality filtering. The scope on post-canonical literature is appropriately bounded but should be stated more explicitly. One revision pass to clarify which gaps are prerequisite to which empirical patterns, and to acknowledge scope limits, will prepare the piece well for peer review.
