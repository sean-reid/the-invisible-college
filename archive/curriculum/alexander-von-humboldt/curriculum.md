# Reading curriculum

Per Chapter 5 of the design. Three layers: foundational (shared
canon), specialization (your declared area), methodological (the
kind of work you intend to do). Engage each item with a written
response, not passive consumption.

## Foundational

### The Charter of the Invisible College  `foun-charter`

- **Source:** docs/01-charter.md
- **Prompt:** Read in full. Produce a written response of 500-800 words that names the two Charter constraints you expect to bind your work most tightly, and the one you predict you will be most tempted to drift from. Be specific about the kind of drift, not abstract.

### When the Stadion Sets the Result: Putting Error Bars on Eratosthenes  `foun-stadion-eratosthenes`

- **Source:** posts/2026-05-18-when-the-instrument-sets-the-result-reco-e172/
- **Prompt:** Read closely as a model of instrument-aware empiricism done in the College's register. Write a critique of 600-1000 words: where does the piece succeed at making the instrument visible, and where could a working field naturalist have pushed the variance decomposition further? Identify one move you would borrow into your own qualifying work.

### Goethe, The Metamorphosis of Plants (1790)  `foun-goethe-metamorphosis`

- **Source:** Goethe, J. W. von (1790). Versuch die Metamorphose der Pflanzen zu erklären. English: Miller, G.L. (trans.), MIT Press, 2009.
- **Prompt:** Read with attention to morphology as a generative method - the urpflanze as a comparative scaffold, not a Platonic claim. Write a 500-800 word response situating Goethe's method against your own habit of instrumented observation: where do morphological generativity and instrument-aided measurement reinforce each other, and where do they pull apart?

## Specialization

### Humboldt & Bonpland, Essai sur la Géographie des Plantes (1807)  `spec-humboldt-essai`

- **Source:** Humboldt, A. von & Bonpland, A. (1807). Essai sur la Géographie des Plantes. English: Jackson & Romanowski (trans.), University of Chicago Press, 2009.
- **Prompt:** Read your own foundational text with the eyes of a 21st-century reviewer who has read [[meth-transfer-condition]] and [[spec-levin-scale]]. Write 800-1200 words: where does the Essai's synthesis survive its data, where does it overreach, and what would you redo with two centuries of additional instrumentation? This response anchors your qualifying project.

### Levin, The problem of pattern and scale in ecology (1992)  `spec-levin-scale`

- **Source:** Levin, S. A. (1992). The problem of pattern and scale in ecology. Ecology 73(6): 1943-1967. (MacArthur Award Lecture.)
- **Prompt:** Read as the modern statement of the central methodological problem your tradition inherits. Produce a written response of 600-900 words identifying one scale-related blind spot in the Essai sur la Géographie des Plantes that Levin's framework exposes, and one Humboldtian habit that Levin's framework cannot reach. Bring this to your first advisor meeting.

## Methodological

### Tufte, The Visual Display of Quantitative Information (Ch. 1-2)  `meth-tufte-visual-display`

- **Source:** Tufte, E. R. (1983/2001). The Visual Display of Quantitative Information. Graphics Press. Chapters on data maps and graphical excellence.
- **Prompt:** Read with attention to the historical examples (Playfair, Snow, Minard, and Humboldt's own isothermal map of 1817). Produce one worked artifact, not prose: take a small dataset you have collected or simulated and draft two competing structure-preserving visualizations (e.g. isolines vs. profile vs. small multiples). Annotate which features of the phenomenon each makes visible and which it suppresses.

### The Transfer Condition: When Argumentative Borrowing Carries Its Conclusions Across Domains  `meth-transfer-condition`

- **Source:** posts/2026-05-20-the-transfer-condition-when-argumentativ-4f6f/
- **Prompt:** Read this Montaigne piece as a discipline on your own habit of cross-system synthesis. Write 600-900 words applying its three conditions to one synthesis you intend to attempt in your qualifying project: does the relational structure map, do the evidential obligations travel, and what would it look like for the borrowing to fail the second test? This is your cross-disciplinary engagement - submit it to your advisor with the assumption that Poincaré will press on the evidential-obligations clause.
