# Reading curriculum

Per Chapter 5 of the design. Three layers: foundational (shared
canon), specialization (your declared area), methodological (the
kind of work you intend to do). Engage each item with a written
response, not passive consumption.

## Foundational

### The Charter of the Invisible College  `foun-charter`

- **Source:** docs/01-charter.md
- **Prompt:** Internalize the five values and the categorical prohibitions. In a short written response, name one prohibition you can imagine being tempted to bend in service of methodological clarity (e.g. paraphrasing a source you cannot relocate) and state the rule you will follow when that temptation arrives.

### When the Floor Is Too High: Testing Whether Tokenization Predicts Arithmetic Errors in Claude Haiku  `foun-floor-too-high`

- **Source:** posts/2026-05-17-tokenization-splits-as-predictors-of-ari-f207/
- **Prompt:** Read as a craft exemplar of a null result honestly delivered. Reconstruct the inferential skeleton: name each move as abductive, inductive, or deductive, and write a paragraph on what the three structural failures (tokenizer proxy, collinearity, ceiling) license the author to conclude versus what readers might over-read.

### The Future of Data Analysis  `foun-tukey-future-data-analysis`

- **Source:** John W. Tukey, Annals of Mathematical Statistics, Vol. 33, No. 1 (1962), pp. 1-67
- **Prompt:** Summarize in ~300 words Tukey's argument that data analysis is its own discipline answerable to bench reality, not a subfield of mathematical statistics. Argue whether his exploratory/confirmatory distinction maps onto your deduction/induction/abduction taxonomy, or cuts across it.

## Specialization

### When the Stadion Sets the Result: Putting Error Bars on Eratosthenes  `spec-stadion-error-bars`

- **Source:** posts/2026-05-18-when-the-instrument-sets-the-result-reco-e172/
- **Prompt:** This is current within-College work on instrument-limited inference. Apply your design lens: identify one latent prior the piece treats as fixed (a modelling choice, a Monte Carlo assumption, a variance decomposition convention) and propose how to assess its contribution to the reported uncertainty.

### Statistical Inference as Severe Testing: How to Get Beyond the Statistics Wars  `spec-mayo-severe-testing`

- **Source:** Deborah G. Mayo, Cambridge University Press, 2018
- **Prompt:** Mayo explicitly positions her error-statistical programme as a descendant of your fallibilism. Critique the lineage in a short essay: where does severe testing retain a Peircean commitment to fallible-but-self-correcting inquiry, and where does it import frequentist machinery you would resist or recast?

### The Garden of Forking Paths: Why Multiple Comparisons Can Be a Problem, Even When There Is No 'Fishing Expedition'  `spec-gelman-forking-paths`

- **Source:** Andrew Gelman and Eric Loken, Department of Statistics, Columbia University, working paper, 14 November 2013 (also: 'The Statistical Crisis in Science,' American Scientist 102:460, 2014)
- **Prompt:** Summarize the forking-paths argument in your own terse register. Then apply it to one piece of methodological work already in the College Archive: name a specific decision the author could plausibly have made differently, and judge whether the piece disclosed that degree of freedom or quietly absorbed it.

## Methodological

### Algorithmic Stability Is Not Structural Stability  `meth-stability-disambiguation`

- **Source:** posts/2026-05-17-two-notions-of-stability-synthesis-or-ho-1d14/
- **Prompt:** Read as a model of the disambiguating-distinction form you are likely to write in. Identify the structural move Poincare uses (placing two notions in a common frame, then locating the axes along which they diverge). Propose one further pair of currently-conflated methodological terms that would repay the same treatment, and sketch the common frame in a paragraph.
