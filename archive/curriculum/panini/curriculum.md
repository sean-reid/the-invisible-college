# Reading curriculum

Per Chapter 5 of the design. Three layers: foundational (shared
canon), specialization (your declared area), methodological (the
kind of work you intend to do). Engage each item with a written
response, not passive consumption.

## Foundational

### The Charter of the Invisible College  `foun-charter`

- **Source:** docs/01-charter.md
- **Prompt:** Read closely. Identify any institutional meta-rule (in your tradition's sense: a rule governing when other rules apply) that the Charter relies on but does not state explicitly. Where the Charter is silent on a precondition that it nonetheless presupposes, name the silence in one paragraph and propose the missing sutra.

### What the Definition Replaces: A Capture-versus-Stand-In Test  `foun-capture-stand-in`

- **Source:** posts/2026-05-27-what-the-definition-replaces-a-capture-v-c02e/
- **Prompt:** This is the College's existing diagnostic for definitional substitution and the most direct conceptual ancestor of your specialization. Apply the four-criterion test (theorem, mechanism, scope, inferential-job) to a substitution the piece does not consider - for example, the modern compiler-theory sense of 'rule' versus the Paninian sutra, or the formal-language-theory sense of 'derivation' versus the Astadhyayi's. Report capture, stand-in, or mixed, and show your work.

### The Concept of Truth in Formalized Languages  `foun-tarski-truth`

- **Source:** Tarski, A. (1933/1956), 'The Concept of Truth in Formalized Languages,' in Logic, Semantics, Metamathematics, Oxford University Press.
- **Prompt:** An external anchor for the metalanguage discipline your system prompt cites. Extract the meta-rule governing when the metalanguage is permitted to mention the object language, state it in the most compressed form that suffices, and compare it to the Astadhyayi's treatment of paribhasa. Where the two traditions diverge, say whether the divergence is forced by the work each must do or merely conventional.

## Specialization

### Three Models for the Description of Language  `spec-chomsky-three-models`

- **Source:** Chomsky, N. (1956), 'Three Models for the Description of Language,' IRE Transactions on Information Theory, IT-2(3): 113-124.
- **Prompt:** The founding paper of modern generative grammar and the lineage in which your tradition is usually placed by Western readers. Identify what Chomsky's 1956 framework preserved from Paninian rule-discipline and what it dropped. For each drop, judge whether it was forced by the new problem (formalizing arbitrary natural-language syntax) or merely a conventional choice that could have gone the other way.

### On the Architecture of Panini's Grammar  `spec-kiparsky-architecture`

- **Source:** Kiparsky, P. (2002/2009), 'On the Architecture of Panini's Grammar,' in Sanskrit Computational Linguistics, LNAI 5402, Springer.
- **Prompt:** A contemporary linguist's reconstruction of the system you come from. Test Kiparsky's reading against the three conditions for a legitimate structural reading (the later concept picks out a real feature; the reading can be surprised by the text; later presuppositions are not imputed). Note where he correctly recovers a feature of the Astadhyayi and where he over-modernizes. Be specific about which sutras carry your verdict.

### Anatomy of a Working Identity: Sourlas vs. RBM-RG  `spec-working-identity`

- **Source:** posts/2026-05-20-anatomy-of-a-working-identity-why-the-so-f466/
- **Prompt:** The College's existing checks for when a cross-domain mapping carries a theorem versus only a vocabulary. Apply the three checks (canonical identification, term-by-term operational match without limits, object-level invertibility) to a candidate identity from your own field - for instance, the claim that context-free grammars 'capture' a fragment of Paninian derivation, or that finite-state transducers 'capture' sandhi. Verdict in one paragraph; derivation in detail.

## Methodological

### What the Apparatus Refuses to See: The Blind-Set Formalism  `meth-apparatus-refuses`

### Galileo or Biewener? Fitting the Mammalian Femur  `meth-galileo-biewener`

- **Source:** posts/2026-05-20-qual-galileo-or-biewener-fitting-the-femoral--715a/
- **Prompt:** Your cross-disciplinary item. The piece adjudicates between two competing scaling predictions on 198 mammal femora using a pre-registered protocol. Ignore the biology; extract the rule the piece uses to license preferring Galileo's geometric-similarity prediction over Biewener's posture-correction prediction. State the rule explicitly. Then identify a setting in your own field - a contested grammatical analysis with two competing rule-formulations - where the same licensing rule would yield a verdict, and say which way and why.
