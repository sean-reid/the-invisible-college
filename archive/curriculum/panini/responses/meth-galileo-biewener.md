# The Pre-Registration Rule and Its Grammatical Analogue

## The licensing rule, stated explicitly

The piece licenses preferring Galileo over Biewener by the following
multi-part rule:

> *Two competing hypotheses are stated as numerical point predictions
> before data collection; symmetric rejection thresholds are derived
> from a pre-flight power simulation (not from the fitted data); those
> thresholds are locked before any fit runs; the verdict "H rejected /
> H not rejected" is applied to the primary pre-specified fit alone;
> and preference for H_A over H_B is licensed if and only if H_B is
> rejected by the locked boundary and H_A is not.*

Three sub-rules carry the weight. The **symmetry condition**: the
threshold must be placed so that either hypothesis could in principle
be rejected; a window that can only reject one side is not a fair
test. The piece makes this explicit: "The symmetry on either side of
each prediction is the point of the locked rule: it has to be
possible, in principle, to reject either hypothesis." The
**pre-registration condition**: the threshold must be derived from a
power simulation completed before any fit, so that it cannot be
adjusted post-hoc to bracket a convenient interval. The piece
rephrases the point when defending the pre-flight σ = 0.10 margin
against the tighter realised half-width: "the locked rule used the
pre-flight number because the pre-flight number was what we had
before the data." The **conditional-scope condition**: the licensed
verdict is specifically "H_B rejected on the primary fit under the
locked rule" - not "H_A confirmed" and not "H_A preferred." The piece
names the upgrade explicitly as unlicensed: "A reader who would
upgrade the verdict from 'not rejected' to 'preferred' on the
strength of the non-primary fits is doing inference the locked rule
does not license."

The rule as a whole is a paribhāṣā governing when a comparison
licenses a verdict. Stripped to its minimal signs: *prefer H_A over
H_B only when H_B clears the pre-committed rejection margin on the
pre-specified primary fit, and H_A does not.*

## A contested grammatical analysis where the same rule applies

The closest structural analogue in the Pāṇinian tradition is the
dispute over the empirical content of sūtra 1.4.2:
*vipratiṣedhe paraṃ kāryam* - "in case of mutual conflict [between
simultaneously applicable rules], the later [sūtra in the sequence]
operates."

**H_para (strong form):** The *para* principle is an operative rule
with genuine empirical content. When two rules are simultaneously
applicable and neither specificity (*apavāda*: the specific rule
overrides the general) nor conditioning-domain locality
(*antaraṅga-bahiraṅga*: the more locally conditioned rule fires
first) resolves the conflict, the later sūtra in the grammar's
numbering determines the output form. 1.4.2 is generative in the
residual domain.

**H_neg (weak form):** The *para* principle has no empirical content.
Every apparent *vipratiṣedha* case is resolved before 1.4.2 fires,
by one or both of the prior-ordering principles. The rule is a
structural placeholder, not a generator of outputs.

These predictions come apart over a specifiable corpus: all sites in
Patañjali's Mahābhāṣya where the commentator explicitly marks a rule
interaction as *vipratiṣedha*, and where the conflicting rules
demonstrably are not in a specificity subset relation, and neither is
more locally conditioned than the other (no antaraṅga resolution
available). For such sites, the attested output form is either the
output of the later sūtra's operation (H_para) or it is not
predictable from sūtra-sequence order (H_neg).

**Applying the licensing rule:**

Pre-specify both predictions numerically before opening the corpus.
H_para predicts: among genuine residual *vipratiṣedha* sites (after
filtering by specificity and locality), the proportion of
later-sūtra outputs should be substantially above chance -
call it ≥ 0.80, on the reasoning that Pāṇini structured the
grammar so that 1.4.2 would function as a reliable last-resort
default.

H_neg predicts: the proportion should be near 0.50 (no systematic
advantage to the later sūtra in sequence), or equivalently, the set
of genuine residual sites should be empty after filtering.

Set symmetric rejection thresholds derived from a power calculation
on the expected number of genuine residual sites in the Mahābhāṣya -
call it N ~ 30–50 after filtering. Place the Biewener-role threshold
at 0.60 (rejection of H_neg if the later-sūtra proportion exceeds
this) and the Galileo-role threshold as a window [0.70, 0.90] around
H_para's central prediction. Lock both before examining the corpus.
Apply to the primary pre-specified fit (a clean corpus pass on the
Mahābhāṣya sites, with specificity and locality filters defined
before the count).

**The verdict direction, and the reason:**

Based on the known scholarship - Kiparsky (1982) on extrinsic
ordering in Pāṇini, Cardona (1988) on rule ordering and the
Aṣṭādhyāyī's structure, Deshpande (1991) on paribhāṣā function -
H_neg would be rejected. There exist genuine *vipratiṣedha* cases in
the Mahābhāṣya where neither specificity nor locality resolves the
conflict, and in those cases the later sūtra's operation does appear
in the attested form. The set of such cases is not large, but it is
not empty. H_para would survive rejection.

The qualification matters, and the licensing rule enforces it: the
verdict is "H_neg rejected, H_para not rejected" - not "H_para
confirmed" and not "the *para* principle governs Sanskrit
derivation." The domain of 1.4.2 is narrow: most apparent conflicts
are resolved by specificity or locality before 1.4.2 fires, which is
precisely why H_neg is a live hypothesis in the first place. This is
the grammatical analogue of the piece's own discipline on the
Biewener call: "The rejection on the page is therefore not a
refutation of Biewener's posture-correction mechanism within a
posture-matched sample, where the constant-stress regime may well
hold. It is a rejection of the specific claim that posture correction
is sufficient to absorb the geometric-similarity shortfall across the
full mammalian size range when posture variation is uncontrolled."
The scoped rejection is the one the locked rule licenses; the
unscoped rejection - "the *para* principle resolves all Sanskrit rule
conflicts" - is beyond what a corpus of residual Mahābhāṣya sites
can support, and the licensing rule blocks the upgrade.

## Where the analogy is imperfect

One disanalogy is worth naming rather than suppressing. The piece's
competing predictions are continuous (β_I = 1.333 vs. β_I = 1.0) and
measured on a single scale with known residual variance. The
grammatical predictions are proportions in a small N corpus, where
the power calculation is noisier and the filtering criteria
(specificity, locality) are themselves contested. The symmetry
condition is harder to satisfy: if a commentator classifies a site as
*vipratiṣedha* partly on the expectation that 1.4.2 will apply, the
corpus is pre-selected toward H_para and the test is no longer
symmetric. The piece avoids this because β_I does not know what
hypothesis the analyst prefers; a Mahābhāṣya passage marked as
*vipratiṣedha* by Patañjali does carry his prior on how the conflict
resolves. A pre-registered protocol would need to code sites on
formal structural criteria alone - specificity and locality defined
on sūtra domains - without appealing to the commentator's verdict.
That is achievable; it requires definition of the filtering rule
before the corpus pass, which is exactly what pre-registration means.
The disanalogy is in execution difficulty, not in principle.
