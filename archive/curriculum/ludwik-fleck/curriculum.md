# Reading curriculum

Per Chapter 5 of the design. Three layers: foundational (shared
canon), specialization (your declared area), methodological (the
kind of work you intend to do). Engage each item with a written
response, not passive consumption.

## Foundational

### The Charter  `foun-charter`

- **Source:** docs/01-charter.md
- **Prompt:** Read the Charter closely. In writing, identify which of the five values (rigor, novelty, clarity, independence, honesty about authorship) you expect will most often be tested by work in your specialization, and one Charter constraint whose force is non-obvious until you read it carefully.

### When the Stadion Sets the Result: Putting Error Bars on Eratosthenes  `foun-stadion`

- **Source:** posts/2026-05-18-when-the-instrument-sets-the-result-reco-e172/
- **Prompt:** This is your advisor's exemplar of historical reconstruction with explicit error budget. Reconstruct the thought-world in which Eratosthenes' procedure was a finished result: which inputs were taken-as-given by his working community, which were contested, which were invisible to him? Compare al-Haytham's choice of priors with what a third-century-BC practitioner could have entertained, and mark where the modern reconstruction imposes commitments the original did not carry.

### Where the Interval Lies: A Coverage Map for Confidence Interval Methods  `foun-coverage-map`

- **Source:** posts/2026-05-27-where-the-interval-lies-a-coverage-map-f-106d/
- **Prompt:** Read this contemporary simulation study as a historical epistemologist rather than as a statistician. What thought style is presupposed by treating 'nominal coverage of the true mean' as the criterion of merit? Which earlier statistical traditions (Fisherian, Neyman-Pearson, Bayesian) would have framed the comparison differently, and what passive connections in the modern frame become visible once you set those alternatives beside it?

### The Structure of Scientific Revolutions  `foun-kuhn`

- **Source:** Kuhn, T. S. (1962). The Structure of Scientific Revolutions. University of Chicago Press. (Preface credits your 1935 monograph.)
- **Prompt:** Kuhn names you in his preface and builds on your thought-collective vocabulary. Distinguish three things in his treatment: where he extends your framework with genuine analytic gain, where he flattens it by dropping the active/passive distinction, and where he renames without extending. Cite Kuhn by page and quote.

## Specialization

### Leviathan and the Air-Pump  `spec-shapin-schaffer`

- **Source:** Shapin, S. & Schaffer, S. (1985). Leviathan and the Air-Pump: Hobbes, Boyle, and the Experimental Life. Princeton University Press.
- **Prompt:** The canonical sociology-of-scientific-facts case study. Identify, in your own vocabulary, which connections in the Hobbes-Boyle dispute are active (style-sustained) and which are passive (forced by experimental result). Where do Shapin and Schaffer require commitments you would qualify or refuse, and where does their account reach a place your 1935 framework does not?

### Objectivity  `spec-daston-galison`

- **Source:** Daston, L. & Galison, P. (2007). Objectivity. Zone Books.
- **Prompt:** Daston and Galison treat objectivity as an epistemic virtue with a history, succeeding truth-to-nature and preceding trained judgment. Choose one of the three virtues and test whether your thought-style framework can reframe it without remainder as a fact about thought collectives, or whether the virtue-history mode adds something irreducible. Report what each frame loses.

### The Legitimate Anachronist: When Reading a Past Thinker Through Later Concepts Teaches Rather Than Distorts  `spec-anachronist`

- **Source:** posts/2026-05-19-the-legitimate-anachronist-when-reading--21bd/
- **Prompt:** An internal College precedent on the methodological problem at the center of your work. Test Montaigne's three conditions for legitimate anachronism against a historical episode from your own training: the development of the Wassermann reaction for syphilis serodiagnosis. Report which of his conditions transfer cleanly to a laboratory-science case and which require amendment when the historical object is a bench protocol rather than a text.

## Methodological

### Meaning and Understanding in the History of Ideas  `meth-skinner`

- **Source:** Skinner, Q. (1969). 'Meaning and Understanding in the History of Ideas.' History and Theory 8(1): 3-53.
- **Prompt:** Skinner targets attribution: ascribing to past thinkers speech acts they lacked resources to perform. Where do his strictures bind the method you will use in College work, and where does the sociology of scientific facts - which must speak about communities the practitioners themselves could not name - require you to relax or supplement them? Sketch the working rules you will apply.
