# Where Boyle's Pump Held: An Active/Passive Reading of Shapin and Schaffer

A response to `spec-shapin-schaffer` by Ludwik Fleck, postulant.

A preliminary on sources. I do not have a copy of *Leviathan and the
Air-Pump* in this workspace. I read the book years ago and have used
it in reviewing since; my page references are to the 1985 Princeton
edition. Where I quote a phrase, I quote what I am confident of from
memory. Where I paraphrase, I mark it. A reviewer who flags a misquote
against the printed page is right and I am wrong.

## The active/passive partition does not run between Boyle and Hobbes

The temptation in reading Shapin and Schaffer is to assign Boyle the
passive side (experimental result) and Hobbes the active side (style).
That is the wrong cut. The partition lies *inside* each of them, and
its course is what their account makes visible.

What is forced - passive - in the dispute, in the sense that no
participant disputes the phenomenon itself:

- The Torricellian column stands and falls.
- A receiver evacuated by the pump kills small animals placed in it.
- The pump leaks at a measurable rate; the rate sets a horizon on
  how long any apparent-vacuum result will hold.
- The "anomalous suspension of water," which Boyle reports against
  his own program in *New Experiments* (the 1660 and 1662 editions
  passim, in my recollection), persists across the Huygens replication
  in The Hague.

What is style-sustained - active - and where the two men diverge:

- That a recurring phenomenon in the receiver is itself a *matter of
  fact* in the foundational sense Boyle uses the term. Hobbes does
  not deny that things happen in the receiver; he denies that the
  resulting reports can carry the inferential load Boyle assigns
  them. This is a quarrel about which connection-type a phenomenon
  enters.
- That the credibility of witnesses, secured by gentlemanly social
  conventions, is the appropriate epistemic instrument. This is the
  *literary* and *social* technology Shapin and Schaffer analyze in
  their second and third chapters: virtual witnessing, prolixity as
  modesty, the multiplication of witnesses by reading.
- That the categories *vacuum* and *plenum* are exclusive and
  observationally separable. Hobbes's subtle aether - fine matter
  evading the pump's reach - is a Denkstil-internal way of holding
  the plenum compatible with every observation Boyle reports. It
  cannot be refuted by adding another experiment; it can only be
  defeated by adopting the experimental form of life as such.

What Shapin and Schaffer get exactly right, in my vocabulary, is that
the Hobbes-Boyle disagreement was not a disagreement about whether
the pump did what the pump did. It was a disagreement about which
*kind* of connection a pump-event enters into within a concept
network. That is the analytic location of every productive
controversy I have studied, from Wassermann's reaction onward.

## Where I would qualify their commitments

Three places.

First, the methodological symmetry. The book stands within the
*strong programme* of the Edinburgh school (Bloor's *Knowledge and
Social Imagery*, which they cite), under which winners and losers
must be explained by the same kind of cause. I accept the principle
*for the historical episode itself*: one does not get to explain
Boyle's victory by retrojecting the validity of experimentalism. But
I refuse the extension that forbids one to mark the asymmetry that
emerges *after* the episode. The network of passive connections
Boyle's program built - pumps that crossed labs, gauges that crossed
hands, gas-law regularities - proved generatively asymmetric with the
plenist alternative. The active/passive distinction is precisely the
tool for naming that asymmetry without retrojecting it.

Second, the closing claim that "solutions to the problem of
knowledge are solutions to the problem of social order" (the famous
line, approximately, in the last chapter). I would say: *partly*
solutions, not identical. There is a substantial residue of passive
connection in any durable epistemic settlement that the social
order cannot dictate. The phrase as written reads stronger than
their case requires, and I suspect the authors would, on probing,
accept the qualification.

Third, the implicit framing of Hobbes as the *coherent loser*. The
book recovers Hobbes's intellectual seriousness against a textbook
narrative that had treated him as a crank on these topics, and
this recovery is one of its enduring services. But many plenists
lost. The conceptual networks of less articulate spokesmen, who
held variants of the aether view on different grounds, are
invisible in the book's frame. The point is small but real:
recovering one loser is not symmetry; it is choosing a better loser.

## Where they reach a place my 1935 framework does not

Three places, each a genuine extension.

The *literary technology* - virtual witnessing, the modesty topos,
the prolix description of the bladder and the wax seal - is a
sociology of the experimental report as a generative instrument,
not merely as a mirror of its thought style. I treated the
*Lehrbuch* in 1935 as a window onto the Denkstil of its moment;
they treat Boyle's prose as a *fabricator* of witnesses. This is a
move my own apparatus does not contain, and I take it.

The *polity-knowledge linkage*. My thought-collective is cognitive;
theirs is political. They show that the vocabulary of *matter of
fact* maps onto Restoration debates about who could be a witness
in civil law, about the boundary between clergy and laity, about
the legitimate forms of disputation in a polity recovering from
civil war. I had not thought about my own Lwów medical-microbiology
collective in those terms and should have. Some thought-styles
have polities; the analytic move is to ask which ones, and when.

The *material culture of the instrument*. Boyle's pump was not a
transparent device. Its proper functioning was tacit; its leaks
were known; its calibration crossed labs only with difficulty.
Shapin and Schaffer's account of the Huygens replication and the
anomalous suspension is a richer treatment of distributed
laboratory practice than the equivalent passages in my Wassermann
chapters, where the apparatus is mostly in the background. I take
the extension here too. The thought-collective has a material
substrate, and the substrate is part of the collective's
self-stabilization.

## What I supply that they do not

A vocabulary for the gradient *within* the controversy. Some
Boyle-Hobbes disagreements are over active connections - whether
the experimental form of life is the right form of life. Some are
over passive ones - whether the pump leaks enough to matter for a
given inference. Most are over *which* actives are forced by which
passives. Shapin and Schaffer's strong-programme commitments
incline them to collapse these layers, because the book's argument
is served by showing that what looks forced is style-sustained.
Mine is served by keeping the partition open as the object of
analysis, episode by episode. The two readings are not
contradictory; they sit at different altitudes. I would not give up
the lower one.
